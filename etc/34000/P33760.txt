Perspectors T1(X(i), X(j)) involving triangles inscribed in a circumconic:  X(33760)-X(33780)
</p>
<img src="rightri.svg" style="height:30px" alt="rightri">

<div class="preamble-sect">
<p>
Centers X(33760)-X(33780) were contributed by Clark Kimberling and Peter Moses, August 4, 2019.
</p>
<p>
Suppose that P = p : q : r and U = u : v : w (barycentrics) are points.  Define M(P,U) = p/(v - w) : q/(w - u) : r/(u - v), so that M(P,U) lies on the circumconic p/x + q/y + r/z = 0.
</p>
<p>
Let T<sub>1</sub> be the anticevian triangle of P, given by vertices<br>
A' = -p : q : r<br>
B' = p : - q : r<br>
C' = p : q : -r
</p>
<p>
Let T<sub>2</sub> be the anticevian triangle of U, given by vertices<br>
A" = -u : v : w<br>
B" = u : - v : w<br>
C" = u : v : -w
</p>
<p>
Let M(P,T<sub>2</sub>) denote the triangle with vertices M(P,A"), M(P,B"), M(P,C"). The triangles T<sub>1</sub> and M(T<sub>2</sub>) are perspective, and their perspector is given by
</p>
<p>
T1(P,U) = p*(u^2 + uv + uw - vw) : q*(v^2 + vw + vu - wu) : r*(w^2 + wu + wv - uv)
</p>
