Perspectors of triangles inscribed in the circumcircle: X(33628)-X(33636)
</p>
<img src="rightri.svg" style="height:30px" alt="rightri">
</header>
<div class="preamble-sect">
<p>
This preamble and centers X(33628)-X(33636) were contributed by Clark Kimberling and Peter Moses, July 21, 2019. Suppose that P = p : q : r is a point. The points
</p>
<p>
q - r : r - p : p - q&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; and &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; 2p - q - r : 2q - r - p : 2r - p - q
</p>
<p>
clearly lie on the line at infinity, so that their isogonal conjugates,
</p>
<p>
g(P) = a^2/(q-r) : b^2/(r-p) : c^2/(p-q) &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; and &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; h(P) = a^2/(2p-q-r) : b^2/(2q-r-p) : c^2/(2r-p-q),
</p>
<p>
lie on the circumcircle.  Thus, if T is a central triangle Then g(T)  and h(T) are central triangles inscribed in the circumcircle.  Suppose that X = x : y : z is a triangle center. Let T(1) be the central triangle with A-vertex  -x : y : z, and let T(2) be the central triangle with A-vertex 0 : 1/y : 1/z.
</p>
<p>
Theorem: the triangles g(T(1)) and h(T(2)) are perspective, and their perspector is
</p>
<p>
M(X) = a^2(-3x+y+z)/(y+z) : b^2(-3y+z+x)/(z+x) : c^2(-3z-x+y)/(x+y).
</p>
<p>
If X is on the circumcircle, then M(X) is on the line X(6)X', where X' is the X(3)-cross conjugate of X. If X is on the line at infinity, then M(X) = X(6). The appearance of (i,j) in the following list means that M(X(i)) = X(j):
</p>
<p>
(1,33628),
(2,2),
(3,33629),
(4,33630),
(5,33631),
(6,33632),
(7,33633),
(8,3973),
(9,33634),
(10, 33635),
(20,33636)
</p>
<p>
If X is on the Kiepert circumhyperbola, KCH, of the anticomplementary triangle, then M(X) is on the Brocard axis. Points on KCH include X(i) for these i: {1, 2 , 20, 63, 147, 194, 368, 487, 488, 616, 617, 627, 628, 1670, 1671, 1764, 2128, 2582, 2583, 2896, 3413, 3414, 6194, 6462, 6463, 7616, 8591, 8782, 9742, 10336, 11148, 13174, 13678, 13798, 16552, 16563, 17147, 18301, 18596, 20371, 21378, 30562, 30564, 30579, 33404, 33405, 33608, 33609, 33610, 33611, 33612, 33613}
</p>
