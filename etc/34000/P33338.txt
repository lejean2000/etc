Half-squares and half-diamonds triangles: X(33338)-X(33503)
</p>
<img src="rightri.svg" style="height:30px" alt="rightri">
</header>
<div class="preamble-sect">
<p>
This preamble and centers X(33338)-X(33503) were contributed by CÃ©sar Eliud Lozada, June 27, 2019.
</p>
<p>
<i>In a triangle ABC, build the triangles T<sub>a</sub> = AB'C', T<sub>b</sub> = BC'A' and T<sub>c</sub> = CA'B', all isosceles with the same vertical angle &theta; in A, B, C, respectively</i>. Barycentrics coordinates of A' are
</p>
<p>
&emsp; &emsp; A' = (a^2*sin(&theta;) + S)/a : b*sin(C-&theta;) : c*sin(B-&theta;)
<br>
<br>and cyclically for B' and C'.
</p>
<p>
There are two triads of such triangles, according as T<sub>a</sub>, T<sub>b</sub>, T<sub>c</sub> have all the same or opposite orientation as ABC (this fact can be translated by using &plusmn;&theta;).  Let A'B' C' and A"B"C" be the respective resulting triangles. Then
</p>

<ol>
<li>
    A'B'C' and A"B"C" are both perspective to ABC with perspectors Q = a*csc(A - &theta;) : :  (barycentrics). These perspectors lie on the Kiepert hyperbola of ABC and their isogonal conjugates satisfy
<br>
&emsp; &emsp; OQ<sup>-1</sup> = OK*cos(&theta;)*cos(&omega;)/cos(&theta; + &omega;), where O=X(3), K=X(6) and &omega; is the Brocard angle of ABC.
</li>
<li>
    A'B'C' and A"B"C" are perspective with perspector Q* = cos(A)*cos(&theta;)+cos(B)*cos(C) : :. Q* lies on the Euler line of ABC and OQ* = OH/(1+2*cos(&theta;)), where H=X(4).
</li>
<li>
    ABC is orthologic to both A'B'C' and A"B"C". The orthologic centers A'B'C'&rarr;ABC and A"B"C"&rarr;ABC are both Q and the reciprocal orthologic centers lie also on the Kiepert hyperbola of ABC.
</li>
</ol>

<p>
Two particular cases are considered here: &theta; = &plusmn;&pi;/2 and &theta; = &plusmn;&pi;/3. For the first case, T<sub>a</sub> is a half-square (and similarly for T<sub>b</sub>, T<sub>c</sub>) and, for the second case, these three triangles are equilateral and we can regard T<sub>a</sub> as a half-diamond. These names are introduced here for the triangles A'B'C' and A"B"C". Their A-vertex barycentrics coordinates are<br>
<br>
&emsp;&emsp;<b>1st half-squares triangle,</b> with A' = -(a^2-S)/a : b*cos(C) : c*cos(B) = -(a^2+S) : SC : SB<br>
<br>
&emsp;&emsp;<b>2nd half-squares triangle,</b> with A" = -(a^2+S)/a : b*cos(C) : c*cos(B) = -(a^2-S) : SC : SB<br>
<br>
&emsp;&emsp;<b>1st half-diamonds triangle,</b> with A' = -(sqrt(3)*a^2-2*S)/(2*a) : b*cos(C-&pi;/6) : c*cos(B-&pi;/6) = -(sqrt(3)*a^2-2*S) : sqrt(3)*SC+S : sqrt(3)*SB+S<br>
<br>
&emsp;&emsp;<b>2nd half-diamonds triangle,</b> with A" = -(sqrt(3)*a^2+2*S)/(2*a) : b*cos(C+&pi;/6) : c*cos(B+&pi;/6) = -(sqrt(3)*a^2+2*S) : sqrt(3)*SC-S : sqrt(3)*SB-S
</p>
<p>Moreover, when &theta; = &pi;/3, the centers A<sub>o</sub>, B<sub>o</sub>, C<sub>o</sub> of the equilateral triangles T<sub>a</sub>, T<sub>b</sub> and T<sub>c</sub> are vertices of an equilateral triangle. These triangles are here named the <b>1st and 2nd diamonds-central triangles</b>. They have center X(2) and A-vertices<br>
<br>
&emsp;&emsp;<b>1st half-diamonds-central triangle:</b> A'<sub>o</sub> = -(a^2+2*sqrt(3)*S) : SC-sqrt(3)*S : SB-sqrt(3)*S<br>
<br>
&emsp;&emsp;<b>2nd half-diamonds-central triangle:</b> A"<sub>o</sub> = -(a^2-2*sqrt(3)*S) : SC+sqrt(3)*S : SB+sqrt(3)*S
</p>
<p>Also, for any value of &theta;, the 1st or 2nd Fermat points of T<sub>a</sub>, T<sub>b</sub>, T<sub>c</sub> are the vertices of other equilateral triangles.</p>
<p>
A complete list of triangles and centers related with the six triangles above defined can be seen <a href="half_squares_lists.pdf">here</a>.
</p>
