<p>
Centers X(35537)-X(35543) were contributed by Peter Moses, July 11, 2019.
</p>
<p>
The name <b>Stammler reflection hyperbola</b>, denoted by SRH, refers to the reflection of the Stammler hyperbola in X(3).  Let T be the hexyl triangle.  Then SRH is the circumhyperbola of T that passes through the orthocenter and nine-point center of T; i.e., SRH = ABCHN-of-T.  Moreover,
</p>
<p>
SRH = Feuerbach hyperbola of tangential triangle of Thomson triangle<br>
SRH = isogonal conjugate of the Euler line of ABC wrt the tangential triangle of Thomson triangle<br>
</p>
<p>
SHR has center X(74), perspector X(2693), and equation
</p>
<p>
b^2 c^2 (b^2-c^2) ((3 a^4-2 a^2 b^2-b^4-2 a^2 c^2+2 b^2 c^2-c^4) x^2 + 4 a^2 (-a^2+b^2+c^2) y z) + (cyclic) = 0
</p>
<p>
SRH passes through the vertices of these triangles:
</p>
<p>
hexyl (TCCT 6.36)<br>
excentral triangle of Thomson triangle (CTC Q113)<br>
tangential triangle of Thomson triangle<br>
anti-Hutson intouch triangle (see X(11363))
</p>
<p>
SRH passes through X(i) for these i: 3, 40, 64, 1350, 2574, 2575, 5373, 5646, 9914, 10117, 10620, 11472, 12163, 12301, 12302, 12307, 14926, 15622, 16010, 19376, 22549, 32345, 33535, 33536, 33537, 33538, 33539, 33540, 33541, 33542, 33543
</p>
<p>
Suppose that P = p : q : r (barycentrics).  Define the Gibert-Euler&rarr;SRH mapping by GE(P) = a^2(8 b^2 c^2 p^2 - 2 c^2 (a^2 - c^2) q^2 - 2 b^2 (a^2 - b^2) r^2 - (a^2 (a^2 - b^2 - c^2) - 4 b^2 c^2) q r - b^2 (a^2 - b^2 - 7 c^2) r p - c^2 (a^2 - 7 b^2 - c^2) p q) : : .  If P is on the Euler line, then GE(P) is on SRH.  The appearance of k(i,j) in the following list means that GE(X(i)) = X(j):
