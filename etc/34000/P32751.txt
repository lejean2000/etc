<p>
Homogeneous tripolar coordinates (henceforth simply <b>tripolars</b>) for a point X are any triple x : y : z of numbers (or functions of a,b,c) that are respectively proportional to the distances |AX|, |BX|, |CX|.  A point X and its circumcircle-inverse have identical tripolars.  The appearance of i, j; f(a,b,c) in the following table means that X(i) and X(j) are such a pair, with tripolars f(a,b,c,) : f(b,c,a) : f(c,a,b).

<p>
If X has tripolars x: y : z, then barycentrics for X are
</p>
<p>
a^2 (D SA + T S) : : and a^2( D SA - T S
) : : , where
</p>
<p>
SA = b c cos A, S = 2 area(ABC), T = b^2 y^2 + c^2 z^2 - a^2 x^2, and
</p>
<p>
D = Sqrt[(a x + b y + c z)(- a x + b y + c z)(a x - b y + c z)(a x + b y - c z)];
</p>
<p>
For details, see X(5002).  Further developments are given at
</p>
<p>
Antreas P. Hatzipolakis, Floor van Lamoen, Barry Wolk, and Paul Yiu, <a href="http://forumgeom.fau.edu/FG2001volume1/FG200109.pdf">Concurrency of Four Euler Lines</a>
</p>
<p>
Albrecht Hess, <a href="http://forumgeom.fau.edu/FG2015volume15/FG201525.pdf">Transforming Tripolar into Barycentric Coordinates</a>
