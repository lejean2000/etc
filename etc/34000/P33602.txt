<p class="preamble-title">
Fermat-Dao-Nhi triangles and related centers: X(33602)-X(33627)
</p>
<img src="rightri.svg" style="height:30px" alt="rightri">
</header>
<div class="preamble-sect">
<p>
This preamble and centers X(33602)-X(33627) were contributed by César Eliud Lozada, July 19, 2019.
</p>
<p>Dao Thanh Oai and Hoang Le Ngoc Nhi have presented the following configurations:</p>

<blockquote class="italicized">
<p>Let ABC be a triangle with centroid G and outer-Fermat (or inner-Fermat) triangle A<sub>f</sub>B<sub>f</sub>C<sub>f</sub>.</p>

<ol>
<li>Let A* = reflection of G in A<sub>f</sub>; let A<sub>1</sub> = reflection of A* in A. Define B<sub>1</sub> and C<sub>1</sub> cyclically.
</li>
<li>Let A* = reflection of G in A; let A<sub>2</sub> = reflection of A* in A<sub>f</sub> .  Define B<sub>2</sub> and C<sub>2</sub> cyclically.
</li>
<li>Let A* = reflection of A in G; let A<sub>3</sub> = midpoint of A*A<sub>f</sub> . Define B<sub>3</sub> and C<sub>3</sub> cyclically.
</li>
<li>Let A* = reflection of A<sub>f</sub>  in G; let A<sub>4</sub> = midpoint of A*A. Define B<sub>4</sub> and C<sub>4</sub> cyclically.
</li>
</ol>

Then triangles A<sub>1</sub>B<sub>1</sub>C<sub>1</sub>, A<sub>2</sub>B<sub>2</sub>C<sub>2</sub>, A<sub>3</sub>B<sub>3</sub>C<sub>3</sub>, A<sub>4</sub>B<sub>4</sub>C<sub>4</sub> are equilateral and perspective to ABC.
</blockquote>

<p><b>Added by César Lozada:</b></p>
For the outer-Fermat triangle, barycentrics coordinates of A<sub>1</sub>...A<sub>4</sub> are:

<blockquote>
<p>A<sub>1</sub> = -7*S-3*sqrt(3)*a^2 :  2*S+3*sqrt(3)*SC :  2*S+3*sqrt(3)*SB</p>
<p>A<sub>2</sub> = -5*S-3*sqrt(3)*a^2 :  4*S+3*sqrt(3)*SC :  4*S+3*sqrt(3)*SB</p>
<p>A<sub>3</sub> =  4*S-3*sqrt(3)*a^2 : -5*S+3*sqrt(3)*SC : -5*S+3*sqrt(3)*SB</p>
<p>A<sub>4</sub> =  8*S-3*sqrt(3)*a^2 :   -S+3*sqrt(3)*SC :   -S+3*sqrt(3)*SB</p>
</blockquote>

For the inner-Fermat triangle, just replace S &rarr; -S in these coordinates.
<p>The preceding triangles are here named here the <b>1st, 2nd, 3rd and 4th outer-Fermat-Dao-Nhi triangles</b>, respectively, and their correspondents built from the inner-Fermat triangle are here named the <b>1st, 2nd, 3rd and 4th inner-Fermat-Dao-Nhi triangles</b>, respectively.
All these triangles have center X(2).
</p>
<p>
Observe that given A-barycentrics have all the form A<sub>&lambda;, &mu;</sub> = &lambda;*S - a^2 : &mu;*S + SC : &mu;*S + SB, where &lambda;, &mu; are real numbers. It results that, for all real numbers (&lambda;, &mu;), the triangle &Fopf;(&lambda;, &mu;) = A<sub>&lambda;, &mu;</sub>B<sub>&lambda;, &mu;</sub>C<sub>&lambda;, &mu;</sub> is perspective to  each triangle in the following set: {ABC, anti-Artzt, 1st anti-Brocard, anti-McCay, anticomplementary, Artzt, 1st Brocard-reflected, 1st Brocard, inner-Fermat, outer-Fermat, 1st half-diamonds-central, 2nd half-diamonds-central, 1st half-diamonds, 2nd half-diamonds, 1st half-squares, 2nd half-squares, McCay, medial, inner-Napoleon, outer-Napoleon, 1st Neuberg, 2nd Neuberg, inner-Vecten, outer-Vecten}. Also &Fopf;(&lambda;, &mu;) and &Fopf;(&lambda;', &mu;') are perspective for any combination of (&lambda;, &mu;, &lambda;', &mu;'). The perspector of ABC and &Fopf;(&lambda;, &mu;) has barycentric coordinates Q = (&mu;*S+SB)*(&mu;*S+SC) : :, not dependending on &lambda;. Q lies on the Kiepert hyperbola of ABC.
</p>
<p>
With the last notation we have: A<sub>1</sub>B<sub>1</sub>C<sub>1</sub> = &Fopf;(-7*k, 2*k), A<sub>2</sub>B<sub>2</sub>C<sub>2</sub> = &Fopf;(-5*k, 4*k), A<sub>3</sub>B<sub>3</sub>C<sub>3</sub> = &Fopf;(4*k, -5*k), A<sub>4</sub>B<sub>4</sub>C<sub>4</sub> = &Fopf;(8*k, -k), where k = &plusmn;sqrt(3)/9 (plus and minus signs for outer- and inner- triangles, respectively). Similarly: 1st/2nd half-diamonds triangles = &Fopf;( &plusmn;2*sqrt(3)/3, &plusmn;sqrt(3)/3 ), 1st/2nd half-diamonds-central triangles = &Fopf;( &mnplus;2*sqrt(3), &mnplus;sqrt(3) ), 1st/2nd half-squares triangles = &Fopf;( &mnplus;1, 0 ), outer-/inner-Fermat = &Fopf;( 0, &plusmn;sqrt(3)/3 ).</p>
<p>The condition for &Fopf;(&lambda;, &mu;) being equilateral is |&lambda; - &mu;| = sqrt(3).</p>

<p>Note that every original construction is made from two reflections. Going further, if homothecies are used instead of reflections then configurations 1 to 4 can be combined and generalized by writing:</p>

<blockquote class="italicized">
<p>Let ABC be a triangle with centroid G and outer-Fermat (or inner-Fermat) triangle A<sub>f</sub>B<sub>f</sub>C<sub>f</sub>.</p>
<ol>
    <li>
        Let A* be the homothecy of G with factor p and center A,
        &Aopf;<sub>1</sub> the homothecy of A* with factor q
        and center A<sub>f</sub>.
        Build &Bopf;<sub>1</sub> and &#8450;<sub>1</sub> cyclically.
        Then, for each value of
        <span class="fontnormal">p&notin;{0, 3}</span>
        there exist two values of
        <span class="fontnormal">q&in;{1/p, 2/(3-p)}</span>
        such that &Aopf;<sub>1</sub>&Bopf;<sub>1</sub>&#8450;<sub>1</sub> is equilateral and perspective to ABC.
    </li>

    <li>
        Let A* be the homothecy of G with factor p and center A<sub>f</sub>,
        &Aopf;<sub>2</sub> the homothecy of A* with factor q and center A.
        Build &Bopf;<sub>2</sub> and &#8450;<sub>2</sub> cyclically.
        Then, for each value of
        <span class="fontnormal">p&notin;{0, 3/2}</span>
        there exist two values of
        <span class="fontnormal">q&in;{1/p, 1/(3-2*p)}</span>
        such that &Aopf;<sub>2</sub>&Bopf;<sub>2</sub>&#8450;<sub>2</sub> is equilateral and perspective to ABC.
    </li>
</ol>
</blockquote>

The following four generalizations were contributed by Dao Thanh Oai, June 29, 2022.
<p></p>
<b>1. Generalization of the First Fermat-Dao-Nhi equilateral triangle
</b>
<p>
Let ABC be a triangle with centroid G and outer-Fermat (or inner-Fermat) triangle AfBfCf.
P be arbitrary point in the plane.
</p>
<p>
Let A* = reflection of P in Af; let A1 = reflection of A* in A; define B1 and C1 cyclically.
 </p>
<p>
1) Then A1B1C1 is an equilateral triangle homothetic to the outer (inner) Napoleon equilateral triangle, with homothetic center H<sub>1</sub>.
</p>
<p>
2) H<sub>1</sub> lies on the segment PG, and |PH<sub>1</sub>|=6|H<sub>1</sub>G|.
</p>
<p>
3) Area(A1B1C1) = 36*(area of outer [or inner] Napoleon equilateral triangle).
</p>
<p>
<b>2. Generalization of the second Fermat-Dao-Nhi equilateral triangle
</b>
<p>
Let ABC be a triangle with centroid G and outer-Fermat (or inner-Fermat) triangle AfBfCf, and let P be an arbitrary point in the plane of ABC. Let A* = reflection of P in A; let A2 = reflection of A* in Af.  Define B2 and C2 cyclically.
</p>
<p>
1) Then A2B2C2 is an equilateral triangle homothetic with the outer (inner) Napoleon equilateral triangle, with homothetic center H<sub>2</sub>.
</p>
<p>
2) G lies on segment PH<sub>2</sub>, and |PG} = 5|GH<sub>2</sub>|.
</p>
<p>
3) Area(A2B2C2)=36*(area of outer [or inner] Napoleon equilateral triangle).
</p>
<p>
<b>3. Generalization of the third Fermat-Dao-Nhi equilateral triangle
</b>
<p>
Let ABC be a triangle with centroid G and outer-Fermat (or inner-Fermat) triangle AfBfCf, and let P be an arbitrary point in the plane of ABC. Let A* = reflection of A in P; let A3 = midpoint of A*Af. Define B3 and C3 cyclically.
</p>
<p>
1) Then A3B3C3 is an equilateral triangle homothetic to the outer (inner) Napoleon equilateral triangle, with homothetic center H<sub>3</sub>.
 </p>
<p>
2) G lies on segment PH<sub>3</sub>, and 2|PG| = |GH<sub>3</sub>|.
 </p>
<p>
3) Area(A3B3C3) = (9/4)*(area of outer (inner) Napoleon equilateral triangle).
</p>
<p>
<b>4. Generalization of the fourth Fermat-Dao-Nhi equilateral triangle
</b>
<p>
Let ABC be a triangle with centroid G and outer-Fermat (or inner-Fermat) triangle AfBfCf.
P be arbitrary point in the plane
</p>
<p>
Let A* = reflection of Af in P; let A4 = midpoint of A*A. Define B4 and C4 cyclically.
</p>
<p>
1) Then A4B4C4 is an equilateral homothetic to the outer (inner)
Napoleon equilateral triangle, with homothetic center H<sub>4</sub>.
</p>
<p>
2) H<sub>4</sub> lies on segment PG, and 2|PH<sub>4</sub>|=3|H<sub>4</sub>G|.
</p>
<p>
3) Area(A4B4C4)=(9/4)*(area of outer (inner) Napoleon equilateral triangle).
</p>
