Centers of bianticevian conics:  X(35513)-X(35516)
</p>
<img src="rightri.svg" style="height:30px" alt="rightri">
</header>
<div class="preamble-sect">
<p>
Centers X(35513)-X(35516) were contributed by Vu Thanh Tung and Vu Quoc My, July 4, 2019.
</p>
<p>
Suppose that P and Q are points not on a sideline of a triangle ABC.  The conic section that passes through P, Q, and the vertices of the anticevian triangle of P also passes through the vertices of the anticevian triangle of Q.  This conic was introduced by Randy Hutson. Barry Wolk found coordinates for the center, and Paul Yiu and Francisco Javier Garcia found other properties.  See Hyacinthos (#21109 and related posts), July 2012.
</p>
<p>
If P = p : q : r and U = u : v : w (barycentrics), then the two anticevian triangles have vertices
</p>
<p>
Pa = -p : q : r, &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Pb = p :-q : r, &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Pc = p : q -r &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; and &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Ua = -u : v : w,&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Ub= u : -v : w, &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Uc = u : v : -w,
</p>
<p>
and the conic, which passes through P, Q, Pa, Pb, Pc, Ua, Ub, Uc, has center 1/(q^2 w^2 - r^2 v^2) : 1/(r^2 u^2 - p^2 w^2) :1/(p^2 v^2 - q^2 w^2).
