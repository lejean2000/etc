Points on the line at infinity: X(33904)-X(33922)

<img src="rightri.svg" style="height:30px" alt="rightri">

<div class="preamble-sect">
<p>
Centers X(33904)-X(33922) were contributed by Clark Kimberling, August 13, 2019.
</p>
<p>
Suppose that U = u : v : w = u(a,b,c) : v(a,b,c) : w(a,b,c) is a point on the line L at infinity, which consists of all points X = x : y : z : = x(a,b,c) : y(a,b,c): z(a,b,c) that satisfy x+y+z = 0.  Suppose also that D = d : e : f is a point, and define
</p>
<p>
g(D) = u(d,e,f) : v(d,e,f) : w(d,e,f).
</p>
<p>
Clearly, g(D) lies on L.  For example, if D = U = b-c : c-a : a-b, then
</p>
<p>
g(D) = c-a - (a-b) : a-b - (b-c) : b-c - (c-a) = 2a-b-c : 2b-c-a : 2c-a-b, and g(g(D)) = D; also,
</p>
<p>
if D = 2a-b-c : 2b-c-a : 2c-a-b, then g(D) = D.
</p>
<p>
In those examples, a,b,c can be replaced by a<sup>m</sup>, b<sup>m</sup>,c<sup>m</sup> for any nonzero real number, so that that following family of triangle centers, all on L, can be regarded as a basis for generating, by composition, many more centers on L:
</p>
<p>
b<sup>m</sup> - c<sup>m</sup> : c<sup>m</sup> - a<sup>m</sup> : a<sup>m</sup> - b<sup>m</sup> and b<sup>-m</sup> - c<sup>-m</sup> : c<sup>-m</sup> - a<sup>-m</sup> : a<sup>-m</sup> - b<sup>-m</sup> = a<sup>m</sup>(b<sup>m</sup> - c<sup>m</sup>) : b<sup>m</sup>(c<sup>m</sup> - a<sup>m</sup>) : c<sup>m</sup>(a<sup>m</sup> - b<sup>m</sup>),
</p>
<p>
where m ranges through the positive integers. Writing these as (m) and (-m), respectively, and concatenating (e.g., (2)(-1) means (2) is performed first, and then (-1)), we have, for example,
</p>
<p>
(2) = b^2-c^2 : :<br>
(3) = b^3-c^3 : :<br>
(2)(3) = (c^2-a^2)^3 - (a^2-b^2)^3 : : = 2a^2-b^2-c^2 : : (after cancellations)<br>
(3)(2) = (c^3-a^3)^2 - (a^3-b^3)^2 : : = (b^3-c^3)(2a^3-b^3-c^3) : :
</p>
