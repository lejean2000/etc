<p>
Let T=A<sub>t</sub>B<sub>t</sub>C<sub>t</sub> be a triangle perspective and orthologic to ABC. Suppose P<sub>t</sub> is the perspector (ABC, T) and O<sub>t</sub> is the orthologic center ABC to T, both expressed with respect to T. The anti-triangle-of-T is ABC and, if T is taken as the reference triangle, then its  anti-triangle T'=A'<sub>t</sub>B'<sub>t</sub>C'<sub>t</sub> is given by:
<br>
<br>&emsp;&emsp;A'<sub>t</sub> = P<sub>t</sub>A &cap; (perpendicular to BC through O<sub>t</sub>)
<br>
<br>and cyclically for B'<sub>t</sub> and C'<sub>t</sub>.
</p>
