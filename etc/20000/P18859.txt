<p>
<i>Let ABC be a triangle and P a point. The perpendicular bisectors of BC, CA, AB intersect the circumcircle at (A<sub>1</sub>, A<sub>2</sub>), (B<sub>1</sub>, B<sub>2</sub>), (C<sub>1</sub>, C<sub>2</sub>) ,respectively. Then the circumcircles of PA<sub>1</sub>A<sub>2</sub>, PB<sub>1</sub>B<sub>2</sub>, PC<sub>1</sub>C<sub>2</sub> are coaxial</i>. (Antreas Hatzipolakis, May 26, 2018, <a href="https://groups.yahoo.com/neo/groups/Anopolis/conversations/messages/7568">Anopolis 7568</a>)
</p>
<p>
Let Q be the point of intersection (other than P) of the three indicated circles. Then:
</p>
<ul>
<li>Q = reflection-in-X(3) of (the circumcircle-inverse-of-P)</li>
<li>Q = circumcircle-inverse-of-(reflection-of-P in X(3))</li>
</ul>

<p></p>
<p>
For P other than X(3), the mapping P &srarr; Q is a conjugacy. The point Q is here named the <b>Q=circumperp conjugate of P</b> (as A<sub>1</sub>, A<sub>2</sub>, B<sub>1</sub>, B<sub>2</sub>, C<sub>1</sub>, C<sub>2</sub> are the vertices of the circumperp triangles). For P= u:v:w (trilinears), coordinates of Q(P) are:
<br>
<br>&emsp;&emsp;Q(P) = (-a*(-b*c*SC*SB*u^2+2*w*v*SA^3)-SA*a*b*c*(b^2*v^2+c^2*w^2)+b*((SA+SB)*S^2-2*SA*SB^2)*w*u+c*((SA+SC)*S^2-2*SA*SC^2)*u*v)*a : :
</p>
<p>
Some properties:
</p>
<ul>
<li>Q(P) lies on the line X(3)P </li>
<li>If P lies on the circumcircle, then Q(P) = reflection of P in X(3), i.e, Q(P) is the antipode of P in the circumcircle.</li>
<li>If P lies on the line at infinity, then Q(P) = X(3)</li>
</ul>
