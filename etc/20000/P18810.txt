Perspectors of Inconics: X(18810)-X(18831)
</p>
<img src="rightri.svg" style="height:30px" alt="rightri">

<div class="preamble-sect">

<p>
Let P be a point in the plane of a triangle ABC.  Let<br>
A' = reflection of P in BC, and define B' and C' cyclically;<br>
Q = circumcenter of A'B'C' = isogonal conjugate of P;<br>
A'' = QA'&cap;BC, and define B'' and C'' cyclically.<br>
The triangle A''B''C'' is perspective to ABC. Let D(P) denote the perspector.  The points P and Q are the foci of the inconic tangent to BC, CA, AB at A'', B'', C'', respectively.  (Based on notes from Thanh Oai Dao, May 17, 2018)
</p>
<p>
If P = p : q : r (barycentrics), then D(P) = q r/(b<sup>2</sup>r<sup>2</sup>+ c<sup>2</sup>q<sup>2</sup> + (b<sup>2</sup> - c<sup>2</sup> - a<sup>2</sup>) q r) : : , and
</p>
<p>
D(P) = isotomic conjugate of the anticomplement of (midpoint of P and isogonal conjugate of P)<br>
D(P) = cevapoint of P and the orthocorrespondent of P<br>
D(P) = cross conjugate of (midpoint of P and isogonal conjugate of P) and G<br>
If P is on the infinity line, then D(P) = isotomic conjugate of P<br>
If P on the circumcircle or infinity line, then D(P) lies on the Steiner circumellipse.<br>
(Peter Moses, May 21, 2018)
</p>
