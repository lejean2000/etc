<h3 id="X18039">X(18039) =  X(4)X(526)&cap;X(5)X(523)</h3>
Barycentrics &nbsp;&nbsp; b^2 c^2 (b^2-c^2) (3 a^8+(b^2-c^2)^4-3 a^6 (b^2+c^2)+a^2 (b^2-c^2)^2
(b^2+c^2)+a^4 (-2 b^4+7 b^2 c^2-2 c^4)) : :
<p>
At the suggestion of Seiichi Kirikami, the line H through X(5) perpendicular to the Euler line of a triangle ABC is here named the <b>Hatzipolakis axis</b> of ABC.  In the plane of a triangle ABDC, let<br>
L = Euler line of ABC;<br>
H = Hatzipolakis axis of ABC;<br>
D = L&cap;BC, and define E and F cyclically;<br>
L<sub>A</sub> = L-of-AEF, and define L<sub>B</sub> and L<sub>C</sub> cyclically;<br>
M<sub>A</sub> = H-of-AEF, and define M<sub>B</sub> and M<sub>C</sub> cyclically.<br>
The lines M<sub>A</sub>, M<sub>B</sub>, M<sub>C</sub> concur in X(18039).
</p>
<p>
See Seiichi Kirikami and Angel Montesdeoca, <a href="http://www.hyacinthos.epizy.com/message.php?msg=27589"> <b>Hyacinthos 27589</b></a>.
</p>
<p>
The Hatzipolakis axis H is parallel to orthic axis and the De Londchamps axis; they meet in X(523).  An equation for H, in barycentric coordinates, follows:
</p>
<p>
h(a,b,c) x + h(b,c,a) y + h(c,a,b) = 0, where h(a,b,c) = a<sup>2</sup>(b<sup>2</sup>c<sup>2</sup> - (a<sup>2</sup> - b<sup>2</sup> - c<sup>2</sup>)<sup>2</sup> .
</p>
<p>
H passes through X(i) for these i: 5, 523, 6757, 8151, 10287, 10288, 10412, 14254, 14356, 14566, 14592, 14670, 15475, and X(18114) - X(18122).   (Peter Moses, April 27, 2018)
</p>
<p>
The isogonal conjugate of H is the circumconic with center X(11597), perspector X(50). An equation for this conic follows:
</p>
<p>
g(a,b,c) y z + g(b,c,a) z x + g(c,a,b) x y = 0, where g(a,b,c) = a<sup>4</sup>(b<sup>2</sup>c<sup>2</sup> - (a<sup>2</sup> - b<sup>2</sup> - c<sup>2</sup>)<sup>2</sup>.
</p>
<p>
The conic passes through X(i) for these i: 54, 110, 10411, 14355, 14385, 14591, 17104. (Peter Moses, April 27, 2018)
</p>
