<p class="preamble-title">
Triangles and centers related to the Ehrmann pivots (PU(5)): X(18300)-X(18587)
</p>
<img src="rightri.svg" style="height:30px" alt="rightri">
</header>
<div class="preamble-sect">

<p></p>
This preamble and centers X(18300)-X(18587) were contributed by Randy Hutson, May 2, 2018.
<p></p>
<p>
Let T<sub>C1</sub>=A<sub>1</sub>B<sub>1</sub>C<sub>1</sub> be the triangle obtained by rotating ABC about the 1st Ehrmann pivot, P(5), by an angle of 2&pi;/3, so that T<sub>C1</sub> circumscribes ABC.  Triangle T<sub>C1</sub> is here named the <b>1st Ehrmann circumscribing triangle</b>.  Let T<sub>C2</sub>=A<sub>2</sub>B<sub>2</sub>C<sub>2</sub> be the triangle obtained by rotating ABC about the 2nd Ehrmann pivot, U(5), by an angle of -2&pi;/3, so that T<sub>C2</sub> circumscribes ABC.  Triangle T<sub>C2</sub> is here named the <b>2nd Ehrmann circumscribing triangle</b>.  T<sub>C1</sub> and T<sub>C2</sub> are inscribed in the Johnson circle (centered at X(4)).
</p>
<p>
Let T<sub>I1</sub>=A'<sub>1</sub>B'<sub>1</sub>C'<sub>1</sub> be the triangle obtained by rotating ABC about the 1st Ehrmann pivot, P(5), by an angle of -2&pi;/3, so that T<sub>I1</sub> is inscribed in ABC.  Triangle T<sub>I1</sub> is here named the <b>1st Ehrmann inscribed triangle</b>.  Let T<sub>I2</sub>=A'<sub>2</sub>B'<sub>2</sub>C'<sub>2</sub> be the triangle obtained by rotating ABC about the 2nd Ehrmann pivot, U(5), by an angle of 2&pi;/3, so that T<sub>I2</sub> is inscribed in ABC.  Triangle T<sub>I2</sub> is here named the <b>2nd Ehrmann inscribed triangle</b>.  T<sub>I1</sub> and T<sub>I2</sub> are inscribed in a common conic, here named the <b>Ehrmann conic</b>.  The center of the Ehrmann conic is X(14993).
</p>
<p>
<br>
The orthocenter of T<sub>C1</sub> is the circumcenter of T<sub>I1</sub>, and is here introduced (and at <a href="BicentricPairs.html" style="font-weight: bold">Bicentric Pairs</a>) as <b>P(173)</b>.  P(173) is therefore the intersection of the Euler lines of T<sub>C1</sub> and T<sub>I1</sub>.  The orthocenter of T<sub>C2</sub> is the circumcenter of T<sub>I2</sub>, and is here introduced as <b>U(173)</b>.  U(173) is therefore the intersection of the Euler lines of T<sub>C2</sub> and T<sub>I2</sub>.  PU(173) lie on the Hatzipolakis axis (line PU(5) or X(5)X(523)).  The midpoint of PU(173) is X(5).  P(173), X(3) and X(4) are the vertices of an equilateral triangle with center P(5).  U(173), X(3) and X(4) are the vertices of an equilateral triangle with center U(5).
</p>
<p>
<br>
The vertex-triangle of T<sub>C1</sub> and T<sub>C2</sub> is here named the <b>Ehrmann vertex-triangle</b>.  The Ehrmann vertex-triangle is the Kosnita triangle of the Johnson triangle (or equivalently, the reflection of the Kosnita triangle in X(5)), and the tangential triangle of the Ehrmann mid-triangle (defined below).  The vertex-triangle of T<sub>I1</sub> and T<sub>I2</sub> is ABC.
</p>
<p>
Let V<sub>A</sub>V<sub>B</sub>V<sub>C</sub> be the Ehrmann vertex-triangle.  Then V<sub>A</sub> is the isogonal conjugate of A'<sub>1</sub> wrt T<sub>C1</sub>, and the isogonal conjugate of A'<sub>2</sub> wrt T<sub>C2</sub>, and cyclically for V<sub>B</sub> and V<sub>C</sub>.
</p>
<p>
The A-vertex of the Ehrmann vertex-triangle has barycentric coordinates:<br>
V<sub>A</sub> = a^6 - a^4(b^2 + c^2) - a^2(b^4 + c^4) + (b^2 - c^2)^2(b^2 + c^2) &nbsp;&nbsp;:&nbsp;&nbsp; -(a^2 + b^2 - c^2)[(a^2 - b^2 - c^2)^2 - b^2c^2] &nbsp;&nbsp;:&nbsp;&nbsp; -(a^2 - b^2 + c^2)[(a^2 - b^2 - c^2)^2 - b^2c^2]
</p>
<p>
The Ehrmann vertex-triangle is orthologic to ABC with orthology center X(4).
</p>
<p>
The Ehrmann vertex-triangle is Eulerologic to ABC at X(7577).
</p>
<p>
The Ehrmann vertex-triangle is perspective to the reflections-of-P triangle for all P.
</p>

<p>
<br>
The side-triangle of T<sub>I1</sub> and T<sub>I2</sub> is here named the <b>Ehrmann side-triangle</b>.  The Ehrmann side-triangle is the circumorthic triangle of the Johnson triangle (or equivalently, the reflection of the circumorthic triangle in X(5)), and also the 2nd isogonal triangle of X(4) (see X(36)).  It is inscribed (along with T<sub>C1</sub> and T<sub>C2</sub>) in the Johnson circle.  The side-triangle of T<sub>C1</sub> and T<sub>C2</sub> is ABC.
</p>
<p>
The A-vertex of the Ehrmann side-triangle has barycentric coordinates:<br>
S<sub>A</sub> = a^2[a^4 + a^2(b^2 + c^2) - 2(b^2 - c^2)^2] &nbsp;&nbsp;:&nbsp;&nbsp; -(a^2 - b^2 + c^2)[(a^2 + b^2 - c^2)^2 - a^2b^2] &nbsp;&nbsp;:&nbsp;&nbsp; -(a^2 + b^2 - c^2)[(a^2 - b^2 + c^2)^2 - a^2c^2]
</p>
<p>
The Ehrmann side-triangle is orthologic to ABC with orthology center X(3).
</p>
<p>
The Ehrmann side-triangle is Eulerologic to ABC at X(3).  ABC is Eulerologic to the Ehrmann side-triangle at X(18403).
</p>

<br>
The mid-triangle of T<sub>C1</sub> and T<sub>C2</sub> is here named the <b>Ehrmann mid-triangle</b>.  The Ehrmann mid-triangle is the Johnson triangle of the Euler triangle (or equivalently, the reflection of the Euler triangle in X(546)), and the intouch triangle of the Ehrmann vertex-triangle if ABC is acute.  The mid-triangle of T<sub>I1</sub> and T<sub>I2</sub> is the medial triangle.
</p>
<p>
The A-vertex of the Ehrmann mid-triangle has barycentric coordinates:<br>
M<sub>A</sub> = 2a^4 - b^4 - c^4 - a^2b^2 - a^2c^2 + 2b^2c^2 &nbsp;&nbsp;:&nbsp;&nbsp; -2a^4 + b^4 - 2c^4 + a^2b^2 + 4a^2c^2 + b^2c^2 &nbsp;&nbsp;:&nbsp;&nbsp; -2a^4 - 2b^4 + c^4 + 4a^2b^2 + a^2c^2 + b^2c^2
</p>
<p>
ABC is Eulerologic to the Ehrmann mid-triangle at X(4).
</p>

<br>
The cross-triangle of T<sub>I1</sub> and T<sub>I2</sub> is here named the <b>Ehrmann cross-triangle</b>.  The Ehrmann cross-triangle is degenerate, its vertices lying on line X(3)X(523) (the trilinear polar of X(2986)).  It is perspective to the Johnson triangle at X(10745).  The vertices of the cross triangle of T<sub>C1</sub> and T<sub>C2</sub> are the infinite points of lines BC, CA, AB.
</p>
<p>
The A-vertex of the Ehrmann cross-triangle has barycentric coordinates:<br>
X<sub>A</sub> = (b^2 - c^2)(2a^4 - b^4 - c^4 - a^2b^2 - a^2c^2 + 2b^2c^2) &nbsp;&nbsp;:&nbsp;&nbsp; -(a^2 - b^2 + c^2)[(a^2 + b^2 - c^2)^2 - a^2b^2] &nbsp;&nbsp;:&nbsp;&nbsp; (a^2 + b^2 - c^2)[(a^2 - b^2 + c^2)^2 - a^2c^2]
</p>
