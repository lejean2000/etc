Collineations inverse-images: X(18588)-X(18752)

<div class="preamble-sect">

<p>
This preamble and centers X(18588)-X(18752) were contributed by CÃ©sar Eliud Lozada, May 4, 2018.
</p>
<p>
Suppose that m is a collination.  If P = m(Q), then P is the m collineation-image of Q, as in the preambles just before X(16286) and X(16504), and Q is here named the <b> m inverse colllineation-image of P</b>.  Explicitly, if
<p>
<p>
P = (A',B',C',U; A'',B'',C''V) collineation image of Q, then Q = (A'',B'',C'',V; A',B',c",U) collination inverse-image of P.
</p>
<p>
Let Q be the (T<sub>1</sub>, X<sub>1</sub>; T<sub>2</sub>, X<sub>2</sub>)-collineation image of P, where T<sub>1</sub> and T<sub>2</sub> are two central triangles, and X<sub>1</sub> and X<sub>2</sub> two centers.  The following table shows the general expressions of the first trilinear coordinate of the image Q when P = u : v : w (trilinears) is given, and the first trilinear coordinate of the inverse-image P, when Q = u : v : w (trilinears) is given.
</p>
