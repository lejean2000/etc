<p>
Let L be the line through X(3) parallel to BC.  Let A<sub>b</sub> = L&cap;AB, and A<sub>c</sub> = L&cap;AC, and let D = midpoint of AX(4).  Let A' be the point, other than A, in which the circles {{B,D,A<sub>b</sub>}} and {{C, D,A<sub>c</sub>}} meet. Define B' and C' cyclically.  The finite fixed point of the affine transformation that carries ABC onto A'B'C' is X(19210). (Angel Montesdeoca, April 13, 2021)
