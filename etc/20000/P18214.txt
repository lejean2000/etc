<p>
In a triangle ABC, let A<sub>m</sub>B<sub>m</sub>C<sub>m</sub> be the medial triangle, I the incenter and A' the touchpoint of the incircle with the side BC. Then the lines AA', IA<sub>m</sub> and B<sub>m</sub>C<sub>m</sub> are concurrent at a point A<sub>1</sub>. Denote B<sub>1</sub> and C<sub>1</sub> cyclically. The triangle A<sub>1</sub>B<sub>1</sub>C<sub>1</sub> is here named the <b>1st Zaniah triangle of ABC</b>.
</p>
<p>
Continuing with the previous construction, let J<sub>a</sub> be the A-excenter and A" the touchpoint of the A-excircle with the side BC. Then the lines AA", J<sub>a</sub>A<sub>m</sub> and B<sub>m</sub>C<sub>m</sub> are concurrent at a point A<sub>2</sub>. Denote B<sub>2</sub> and C<sub>2</sub> cyclically. The triangle A<sub>2</sub>B<sub>2</sub>C<sub>2</sub> is here named the <b>2nd Zaniah triangle of ABC</b>.  [See note (*) below.]
</p>
<p>
Barycentric coordinates of the first vertex of each triangle are:
<br>
<br>&emsp;&emsp;A<sub>1</sub> = 2*a : a+b-c : a-b+c
<br>
<br>&emsp;&emsp;A<sub>2</sub> = 2*a : a-b+c : a+b-c
</p>
<p>
The vertices of both triangles lie on a conic with center X(18214) and perspector X(18215).
</p>
