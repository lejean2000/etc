<p class="preamble-title">
Walsmith Points, X(5000) and X(5001)
</p>
<img src="rightri.svg" style="height:30px" alt="rightri">
</header>
<div class="preamble-sect">

<p>Early in 2012, Russell Walsmith introduced
this point and conjectured that it lies on the Euler line. Peter Moses
found barycentrics for the point and its inverse-in-the-circumcircle,
X(5001), and proved that the two points lie on the Euler line. To
summarize from Walsmith's paper, (not available), in a three dimensional
cartesian coordinate system, let A=(u,0,0), B=(0,v,0), C=(0,0,w), and
let D be the point such that the distances |AD|, |BC|, |CD| are
proportional to u,v,w; that is, D is the point having homogeneous
tripolar coordinates u : v : w. The equations
</p><p>
a<sup>2</sup> = v<sup>2</sup> + w<sup>2</sup>, b<sup>2</sup> =
w<sup>2</sup> + u<sup>2</sup>, c<sup>2</sup> = u<sup>2</sup> +
v<sup>2</sup>
</p><p>
are equivalent to
</p><p>
u<sup>2</sup> = (b<sup>2</sup> + c<sup>2</sup> - a<sup>2</sup>)/2,
v<sup>2</sup> = (c<sup>2</sup> + a<sup>2</sup> - b<sup>2</sup>)/2,
w<sup>2</sup> = (a<sup>2</sup> + b<sup>2</sup> - c<sup>2</sup>)/2.
</p><p>
The Euler line of ABC is given by the tripolar equation
</p><p>
(b<sup>2</sup> - c<sup>2</sup>)x<sup>2</sup> + (c<sup>2</sup> -
a<sup>2</sup>)y<sup>2</sup> + (a<sup>2</sup> -
b<sup>2</sup>)z<sup>2</sup> = 0,
</p><p>
and it is easy to see that this equation is satisfied by putting
(x<sup>2</sup>, y<sup>2</sup>, z<sup>2</sup>) = (u<sup>2</sup>,
v<sup>2</sup>, w<sup>2</sup>).
</p>
