<h3 id="X3645">X(3645)&nbsp;=&nbsp;POINT ALKAID</h3>
Trilinears&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;
f(A,B,C) : f(B,C,A) ; f(C,A,B), where f(A,B,C) = 1 + cos(B/2) +
cos(C/2) - cos(A/2)<br>
Barycentrics&nbsp;&nbsp; (sin A)f(A,B,C) : (sin B)f(B,C,A) ; (sin
C)f(C,A,B)

<p>Denote the incenter and excenters by I, I<sub>A</sub>,
I<sub>B</sub>, I<sub>C</sub>. Let J<sub>A</sub> be the incenter of
triangle BCI, and define J<sub>B</sub> and J<sub>C</sub> cyclically.
The lines I<sub>A</sub>J<sub>A</sub>, I<sub>B</sub>J<sub>B</sub>,
I<sub>C</sub>J<sub>C</sub> concur in X(3645).
</p>
<p>
X(3645) lies on the Euler line of the BCI triangle. (Randy Hutson, January 29, 2018)
</p>
