<h3 id="X3066">X(3066)&nbsp;=&nbsp;2nd LEMOINE HOMOTHETIC CENTER</h3>
Trilinears&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;
f(a,b,c) : f(b,c,a) : f(c,a,b), where f(a,b,c) = a(a<sup>2</sup> +
3b<sup>2</sup> - c<sup>2</sup>)(a<sup>2</sup> - b<sup>2</sup> +
3c<sup>2</sup>)<br>
Barycentrics&nbsp;&nbsp; (sin A)f(A,B,C) : (sin B)f(B,C,A) : (sin
C)f(C,A,B)
<p>Suppose X is a point, with isogonal conjugate X<sup>- 1</sup>. It is
well known that the pedal triangle of X is homothetic to the antipedal
triangle of X<sup>- 1</sup>. The Lemoine homothetic center, X(1285), is
the homothetic center when X = X(6), and X(3066) is the homothetic
center when X = X(2). See also X(1285). (Peter Moses, Dec. 7, 2005)</p>

<p>In general if X = x : y : z (trilinears), then the homothetic center
is given by</p>

<p></p>
a(x + y cos C)(x + z cos B) : b(y + z cos A)(y + x cos C): c(z +
x cos B)(z + y cos A).<br>

<p>This is a correction for trilinears given in
TCCT, p. 188.</p>

</p>
