<p>
X(4846) is the Segovia point of the circumcevian triangle of the circumcenter. (Antreas Hatzipolakis, January 23 2012; Hyacinthos #20737; see <a href="http://anthrakitis.blogspot.com/2012/01/segovia-point-continued.html"
style="font-weight: bold">Segovia Point, continued</a>.)
</p>
<p>
Let A'B'C' be the translation of ABC by the vector X(4)X(3).  Let M<sub>A</sub> be the midpoint of the (possibly nonreal) points in which the circumcircle of ABC meets the line B'C', and define M<sub>B</sub> and M<sub>C</sub> cyclically.  The triangle M<sub>A</sub>M<sub>B</sub>M<sub>C</sub> is perspective to ABC, and the perspector is X(4846).  If you have GeoGebra, you can view <a href="X(4846).ggb"><b>X(4846)</b></a>. (Angel Montesdeoca, May 20, 2018)
</p>
