    <div class="preamble-sect">
    
    <p>
    This section presents points X(3112) to X(3118) as representative of
    points introduced by Quang Tuan Bui in Hyacinthos, August 5, 2006.
    </p>
    
    <p>Suppose X is a point not on a sideline of
    ABC. Let</p>
    
    <p>gX = isogonal conjugate of X<br>
    tX = isotomic conjugate of X<br>
    tgX = isotomic conjugate of gX<br>
    gtX = isogonal conjugate of tX<br>
    Gt = intersection of lines X(tX) and (gX)(gtX)<br>
    Tg = intersection of lines X(gX) and (tX)(tgX)</p>
    
    <p>Then the points A, B, C, gX, tX, Tg, Gt are on a conic. As a
    circumconic, it is the image under the isogonal conjugate mapping of
    line X(gtX). It is also the image under the isotomic conjugate mapping
    of line X(tgX).</p>
    
    <p>If X = x : y : z (trilinears), then</p>
    
    <p></p>
    Gt = (b<sup>2</sup> -
    c<sup>2</sup>)/(x(b<sup>2</sup>y<sup>2</sup> -
    c<sup>2</sup>z<sup>2</sup>)) : (c<sup>2</sup> -
    a<sup>2</sup>)/(y(c<sup>2</sup>z<sup>2</sup> -
    a<sup>2</sup>x<sup>2</sup>)) : (a<sup>2</sup> -
    b<sup>2</sup>)/(z(a<sup>2</sup>x<sup>2</sup> -
    b<sup>2</sup>y<sup>2</sup>))
    
    <p>Tg = (b<sup>2</sup> - c<sup>2</sup>)/(x(y<sup>2</sup> -
    z<sup>2</sup>)) : (c<sup>2</sup> - a<sup>2</sup>)/(y(z<sup>2</sup> -
    x<sup>2</sup>)) : (a<sup>2</sup> - b<sup>2</sup>)/(z(x<sup>2</sup> -
    y<sup>2</sup>))</p>
    
    <p></p>
    <br>
    The X(31)-conic passes through X(i) for I = 75, 92, 313, 321, 561,
    1441, 1821, 1934, 2995, 2997.<br>
    The X(32)-conic passes through X(i) for I = 76, 264, 276, 290, 300,
    301, 308, 313, 327, 349, 1502, 2367.<br>
    The X(76)-conic passes through X(i) for I = 6, 32, 83, 213, 729, 981,
    1918, 1974, 2207, 2281, 2422.
