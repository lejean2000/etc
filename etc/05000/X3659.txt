<p>
Let I be the incenter and I<sub>A</sub> the A-excenter of the
triangle ABC. Let L<sub>A</sub> be the line joining the circumcenter of
triangle BCI and the incenter of triangle BCI<sub>A</sub>, and define
L<sub>B</sub> and L<sub>C</sub> cyclically. The lines L<sub>A</sub>,
L<sub>B</sub>, L<sub>C</sub> concur in X(3659). (Seiichi Kirikami,
April 12, 2010)
</p>
<p>
Let Ea be the ellipse with B and C as foci and passing through X(1), and define Eb and Ec cyclically.  Let La be the line tangent to Ea at X(1), and define Lb and Lc cyclically.  Let A' = La&cap;BC, B' = Lb&cap;CA, C' = Lc&cap;AB.  Then A', B', C' are collinear, and the line A'B'C' meets the line at infinity at the isogonal conjugate of X(3659).  (Randy Hutson, April 9, 2016)
</p>
<p>
Let I<sub>A</sub>I<sub>B</sub>I<sub>C</sub> be the excentral triangle.  The lines X(1)X(3) of triangles BCI<sub>A</sub>, CAI<sub>B</sub>, ABI<sub>C</sub> concur in X(3659).  (Randy Hutson, June 7, 2019)
</p>
