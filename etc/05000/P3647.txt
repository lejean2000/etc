<p class="preamble-title">
Kirikami-Schiffler Points: X(3647)-X(3652)
</p>
<img src="rightri.svg" style="height:30px" alt="rightri">
</header>
<div class="preamble-sect">

<p>Suppose that X is a point and A'B'C' is a
central triangle. Let L<sub>A</sub> be the line through A' parallel to
the Euler line of triangle BCX, let L<sub>B</sub> be the line through
B' parallel to the Euler line of CXA, and let L<sub>C</sub> be the line
through C' parallel to the Euler line of AXB.</p>

<p>It is well known that if X=X(1), the incenter, then the three
aforementioned Euler lines concur in the Schiffler point, X(21). If
their parallels, the lines L<sub>A</sub>, L<sub>B</sub>, L<sub>C</sub>
concur, the point of concurrence is the Kirikami-Schiffler point of the
triangle A'B'C', denoted by KS(A'B'C'). Seiichi Kirikami (February 1,
2011) found that those lines concur if A'B'C' is the reference triangle
ABC and also concur if A'B'C' is the medial triangle. Peter Moses found
additional cases and properties. A summary follows:</p>

<tr> <th>triangle A'B'C'</th> <th>L<sub>A</sub>&cap;L<sub>B</sub>&cap;L<sub>C</sub></th>
</tr>

<p>
Suppose that A'B'C' is the cevian triangle of a point P. Then
L<sub>A</sub>, L<sub>B</sub>, L<sub>C</sub> concur if and only if P
lies on the cubic <a href=
"https://bernard-gibert.pagesperso-orange.fr/Exemples/k455.html" style=
"font-weight: bold">K455</a>.
</p>

<p>Suppose that A'B'C' is the anticevian triangle of a point P. Then
L<sub>A</sub>, L<sub>B</sub>, L<sub>C</sub> concur if and only if P
lies on the cubic given by the barycentric equation</p>

<p>a(2a + b + c)yz(y - z) + b(2b + c + a)zx(z - x) + c(2c + a + b)xy(x
- y) = 0.</p>

<p>Suppose that A'B'C' is the pedal triangle of a point P on the line
X(1)X(3). Then L<sub>A</sub>, L<sub>B</sub>, L<sub>C</sub> concur.</p>

<p>Suppose that A'B'C' is the antipedal triangle of a point P on the
line X(1)X(4). Then L<sub>A</sub>, L<sub>B</sub>, L<sub>C</sub>
concur.</p>
