<br>
Trilinears &nbsp;&nbsp; (1 - 2 cos A) csc A :  : &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; (M. Iliev, 4/12/07)
<p>
This point occurs in a Sadi Abu-Saymeh, Mowaffaq Hajja, and Hellmuth Stachel, "Another cubic associated with the triangle," <i>Journal for Geometry and Graphics</i> 11 (2007) 15-26.
</p>
<p>
Let La, Lb, Lc be the lines through A, B, C, resp. parallel to the antiorthic axis.  Let Ma, Mb, Mc be the reflections of BC, CA, AB in La, Lb, Lc, resp.  Let A' = Mb&cap;Mc, and define B' and C' cyclically.  Triangle A'B'C' is inversely similar to, and 3 times the size of, ABC.  Let A"B"C" be the reflection of A'B'C' in the antiorthic axis.  The triangle A"B"C" is homothetic to ABC, with center of homothety X(908) and centroid X(3218); see Hyacinthos #16741/16782, Sep 2008.    (Randy Hutson, March 25, 2016)
</p>
