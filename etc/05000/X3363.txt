<h3 id="X3363">X(3363)&nbsp;=&nbsp;X(6) OF PEDAL TRIANGLE OF X(2)
</h3>
Trilinears&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; f(a,b,c)
: f(b,c,a) : f(c,a,b), where f(a,b,c) = bc(-4a<sup>4</sup> +
5b<sup>4</sup> + 5c<sup>4</sup> - 14b<sup>2</sup>c<sup>2</sup> -
5c<sup>2</sup>a<sup>2</sup> - 5a<sup>2</sup>b<sup>2</sup>)
<br>
Barycentrics&nbsp;&nbsp; af(a,b,c) : bf(b,c,a) : cf(c,a,b)
<p>
As a point on the Euler line, X(3363) has Shinagawa coefficients
(2(E + F)<sup>2</sup> + 3S<sup>2</sup>,9S<sup>2</sup>).
</p>
<p>
X(3363) is the symmedian point of the pedal triangle of the
centroid. A second construction uses the midpoint X(597) of the
symmedian point and centroid and the midpoint X(115) of the two Fermat
points, X(13) and X(14); specifically, X(3363) is the point in which
the line X(115)X(597) meets the Euler line. Contributed by Po-chieh
Chen and Shao-cheng Liu, May 29, 2008.</p>

"X(3363).gsp" style="font-weight: bold">X(3363)</a>.</p>
