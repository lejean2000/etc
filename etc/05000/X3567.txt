<h3 id="X3567">X(3567)&nbsp;=&nbsp;DON QUIXOTE POINT</h3>
Trilinears&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; f(a,b,c)
: f(b,c,a) : f(c,a,b), where f(a,b,c) = a[a<sup>6</sup>(b<sup>2</sup> +
c<sup>2</sup>) - a<sup>4</sup>(3b<sup>4</sup> +
b<sup>2</sup>c<sup>2</sup> + 3c<sup>4</sup>) +
3a<sup>2</sup>(b<sup>2</sup> + c<sup>2</sup>)(b<sup>2</sup> -
c<sup>2</sup>)<sup>2</sup> - (b<sup>4</sup> -b<sup>2</sup>c<sup>2</sup>
+ c<sup>4</sup>)(b<sup>2</sup> - c<sup>2</sup>)<sup>2</sup>]

<p>Barycentrics&nbsp;&nbsp; g(a,b,c) : g(b,c,a) : g(c,a,b), where
g(a,b,c) = a<sup>2</sup>[2S<sup>4</sup> +
S<sub>B</sub>S<sub>C</sub>(2S<sup>2</sup> - S<sub>B</sub>S<sub>C</sub>
- (1/2)(<sup>2</sup> + b<sup>2</sup> +
c<sup>2</sup>)S<sub>A</sub>S<sub>B</sub>S<sub>C</sub></p>

<p>This point is the first of three Spanish Points developed by Antreas
P. Hatzipolakis and Javier Garcia Capitan in 2009; see</p>

<p><a href="http://anopolis.epizy.com/message.php?msg=115" class="bold">Spanish Points</a>.
</p>

<p>The other two Spanish Points are the Sancho Panza Point X(3060) and
the Miguel de Cervantes Point X(143).</p>

<p>X(3567) = X(1698)-of-the-orthic-triangle, if ABC is acute. (See also
X(3060).)</p>
