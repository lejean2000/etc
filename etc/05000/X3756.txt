<h3 id="X3756">X(3756)&nbsp;=&nbsp;X(106)com[INVERSE(n(HEXYL TRIANGLE))]</h3>
Barycentrics&nbsp;&nbsp; f(a,b,c) : f(b,c,a) :
f(c,a,b), where f(a,b,c) = (3a - b - c)(b - c)<sup>2</sup><br>
<b>X(3756) = X(1293)com[Extouch]</b>
<p>
Let La, Lb, Lc be the lines through A, B, C, resp. parallel to the Nagel line.  Let Ma, Mb, Mc be the reflections of BC, CA, AB in La, Lb, Lc, resp.  Let A' = Mb&cap;Mc, B' = Mc&cap;Ma, C' = Ma&cap;Mb.  Triangle A'B'C' is inversely similar to, and 3 times the size of, ABC.  Let A"B"C" be the reflection of A'B'C' in the Nagel line.  The triangle A"B"C" is homothetic to ABC, with center of homothety X(3756); see Hyacinthos #16741/16782, Sep 2008.  (Randy Hutson, March 25, 2016)
</p>
<p>

{513, 1357}, {528, 1054}, {1015, 1146}, {1616, 1788}
</p>
