<p>
Two proofs of Dao's theorem about cyclic hexagons were published in 2014:  Nikolaos Dergiades's
<a href="http://forumgeom.fau.edu/FG2014volume14/FG201424.pdf" class="bold">Dao's Theorem on Six Circumcenters Associated with a Cyclic Hexagon</a> and Telv Cohl's
<a href="http://forumgeom.fau.edu/FG2014volume14/FG201429.pdf" class="bold">A Purely Synthetic Proof of Dao's Theorem on Six Circumcenters Associated with a Cyclic Hexagon</a>.
In the degenerate case that the hexagon is the intouch triangle A'B'C', the triangle of the circumcenters of AB'C', A'BC', A'B'C is perspective to A'B'C' with perspector X(3649).  (Dao Thanh Oai, Francisco Javier Garcia Capitan,
<a href="www.adgeom.epizy.com/message.php?msg=1717" class="bold">ADGEOM #1718</a>, September 16, 2014)
</p>
<p>
Let A' be the midpoint of A and X(1), and define B' and C' cyclically.  Triangle A'B'C' is perspective to the intouch triangle at X(3649).  (Randy Hutson, June 7, 2019)
