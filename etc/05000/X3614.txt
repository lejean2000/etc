<h3 id="X3614">X(3614)&nbsp;=&nbsp;1st HUTSON-FEUERBACH POINT</h3>
Trilinears&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;1 + 3
cos(B - C) : 1 + 3 cos(C - A) : 1 + 3 cos(A - B)
<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;=
1 - 3 cos<sup>2</sup>(B/2 - C/2) : 1 - 3 cos<sup>2</sup>(C/2 - A/2) : 1 - 3 cos<sup>2</sup>(A/2 - B/2)
<br>
Barycentrics&nbsp;&nbsp; (sin A)(1 + 3 cos(B - C)) : (sin B)(1 + 3 cos(C - A)) : (sin C)(1 + 3 cos(A - B))
<b><br>
X(3614) = s*R*X(1) + 3*S*X(5)
</b>
<p>
Let A'B'C' be the Feuerbach triangle, L the line through A and X(5),
and A'' = L&cap;B'C'; define B'' and C'' cyclically. Then the lines
A'A'', B'B'', C'C'' concur in X(3614). X(3614) = {X(5),X(12)}-harmonic
conjugate of X(11). Further, X(3614) is the trilinear pole (with
respect to the Feuerbach triangle) of the perspectrix of ABC and the
Feuerbach triangle. (Randy Hutson, August 30, 2011.)
</p>
<p>
See also X(3615) and X(5954).
</p>
<p>
Let A'B'C' be the Feuerbach triangle.  Let A" be the trilinear pole, wrt A'B'C', of line BC; define B" and C" cyclically.  The lines A'A", B'B", C'C" concur in X(3614).  (Randy Hutson, July 11, 2019)
</p>
