<h3 id="X3822">X(3822)&nbsp;=&nbsp;X(2099)com(3rd EULER TRIANGLE)</h3>
Barycentrics&nbsp;&nbsp; (b + c)(b<sup>3</sup> + c<sup>3</sup> - a<sup>2</sup>b - a<sup>2</sup>c - b<sup>2</sup>c - bc<sup>2</sup> - abc) : :
<p>
Suppose that ABC is an acute triangle.  Let L<sub>A</sub> be the circle that is externally tangent to the nine-point circle and to the sidelines AB and AC.  Define L<sub>B</sub> and L<sub>C</sub> cyclically.  The three circles are here named the <b>Odehnal tritangent circles</B>.  Their radical center is X(3822).  The center of the Apollonian circle of  L<sub>A</sub>, L<sub>B</sub>, L<sub>C</sub> is X(6167).  (Boris Odehnal, "A Triad of Tritangent Circles, <I>Journal for Geometry and Graphics</I> 18 (2014) no. 1, 61-71)
</p>
