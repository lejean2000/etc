<h3 id="X3640">X(3640) = 1st KIRIKAMI POINT</h3>
Barycentrics &nbsp;&nbsp; a*(b^2 + c^2 - a*(b + c) + S) : :
<b><br>
X(3640) = 3 X[1] - 2 X[45714], 3 X[3576] - 4 X[45715], X[3641] - 4 X[45713], 3 X[3641] - 4 X[45714], 3 X[5860] - X[12628], 3 X[45713] - X[45714]
</b>
<p>
Seiichi Kirikami (November 1, 2010), defined a point P having actual trilinear distances (p,q,r) by the equations a + p = b + q = c + r and a point P' with distances (p',q',r') satisfying p - a = q - b = r - c. Here, P = X(3640) and P' = X(3641). Kirikami also discovered a
bicentric pair of points satisfying p + b = q + c = r + a and another bicentric pair satisfying p - b = q - c = r - a. The six point are
given by homogeneous trilinears t(a,b,c) : t(b,c,a) : t(c,a,b) as follows:
</p>
<p>
X(3640): t(a,b,c) = b<sup>2</sup> + c<sup>2</sup> - ab - ac +
2*area(ABC)<br>
X(3641): t(a,b,c) = b<sup>2</sup> + c<sup>2</sup> - ab - ac -
2*area(ABC)<br>
K<sub>1</sub>: t(a,b,c) = ac - b<sup>2</sup> + 2*area(ABC)<br>
K<sub>2</sub>: t(a,b,c) = ab - c<sup>2</sup> + 2*area(ABC)<br>
K<sub>3</sub>: t(a,b,c) = ac - b<sup>2</sup> + 2*area(ABC)<br>
K<sub>4</sub>: t(a,b,c) = ab -cb<sup>2</sup> + 2*area(ABC)
</p>
<p>
Peter Moses (November 1, 2010) found that the six points lie on an ellipse, E, here called the Kirikami-Moses ellipse. The center of E is the incenter of ABC, and an equation for E is as follows:
</p>
<p>
e(a,b,c,x,y,z) + e(b,c,a,y,z,x) + e(c,a,b,z,x,y) = 0, where
</p>
<p>
e(a,b,c,x,y,z) = h(a,b,c)x<sup>2</sup> - j(a,b,c)yz, where
</p>
<p>
h(a,b,c) = (5a<sup>4</sup> + b<sup>4</sup> + c<sup>4</sup> - 4a<sup>3</sup>b - 4a<sup>3</sup>c - 4a<sup>2</sup>bc +
2b<sup>2</sup>c<sup>2</sup> + 2c<sup>2</sup>a<sup>2</sup> + 2a<sup>2</sup>b<sup>2</sup>)
</p>
<p>
and
</p>
<p>
j(a,b,c) = a<sup>4</sup> + b<sup>4</sup> + c<sup>4</sup> -
8b<sup>3</sup>c - 8bc<sup>3</sup> + 6b<sup>2</sup>c<sup>2</sup> -
2a<sup>2</sup>b<sup>2</sup> - 2a<sup>2</sup>c<sup>2</sup> +
8abc(b+c-a).
</p>
<p>
Moses found six more points on E:
</p>
<p>
M<sub>1</sub> = (b - c) (a + b + c) + 2*sqrt(3)*area(ABC) : :<br>
M<sub>2</sub> = (b - c) (a + b + c) - 2*sqrt(3)*area(ABC) : :<br>
M<sub>3</sub> = 2ab + ac - b<sup>2</sup> - 2c<sup>2</sup> +
2*sqrt(3)*area(ABC)&nbsp;&nbsp; :&nbsp;&nbsp; :<br>
M<sub>4</sub> = 2ac + ab - c<sup>2</sup> - 2b<sup>2</sup> +
2*sqrt(3)*area(ABC)&nbsp;&nbsp; :&nbsp;&nbsp; :<br>
M<sub>5</sub> = 2ab + ac - b<sup>2</sup> - 2c<sup>2</sup> -
2*sqrt(3)*area(ABC)&nbsp;&nbsp; :&nbsp;&nbsp; :<br>
M<sub>6</sub> = 2ac + ab - c<sup>2</sup> - 2b<sup>2</sup> -
2*sqrt(3)*area(ABC)&nbsp;&nbsp; :&nbsp;&nbsp; :
'</p>
<p>
These six points comprise three bicentric pairs: {M<sub>1</sub>, M<sub>2</sub>}, {M<sub>3</sub>, M<sub>4</sub>}, {M<sub>5</sub>, M<sub>6</sub>}.
</p>
<p>
Further properties found by Moses:
</p>
<p>
(1) The incenter is the midpoint of the segments X(3640)X(3641),
K<sub>1</sub>K<sub>2</sub>, and K<sub>3</sub>K<sub>4</sub>.
</p>
<p>
(2) X(3640) and X(3641) lie on the line X(1)X(6).
</p>
<p>
(3) The lines K<sub>1</sub>K<sub>4</sub> and K<sub>2</sub>K<sub>3</sub> are parallel to the line X(1)X(6).
</p>
<p>
(4) The lines X(3640)K<sub>4</sub>, K<sub>1</sub>K<sub>3</sub>, and X(3641)K<sub>2</sub> are parallel; indeed, the reflection of the first in the second is the third.\
</p>
<p>
(5) The lines X(3640)K<sub>3</sub>, K<sub>2</sub>K<sub>3</sub>, and X(3641)K<sub>1</sub> are parallel, and the reflection of the first in the second is the third.
</p>
<p>
(6) The lines tangent to E at X(3640) and X(3641) are perpendicular to the line X(1)X(3), as are the lines K<sub>1</sub>K<sub>2</sub> and K<sub>3</sub>K<sub>4</sub>.
</p>
<p>
(7) The following four lines are parallel: the tangents to E at K<sub>2</sub> and K<sub>4</sub>, and X(3640)K<sub>1</sub> and
X(3641)K<sub>3</sub>.
</p>
<p>
(8) The following four lines are parallel: the tangents to E at K<sub>1</sub> and K<sub>3</sub>, and X(3640)K<sub>2</sub> and
X(3641)K<sub>4</sub>.
</p>
