<p>
Let A'B'C' be the tangential triangle of ABC, and let L be the line through X(1) parallel to BC.  Let A'' = L&cap;B'C', and define B'' and C'' cyclically.  Let A* = B'B''&cap;C'C'', and define B* and C* cyclically.  The lines AA*, BB*, CC* concur in X(3445); also, the lines A'A*, B'B*, C'C* concur in X(3052).  (Angel Montesdeoca, April 29, 2016)
</p>
