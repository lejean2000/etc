<p>
Let O<sub>A</sub> be the circle tangent to side BC at its midpoint
and to the circumcircle on the side of BC opposite A. Define
O<sub>B</sub> and O<sub>C</sub> cyclically. Let L<sub>A</sub> be the
external tangent to circles O<sub>B</sub> and O<sub>C</sub> that is the
nearer of the two to O<sub>A</sub>. Define L<sub>B</sub> and
L<sub>C</sub> cyclically. Let A' = L<sub>B</sub>&cap;L<sub>C</sub>, and
define B' and C' cyclically. Then A'B'C' is homothetic to ABC, and the
center of homothety is X(3616). See the reference at X(1001).
</p>
<p>
See Dasari Naga Vijay Krishna, <a href="http://www.ijaamm.com/uploads/2/1/4/8/21481830/v7n2p1_1-15.pdf"> On a Conic Through Twelve Notable Points</a>, <i>Int. J. Adv. Math. and Mech.</i> 7(2) (2019) 1-15.
</p>
