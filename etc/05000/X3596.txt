<h3 id="X3596">X(3596)&nbsp;=&nbsp;1st ODEHNAL POINT</h3>
Trilinears&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; f(a,b,c)
: f(b,c,a) : f(c,a,b), where f(a,b,c) = b<sup>3</sup>c<sup>3</sup>(b +
c - a)<br>
Trilinears&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; g(A,B,C) :
g(B,C,A) : g(C,A,B), where g(A, B, C) = csc<sup>2</sup>A
csc<sup>2</sup>(A/2)<br>
Barycentrics&nbsp;&nbsp; af(a,b,c) : bf(b,c,a) : cf(c,a,b)

<p>Let A', B', C' denote the respective excircles of a triangle ABC.
Let A'' be the circle tangent to A', B', C' whose interior includes A',
and likewise for circles B'' and C''. Let K<sub>aa</sub> be the point
of tangency of circles A' and A'', and likewise for Let K<sub>bb</sub>
and Let K<sub>cc</sub>. The lines A-to-K<sub>aa</sub>, B-to-Let
K<sub>bb</sub>, C-to-Let K<sub>cc</sub> concur in X(3596). For more,
see</p>

<p><b>Boris Odehnal,</b> "Some Triangle Centers Associated with the
Circles Tangent to the Excircles," <a href=
"http://forumgeom.fau.edu/FG2010volume10/FG201006.pdf">http://forumgeom.fau.edu/FG2010volume10/FG201006.pdf</a></p>
<p>Barycentrics for the center of the circle A'' are given by Peter
Moses (August 26, 2014):</p>
<p>
-2 a^4 (b+c)-(b-c)^2 (b+c)^3+a (b^2-c^2)^2+a^2 (b+c) (b^2+c^2)-a^3
(3 b^2+4 b c+3 c^2)<br>
: (b-c) c^2 (b+c)^2+a (b-c) c (b+c) (2 b+c)+a^2 (b+c) (2 b^2+c^2)+a^3
(2 b^2+2 b c+c^2)<br>
: (a-b) b^2 (a+b)^2+(a-b) b (a+b) (2 a+b) c+(a+b) (2 a^2+b^2) c^2+(2
a^2+2 a b+b^2) c^3</p>
