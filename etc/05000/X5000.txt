<h3 id="X5000">X(5000)&nbsp;=&nbsp;WALSMITH POINT</h3>
Barycentrics &nbsp;&nbsp; (SB+SC)*(S*SB*SC + SA*sqrt(SA*SB*SC*SW))  : :
<br>
Barycentrics &nbsp;&nbsp; S<sup>2</sup> -S<sub>B</sub>S<sub>C</sub> - k<sup>2</sup>(S<sup>2</sup> - 3S<sub>B</sub>S<sub>C</sub>) : : ,
<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;where k<sup>2</sup> = (3S<sub>A</sub>S<sub>B</sub>S<sub>C</sub> - S<sup>2</sup>S<sub>&omega;</sub> +      2(S<sub>A</sub>S<sub>B</sub>S<sub>C</sub>S<sup>2</sup>S<sub>&omega;</sub><sup>)1/2</sup>)/(9S<sub>A</sub>S<sub>B</sub>S<sub>C</sub> - S<sup>2</sup>S<sub>&omega;</sub>)
<br>
Barycentrics &nbsp;&nbsp; SS<sub>B</sub>S<sub>C</sub>(3S<sub>A</sub> - S<sub>&omega;</sub>) - (S<sup>2</sup> - 3S<sub>B</sub>S<sub>C</sub>)(S<sub>A</sub>S<sub>B</sub>S<sub>C</sub>S<sub>&omega;</sub>)^1/2 : :

<b>
X(5000) = (1 - k<sup>2</sup>)X(3) + k<sup>2</sup>X(4)<br>
X(5000) = 3k<sup>2</sup>X(2) + (1 - 3k<sup>2</sup>)X(3)  </b>
<p>
As a point on the Euler line, X(5000) has Shinagawa coefficients (1 -k<sup>2</sup>, -1 + 3k<sup>2</sup>).
</p>
<p>
The position of the point D = X(5000) on the Euler line is shown by distance ratios involving the familiar triangle centers G=X(2), O=X(3), and H=X(4) on the Euler line, as follows:
<br>
|OD|/|OG| = 3k<sup>2</sup><br>
|OD|/|OH| = k<sup>2</sup><br>
|GD|/|DH| = (1 - 3k<sup>2</sup>)/(3k<sup>2</sup> - 3).
</p>
<p>
See the preamble just before X(5000).
</p>
<p>
If the reference triangle ABC is obtuse, then the barycentrics of
X(5000) are a nonreal complex numbers. Nevertheless, for all triangles
ABC, the midpoint of X(5000) and X(5001) is X(468). The circles
mentioned just below are members of a coaxal system of which X(5000)
and X(5001) are the so-called point-circles or limiting points, so that
the circle with diameter X(5000)X(5001) is orthogonal to the circles in
the system.
</p>
<p>
X(5000) and X(5001) are the antiorthocorrespondents of X(6); i.e. they share the same orthocorrespondent, X(6). See Bernard Gibert, <a href="https://bernard-gibert.pagesperso-orange.fr/Tables/table55.html"><b>Table 55: X(5000, X(5001) and related curves</b></a>.
</p>
<p>
For a construction of X(5000) and X(5001) see <a href="http://www.adgeom.epizy.com/message.php?msg=5185&i=1"><b>AdGeom 5185</b></a>.
</p>
<p>
If you have The Geometer's Sketchpad, you can view <a href="X(5000).gsp" style="font-weight: bold">X(5000)</a>, or click <a href="X(5000).gif" style="font-weight: bold">here</a> for a 3-dimensional representation of X(5000) as a gif.
</p>
<p>
The Walsmith point, as well as X(5001), lie on the <b>Walsmith rectangular hyperbola</b>, introduced in the preamble just before X(32110).
</p>
<p>
In the plane of a triangle ABC, let P be a point on the Euler line and P' = isogonal conjugate of P, and let DEF = medial triangle. Let &ell;<sub>a</sub> = reflection of AP' in EF, and define  &ell;<sub>b</sub> and  &ell;<sub>c</sub> cyclically. The lines  &ell;<sub>a</sub>,  &ell;<sub>b</sub>,  &ell;<sub>c</sub> concur in a point, P", that lies on the Euler line.  The mapping P &srarr; P'' is a projective transformation of  the Euler line, and its fixed points are X(5000) and X(5001).  If P=X(3) + t X(4), then P"= 4 t SA SB SC X(3) + (a^2 b^2 c^2 + 4 t SA SB SC) X(4). The fixed points are obtained for t = (2 SA SB SC &pm; S Sqrt[2 (a^2+b^2+c^2) SA SB SC])/(4 SA SB SC).  (Angel Montesdeoca, April 19, 2021)
</p>
