<h3 id="X3644">X(3644)&nbsp;=&nbsp;REFLECTION OF X(75) IN X(192)</h3>
Trilinears&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;
f(a,b,c) : f(b,c,a) : f(c,a,b), where f(a,b,c) = bc(3/a - 2/b -
2/c)<br>
Barycentrics&nbsp;&nbsp; 3/a - 2/b - 2/c : 3/b - 2/c - 2/a : 3/c - 2/a
- 2/b<br>
<b>X(3644) = 9*X(2) - 10*X(37) = 6*X(2) - 5*X(75) = 3*X(2) - 5*X(192),
etc.  </b>

<p>For any point P, let L(A) be the line through P parallel to BC. Let
U = AB&cap;L(A) and V = AC&cap;L(A), and let a' = |UV|. Define b' and
c' cyclically. Let L be the line consisting of points P such that a +
ta' = b + tb' = c + tc'. Then X(3644) is given by t=1. The points
corresponding to t = -1, -1/2, and 0, are X(75), X(37), and X(192),
respectively. (Seiichi Kirikami, November 7, 2010)</p>
<p>
X(3644) = reflection of X(i) in X(j) for these (i,j): (75,192),
(1278,37)
</p>
