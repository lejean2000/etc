<h3 id="X3657">X(3657)&nbsp;=&nbsp;AYME PERSPECTOR</h3>
Trilinears&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;
f(a,b,c) : f(b,c,a) : f(c,a,b), where f(a,b,c) = (b<sup>2</sup> -
c<sup>2</sup>)/[2a(b + c)s<sub>B</sub>s<sub>C</sub> - S<sup>2</sup> -
S<sub>B</sub>S<sub>C</sub>]
<br>
Barycentrics&nbsp;&nbsp; af(a,b,c) : bf(b,c,a) : cf(c,a,b)
<p>
Let A'B'C' be the medial triangle of the reference triangle ABC. Let
P be the point of intersection of the lines X(1)X(3) and BC, and let P'
be the point where the line through P perpendicular to line AX(3) meets
that line. Let L<sub>A</sub> be the line PP', and define L<sub>B</sub>
and L<sub>C</sub> cyclically. Let A''=L<sub>B</sub>&cap;L<sub>C</sub>,
and define B'' and C'' cyclically. The lines AA'', BB'', CC'' concur in
X(3657). (Jean-Louis Ayme, Hyacinthos #16676, August 21, 2008)</p>
<p>The Ayme triangle A''B''C'' is also perspective to these triangles:
tangential, orthic, intangents, extangents, and the circumorthic.
(Peter Moses, November 7, 2011)</p>
