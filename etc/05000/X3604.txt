<h3 id="X3604">X(3604)&nbsp;=&nbsp;ISOGONAL CONJUGATE OF X(3273)</h3>
Trilinears&nbsp; &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; csc(A/3 + &pi;/3) : csc(B/3 + &pi;/3) : csc(C/3 + &pi;/3)
<br>
Trilinears &nbsp;&nbsp; cos(A/3) + cos(B/3 - C/3) : :
<br>
Barycentrics&nbsp;&nbsp; (sin A)csc(A/3 + &pi;/3) : (sin B)csc(B/3 + &pi;/3) : (sin C)csc(C/3 + &pi;/3)
<p>
X(3604) is {X(1134), X(1135)}-harmonic conjugate of X(3277).
(Bernard Gibert, November 3, 2010)</p>

<p>
Let A'B'C' be the 1st Morley triangle.  Let Ba and Ca be points on BC such that A'BcCa is an equilateral triangle; define Cb and Ac cyclically, and define Ab and Bc cyclically. Let A'' be the midpoint of Ab and Ac, and define B'' and C'' cyclically.  Then A''B''C'', here named the <b>1st MORLEY-MIDPOINT TRIANGLE,</b> is an equilateral triangle that is homothetic to A'B'C' and perspective to ABC, with perspector X(3604).  (Thanh Oai Dao and Peter Moses, March 30, 2018)
</p>
<p>
Let A'B'C' be the 3rd Morley triangle.  Let A" be the trilinear pole of line B'C', and define B" and C" cyclically.  The lines AA", BB", CC" concur in X(3604).  (Randy Hutson, July 11, 2019)
</p>
<p>
Let A'B'C' be the 3rd Morley triangle.  Let A* be the foot of the perpendicular from A to line B'C', and define B* and C* cyclically.  The lines A'A*, B'B*, C'C* concur in X(3604).  (Randy Hutson, July 11, 2019)
</p>
