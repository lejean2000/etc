<h3 id="X3411">X(3411)&nbsp;=&nbsp;1st BROCARD-KIEPERT-FERMAT
CUSP</h3>
Trilinears&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; f(A,B,C)
: f(B,C,A) : f(C,A,B), where f(A,B,C) = cos A - 2 sin B sin C - 3 cos(A
+&pi;/3)

<p>Trilinears&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; g(A,B,C) :
g(B,C,A) : g(C,A,B), where g(A,B,C) = cos A + 4 sin B sin C -
3<sup>3/2</sup>sin A</p>

<p>Barycentrics&nbsp;&nbsp; h(A,B,C) : h(B,C,A) : h(C,A,B), where
h(A,B,C) = 16S<sup>2</sup> - 2*3<sup>3/2</sup>a<sup>2</sup>S +
a<sup>2</sup>S<sub>A</sub>, where S = 2(area(ABC))</p>

<p>Barycentrics&nbsp;&nbsp; h(A,B,C) : h(B,C,A) : h(C,A,B), where
h(A,B,C) = 20S<sup>2</sup> - 2*3<sup>3/2</sup>S -
S<sub>B</sub>S<sub>C</sub></p>

<p>X(3411) and X(3412) occur in Bernard Gibert's study of the
Brocard-Kiepert quartic, which has three cusps: X(20) with Euler line
as tangent; X(3411) with line X(5)X(13) as tangent, and X(3412) with
line X(5)X(14) as tangent.</p>

<p>X(3411) lies on the <a href=
"http://pagesperso-orange.fr/bernard.gibert/curves/q073.html" style=
"font-weight: bold">Brocard-Kiepert quartic, Q073</a>, and on these
lines: 5,13&nbsp;&nbsp; 6,3412&nbsp;&nbsp; 14,382&nbsp;&nbsp; 16,20&nbsp;&nbsp;
61,631&nbsp;&nbsp; 398,548&nbsp;&nbsp; 630,3180
</p>
<p>
