<p class="preamble-title">
Barycentric Products of Perpendicular Directions
</p>
<img src="rightri.svg" style="height:30px" alt="rightri">
</header>
<div class="preamble-sect">

<p>
Suppose P and U are points on the line at infinity, given in
barycentric coordinates by P = p : q : r and U = u : v : w. We call P
and U <i>perpendicular directions</i> if for every point X not in the
line at infinity, the lines XP and XU are perpendicular. (It is well
known that if P and U are an antipodal pair on the circumcircle, then
their isogonal conjugates are perpendicular directions.)
</p>
<p>Theorem: If P and U are perpendicular directions, then their
barycentric product lies on the orthic axis.</p>
<p>An outline of a proof follows. For given P on the line at infinity,
the direction perpendicular to P is given by</p>


<p>
a/d<sub>1</sub> : b/d<sub>2</sub> : c/d<sub>3</sub>,

<br>
where
</p>

<p>d<sub>1</sub> = a<sup>2</sup>qr cos B cos C - p(b<sub>2</sub>r +
c<sub>2</sub>q) cos A<br>
d<sub>2</sub> = b<sup>2</sup>rp cos C cos A - q(c<sub>2</sub>p +
a<sub>2</sub>r) cos B<br>
d<sub>3</sub> = c<sup>2</sup>pq cos A cos B - r(a<sub>2</sub>q +
b<sub>2</sub>p) cos C,</p>

<p>and the barycentric product P*U is the point x : y : z given by</p>

<p>ap/d<sub>1</sub> : bq/d<sub>2</sub> : cr/d<sub>3</sub>.</p>

<p>For P to lie on the line at infinity means that r = - p - q.
Substitution for r, a computer quickly shows that</p>

<p>x cot A + y cot B + z cot C = 0,</p>

<p>as desired.</p>

<p>The orthic axis is perpendicular to the Euler line. For this and
other properties, visit <a href=
"http://mathworld.wolfram.com/OrthicAxis.html" style=
"font-weight: bold">MathWorld</a>. The appearance of (I, J, K) in the
following list means that X(K) is the barycentric product of
perpendicular directions X(i) and X(j).</p>
<p>
(30,1511,1637)<br>
(511,523,2491)<br>
(514,516,676)<br>
(2574,2575,647)<br>
(513,517,3310)<br>
</p>
