<p>
Let A'B'C' be the intouch triangle.  Let L<sub>A</sub> be the reflection of line B'C' in the perpedicular bisector of BC, and define L<sub>B</sub> and L<sub>C</sub> cyclically.  Let A" = L<sub>B</sub>&cap;L<sub>C</sub>, and define B" and C" cyclically.  The lines AA", BB", CC" concur in X(3417).  The results are the same if 'hexyl triangle' is substituted for 'intouch triangle'.  (Randy Hutson, July 11, 2019)
