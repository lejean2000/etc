<p>
In the plane of a triangle ABC, let I = X(1) and<br>
L = line through midpoint of CA perpendicular to BI<br>
L' = line through midpoint of AB perpendicular to CI<br>
L'' = line through midpoint of AI perpendicular to BC<br>
The lines L, L', L'' concur in a point, A'; define B' and C' cyclically.  Ten X(3817) = X(2)-of-A'B'C'.
Also, A'B'C' = complement of the excentral triangle, and A'B'C' = extraversion triangle of X(10).    (Randy Hutson, September 14, 2016)
</p>
<p>
Let A'B'C' be the intouch triangle, and AaAbAc, BaBbBc, CaCbCc the A-, B-, and C-extouch triangles.  Let Ab' = B'C'&cap;BcBa and Ac' = B'C'&cap;CaCb.  Define Bc' and Ba', Ca', Cb' cyclically.  Then X(3817) is the centroid of {Ab',Ac',Bc',Ba',Ca',Cb'} is X(3817).   The six points lie on a common ellipse.    (Randy Hutson, September 14, 2016)
</p>
<p>
Let Wa be the inverter of the B- and C-excircles, and define Wb and Wc cyclically; see X(5577).  Let Ia be Wa-inverse of the incircle, and define Ib and Ic cyclically.  The radical center of circles Ia, Ib, Ic is X(3817).    (Randy Hutson, September 14, 2016)
</p>
<p>
Let A'B'C' be the circumcevian triangle of X(1).  Let L1 be the Simson line of A', and define L2 and L3 cyclically.  Let A'' = L2&cap;L3, and define B'' and C'' cyclically.  Then X(3817) = X(2)-of-A''B''C''.  (Angel Montesdeoca, June 28, 1017)
</p>
<p>
X(3817) is the centroid of AbAcBcBaCaCb in the construction of the perspeconic of the Ursa-minor and Ursa-major triangles. (Randy Hutson, June 27, 2018)
