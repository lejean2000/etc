<p class="preamble-title">
Points Associated with Equilateral Triangles
</p>
<img src="rightri.svg" style="height:30px" alt="rightri">
</header>
<div class="preamble-sect">
<p>
The points X(3272) to X(3283) are associated with six equilateral
triangles related to the (1st) Morley triangle; trilinears for vertices
of these triangles are given just below. Received from Milorad R.
Stevanovic, Nov. 24, 2007 and Dec. 25, 2007.
</p>

<p><b>1st Morley triangle,</b> M<sub>1</sub>M<sub>2</sub>M<sub>3</sub>,
where<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;M<sub>1</sub> = 1 : 2 cos C/3 : 2 cos B/3<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;M<sub>2</sub> = 2 cos C/3 : 1 : 2 cos A/3<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;M<sub>3</sub> = 2 cos B/3 : 2 cos A/3 : 1</p>

<p><b>2nd Morley triangle,</b> P<sub>1</sub>P<sub>2</sub>P<sub>3</sub>,
where<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;P<sub>1</sub> = 1 : 2 cos(C/3 - 2&pi;/3) : 2
cos(B/3 - 2&pi;/3)<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;P<sub>2</sub> = 2 cos(C/3 - 2&pi;/3) : 1 : 2
cos(A/3 - 2&pi;/3)<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;P<sub>3</sub> = 2 cos(B/3 - 2&pi;/3) : 2
cos(A/3 - 2&pi;/3) : 1</p>

<p><b>3rd Morley triangle,</b> S<sub>1</sub>S<sub>2</sub>S<sub>3</sub>,
where<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;S<sub>1</sub> = 1 : 2 cos(C/3 - 4&pi;/3) : 2
cos(B/3 - 4&pi;/3)<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;S<sub>2</sub> = 2 cos(C/3 - 4&pi;/3) : 1 : 2
cos(A/3 - 4&pi;/3)<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;S<sub>3</sub> = 2 cos(B/3 - 4&pi;/3) : 2
cos(A/3 - 4&pi;/3) : 1</p>

<p>The triangles M<sub>1</sub>M<sub>2</sub>M<sub>3</sub>,
P<sub>1</sub>P<sub>2</sub>P<sub>3</sub>,
S<sub>1</sub>S<sub>2</sub>S<sub>3</sub> are discussed in TCCT, page
165-166. The three are pairwise homothetic. See also<br>
<a href="http://mathworld.wolfram.com/FirstMorleyTriangle.html">1st
Morley triangle at MathWorld</a><br>
<a href="http://mathworld.wolfram.com/SecondMorleyTriangle.html">2nd
Morley triangle at MathWorld</a><br>
<a href="http://mathworld.wolfram.com/ThirdMorleyTriangle.html">3rd
Morley triangle at MathWorld</a></p>

<p><b>Unique equilateral triangle inscribed in ABC and homothetic to
the 1st Morley triangle</b>, J<sub>1</sub>J<sub>2</sub>J<sub>3</sub>,
where<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;J<sub>1</sub> = 0 : sin(A/3 - C/3 + &pi;/3) :
sin(A/3 - B/3 + &pi;/3)<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;J<sub>2</sub> = sin(B/3 - C/3 + &pi;/3) : 0 :
sin(B/3 - A/3 + &pi;/3)<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;J<sub>3</sub> = sin(C/3 - B/3 + &pi;/3) :
sin(C/3 - A/3 + &pi;/3) : 0</p>

<p><b>Circumtangential triangle,</b> inscribed in the circumcircle of
ABC and homothetic to the 1st Morley triangle,
T<sub>1</sub>T<sub>2</sub>T<sub>3</sub>. For trilinears, of the
vertices, see TCCT, page 166, or <a href=
"http://mathworld.wolfram.com/CircumtangentialTriangle.html" style=
"font-weight: bold">MathWorld</a>. Trilinears found by M.
Stevanovic:</p>

<p>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;T<sub>1</sub> = sin(B/3 - A/3 + &pi;/3)
sin(C/3 - A/3 + &pi;/3) : sin(C/3 - B/3) sin(B/3 - A/3 + &pi;/3) :
sin(B/3 - C/3) sin(C/3 - A/3 + &pi;/3)<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;T<sub>2</sub> = sin(C/3 - A/3) sin(A/3 - B/3 +
&pi;/3) : sin(C/3 - B/3 + &pi;/3) sin(A/3 - B/3 + &pi;/3) : sin(A/3 -
C/3) sin(C/3 - B/3 + &pi;/3)<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;T<sub>3</sub> = sin(B/3 - A/3) sin(A/3 - C/3 +
&pi;/3) : sin(A/3 - B/3) sin(B/3 - C/3 + &pi;/3) : sin(A/3 - C/3 +
&pi;/3) sin(B/3 - C/3 + &pi;/3)</p>

<p><b>Circumnormal triangle</b> inscribed in the circumcircle of ABC
and homothetic to the 1st Morley triangle,
N<sub>1</sub>N<sub>2</sub>N<sub>3</sub>. For trilinears, of the
vertices, see TCCT, page 166, or <a href=
"http://mathworld.wolfram.com/CircumnormalTriangle.html" style=
"font-weight: bold">MathWorld</a>. Trilinears found by M.
Stevanovic:</p>

<p>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;N<sub>1</sub> = - cos(B/3 - A/3 + &pi;/3)
cos(C/3 - A/3 + &pi;/3) : cos(B/3 - C/3) cos(B/3 - A/3 + &pi;/3) :
cos(B/3 - C/3) cos(C/3 - A/3 + &pi;/3)<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;N<sub>2</sub> = cos(C/3 - A/3) cos(A/3 - B/3 +
&pi;/3) : - cos(C/3 - B/3 + &pi;/3) cos(A/3 - B/3 + &pi;/3) : cos(A/3 -
A/3) cos(C/3 - B/3 + &pi;/3)<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;N<sub>3</sub> = cos(A/3 - B/3) cos(A/3 - C/3 +
&pi;/3) : cos(A/3 - B/3) cos(B/3 - C/3 + &pi;/3) : - cos(A/3 - C/3 +
&pi;/3) cos(B/3 - C/3 + &pi;/3)</p>

<p>See also X(3602)-X(3609) and</p>

<p>Jean-Pierre Ehrmann and Bernard Gibert, "A Morley Configuration,"
<i>Forum Geometricorum</i> 1 (2001) 51-58. (Click <a href=
"http://forumgeom.fau.edu/FG2001volume1/FG200108.pdf">here</a> to
download a PDF of this article.)</p>
