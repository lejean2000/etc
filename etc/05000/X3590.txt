<h3 id="X3590">X(3590)&nbsp;=&nbsp;1st DIXIT POINT</h3>
Trilinears&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; f(a,b,c)
: f(b,c,a) : f(c,a,b), where f(a,b,c) = bc/(b<sup>2</sup> +
c<sup>2</sup> - a<sup>2</sup> + 6*area(ABC))<br>
Trilinears&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; g(A,B,C) :
g(B,C,A) : g(C,A,B), where g(A, B, C) = 1/(2 cos A + 3 sin A)<br>
Barycentrics&nbsp;&nbsp; 1/(2S<sub>A</sub> + 3S) : 1/(2S<sub>B</sub> +
3S) : 1/(2S<sub>C</sub> + 3S)&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; [Conway notation]

<p>On Dec. 22, 2000, Atul Dixit constructed this point in Hyacinthos
message #2183, as follows. Let ABC be a triangle with medians AD, BE,
FC and centroid G. Construct semicircles with diameters BD, DC, BC
outwardly. Let T<sub>A</sub> be the circle tangent to the three
semicircles BD, DC, BC, and let A' be the center of T<sub>A</sub>.
Define B' and C' cyclically. Then the lines AA', BB', CC' concur in
X(3590). Barycentric coordinates were found by Paul Yiu (2000), and
further properties and related points, by Peter Moses (2010).</p>

<p>The radius of circle T<sub>A</sub> is |BC|/6, and the Kiepert angle
(e.g., B-to-C-to-X(3890)) is arctan(2/3).</p>
