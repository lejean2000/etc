<p>
Let M<sub>A</sub>, M<sub>B</sub>, M<sub>C</sub> be the centers of
the circles A'', B'', C'' used to construct X(3596), respectively. Then
the lines A-to-M<sub>A</sub>, B-to-M<sub>B</sub>, C-to-M<sub>C</sub>
concur in X(3597). For more, see the reference and sketch at X(3596).
</p>
