<h3 id="X1142">X(1142)&nbsp;=&nbsp;1st MALFATTI-RABINOWITZ POINT</h3>
Trilinears&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;f(A,B,C) : f(B,C,A) : f(C,A,B),<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;where
f(A,B,C) = 1 - 2(1 + cos B/2)(1 + cos C/2)/(1 + cos A/2)
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; (M. Iliev, 5/13/07)<br>
Trilinears&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;g(A,B,C) : g(B,C,A)
: g(C,A,B),<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;where
g(A,B,C) = 1 - 4
sec<sup>2</sup>(A/4)cos<sup>2</sup>(B/4)cos<sup>2</sup>(C/4)
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; (M. Iliev, 5/13/07)
<p>Barycentrics&nbsp;&nbsp;(sin A)f(A,B,C) : (sin B)f(B,C,A) : (sin
C)f(C,A,B)</p>

<p>Let A', B', C' be the respective centers of the three Malfatti
circles of ABC. Let A" be the point of intersection of lines BC' and
CB', and define B" and C" cyclically. Then triangle A"B"C" is
perspective to ABC, and the perspector is X(1142). (Stanley Rabinowitz,
#4610, 12/29/01; coordinates by Paul Yiu, #4614, 12/30/01)
</p>
<p>
Let A', B', C' be the centers of the Malfatti circles.  Let A" be the trilinear product B'*C', and define B" and C" cyclically.  The lines AA", BB", CC" concur in X(1142).  The lines A'A", B'B", C'C" concur in X(179).  (Randy Hutson, March 21, 2019)
</p>
