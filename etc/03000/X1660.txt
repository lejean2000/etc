<h3 id="X1660">X(1660)&nbsp;=&nbsp;1st GRINBERG MIDPOINTS PERSPECTOR</h3>
Trilinears&nbsp; a<sup>3</sup>[b<sup>8</sup> + c<sup>8</sup> - a<sup>8</sup> - 2a<sup>2</sup>(b<sup>6</sup> + c<sup>6</sup>) + 2b<sup>2</sup>c<sup>2</sup>(b<sup>4</sup> + c<sup>4</sup> - 5a<sup>4</sup> + 3a<sup>2</sup>b<sup>2</sup> + 3a<sup>2</sup>c<sup>2</sup> - 3b<sup>2</sup>c<sup>2</sup>) + 2a<sup>6</sup>(b<sup>2</sup> + c<sup>2</sup>)]
<p>
Let A<sub>B</sub> be the point in which the line through A
perpendicular to CA meets line BC, and define points A<sub>C</sub>,
B<sub>C</sub>, B<sub>A</sub>, C<sub>A</sub>, C<sub>B</sub>
functionally. Let</p>
<p>X<sub>A</sub> = midpoint{A<sub>B</sub>, A<sub>C</sub>},<br>
Y<sub>A</sub> = midpoint{B<sub>A</sub>, C<sub>A</sub>},<br>
Z<sub>A</sub> = midpoint{B<sub>C</sub>, C<sub>B</sub>},</p>
<p>and define X<sub>B</sub>, X<sub>C</sub>, Y<sub>B</sub>,
Y<sub>C</sub>, Z<sub>B</sub>, Z<sub>C</sub> functionally.</p>
<p>The lines AX<sub>A</sub>, BX<sub>B</sub>, CX<sub>C</sub> concur in
X(20).<br>
The lines AY<sub>A</sub>, BY<sub>B</sub>, CY<sub>C</sub> concur in
X(393).<br>
The lines AZ<sub>A</sub>, BZ<sub>B</sub>, CZ<sub>C</sub> concur in
X(6).<br>
The lines X<sub>A</sub>Y<sub>A</sub>, X<sub>B</sub>Y<sub>B</sub>,
X<sub>C</sub>Y<sub>C</sub> concur in X(1660).<br>
The lines Y<sub>A</sub>Z<sub>A</sub>, Y<sub>B</sub>Z<sub>B</sub>,
Y<sub>C</sub>Z<sub>C</sub> concur in X(3).<br>
The lines Z<sub>A</sub>X<sub>A</sub>, Z<sub>B</sub>X<sub>B</sub>,
Z<sub>C</sub>X<sub>C</sub> concur in X(1661).</p>
