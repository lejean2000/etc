<h3 id="X1118">X(1118)&nbsp;=&nbsp;1st HATZIPOLAKIS PERSPECTOR</h3>
Trilinears&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;f(A,B,C)
: f(B,C,A) : f(C,A,B), where f(A,B,C) = (1 - cos A)/cos<sup>2</sup>A<br>
Barycentrics (sin A)f(A,B,C) : (sin B)f(B,C,A) : (sin
C)f(C,A,B)
<p>
Let A'B'C' be the intouch triangle of ABC. Let C<sub>A</sub> be the
point other than C' in which the perpendicular to BC from C' meets the
incircle, let B<sub>A</sub> be the point other than B' in which the
perpendicular to BC from B' meets the incircle, and let A<sub>0</sub>
be the point of intersection of lines BC<sub>A</sub> and
CB<sub>A</sub>. Define B<sub>0</sub> and C<sub>0</sub> cyclically. Then
triangle A<sub>0</sub>B<sub>0</sub>C<sub>0</sub> is perspective to ABC,
and the perspector is X(1118). (Antreas Hatzipolakis, #5321,
4/30/02)
</p>
<p>
X(1118) is the trilinear product A<sub>0</sub>*B<sub>0</sub>*C<sub>0</sub>, where A<sub>0</sub>, B<sub>0</sub>, C<sub>0</sub> are as defined above.  (Randy Hutson, January 15, 2019)
</p>
