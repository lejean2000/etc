<br>

<p>
Let A'B'C' = circumcevian triangle of X(1), and let<br>
Oa = center of A-mixtilinear incircle<br>
Ta = touchpoint of A-mixtilinear incircle and circumcircle<br>
A1 = circumcenter of A'OaTa, and define B1 and C1 cyclically.
Then AA1, BB1, CC1 concur in X(2334).  (Angel Montesdeoca, November 29, 2018)
</p><p>
Let DEF be the cevian triangle of X(8), and let A5 is the QA-P5 center (Isotomic Center) of AEFX(8) (see Chris van Tienhoven, Quadrangle points and other objects). Let
B5 be the QA-P5 center of BFDX(8) and C5 the QA-P5 center of CDEX(8).  Then the triangles ABC and A5B5C5 are perspective, and X(2334) is their perspector.  (Angel Montesdeoca, March 20, 2019)
</p><p>
In the plane of a triangle ABFC, let<br>
DEF =  cevian triangle of X(1); <br>
&Gamma; = circumcircle;<br>
A<sub>a</sub> = pole of EF wrt &Gamma;<br>
T<sub>1</sub> and T<sub>2</sub> = lines through A<sub>a</sub> tangent to BC;<br>
A<sub>b</sub> and A<sub>c</sub> = T<sub>1</sub>&cap;BC and T<sub>2</sub>&cap;BC;<br> 
Define B<sub>c</sub> and C<sub>a</sub> cyclically, and define B<sub>a</sub> and C<sub>b</sub> cyclically.<br>
The six points A<sub>b</sub>, A<sub>c</sub>, B<sub>c</sub>, B<sub>a</sub>, C<sub>a</sub>, C<sub>b</sub> lie on a conic, &Cscr;, given by the barycentric equation
<br>
b^2 c^2x^2+a^2 c^2y^2+a^2 b^2z^2+a^2 (a^2+b^2+c^2+2 a (b+c))yz+b^2 (a^2+2 a b+(b+c)^2)zx+c^2 (a^2+2 a c+(b+c)^2)xy= 0.
</br>
The polars of A<sub>a</sub>, B<sub>b</sub>, C<sub>c</sub> with respect to &Cscr; form a perspective triangle to ABC, and the perspector is X(2334). (Angel Montesdeoca, November 27, 2022)
