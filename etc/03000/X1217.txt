<h3 id="X1217">X(1217)&nbsp;=&nbsp;CRETAN PERSPECTOR</h3>
Trilinears&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;f(A,B,C)
: f(B,C,A) : f(C,A,B), where f(A,B,C) = (sec A)/(1 - cos A sin B sin
C)<br>
Barycentrics&nbsp;&nbsp;g(A,B,C) : g(B,C,A) : g(C,A,B), where g(A,B,C)
= (tan A)/(1 - cos A sin B sin C)<br>
Barycentrics&nbsp;&nbsp;h(a,b,c) : h(b,c,a) : h(c,a,b), where h(a,b,c)
= 1/(a<sup>2</sup> - 3b<sup>2</sup> -
3c<sup>2</sup>)&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;(Peter Moses, Oct.
22, 2012)
<p>Let A'B'C' be the medial triangle of the orthic triangle of triangle
ABC. Let A" be the reflection of X(4) in A', and define B" and C"
cyclically. Let K<sub>ab</sub> and K<sub>ac</sub> be the circumcenters
of triangles A"BA and A"CA, respectively. Let A''' =
BK<sub>ac</sub>&cap;CK<sub>ab</sub>, and define B''' and C'''
cyclically. The lines AA''', BB''', CC''' concur in X(1217). (Antreas
Hatzipolakis, Anopolis #39, 3/19/2002)</p>

<p>Using barycentric coordinates, let P = p : q : r be a point not on a
sideline of triangle ABC, and let P' be the isogonal conjugate of P.
Let DEF and D'E'F' be the pedal triangles of P and P', respectively.
Let</p>

<p>X = PD'&cap;P'D,&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Y =
PE'&cap;P'E,&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Z = PF'&cap;P'F.</p>

<p>Then the triangle XYZ is perspective to ABC (Dominik Burek, June 8,
2012) and the perspector is given by barycentric coordinates k(a,b,c) :
k(b,c,a) : k(c,a,b), where k = 1/(pqr(b<sup>2</sup> + c<sup>2</sup> -
a<sup>2</sup>) + 2p(b<sup>2</sup>r<sup>2</sup> +
c<sup>2</sup>q<sup>2</sup>)) &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; (Peter Moses,
Oct. 22, 2012). The point X(1217) results from taking P = X(3).</p>

<p>X(1217) lies on these lines:
2,1093&nbsp;&nbsp; 3,393&nbsp;&nbsp; 4,394&nbsp;&nbsp;
5,1073&nbsp;&nbsp; 20,97&nbsp;&nbsp; 254,378</p>
