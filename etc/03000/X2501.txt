<p>
Let L<sub>A</sub> be the orthic axis of triangle BCX(4), and define L<sub>B</sub>, L<sub>C</sub> cyclically.  Let A' = L<sub>B</sub>&cap;L<sub>C</sub>, B' = L<sub>C</sub>&cap;L<sub>A</sub>, C' = L<sub>A</sub>&cap;L<sub>B</sub>.  Triangle A'B'C' is here named the <b>orthic axes triangle</b>.  A'B'C' is also the cevian triangle of X(4) wrt the orthic triangle.  A'B'C' is perspective to ABC at X(4), and homothetic to the polar triangle of the nine-point circle at X(427).  The orthic axes of ABC, the orthic triangle, and the orthic axes triangle concur in X(2501).  (Randy Hutson, August 19, 2019)
</p>
<p>
Let A"B"C" be the reflection triangle.  Then A' is the polar-circle-inverse of A".  (Randy Hutson, January 19, 2020)
</p>
