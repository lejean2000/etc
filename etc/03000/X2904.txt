<h3 id="X2904">X(2904)&nbsp;=&nbsp;ORTHIC-ISOGONAL CONJUGATE OF X(24)</h3>
Trilinears &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; f(a,b,c) : f(b,c,a) : f(c,a,b), where f(a,b,c) is as described just before X(2883)
<br>
Barycentrics &nbsp;&nbsp; a^2(a^4 + b^4 + c^4 - 2a^2b^2 - 2a^2c^2)[a^6 - 3a^4(b^2 + c^2) + 3a^2(b^4 + c^4) - (b^2 - c^2)^2(b^2 + c^2)]/(b^2 + c^2 - a^2) : :
<p>
Let O be the circumcircle of a triangle ABC.  Let<br>
A' = perpendicular bisector of segment OA, and define B' and C' cyclically<br>
A'' = B'&cap;C', and define B'' and C'' cyclically<br>
A* = reflection of A* in BC, and define B* and C* cyclically.<br>
Then A*B*C* is perspective to the orthic triangle of ABC, and the perspector is X(2904).  (Angel Montesdeoca, November 12, 2016).
</p>
<p>
Let P be an arbitrary point of the circumcircle , O, and let S(P) be the Simson-Wallace line of P, and P' the reflection of P in S(P).  The locus of P' as P ranges through O is a bicircular circumquartic that meets the sidelines of ABC in 6 points other than A, B, C.  The 6 points lie on a conic, here called the <b>Montesdeoca conic</b>, M.  The center of M is X(2904), and the perspector of M is X(14111).  Let u = S<sub>A</sub>, v = S<sub>B</sub>, w = S<sub>C</sub>, f(a,b,c) = u<sup>2</sup>(S<sup>2</sup> - v<sup>2</sup>)(S<sup>2</sup> - w<sup>2</sup>), g(a,b,c) = 2vw(u<sup>2</sup> - S<sup>2</sup>)(S<sup>2</sup> + vw).  A barycentric equation for M is
</p>
<p>
f(a,b,c)x<sup>2</sup> + f(b,c,a)y<sup>2</sup> + (f,c,a,b)z<sup>2</sup> + g(a,b,c)yz + g(b,c,a)zx + g(c,b,a)xy = 0.
</p>

"X(2904).png" style="font-weight: bold">X(2904)</a>.
</p>
