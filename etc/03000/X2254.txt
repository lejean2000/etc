<p>
The tangent to the nine-point circle at the point where it is tangent to the incircle (X(11), the Feuerbach point) is here named the <b>Feuerbach tangent line</b>.  It is line X(11)X(244).  The inverse of the incircle in the excircles radical circle is a circle tangent to the Apollonius circle at X(3030).  The line tangent to the Apollonius circle at X(3030) is here named the <b>Apollonius tangent line</b>.  It is line X(2254)X(3030).  X(2254) is the intersection of the Feuerbach tangent line and the Apollonius tangent line.  (Randy Hutson, January 15, 2019)
</p>
