<h3 id="X1490">X(1490)&nbsp;=&nbsp;X(1)X(4)&cap;X(3)X(9)</h3>
Trilinears &nbsp;&nbsp; 1 + sec A - sec B - sec C : :
<p>
X(1490) is X(68)-of-the-excentral triangle and the reflection of
X(84) in X(3). These and other properties itemized here were reported
by Darij Grinberg, May 19, 2003.
</p>
<p>
Let U(A) be the circle with center A having the radius of the
A-excircle, and define U(B) and U(C) cyclically. Then X(1490) is the
radical center of the three circles. (Hauke Reddmann, Hyacinthos, Jan.
8, 2009)
</p>
<p>
Let Oa be the circle centered at A and passing through the A-excenter, and define Ob and Oc cyclically.  The radical center of Oa, Ob, Oc = X(1490).  (Randy Hutson, September 14, 2016)
</p>
<p>
Let A'B'C' be the cevian triangle of X(1034).  Let A" be the orthocenter of AB'C', and define B" and C" cyclically.  The lines AA", BB", CC" concur in X(1490).  (Randy Hutson, September 14, 2016)
</p>
