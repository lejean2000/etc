<b>

</b>
<p>The circle having center X(39) and radius 2R sin<sup>2</sup>&omega;,  where R denotes the circumradius of triangle ABC, is here introduced as the Moses circle. It is tangent to the nine-point circle at X(115), and its internal and external centers of similitude with the incircle are X(1500) and X(1015), respectively. (based on notes from Peter J. C. Moses, 5/29/03)
</p>
<p>
X(1015) is the center of the hyperbola that passes through the points A, B, C, X(1), X(2), and X(28); also, X(1015) lies on the ellipse described at X(1125). (Randy Hutson, Hyacinthos #20179, 8/13/2011)</p>
