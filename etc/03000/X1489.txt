<h3 id="X1489">X(1489)&nbsp;=&nbsp;3rd STEVANOVIC POINT</h3>
Trilinears&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;f(A,B,C)
: f(B,C,A) : f(C,A,B), where f(A,B,C) = (csc A)[1 - cos(A/2) -
sin(A/2)]<br>
Barycentrics&nbsp;&nbsp;1 - cos(A/2) - sin(A/2) : 1 - cos(B/2) -
sin(B/2) : 1 - cos(C/2) - sin(C/2)
<p>Let U be the A-excenter of triangle ABC; let A' be the incenter of
triangle UBC, and define B', C' cyclically. Let I be the incenter of
ABC, let A'' be the incenter of triangle IBC, and define B'', C''
cyclically. Let X = A'A''&cap;BC, and define Y, Z cyclically. Then AX,
BY, CZ concur in X(1489). (Milorad R. Stevanovic, Hyacinthos #7185,
5/21/03. See also X(1130) and X(1489).)</p>

<p>X(1489) lies on these lines: 1,188&nbsp;&nbsp; 2,1143&nbsp;&nbsp;
174,558&nbsp;&nbsp; 258,483</p>
<p>
X(1489) = complement of X(1143)<br>
X(1489) = cevapoint of X(1) and X(258)<br>
X(1489) = crosspoint of X(2) and X(1274)<br>
