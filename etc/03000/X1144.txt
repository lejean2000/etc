<h3 id="X1144">X(1144)&nbsp;=&nbsp;EHRMANN CONGRUENT SQUARES POINT</h3>
Trilinears &nbsp;&nbsp; a/(a - L) : b/(b - L) : c/(c - L), where L = L(a,b,c) is the smallest root of a<sup>2</sup>/(a - L) +
b<sup>2</sup>/(b - L) + c<sup>2</sup>/(c - L) = 2D/L, and D = area(ABC)
<br>
Barycentrics a<sup>2</sup>/(a - L) : b<sup>2</sup>/(b - L) : c<sup>2</sup>/(c - L)
<p>
Suppose that P is a point inside triangle ABC. Let S<sub>A</sub> be the square inscribed in triangle PBC, having two vertices on segment BC, one on PB, and one on PC. Define S<sub>B</sub> and S<sub>C</sub> cyclically. Then X(1144) is the unique choice of P for which the three squares are congruent. The function L(a,b,c) is symmetric, homogeneous of degree 1, and satisfies 0 &lt; L(a,b,c) &lt; min{a,b,c}. Also, X(1144) lies on the hyperbola {A,B,C,X(1),X(6)}; indeed, X(1144) lies on the open arc from X(1) to the vertex of ABC opposite the shortest side. L(a,b,c) is the common length of the sides of the three squares. (Jean-Pierre Ehrmann, 12/16/01)
</p>
<p>
See <b>Jean-Pierre Ehrmann,</b>  <a href="http://forumgeom.fau.edu/FG2002volume2/FG200203.pdf"><b>Congruent Inscribed Rectangles</b></a>.
</p>
