<h3 id="X1314">X(1314)&nbsp;=&nbsp;3rd MOSES INTERSECTION</h3>
Trilinears&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; bcf(a,b,c) : caf(b,c,a): abf(c,a,b), where f(a,b,c) is the 1st
barycentric given below
<p>Barycentrics&nbsp;&nbsp;f(a,b,c) : f(b,c,a) : f(c,a,b), where
f(a,b,c) = [d<sup>2</sup> + (4r - R)R +
sqrt(Q)]S<sub>B</sub>S<sub>C</sub> + (d<sup>2</sup> + 2r<sup>2</sup> -
R<sup>2</sup>)a<sup>2</sup>S<sub>A</sub>,<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;where
Q = 4d<sup>2</sup>R(4r - R) + [d<sup>2</sup> - 3R<sup>2</sup> + 4r(r +
R)]<sup>2</sup>,<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;d
= distance between X(3) and X(4),<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;R
= circumradius, r = inradius,<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;S<sub>A</sub> = (b<sup>2</sup> + c<sup>2</sup> - a<sup>2</sup>)/2, and S<sub>B</sub> and S<sub>C</sub> are defined cyclically (Peter J. C. Moses, 3/2003)
<p>
As a point on the Euler line, X(1314) has Shinagawa coefficients (d<sup>2</sup> + 2r<sup>2</sup> - R<sup>2</sup>,4rR - 2r<sup>2</sup> + Q<sup>1/2</sup>).
</p>
<p>
X(1314) is a point of intersection of the Euler line and the
incircle. For some obtuse triangles, this point is not in the real
plane (specifically, for those a,b,c such that Q &lt; 0).
</p>
<p>
X(1314) lies on the incircle and this line: 2,3
</p>
