Let A'B'C' and A"B"C" be the cevian and anticevian triangles of X(4), resp.  Let O<sub>A</sub> be the cevian circle of A".  Let A* be the intersection, other than A', of O<sub>A</sub> and line BC.  Define B* and C* cyclically.  X(1637) is the centroid of triangle A*B*C*.  (Randy Hutson, August 28, 2020)
