<h3 id="X1128">X(1128)&nbsp;=&nbsp;2nd DE VILLIERS POINT</h3>
Trilinears&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;f(A,B,C)
: f(B,C,A) : f(C,A,B),<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;where
f(A,B,C) = (sin((&pi; - A)/4))/sin((&pi; + 3A)/4)<br>
Trilinears&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;gA,B,C) : g(B,C,A)
: g(C,A,B),<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;where
g(A,B,C) = [1 - 2 sin(A/2)]/(1 - 2 cos A)&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; (M.
Iliev, 5/13/07)<br>
Barycentrics (sin A)f(A,B,C) : (sin B)f(B,C,A) : (sin
C)f(C,A,B)
<p>Let A",B",C" be the excenters of ABC, and let A', B', C' be the
respective incenters of triangles A"BC, B"CA, C"AB, respectively. The
triangle A'B'C' is perspective to ABC, and the perspector is X(1128).
(Darij Grinberg, 8/22/02). See references at X(1127).</p>
