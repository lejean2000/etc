<h3 id="X1130">X(1130)&nbsp;=&nbsp;1st STEVANOVIC POINT</h3>
Trilinears&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;f(A,B,C) : f(B,C,A) : f(C,A,B),<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;where
f(A,B,C) = sin[(&pi; + 3A)/4]/sin[(&pi; - A)/4]
<p>Trilinears&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;g(A,B,C) :
g(B,C,A) : g(C,A,B),<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;where
g(A,B,C) = 1 + 2 sin(A/2)</p>

<p>Barycentrics&nbsp;&nbsp;(sin A)f(A,B,C) : (sin B)f(B,C,A) : (sin
C)f(C,A,B)</p>

<p>Let U be the A-excenter of triangle ABC; let A' be the incenter of
triangle UBC, and define B', C' cyclically. Let A" = BC'&cap;CB', and
define B", C" cyclically. The lines AA", BB", CC" concur in X(1130).
(Milorad R. Stevanovic, Hyacinthos #7185, 5/21/03. See also X(1488) and
X(1489).)</p>

<p>X(1130) lies on these lines: 1,164&nbsp;&nbsp; 173,505</p>

<p>X(1130) = isogonal conjugate of X(1128)</p>
