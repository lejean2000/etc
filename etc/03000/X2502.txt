<h3 id="X2502">X(2502)&nbsp;=&nbsp;RADICAL CENTER OF {CIRCUMCIRCLE, BROCARD CIRCLE, PARRY CIRCLE}</h3>
Trilinears &nbsp;&nbsp; a(2a<sup>4</sup> - b<sup>4</sup> - c<sup>4</sup> + 4b<sup>2</sup>c<sup>2</sup> -
2a<sup>2</sup>b<sup>2</sup> - 2a<sup>2</sup>c<sup>2</sup>)
<p>
Let L be the trilinear polar of X(6), i.e. the Lemoine axis.  Let A' = L&cap;AX(6), and define B' and C' cyclically; then X(2502) is the centroid of A'B'C'.  (Randy Hutson, February 10, 2016)
</p>

If you have The Geometer's Sketchpad, you can view <a href="X(2502).gsp">X(2502)</a>.
