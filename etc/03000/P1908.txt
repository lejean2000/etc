Centers from Bicentric Pairs, 1908-1982
</p>
<img src="rightri.svg" style="height:30px" alt="rightri">

<div class="preamble-sect">
<p>For a definition of a bicentric pair (e.g., the 1st and 2nd Brocard
points) click <b>Tables</b> at the top of this page. Suppose P and U
are a bicentric pair. Many operations on P and U result in triangle
centers. Among these are trilinear and barycentric product, bicentric
sum, bicentric difference, crosssum, and crossdifference. For
definitions of these, click <b>Tables</b>. At the time this section is
added to ETC (September 15, 2003), bicentric pairs
</p>
<p>
P(1),U(1); P(2),U(2); ...; P(42),U(42)
</p>
<p>
are defined in <b>Tables</b>. In this present section, the abbreviation
<b>PU(n)</b> means the bicentric pair P(n),U(n).
</p>
