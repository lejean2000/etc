<p>Let A',B',C' be the respective excenters of ABC, and let
A<sub>B</sub> be the projection of A on A'B', let A<sub>C</sub> be the
projection of A on A'C', and define B<sub>C</sub>, B<sub>A</sub>,
C<sub>A</sub>, C<sub>B</sub> cyclically. The Euler lines of the three
triangles A'A<sub>B</sub>A<sub>C</sub>, B'B<sub>C</sub>B<sub>A</sub>,
C'C<sub>A</sub>C<sub>B</sub> concur in X(1145). Also, X(1145) is X(974)
of the excentral triangle. (Analogously, X(442) is X(973) of the
excentral triangle; see the note at X(442).) Jean-Pierre Ehrmann
(#4200, 10/24/01)</p>
