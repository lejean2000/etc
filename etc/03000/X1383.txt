<p>
Let A'B'C' be the circumcevian triangle of X(2), and let P(A) be the line through A' parallel to line BC. Define P(B) and P(C) cyclically. Let A" = P(B)&cap;P(C), and define B" and C" cyclically. Triangle A"B"C" is homothetic to ABC, and X(1383) is the center of the homothety.
</p>
<p>
The center of the homothety is also X(1383) if "circumcevian triangle of X(6) (circumsymmedial triangle)" or "4th Brocard triangle" is substituted for "circumcevian triangle of X(2)".  (Randy Hutson, March 21, 2019)
</p>
