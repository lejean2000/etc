<h3 id="X1113">X(1113)&nbsp;=&nbsp;1st EULER-LINE-CIRCUMCIRCLE INTERSECTION</h3>
Trilinears &nbsp;&nbsp; (R - d)cos A - 2R cos B cos C : (R - d)cos B - 2R cos C cos A : (R - d)cos C - 2R cos A cos B,
where R = circumradius, d = distance |OH| between X(3) and X(4). (Joe  Goggins, 2002)
<br>
Trilinears &nbsp;&nbsp; (1 - J) cos A - 2 cos B cos C : : , where J = |OH|/R = (1/abc)[S(6) - S(2,4) + 3a<sup>2</sup>b<sup>2</sup>c<sup>2</sup>]<sup>1/2</sup>, where S(6) = a<sup>6</sup> + b<sup>6</sup> + c<sup>6</sup>, and S(2,4) = a<sup>2</sup>b<sup>4</sup> + a<sup>2</sup>c<sup>4</sup> + b<sup>2</sup>c<sup>4</sup> + b<sup>2</sup>a<sup>4</sup> + c<sup>2</sup>a<sup>4</sup> + c<sup>2</sup>b<sup>4</sup> (Peter J. C. Moses, 10/2/03)
<br>
Barycentrics &nbsp;&nbsp; 2RS<sub>B</sub>S<sub>C</sub> + (|OH| - R)a<sup>2</sup>S<sub>A</sub> : : , where |OH| = distance between X(3) and X(4), and R = circumradius (Peter J. C. Moses, 3/2003; cf. X(1313), X(1314))
<br>
Barycentrics &nbsp;&nbsp; (a^2 + b^2 - c^2)*(a^2 - b^2 + c^2) - a^2*(-a^2 + b^2 + c^2)*(1 - J) : :
<b><br>
X(1113) = 3X(2) + (-3 + |OH|/R)*X(3) = X(1113) = (- 1 + |OH|/R)*X(3) + X(4)
</b>
<p>
As a point on the Euler line, X(1113) has Shinagawa coefficients (R - |OH|, -3R + |OH|).
</p>
<p>
X(1113) is a point of intersection of the Euler line and the circumcircle. The other is X(1114). Of the two, X(1113) is the one closer to X(4).
</p>
<p>
X(1113) is one of 2 points P such that P is the circumcircle-antipode of &Lambda; (trilinear polar of P); the other is X(1114). (Randy Hutson, November 2, 2017)
