<p class="preamble-title">
PK and NK Transforms, 2081 - 2088
</p>
<img src="rightri.svg" style="height:30px" alt="rightri">
</header>
<div class="preamble-sect">
<p>On October 1, 2003, Bernard Gibert described two transforms, given for
X = u : v : w (barycentrics) by
</p>
<p>PK(X) = a<sup>2</sup>(c<sup>2</sup>v<sup>2</sup> -
b<sup>2</sup>w<sup>2</sup>)u : b<sup>2</sup>(a<sup>2</sup>w<sup>2</sup>
- c<sup>2</sup>u<sup>2</sup>)v :
c<sup>2</sup>(b<sup>2</sup>u<sup>2</sup> -
a<sup>2</sup>v<sup>2</sup>)w;</p>

<p>NK(X) = a<sup>2</sup>(c<sup>2</sup>v<sup>2</sup> +
b<sup>2</sup>w<sup>2</sup>)u : b<sup>2</sup>(a<sup>2</sup>w<sup>2</sup>
+ c<sup>2</sup>u<sup>2</sup>)v :
c<sup>2</sup>(b<sup>2</sup>u<sup>2</sup> +
a<sup>2</sup>v<sup>2</sup>)w.</p>

<p>
Let X <sup>-1</sup> denote the isogonal conjugate of X. Then PK(X) is
the point of intersection of the trilinear polar of X and the trilinear
polar of X <sup>-1</sup>, and NK(P) is the pole of the line XX
<sup>-1</sup> with respect to the conic that passes through points A,
B, C, X, and X <sup>-1</sup>.
</p>
<p>Also, PK(X) is the crossdifference, and NK(X) the crosssum, of X and
X <sup>-1</sup>.</p>

<p>The transform PK carries any point on the cubic pK(X6,X) to the
trilinear polar of X <sup>-1</sup>, and NK carries any point on the
cubic nK0(X6,X) to the trilinear polar of X <sup>-1</sup>. (The
notations for these cubics are given at <a href=
"http://perso.wanadoo.fr/bernard.gibert/index.html">Bernard Gibert's
<b>Cubics in the Triangle Plane</b></a>.)
</p>

<p>PK(X(i)) = X(j) for these (i,j):<br>
(2,512), (3,647), (4,647), (6,512), (9,663), (19,810), (57,663),
(63,810)</p>

<p>NK(X(i)) = X(j) for these (i,j):<br>
(1,1), (2,30), (3,185), (4,185), (6,39), (98,446), (511,446)</p>

<p>In trilinears: suppose X = x : y : z; then</p>

</p>
PK(X) = (y<sup>2</sup> - z<sup>2</sup>)x : (z<sup>2</sup> -
x<sup>2</sup>)y : (x<sup>2</sup> - y<sup>2</sup>)z;
<p>NK(X) = (y<sup>2</sup> + z<sup>2</sup>)x : (z<sup>2</sup> +
x<sup>2</sup>)y : (x<sup>2</sup> + y<sup>2</sup>)z.</p>

</p>
<br>
The self-isogonal cubic pK(X6,X) is denoted as Z(X) in TCCT (p. 240),
and nK0(K6,X), as Z<sup>+</sup>(L). Extending examples on p. 240-241,
<p>Z(X<sub>2</sub>) passes through X(i) for I = 1,2,3,4,6,9,57, 223,
282, 1073, 1249.<br>
Z(X<sub>4</sub>) passes through X(i) for I =
1,3,4,46,90,155,254,371,372,485,486,487,488.<br>
Z(X<sub>5</sub>) passes through X(i) for I =
1,3,4,5,17,18,54,61,62,195,627,628.<br>
Z(X<sub>20</sub>) passes through X(i) for I =
1,3,4,20,40,64,84,1490,1498.<br>
Z(X<sub>30</sub>) passes through X(i) for I = 1,3,4,13,14,15,16, 30,
74, 399, 484, 616,617,1138,1157,1263,1276,1277,1337,1338.<br>
Z(X<sub>98</sub>) passes through X(i) for I =
1,98,511,1687,1688,1756,2009,2010.<br>
Z(X<sub>99</sub>) passes through X(i) for I =
1,39,83,99,512,1018,1019,1379,1380.<br>
Z(X<sub>100</sub>) passes through X(i) for I = 1,100,513,1381,1382.<br>
Z(X<sub>110</sub>) passes through X(i) for I =
1,5,54,110,523,<b>1113,1114</b>.</p>

<p>Z<sup>+</sup>(X<sub>1</sub>X<sub>6</sub>) passes through X(i) for I
= 1,44,88,239,241,292,294,1931.<br>
Z<sup>+</sup>(X<sub>3</sub>X<sub>6</sub>) passes through X(i) for I =
2,6,13,14,15,16,111,524<br>
Z<sup>+</sup>(X<sub>1</sub>X<sub>2</sub>) passes through X(i) for I =
1,238,291,899<br>
Z<sup>+</sup>(X<sub>1</sub>X<sub>3</sub>) passes through X(i) for I =
1,105,243,296,518,1155,1156</p>

<p>Additional pass-through points for these and other cubics are given
at	<a href="http://perso.wanadoo.fr/bernard.gibert/index.html">Bernard Gibert's
<b>Cubics in the Triangle Plane</b></a>.
