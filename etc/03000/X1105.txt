<h3 id="X1105">X(1105)&nbsp;=&nbsp;CEVAPOINT OF X(3) AND X(4)</h3>
Trilinears&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; f(A,B,C)
: f(B,C,A) : f(C,A,B),<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;where
f(A,B,C) = (sec A)/(cos<sup>2</sup>B + cos<sup>2</sup>C)<br>
Barycentrics g(A,B,C) : g(B,C,A) : g(C,A,B), where g(A,B,C)
= (sin A) f(A,B,C)
<p>Let A'B'C' be the cevian triangle of X(3). Let L<sub>A</sub> be the
reflection of the line B'C' in the line BC, and define L<sub>B</sub>
and L<sub>C</sub> cyclically. Let A'' =
L<sub>B</sub>&cap;L<sub>C</sub>, and define B'' and C'' cyclically. The
lines AA'', BB'', CC'' concur in X(1105). (Randy Hutson, 9/23/2011)</p>

<p>X(1105) lies on these lines: 3,1093&nbsp;&nbsp; 4,801&nbsp;&nbsp;
20,393&nbsp;&nbsp; 185,648&nbsp;&nbsp; 225,412&nbsp;&nbsp;
243,411&nbsp;&nbsp; 378,847
</p>
