<h3 id="X1119">X(1119)&nbsp;=&nbsp;2nd HATZIPOLAKIS PERSPECTOR</h3>
Trilinears&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;f(A,B,C)
: f(B,C,A) : f(C,A,B),<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;where
f(A,B,C) = (1 - sec A)/(1 + cos A)<br>
Barycentrics (sin A)f(A,B,C) : (sin B)f(B,C,A) : (sin
C)f(C,A,B)
<p>Let triangle A<sub>0</sub>B<sub>0</sub>C<sub>0</sub> be as defined
for X(1118). Let A<sub>1</sub> be the orthogonal projection of
A<sub>0</sub> onto line BC, and define B<sub>1</sub> and C<sub>1</sub>
cyclically. Then triangle A<sub>1</sub>B<sub>1</sub>C<sub>1</sub> is
perspective to ABC, and the perspector is X(1119). (Antreas
Hatzipolakis, #5321, 4/30/02)</p>
