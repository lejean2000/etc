<h3 id="X1323">X(1323)&nbsp;=&nbsp;FLETCHER POINT</h3>
Trilinears&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;f(A,B,C)
: f(B,C,A) : f(C,A,B), where f(A,B,C) = (sec<sup>2</sup>A/2)(2
cos<sup>2</sup>A/2 - cos<sup>2</sup>B/2 - cos<sup>2</sup>C/2)<br>
Barycentrics&nbsp;&nbsp;(sin A)f(A,B,C) : (sin B)f(B,C,A) : (sin
C)f(C,A,B)<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;=
g(a,b,c) : g(b,c,a) : g(c,a,b),<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;where
g(a,b,c) = (2a<sup>2</sup> - b<sup>2</sup> - c<sup>2</sup> - ab - ac +
2bc)/(b + c - a) [P. J. C. Moses, 6/25/04]
<p>X(1323) is the point of intersection of the line X(1)X(7) and the
trilinear polar of X(7). These two lines are orthogonal.<br>
X(1323) is named in honor of T. J. Fletcher in</p>

<p><b>Adrian Oldknow,</b> "The Euler-Gergonne-Soddy Triangle of a
Triangle," <i>American Mathematical Monthly</i> 103 (1996) 319-329.</p>
<p> See also <B>MathWorld, <a href=
"http://mathworld.wolfram.com/FletcherPoint.html">Fletcher Point</a></B>
</p>

<p>X(1323) is the radical trace of the inner and outer Soddy
circles.</p>
