<h3 id="X1981">X(1981)&nbsp;=&nbsp;VEGA TRANSFORM OF X(647)</h3>
Trilinears &nbsp;&nbsp; [sin 2B sin(C - A) - sin 2C sin(A - B)]/[sin 2A sin(B - C)] : :
<p>
As a point on the Euler line, X(1981) has Shinagawa coefficients
($aS<sub>B</sub>S<sub>C</sub>$(E+F)F+$aS<sub>A</sub>$FS<sup>2</sup>
-$a$(E-2F)FS<sup>2</sup>, -$aS<sub>B</sub>S<sub>C</sub>$S<sup>2</sup>
-3$aS<sub>A</sub>$FS<sup>2</sup>-$a$[(E+F)F-S<sup>2</sup>]S<sup>2</sup>).
</p>
<p>
If X = x : y : z is a triangle center other than X(1), then the Vega
transform of X, defined by trilinears</p>
<p></p>
(y - z)/x : (z - x)/y : (x - y)/z
<p></p>
<br>
lies on the line [x:y:z] that has x,y,z as coefficients. (The line
[x:y:z] is the trilinear polar of the isogonal conjugate of X.) Thus,
the Vega transform of X(647) lies on the Euler line.
<p>In general, the bicentrics of X also lie on [x:y:z]; for details,
click <b>Tables</b> at the top of this page.</p>
<p>X(1981) lies on these lines: 2,3&nbsp;&nbsp; 651,653&nbsp;&nbsp;
662,811
</p>
<p>

X(1981) = PU(30)-harmonic conjugate of X(1982)<br>
X(1981) = intersection of lines P(15)U(16) and U(15)P(16)<br>
</p>
