<h3 id="X1375">X(1375)&nbsp;=&nbsp;EVANS POINT</h3>
Trilinears&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;f(a,b,c)
: f(b,c,a) : f(c,a,b), where f(a,b,c) = bc[- 2a<sup>2</sup>cos A + b(a
- b + c)cos B + c(a + b - c)cos C]<br>
Barycentrics&nbsp;&nbsp;g(a,b,c) : g(b,c,a) : g(c,a,b), where g(a,b,c)
= - 2a<sup>2</sup>cos A + b(a - b + c)cos B + c(a + b - c)cos C
<p>
    As a point on the Euler line, X(1375) has Shinagawa coefficients
(2$aS<sub>B</sub>S<sub>C</sub>$ - $a$S<sup>2</sup>, $a$S<sup>2</sup>).
<b>Adrian Oldknow,</b> "The Euler-Gergonne-Soddy Triangle of a
Triangle," <i>American Mathematical Monthly</i> 103 (1996) 319-329. See
page 328.</p>

"http://mathworld.wolfram.com/EvansPoint.html">Evans Point</a></B>.
</p>
