More Points on the Circumcircle, 2365-2384
</p>
<img src="rightri.svg" style="height:30px" alt="rightri">

<div class="preamble-sect">
<p>
Suppose that U = u : v : w (trilinears) is a triangle center other than X(6), and let CIR(U) be the point given by trilinears
</p>
<p>
F(u,v,w) : F(v,w,u) : F(w,u,v) = avw/(av<sup>2</sup> + aw<sup>2</sup> - buv - cuw) : bwu/(bw<sup>2</sup> + bu<sup>2</sup> - cvw - avu) : cuv/(cu<sup>2</sup> + cv<sup>2</sup> - awu - bwv)
</p>

<p>
CIR(U) lies on the circumcircle.  Pairs (i,j) such that X(j) = CIR(X(i)) include the following:
</p>
(1,106), (2,729), (3,1300), (4,1294), (9,1477), (31,767), (37,741),
(41,767), (43,106), (44,106), (74,1294), (75,701), (110,99), (125,827),
(194,729), (238,741), (265,1300), (1084,689), (1154,1141),
(1279,(1477), (1499,1296), (1503,1297), (1510,930), (1634,689)
<p>This section was added to ETC on 10/20/03; the preamble was revised on January 20, 2015, following suggestions by Viktor Kitaysky.  Points with trilinears similar to those of CIR(U), called circum-eigentransforms, denoted by CET(U), are given in Section 4.</p>
</p>
