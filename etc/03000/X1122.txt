<h3 id="X1122">X(1122)&nbsp;=&nbsp;1st GRINBERG POINT</h3>
Trilinears&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;f(a,b,c)
: f(b,c,a) : f(c,a,b), &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;where f(a,b,c) = [a(b + c) +
(b - c)<sup>2</sup>]/(b + c - a)<br>
Barycentrics af(a,b,c) : bf(b,c,a) : cf(c,a,b)
<p>Let A<sub>B</sub> be the touchpoint of the A-excircle and line AB,
let A<sub>C</sub> be the touchpoint of the A-excircle and line AC, and
let M<sub>A</sub> be the midpoint of segment
A<sub>B</sub>A<sub>C</sub>. Define M<sub>B</sub> and M<sub>C</sub>
cyclically. Let A', B', C' be the touchpoints of the incircle with
lines BC, CA, AB, respectively. The triangles
M<sub>A</sub>M<sub>B</sub>M<sub>C</sub> and A'B'C' are perspective, and
the perspector is X(1122). (Darij Grinberg, 12/28/02)</p>
<p>If you have The Geometer's Sketchpad, you can view <a href=
"X(1122).gsp" style="font-weight: bold">X(1122)</a>.</p>
<p>X(1122) lies on these lines: 7,8&nbsp;&nbsp; 56,269</p>
<p>X(1122) = isogonal conjugate of X(1261)<br>
X(1122) = crosspoint of X(7) and X(269)<br>
X(1122) = crosssum of X(55) and X(200)</p>
