Beth Conjugates 1393-1477
</p>
<img src="rightri.svg" style="height:30px" alt="rightri">

<div class="preamble-sect">

If X is a triangle center then X = P-beth conjugate of P for some
triangle center P. Using the definition of beth conjugate (in the
Glossary), it is easy to prove that the P-beth conjugate of P is the
trilinear product P*X(56),<br>
so that P = X*X(8). In trilinears,
<p>if P = p : q : r, then (P-beth conjugate of P) = up : vq : wr,
where</p>

<p>u : v : w = a/(b + c - a) : b/(c + a - b) : c/(a + b - c), or
equivalently,<br>
u : v : w = 1 - cos A : 1 - cos B : 1 - cos C.</p>

conjugate of X(j):</p>
