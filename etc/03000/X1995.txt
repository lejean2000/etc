<p>
Let P be a point in the plane of ABC and not on the circumcircle.  Let (O<sub>A</sub>) be the circle tangent to the circumcircle at A and passing through P.  Let A' be the antipode of A in (O<sub>A</sub>).  Let L<sub>A</sub> be the tangent to (O<sub>A</sub>) at A'.  Define L<sub>B</sub> and L<sub>C</sub> cyclically.  Let T<sub>A</sub> = L<sub>B</sub>&cap;L<sub>C</sub>, and define T<sub>B</sub> and T<sub>C</sub> cyclically.  Triangle T<sub>A</sub>T<sub>B</sub>T<sub>C</sub> is homothetic to the tangential triangle.  When P = X(6), the center of homothety is X(1995).  (Randy Hutson, June 7, 2019)
