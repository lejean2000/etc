<h3 id="X1131">X(1131)&nbsp;=&nbsp;ARCTAN(2) KIEPERT POINT</h3>
Trilinears &nbsp;&nbsp; 1/(sin A + 2 cos A) : :
<br>
Trilinears &nbsp;&nbsp; csc(A + t) : : , where t = arctan(2)
<br>
Barycentrics &nbsp;&nbsp; 1/(2 SA + S) : :
<p>
To construct the Vecten point, X(485), squares are erected outward
on the sides of ABC. If A', B', C' are the centers of these squares,
then triangle A'B'C' is perspective to ABC with perspector X(485). Now
let A" be the midpoint of the side of the A-square that does not touch
line BC, and define B" and C" cyclically. Then triangle A"B"C" is
perspective to ABC with perspector X(1131). Angle(A"BC) = angle(B"CA) =
angle(C"AB), so that X(1131) lies on the Kiepert hyperbola. Here, the
common angle is arctan(2). (Darij Grinberg 9/22/02)</p>
