<h3 id="X1487">X(1487)&nbsp;=&nbsp;NAPOLEON CEVAPOINT</h3>
Trilinears&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;f(A,B,C)
: f(B,C,A) : f(C,A,B), where f(A,B,C) = (csc A)/[(3 - cot B cot C)(3 -
cot<sup>2</sup>A)]<br>
Barycentrics&nbsp;&nbsp;(sin A)f(A,B,C) : (sin B)f(B,C,A) : (sin
C)f(C,A,B)<br>
<p>Let N denote the nine-point center, X(5). Let N<sub>A</sub> =
N-of-triangle NBC, and define N<sub>B</sub> and N<sub>C</sub>
cyclically. Triangle N<sub>A</sub>N<sub>B</sub>N<sub>C</sub> is
perspective to ABC, and X(1487) is the perspector. X(1487) is the
cevapoint of the Napoleon points, X(17) and X(18). (Coordinates found
by Paul Yiu.)</p>
<p>The construction just given for X(1487) shows that it is a solution
X of the "four-triangle problem" posed in</p>
<p><b>C. Kimberling,</b> "Triangle centers as functions," <i>Rocky
Mountain Journal of Mathematics</i> 23 (1993) 1269-1286. See Section 5;
a complete solution to the problem remains to be found.</p>
<p>X(1487) lies on these lines: 4,252&nbsp;&nbsp; 5,1173&nbsp;&nbsp;
140,930</p>
