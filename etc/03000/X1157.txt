<p>
For any point X, let X<sub>A</sub> be the reflection of X in sideline BC, and define X<sub>B</sub> and X<sub>C</sub> cyclically.  Then X(1157) is the unique point X for which the lines AX<sub>A</sub>,
BX<sub>B</sub>, CX<sub>C</sub> concur on the circumcircle; the point of concurrence is X(1141).
</p>
<p>X(1157) is the tangential of X(3) on the Neuberg cubic.
</p>
<p>
Let A'B'C' be the reflection triangle.  The circumcircles of AB'C', BC'A', CA'B' (i.e., the Yiu circles) concur in X(1157).  (Randy Hutson, July 20, 2016)
</p>
