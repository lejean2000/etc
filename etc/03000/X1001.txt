<h3 id="X1001" class="auto-style4">X(1001)&nbsp;=&nbsp;MIDPOINT OF X(1) AND X(9)</h3>
Trilinears &nbsp;&nbsp;  a<sup>2</sup> - a(b + c) - 2bc : :
<br>
<b>
X(1001) = X(1) + 3X(2) + (r/R)X(3)
</b>
<p>
Let O<sub>A</sub> be the circle tangent of side BC at its midpoint and to the circumcircle on the side of BC opposite A. Define O<sub>B</sub> and O<sub>C</sub> cyclically. X(1001) is the radical center of the circles O<sub>A</sub>, O<sub>B</sub>, O<sub>C</sub>.  (Randy Hutson, 9/23/2011)
</p>
<p>
Let L<sub>A</sub> and M<sub>A</sub> be the external tangents to circles O<sub>B</sub> and O<sub>C</sub>, with L<sub>A</sub> being the farther from O<sub>A</sub>. Define L<sub>B</sub>, L<sub>C</sub>, M<sub>B</sub>, and M<sub>C</sub> cyclically. Let A' = L<sub>B</sub>&cap;L<sub>C</sub> and A'' = M<sub>B</sub>&cap;M<sub>C</sub>, and define B', B'', C', and C'' cyclically. The lines A'A'', B'B'', C'C'' concur in X(1001).
</p>
<p>
Let A* be the tangency point of O<sub>A</sub> and the circumcircle, and define B* and C* cyclically. Let A** be the radical trace of O<sub>B</sub> and O<sub>C</sub>. The lines A*A**, B*B**, C*C** concur in X(1001).
</p>
<p>
For details and relationships among X(1001), X(1), X(145), X(3361), X(3616), X(3913), and X(4719), see</p>
<p><b>Luis Gonz&aacute;lez,</b> "On a Triad of Circles Tangent to the Circumcircle and the Sides at Their Midpoints," <a href="http://forumgeom.fau.edu/FG2011volume11/FG201115index.html"><b style="font-style: italic">Forum Geometricorum</b></a> 11 (2011) 145-154.
</p>
<p>
Let A' be the line through X(1) parallel to line BC.  Let A<sub>B</sub> = A'&cap;AB and A<sub>C</sub> = A'&cap;AC.  Define B<sub>C</sub> and C<sub>A</sub> cyclically, and define B<sub>A</sub> and C<sub>B</sub> cyclically.  The six points A<sub>B</sub>, B<sub>C</sub>, C<sub>A</sub>, A<sub>B</sub>, B<sub>C</sub>, C<sub>A</sub> lie on a conic whose center is X(1001).  (Angel Montesdeoca, April 27, 2016)
</p>
