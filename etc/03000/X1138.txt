<h3 id="X1138">X(1138)&nbsp;=&nbsp;ISOGONAL CONJUGATE OF X(399)</h3>
Trilinears&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;f(A,B,C) : f(B,C,A) : f(C,A,B),<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;where f(A,B,C) = 1/(5 cos A - 4 cos B cos C - 8 cos<sup>2</sup>A sin B sin C)
<br>
Barycentrics &nbsp;&nbsp; 1/(a^8 - 4 a^6 (b^2 + c^2) + a^4 (6 b^4 + b^2 c^2 + 6 c^4) - a^2 (4 b^6 - b^4 c^2 - b^2 c^4 + 4 c^6) + (b^2 - c^2)^2 (b^4 + 4 b^2 c^2 + c^4)) : :<br>
<p>
There are only two points X such that the pedal triangle of X is similar to the cevian triangle of X. They are X(4) and X(1138). (Jean-Pierre Ehrmann, January 4, 2003)
</p>
<p>
Let A'B'C' be the anticomplementary triangle of a triangle ABC, and let E<sub>A</sub> be the line through A parallel to the Euler line.  Let A" be the point of intersection, other than A, of E<sub>A</sub> and the circumcircle.  Define E<sub>B</sub> and E<sub>C</sub> cyclically.  The locus of a point P such that the Euler line of PBC is parallel to the Euler line of ABC is a conic ABCA'A'' having the midpoint of segment BC as center.  The conics, ABCA'A'', ABCB'B'', ABCC'C'' pass through X(1138).  (Francisco Javier García Capitán, April 3, 2015:  <a href="http://www.adgeom.epizy.com/message.php?msg=2458" style=
"font-weight: bold">ADGEOM 2458</a>)
</p>
