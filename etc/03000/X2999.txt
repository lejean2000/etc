<p>Let R<sub>A</sub> be the radical axis of the Bevan circle and the
A-excircle. Define R<sub>B</sub> and R<sub>C</sub> cyclically. The
three radical axes form a triangle homothetic to triangle ABC, and
X(2999) is the center of homothety. (Eric Danneels, Hyacinthos #10926,
Dec. 5, 2004.</p>

<p>Let B<sub>A</sub> be the inverse-in-the-A-excircle of the B-excenter
and C<sub>A</sub> the inverse-in-the-A-excircle of the C-excenter. Let
L<sub>A</sub> be the line B<sub>A</sub>C<sub>A</sub>, and define
L<sub>B</sub> and L<sub>C</sub> cyclically. Let A' =
L<sub>B</sub>&cap;L<sub>C</sub>, and define B' and C' cyclically. The
triangle A'B'C' is homothetic to triangle ABC, and the center of
homothety is X(2999). (Randy Hutson, 9/23/2011)</p>
