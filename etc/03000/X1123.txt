<h3 id="X1123">X(1123)&nbsp;=&nbsp;PAASCHE POINT</h3>
Trilinears &nbsp;&nbsp; 1/(1 +sin A) : 1/(1 + sin B) : 1/(1 + sin C)
<br>
Barycentrics (sin A)/(1 + sin A) : (sin B)/(1 + sin B) : (sin C)/(1 + sin C)
<p>
Let D and E be the congruent circles each tangent to the other and
to line BC, with D also tangent to line AB and E also tangent to line
CA, meeting in a point A' lying outside triangle ABC. Define B' and C'
cyclically. Then A'B'C' is perspective to ABC, and the perspector is
X(1123). See
</p>
<p>
<b>Ivan Paasche,</b> Aufgabe P 933, <i>Praxis der Mathematik</i> 1
(1990), page 40.
</p>
<p>
Let P<sub>A</sub> be the parabola with focus A and directrix BC, and
let L<sub>A</sub> be the line of the points of intersection of
P<sub>A</sub> with the segments AB and AC. Define L<sub>B</sub> and
L<sub>C</sub> cyclically. Let A' = L<sub>B</sub>&cap;L<sub>C</sub>, and define B' and C' cyclically. The
lines AA', BB', CC' concur in X(1123). (Randy Hutson, 9/23/2011)
</p>
<p>
Suppose that ABC is an acute triangle. Let o<sub>A</sub> be the
circle with diameter BC. Let w<sub>A</sub> be the circle tangent to
segments AB and AC and also externally tangent to o<sub>A</sub>, in
point X<sub>A</sub>. Define X<sub>B</sub> and X<sub>C</sub> cyclically.
The lines AX<sub>A</sub>, BX<sub>B</sub>, CX<sub>C</sub> concur in
X(1123). (Tomasz Cie&sacute;la, 19 January 2013) For a related
construction, see X(1336).
</p>
<p>
In Hutson's construction of X(1123) given above, the parabola P<sub>A</sub> meets segments AB and AC in two points, here denoted by Ab and Ac. Cyclically, the parabolas P<sub>B</sub> and P<sub>C</sub> determine four more points; the six points are then Ab = 0 : 2R : c,  Ac = 0 : b : 2R, Bc = a : 0 : 2R, Ba = 2R : 0 : c, Ca = 2R : b : 0, Cb = a : 2R : 0.  These points lie on a conic, named the Paasche conic at X(37861).  (Vijay Krishna, April 14, 2020)
</p>
<p>
For a construction and relationships to other points, see the preamble just before X(37994).
</p>
