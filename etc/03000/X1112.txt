If you have The Geometer's Sketchpad, you can view <a href="X(1112).gsp">X(1112)</a>.</p>
<p>X(1112) is the center of the hyperbola that passes through the
vertices of the cevian triangles of X(4) and X(648), and also through
the centers X(i) for I = 4, 113, 155, 193. (Paul Yiu, Oct. 16, 2001, as
contributing editor for Clark Kimberling, "Conics associated with a
cevian nest," <i>Forum Geometricorum</i> 1 (2001) 141-150; see Example
2.)</p>

<p>X(1112) is X(11)-of-the-orthic-triangle if ABC is acute. (Peter
Moses, July 7, 2009)</p>

4,94&nbsp;&nbsp; 6,1177&nbsp;&nbsp; 25,110&nbsp;&nbsp; 51,125&nbsp;&nbsp;
