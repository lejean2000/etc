<p class="preamble-title">
GIBERT-SIMSON TRANSFORMS, X(2394)-X(2419)
</p>
<img src="rightri.svg" style="height:30px" alt="rightri">
</header>
<div class="preamble-sect">
<p>
On October 19, 2003, Bernard Gibert contributed points that lie on the
Simson cubic. Indeed, this cubic is the image of the circumcircle under
a mapping here named the Gibert-Simson transform. If U = u : v : w
(barycentrics) and U is not X(3), then the transform is given by
</p>
<p>
GS(U) = (b<sup>2</sup>S<sub>B</sub>/v -
c<sup>2</sup>S<sub>C</sub>/w)u/a<sup>2</sup> :
(c<sup>2</sup>S<sub>C</sub>/w -
a<sup>2</sup>S<sub>A</sub>/u)v/b<sup>2</sup> :
(a<sup>2</sup>S<sub>A</sub>/u -
b<sup>2</sup>S<sub>B</sub>/v)w/c<sup>2</sup>.
</p>
<p>
In case U lies on the circumcircle, these barycentrics are proportional
to the respective directed distances from A, B, C, to the Simson line
of U. If U* is the antipode of U, then GS(U*) is the isotomic conjugate
of GS(U). In the Catalogue at <a href=
"http://perso.wanadoo.fr/bernard.gibert/index.html" style=
"font-style: italic">Cubics in the Triangle Plane</a>, the Simson cubic
is discussed as <a href=
"http://perso.wanadoo.fr/bernard.gibert/ctc.html" style=
"font-weight: bold">K010</a>, and its isogonal transform is the cubic
<a href="http://perso.wanadoo.fr/bernard.gibert/ctc.html" style=
"font-weight: bold">K162</a>. The isogonal conjugates of centers
X(2365)-X(2419) are indexed as X(2420)-X(2445).
<p>If U = u : v : w = u(a,b,c) : u(b,c,a) : u(c,a,b) (trilinears),
then
</p>
<p>
GS(U) = f(a,b,c) : f(b,c,a) : f(c,a,b), where f(a,b,c) = (v cos C - w cos B)(u/a)<sup>2</sup>,
<br>
and the cubic K010 is given by trilinears
</p>
<p>
(cos A)x(b<sup>2</sup>y<sup>2</sup> +
c<sup>2</sup>z<sup>2</sup>) + (cos B)y(c<sup>2</sup>z<sup>2</sup> +
a<sup>2</sup>x<sup>2</sup>) + (cos C)z(a<sup>2</sup>x<sup>2</sup> +
b<sup>2</sup>x<sup>2</sup>) - (a<sup>2</sup> + b<sup>2</sup> +
c<sup>2</sup>)xyz = 0,
</p>
<p>
and the cubic K162 by
</p>
<p>
(cos A)x(c<sup>2</sup>y<sup>2</sup> +
b<sup>2</sup>z<sup>2</sup>) + (cos B)y(a<sup>2</sup>z<sup>2</sup> +
c<sup>2</sup>x<sup>2</sup>) + (cos C)z(b<sup>2</sup>x<sup>2</sup> +
a<sup>2</sup>x<sup>2</sup>) - (a<sup>2</sup> + b<sup>2</sup> +
c<sup>2</sup>)xyz = 0,
</p>
<p>
In the definition of eigentransform before X(2120), if <b>cevian
triangle</b> is replaced by <b>circumcevian triangle</b>, the result is
CET(U) as discussed before X(2365). Gibert observed that if <b>cevian
triangle</b> is replaced by <b>pedal triangle, for U on the
circumcircle</b>, the result is the isogonal conjugate of GS(U). That
is, [GS(U)] <sup>-1</sup> is the eigencenter of the pedal triangle of U
(which is a degenerate triangle lying in the Simson line of U).
</p>
<p>
Regarding triangle centers that do not lie on the circumcircle,
GS(X(i)) = X(j) for these (i,j): (32,669), (48,1459), (187,1649),
(248,879), (485,850), (486,850).
</p>
<p>This section was added to ETC on 10/23/03.  For more Gibert-Simson transforms, see X(15411)-X(15423)</p>
</p>
