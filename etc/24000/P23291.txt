<p>
The <b>anti-Ursa-minor triangle</b> of an <u>acute</u> triangle ABC is the triangle A'B'C' whose Ursa-minor triangle is ABC. This triangle A'B'C' can be constructed as the anti-Hutson-intouch triangle of the Euler triangle of ABC, and A'B'C' has the following properties:
</p>

<ul>
<li>A' = b^2+c^2 : c^2-a^2 : b^2-a^2  (barycentrics)</li>
<li>|B'C'| = (a/4)*|sec(B)*sec(C)|</li>
<li>area(A'B'C') = |sec(A)*sec(B)*sec(C)|*area(ABC)/8</li>
</ul>
