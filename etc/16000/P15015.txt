<p class="preamble-title">
Centers related to altimedial triangles: X(15015)-X(15142)
</p>
<img src="rightri.svg" style="height:30px" alt="rightri">
</header>
<div class="preamble-sect">
<p>
This preamble and centers X(15015)-X(15142) were contributed by Randy Hutson, October 27, 2017.
</p>
<p>
The term <b>altimedial triangle</b> was introduced in <a href="http://www.hyacinthos.epizy.com/message.php?msg=253" target="blank">Hyacinthos 253</a>, by Antreas Hatzipolakis, January 30, 2000.  A recap follows:
</p>
<p>
Let M<sub>A</sub>, M<sub>B</sub>, M<sub>C</sub> be the midpoints of sides BC, CA, AB, resp.<br>
Let H<sub>A</sub>, H<sub>B</sub>, H<sub>C</sub> be the feet of the altitudes from A, B, C, resp.
</p>
<p>
The triangles H<sub>A</sub>M<sub>C</sub>M<sub>B</sub>, M<sub>C</sub>H<sub>B</sub>M<sub>A</sub>, M<sub>B</sub>M<sub>A</sub>H<sub>C</sub> are the A-, B- and C-altimedial triangles.  Each is inversely congruent to the medial triangle and inversely similar to ABC (with center of inverse similitude the respective vertex of the orthocentroidal triangle).
</p>
<p>
The A-altimedial triangle has barycentric vertex matrix:
</p>
<p>
0&nbsp;&nbsp;:&nbsp;&nbsp;tan B&nbsp;&nbsp;:&nbsp;&nbsp;tan C<br>
1&nbsp;&nbsp;:&nbsp;&nbsp;1&nbsp;&nbsp;:&nbsp;&nbsp;0<br>
1&nbsp;&nbsp;:&nbsp;&nbsp;0&nbsp;&nbsp;:&nbsp;&nbsp;1<br>
</p>
<p>
The A-, B- and C-<b>anti-altimedial triangles</b>, A<sub>A</sub>B<sub>A</sub>C<sub>A</sub>, A<sub>B</sub>B<sub>B</sub>C<sub>B</sub>, A<sub>C</sub>B<sub>C</sub>C<sub>C</sub> are here introduced as the triangles of which ABC is the A-, B- and C-altimedial triangle, resp.  To construct the A-anti-altimedial triangle, take A<sub>A</sub> as the reflection of A in line BC.  Then B<sub>A</sub> is the reflection of A<sub>A</sub> in B, and C<sub>A</sub> is the reflection of A<sub>A</sub> in C.  The B- and C-anti-altimedial triangles are constructed cyclically.  The anti-altimedial triangles are each inversely congruent to the anticomplementary triangle and homothetic to the respective altimedial triangle, with center of homothety the respective vertex of the orthocentroidal triangle.
</p>
<p>
The A-anti-altimedial triangle has barycentric vertex matrix:
</p>
<p>
-a<sup>2</sup>&nbsp;&nbsp;:&nbsp;&nbsp;a<sup>2</sup> + b<sup>2</sup> - c<sup>2</sup>&nbsp;&nbsp;:&nbsp;&nbsp;a<sup>2</sup> - b<sup>2</sup> + c<sup>2</sup><br>
a<sup>2</sup>&nbsp;&nbsp;:&nbsp;&nbsp;a<sup>2</sup> - b<sup>2</sup> + c<sup>2</sup>&nbsp;&nbsp;:&nbsp;&nbsp;-a<sup>2</sup> + b<sup>2</sup> - c<sup>2</sup><br>
a<sup>2</sup>&nbsp;&nbsp;:&nbsp;&nbsp;-a<sup>2</sup> - b<sup>2</sup> + c<sup>2</sup>&nbsp;&nbsp;:&nbsp;&nbsp;a<sup>2</sup> + b<sup>2</sup> - c<sup>2</sup>
</p>
<p>
The triangles A<sub>A</sub>A<sub>B</sub>A<sub>C</sub>, B<sub>A</sub>B<sub>B</sub>B<sub>C</sub>, C<sub>A</sub>C<sub>B</sub>C<sub>C</sub> are here named the A-, B- and C-<b>adjunct anti-altimedial triangles</b>.  Each are inversely similar to ABC, with center of inverse similitude X(3).
</p>
<p>
The A-adjunct anti-altimedial triangle has barycentric vertex matrix:
</p>
<p>
-a<sup>2</sup>&nbsp;&nbsp;:&nbsp;&nbsp;a<sup>2</sup> + b<sup>2</sup> - c<sup>2</sup>&nbsp;&nbsp;:&nbsp;&nbsp;a<sup>2</sup> - b<sup>2</sup> + c<sup>2</sup><br>
-a<sup>2</sup> + b<sup>2</sup> + c<sup>2</sup>&nbsp;&nbsp;:&nbsp;&nbsp;b<sup>2</sup>&nbsp;&nbsp;:&nbsp;&nbsp;a<sup>2</sup> - b<sup>2</sup> - c<sup>2</sup><br>
-a<sup>2</sup> + b<sup>2</sup> + c<sup>2</sup>&nbsp;&nbsp;:&nbsp;&nbsp;a<sup>2</sup> - b<sup>2</sup> - c<sup>2</sup>&nbsp;&nbsp;:&nbsp;&nbsp;c<sup>2</sup>
</p>
<p>
</p>
<p>
Let P be a triangle center.  Let P<sub>A</sub> be P-of-A-altimedial-triangle, and define P<sub>B</sub>, P<sub>C</sub> cyclically.  Triangle P<sub>A</sub>P<sub>B</sub>P<sub>C</sub> is here named the <b>P-of-altimedial-triangles triangle</b>, or <b>P-altimedial triangle</b> for short.  For all finite P other than X(3) (for which the P-altimedial triangle is the triple point X(5)), the P-altimedial triangle is inversely similar to the medial triangle, with center of inverse similitude X(5).
</p>
<p>
The X(1)-altimedial triangle is also the orthic triangle of the Fuhrmann triangle.<br>
The X(20)-altimedial triangle is also the X(4)-Brocard triangle.
</p>
<p>
Let P'<sub>A</sub> be P-of-A-anti-altimedial-triangle, and define P'<sub>B</sub>, P'<sub>C</sub> cyclically.  Triangle P'<sub>A</sub>P'<sub>B</sub>P'<sub>C</sub> is here named the <b>P-of-anti-altimedial-triangles triangle</b>, or <b>P-anti-altimedial triangle</b> for short.  For all finite P other than X(5) (for which the P-anti-altimedial triangle is the triple point X(3)), the P-anti-altimedial triangle is inversely similar to ABC, with center of inverse similitude X(3).
</p>
<p>
The X(3)-anti-altimedial triangle is also the X(3)-Fuhrmann triangle.<br>
The X(10)-anti-altimedial triangle is also the inner Garcia triangle.
</p>
<p>
If P is a point on the Euler line, the P-altimedial and P-anti-altimedial triangles are both homothetic to the orthocentroidal and anti-orthocentroidal triangles.
</p>
<p>
Let P"<sub>A</sub> be P-of-A-adjunct-anti-altimedial-triangle, and define P"<sub>B</sub>, P"<sub>C</sub> cyclically.  Triangle P"<sub>A</sub>P"<sub>B</sub>P"<sub>C</sub> is here named the <b>P-of-adjunct-anti-altimedial-triangles triangle</b>, or <b>P-adjunct-anti-altimedial triangle</b> for short(er).
</p>
<p>
The X(1)-adjunct anti-altimedial triangle is also the anticomplementary triangle of the inner Garcia triangle.
</p>
<p>
The P-altimedial and P-adjunct anti-altimedial triangles are homothetic for all finite P, and the center of homothety is P-of-orthocentroidal-triangle.
</p>
<p>
<br>
Let L<sub>A</sub> be the orthic axis of the A-altimedial triangle, and define L<sub>B</sub>, L<sub>C</sub> cyclically.  Let A' = L<sub>B</sub>&cap;L<sub>C</sub>, and define B', C' cyclically.  The triangle A'B'C' is here named the <b>altimedial orthic axes triangle</b>, or <b>AOA triangle</b> for short.  The AOA triangle is inversely similar to the orthic triangle with center of inverse similitude X(15127).  It is also homothetic to the 1st Hyacinth triangle at X(15120).
</p>
<p>
Let L'<sub>A</sub> be the orthic axis of the A-anti-altimedial triangle, and define L'<sub>B</sub>, L'<sub>C</sub> cyclically.  Let A" = L'<sub>B</sub>&cap;L'<sub>C</sub>, and define B", C" cyclically.  The triangle A"B"C" is here named the <b>(anti-altimedial) orthic axes triangle</b>, or <b>AAOA triangle</b> for short.  The AAOA triangle is perspective to ABC at X(67), homothetic to the 1st Hyacinth triangle at X(15134), and to the AOA triangle at X(5094).  The endo-homothetic center of the AOA and AAOA triangles is X(5219).<br>
<p></p>
