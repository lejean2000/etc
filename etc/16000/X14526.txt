<h3 id="X14526">X(14526) &nbsp;=&nbsp;CENTER OF THE 1st SCHIFFLER CIRCLE</h3>
Trilinears &nbsp;&nbsp; 2*(cos(A)+1)+(cos(B)+cos(C))*(4*sin(B)*sin(C)+1)+cos(B-C)  :  :
<br>
Barycentrics &nbsp;&nbsp; (b+c)*a^6+2*b*c*a^5-(b+c)*(3*b^2-b*c+3*c^2)*a^4-b*c*(3*b^2+4*b*c+3*c^2)*a^3+3*(b^3-c^3)*(b^2-c^2)*a^2+(b^2-c^2)^2*b*c*a-(b^2-c^2)^3*(b-c)  :  :  <br>
<br>
<b>
X(14526) = X(5086)-3*X(6175)
</b>
<p>
Let A'B'C' be the 1st Schiffler triangle of ABC. Let A<sub>a'</sub>, B<sub>b'</sub>, C<sub>c'</sub> be the orthogonal projections of A, B, C on B'C', C'A', A'B', respectively, and A'<sub>a</sub>, B'<sub>b</sub>, C'<sub>c</sub> the orthogonal projections of A', B', C'  on BC, CA, AB. These six points lie on a circle here named the <b>1st Schiffler circle</b>, with center X(14526) and squared radius ((9*R+2*r)*R^2*r^2+(R+2*r)*S^2)*R/((R+2*r)^2*(3*R+2*r)^2). This circle passes through X(11) and X(1365) and is the circumcircle of the pedal triangles of the isogonal conjugate pair X(35) and X(79).
</p>
<p>
Next, if "1st Schiffler triangle" is replaced by "2nd Schiffler triangle", the resulting six points lie on a circle, here named the <b>2nd Schiffler circle</b>, with center X(1737) and squared radius R*r^2/(R-2*r). This circle passes through X(11), X(5532), X(13141), X(14027) and is the circumcircle of the pedal triangles of the isogonal conjugate pair X(36) and X(80).
</p>
<p>
Continuing, let A'B'C' be the 1st Schiffler triangle and A"B"C" the 2nd Schiffler triangle of ABC. Let A<sub>1</sub>, B<sub>1</sub>, C<sub>1</sub> be the orthogonal projections of A', B', C' on B"C", C"A", A"B", respectively, and A<sub>2</sub>, B<sub>2</sub>, C<sub>2</sub> the orthogonal projections of A", B", C"  on B'C', C'A' and A'B'. These six points A<sub>1</sub>, B<sub>1</sub>, C<sub>1</sub> , A<sub>2</sub>, B<sub>2</sub>, C<sub>2</sub> lie on a circle here named the <b>3rd Schiffler circle</b>, with center X(14527). This circle also passes through X(11).
</p>
