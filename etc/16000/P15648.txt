Let T<sub>1</sub>=A<sub>1</sub>B<sub>1</sub>C<sub>1</sub> and T<sub>2</sub>=A<sub>2</sub>B<sub>2</sub>C<sub>2</sub> be two perspective triangles, neither inscribed in the other.
<br>
<br>
Denote:
<br>
<br>
&emsp;&emsp;1) a<sub>b</sub> = A<sub>1</sub>B<sub>2</sub>, a<sub>c</sub> = A<sub>1</sub>C<sub>2</sub>, b<sub>c</sub>= B<sub>1</sub>C<sub>2</sub>, b<sub>a</sub> = B<sub>1</sub>A<sub>2</sub>, c<sub>a</sub> = C<sub>1</sub>A<sub>2</sub>, c<sub>b</sub> = C<sub>1</sub>B<sub>2</sub>
<br>
<br>
&emsp;&emsp;2) A<sub>b</sub> = a<sub>b</sub>&cap;b<sub>c</sub>, A<sub>c</sub> = a<sub>c</sub>&cap;c<sub>b</sub>, B<sub>c</sub> = b<sub>c</sub>&cap;c<sub>a</sub>, B<sub>a</sub> = b<sub>a</sub>&cap;a<sub>c</sub>, C<sub>a</sub> = c<sub>a</sub>&cap;a<sub>b</sub>, C<sub>b</sub> = c<sub>b</sub>&cap;b<sub>a</sub>
<br>
<br>
Then the six points A<sub>b</sub>, A<sub>c</sub>, B<sub>a</sub>, B<sub>c</sub>, C<sub>a</sub>, C<sub>b</sub> lie on a conic, here named the <b>cross-perspeconic of T<sub>1</sub> and T<sub>2</sub></b>.
</p>
