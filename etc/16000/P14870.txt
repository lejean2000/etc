<p>
"To determine a point P whose pedal triangle A'B'C' with regard to a given triangle ABC shall be similar to a given triangle A*B*C*". <i>(Johnson, Roger A.: Advanced Eucliden Geometry, Dover, New York, 1960, problem 205, pp. 142.)</i>
</p>
<p>
Assume ABC and A*B*C* are not inversely similar. For finding P, take any two points B<sub>1</sub>, C<sub>1</sub> on AC and AB, respectively, and build A<sub>1</sub>B<sub>1</sub>C<sub>1</sub> directly similar to A*B*C*. Then, through A<sub>2</sub>=AA<sub>1</sub>&cap;BC, draw parallels to A<sub>1</sub>B<sub>1</sub> and A<sub>1</sub>C<sub>1</sub>, cutting AC and AB at B<sub>2</sub> and C<sub>2</sub>, respectively. The required P is the Miquel point of A<sub>2</sub>B<sub>2</sub>C<sub>2</sub> with respect to ABC. (Proof in the cited reference).
</p>
<p>
The point P and the triangle A'B'C' will be named here the <b>simpedal point of A*B*C* in ABC</b> and the <b>simpedal triangle of A*B*C* in ABC</b>, respectively.
</p>
