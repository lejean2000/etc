<h3 id="X14220">X(14220)&nbsp;=&nbsp; ISOGONAL CONJUGATE OF X(7480)</h3>
Barycentrics &nbsp;&nbsp; (b^2-c^2) (-a^2+b^2+c^2) (a^8+a^6 b^2-4 a^4 b^4+a^2 b^6+b^8-3 a^6 c^2+2 a^4 b^2 c^2+2 a^2 b^4 c^2-3 b^6 c^2+3 a^4 c^4-2 a^2 b^2 c^4+3 b^4 c^4-a^2 c^6-b^2 c^6) (-a^8+3 a^6 b^2-3 a^4 b^4+a^2 b^6-a^6 c^2-2 a^4 b^2 c^2+2 a^2 b^4 c^2+b^6 c^2+4 a^4 c^4-2 a^2 b^2 c^4-3 b^4 c^4-a^2 c^6+3 b^2 c^6-c^8) : :
<p>
See Bernard Gibert, <a href="https://bernard-gibert.pagesperso-orange.fr/Exemples/k186.html"> <b>Cubics: K186</b></a>.
</p>
<p>
Let A', B', C' be points on the circumcircle such that AA' || BB' || CC' || Euler line.  (i.e., A'B'C' is the circumcevian triangle of X(30).)  Let A0B0C0 be the triangle bounded by the Simson lines of A', B', C'.   The triangle A0B0C0 is perspective to ABC at X(14220).  (See ADGEOM #1674, Dao Thanh Oai, 9/11/2014 and related messages). (Randy Hutson, November 2, 2017)
</p>
