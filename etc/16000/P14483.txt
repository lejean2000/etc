Nguyen-Moses images: X(14483)-X(14498)
</p>
<img src="rightri.svg" style="height:30px" alt="rightri">

<div class="preamble-sect">
<p>
Let ABC be a triangle, let L be the line through B orthogonal to BC, let B<sub>A</sub> be any point on L, and let B<sub>C</sub> be the point such that BB<sub>A</sub>B<sub>C</sub>C is a rectangle.  Likewise, let CC<sub>B</sub>C<sub>A</sub>A be a rectangle with base CA and let AA<sub>C</sub>A<sub>B</sub>B be a rectangle with base AB.  Let L<sub>A</sub> be the line through A perpendicular to B<sub>C</sub>C<sub>B</sub>, and define L<sub>B</sub> and L<sub>C</sub> cyclically.  Starting with Nguyen Ngoc Giang's rectangles (as in the preamble just before X(14206)), Peter Moses (September 19, 2017) found that the lines L<sub>A</sub>, L<sub>B</sub>, L<sub>C</sub> concur.
</p>
<p>
</p>
<p>
Let U be the ratio of the height of the rectangle BB<sub>A</sub>B<sub>C</sub>C to the base; that is, U = |BA<sub>B</sub>|/a.  Define V and W cyclically.  The point X' of concurrence is given by
</p>
<p>
X' = 1 / (-a^2 + b^2 + c^2 + 2 S U) : :  (barycentrics)
</p>
<p>
If X is a point in the plane of ABC, then it has actual trilinear distances (possibly nonpositive) that are the heights of rectangles as in the above construction.  Therefore, starting with X = x : y : z (barycentrics), we have U = kx/a<sup>2</sup>, where k = S/(x + y + z), and V = ky/b<sup>2</sup> and W = kz/c<sup>2</sup>.  Consequently,
</p>
<p>
X' = a^2 / (a^2 (-a^2 + b^2 + c^2) + 2 S^2 x (x + y + z)) : :
</p>
<p>
The point X' is here named the <b>Nguyen-Moses image</b> of X.
</p>
