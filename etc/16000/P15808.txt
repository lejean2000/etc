<p>
If A'B'C' and A"B"C" are two perspective triangles, neither inscribed in the other, then the six lines A'B", A'C", B'C", B'A", C'A" and C'B" are tangent to a unique conic, here named <b>the tangential perspeconic of A'B'C' and A"B"C"</b>.
</p>
<p>
Suppose the first triangle is the reference triangle ABC and the second is a central triangle A'B'C', perspective to ABC at X = x : y : z (barycentrics). Then XA'/XA = &lambda;<sub>a</sub>, XB'/XB = &lambda;<sub>b</sub> and XC'/XC = &lambda;<sub>c</sub>, where &lambda;<sub>a</sub> = &lambda;(a,b,c), &lambda;<sub>b</sub> = &lambda;(b,c,a), &lambda;<sub>c</sub> = &lambda;(c,a,b) and &lambda;(a,b,c) is a degree-0 homogeneous function of a,b,c. In this case, the center Q of the tangential perspeconic of ABC and A'B'C' is:
<br>
<br>&emsp;&emsp;Q = &lambda;<sub>a</sub> (&lambda;<sub>b</sub> &lambda;<sub>c</sub> - 1) (y + z) + (&lambda;<sub>b</sub> + &lambda;<sub>c</sub> - 2) x : :  (barycentrics)
</p>
