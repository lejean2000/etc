<h3 id="X14052">X(14052)&nbsp;=&nbsp; PERSPECTOR OF FEUERBACH HYPERBOLA OF THE ORTHIC TRIANGLE</h3>
Barycentrics &nbsp;&nbsp; SB*SC /(5*S^2+SA^2+2*SB*SC-2*SW^2) : :
<br>
Barycentrics &nbsp;&nbsp; (sec A)/(sin B sin C - e^2 sin(B + &omega;) sin(C + &omega;) - sin B sin(C + 2&omega;) - sin C sin(B + 2&omega;) + sin(B + 2&omega;) sin(C + 2&omega;)) : :
<p>
Let A'B'C' be the orthic triangle, and for arbitrary point P, let La be the line through A' parallel to AP, and define Lb and Lc cyclically.  The locus of P such that La, Lb, Lc concur is the Jerabek hyperbola.  The locus of the point of concurrence is the Feuerbach hyperbola of the orthic triangle, given by the barycentric equation
</p>
<p>
(S<sub>B</sub> - S<sub>C</sub>)(S<sup>2</sup><sub>A</sub>x<sup>2</sup> + S<sub>B</sub>S<sub>C</sub>yz) + (S<sub>C</sub> - S<sub>A</sub>)(S<sup>2</sup><sub>B</sub>y<sup>2</sup> + S<sub>C</sub>S<sub>A</sub>zx) +
(S<sub>A</sub> - S<sub>B</sub>)(S<sup>2</sup><sub>C</sub>z<sup>2</sup> + S<sub>A</sub>S<sub>B</sub>xy) = 0.
</p>
<p>
The center of the hyperbola is X(1112), and the perspector is X(14052.  The hyperbola passes through X(i) for these i:
4, 6, 52, 113, 155, 185, 193, 1162, 1163, 1829, 1839, 1843, 1858, 1986, 2574, 2575, 2904, 2905, 2906, 2907, 2914, 3574, 5095, 5895, 6152, 10294, 11817, 13202, 13420, 13431, as well and A' B' C' and the vertices of the cevian triangle of X(648).
</p>
<p>
See Antreas Hatzipolakis and César Lozada, <a href="http://www.hyacinthos.epizy.com/message.php?msg=26413"> <b>Hyacinthos 26413</b></a>.
</p>
<p>
Peter Moses (August 14, 2017) noted that this hyperbola appears in Example 2, page 145, in Clark Kimberling, "Conics associated with a cevian nest", <a href="http://forumgeom.fau.edu/FG2001volume1/FG200121.pdf"> <b><i>Forum Geometricorum</i></b></a> 1 (2001) 141-150.
</p>
<p>
The Hatzipolakis-Lozada hyperbola is the orthic isogonal conjugate of the Euler line, and the Feuerbach hyperbola of the orthic triangle if ABC is acute, and also the bicevian conic of X(4) and X(648). (Randy Hutson, November 2, 2017)
</p>
