<p class="preamble-title">
Nguyen orthoimages: X(14538)-X(14541)
</p>
<img src="rightri.svg" style="height:30px" alt="rightri">
</header>
<div class="preamble-sect">

<p>
Let ABC be a triangle, let L be the line through B orthogonal to BC, let B<sub>A</sub> be any point on L, and let B<sub>C</sub> be the point such that BB<sub>A</sub>B<sub>C</sub>C is a rectangle.  Likewise, let CC<sub>B</sub>C<sub>A</sub>A be a rectangle with base CA and let AA<sub>C</sub>A<sub>B</sub>B be a rectangle with base AB.  Let A' = A<sub>B</sub>C<sub>B</sub>&cap;A<sub>C</sub>B<sub>C</sub>, and define B' and C' cyclically.  Let L<sub>A</sub> be the line through A' orthogonal to line BC, and define L<sub>B</sub> and L<sub>C</sub> cyclically.  Nguyen Ngoc Giang found that the lines L<sub>A</sub>, L<sub>B</sub>, L<sub>C</sub> concur.  See the preamble just before A(14206).
</p>
<p>
If you have The Geometer's Sketchpad, you can view <a href="NguyenTriangle.gsp"><b>Nguyen Triangle</b></a>.
</p>
<p>
Let U be the ratio of the height of the rectangle BB<sub>A</sub>B<sub>C</sub>C to the base; that is, U = |BA<sub>B</sub>|/a.  Define V and W cyclically.  Peter Moses found (September 23, 2017) that the point X' of concurrence of the lines L<sub>A</sub>, L<sub>B</sub>, L<sub>C</sub> is given by
</p>
<p>
X' = 2a<sup>2</sup>(a<sup>2</sup> - b<sup>2</sup> - c<sup>2</sup>)UV + 2a<sup>2</sup>(a<sup>2</sup> - b<sup>2</sup> - c<sup>2</sup>)UW + (a<sup>2</sup> + b<sup>2</sup> - c<sup>2</sup>)(a<sup>2</sup> - b<sup>2</sup> + c<sup>2</sup>)VW : :
</p>
<p>
If X is a point in the plane of ABC, then it has actual trilinear distances (possibly nonpositive) that are the heights of rectangles as in the above construction. Therefore, starting with X = x : y : z (barycentrics), we have U = kx/a<sup>2</sup>, where k = S/(x + y + z), and V = ky/b<sup>2</sup> and W = kz/c<sup>2</sup>.  Consequently,
</p>
<p>
X' = 2a<sup>2</sup>c<sup>2</sup>(a<sup>2</sup> - b<sup>2</sup> - c<sup>2</sup>)xy + 2a<sup>2</sup>b<sup>2</sup>(a<sup>2</sup> - b<sup>2</sup> - c<sup>2</sup>)xz + a<sup>2</sup>(a<sup>2</sup> + b<sup>2</sup> - c<sup>2</sup>)(a<sup>2</sup> - b<sup>2</sup> + c<sup>2</sup>)yz : :
</p>
<p>
X' = a<sup>2</sup>(S<sub>B</sub>S<sub>C</sub>yz - b<sup>2</sup>S<sub>A</sub>xz - c<sup>2</sup>S<sub>A</sub>xy : :
</p>
<p>
X' = isogonal conjugate of the X(3)-vertex conjugate of X<br>
X' = reflection-in-X(3) of the isogonal conjugate of P
</p>
<p>
The triangle A'B'C' is here named the <b>X-Nguyen triangle</b>, and the point X' is named the <b>Nguyen orthoimage</b> of X.
</p>

<p>
The X-Nguyen triangle A'B'C' is perspective to ABC if X is on one of the following:  (1) the line at infinity; (2), the circumcircle; (3) a quartic curve that passes through X(i) for i = 3, 4, 64, 2574, 2575; and (4) the circle with center X(3) and radius R*sqrt(5), here named the <b>Nguyen-Moses circle.</b>  If X is on the circumcircle, then the points given by combos 4*X(3) - (5 + sqrt(5))*X and 4*X(3) - (5 - sqrt(5))*X is on the Nguyen-Moses circle.
