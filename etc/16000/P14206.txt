<p class="preamble-title">
Nguyen Images: X(14206) - X(14213)
</p>
<img src="rightri.svg" style="height:30px" alt="rightri">
</header>
<div class="preamble-sect">
<p>
Let ABC be a triangle, let L be the line through B orthogonal to BC, let B<sub>A</sub> be any point on L, and let B<sub>C</sub> be the point such that BB<sub>A</sub>B<sub>C</sub>C is a rectangle.  Likewise, let CC<sub>B</sub>C<sub>A</sub>A be a rectangle with base CA and let AA<sub>C</sub>A<sub>B</sub>B be a rectangle with base AB.  Let A' be the midpoint of B<sub>A</sub>C<sub>A</sub>, and define B' and C' cyclically.
Let L<sub>A</sub> be the line through A' perpendicular to BC, and define L<sub>B</sub> and L<sub>C</sub> cyclically.  Nguyen Ngoc Giang found that the lines L<sub>A</sub>, L<sub>B</sub>, L<sub>C</sub> concur.  (Received from Dao Thanh Oai, August 11, 2017)
</p>
<p>
If you have The Geometer's Sketchpad, you can view <a href="NguyenRectangles.gsp"><b>Nguyen Rectangles</b></a>.
</p>
<p>
Peter Moses analyzed the construction (August, 2017) as follows.  Let U be the ratio of the height of the rectangle BB<sub>A</sub>B<sub>C</sub>C to the base; that is, U = |BA<sub>B</sub>|/a.  Define V and W cyclically.  The point X' of concurrence is given by
</p>
<p>
X' = (a<sup>2</sup> + b<sup>2</sup> - c<sup>2</sup>)(a<sup>2</sup> - b<sup>2</sup> + c<sup>2</sup>) - 2a<sup>2</sup>SU + (a<sup>2</sup> + b<sup>2</sup> - c<sup>2</sup>)SV + (a<sup>2</sup> - b<sup>2</sup> + c<sup>2</sup>)SW : :  (barycentrics)
</p>
<p>
The triangles ABC and A'B'C' are orthologic, and X' is the A'B'C'-to-ABC orthologic center.  The ABC-to-A'B'C' orthologic center, X'', is given by
</p>
<p>
X'' = 1/(-a<sup>2</sup> + b<sup>2</sup> + c<sup>2</sup> + SU) : :
</p>
<p>
If X is a point in the plane of ABC, then it has actual trilinear distances (possibly nonpositive) that are the heights of rectangles as in the above construction.  Therefore, starting with X = x : y : z (barycentrics), we have U = kx/a<sup>2</sup>, where k = S/(x + y + z), and V = ky/b<sup>2</sup> and W = kz/c<sup>2</sup>.  Consequently,
</p>
<p>
X' = 2a(abc)<sup>2</sup> (cos B cos C)(x + y + z) + S<sup>2</sup> (-abcx + a<sup>2</sup> cy cos C + a<sup>2</sup> bz cos B) : :
</p>
<p>
Equivalently,
</p>
<p>
X' = X' = (H - abcS<sup>2</sup>)x + (H + S<sup>2</sup> a<sup>2</sup> c cos C)y + (H + S<sup>2</sup> a<sup>2</sup> b cos B)z : : , where H = 2a(abc)<sup>2</sup> (cos B cos C).
</p>
<p>
Also,
</p>
<p>
X' = a (c<sup>2</sup> S<sub>C</sub> (3 S<sup>2</sup> - 2 S<sub>C</sub> S<sub>A</sub>) y + b^2 S<sub>B</sub> (3 S<sup>2</sup> - 2 S<sub>A</sub> S<sub>B</sub>) z - b<sup>2</sup> c<sup>2</sup> (S^2 - 2 S<sub>B</sub> S<sub>C</sub>) x) : :
</p>
<p>
The point X' is here named the <b>Nguyen image</b> of X, and the point X'', the <b>adjoint Nguyen image</b> of X, given by
</p>
<p>
X'' = (x + y + z)/(a<sup>2</sup>S<sub>A</sub>(x + y + z) + 4S<sup>2</sup>x)
</p>
<p>
The appearance of (i,j) in the following list means that the X(j) = Nguyen image of X(i):  (3,14211), (6,92), (511,14206), (512,14207), (513,4468), (518,908), (520,14208), (523,661), (524,1959), (525,14209), (1350,14212), (1351,14213), (2574,2582), (2575,2583), (8675,1577), (8999,4391), (9000,693), (9001,514), (9002,4462), (9004,3912), (9026,4358), (9027,14210), (9051,6332), (11477,63).
</p>
<p>
For Nguyen-Euler centers, X(14226) - X(14245), and a link to Nguyen's research paper, see the preamble just before X(14226).
</p>
