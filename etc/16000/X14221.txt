<h3 id="X14221">X(14221)&nbsp;=&nbsp; REFLECTION OF X(316) IN X(3260)</h3>

<p>
See Bernard Gibert, <a href="https://bernard-gibert.pagesperso-orange.fr/Exemples/k186.html"> <b>Cubics: K186</b></a>.</p>
<p>
X(14221) lies on the cubic K186 and these lines: {4,69}, {99,523}, {107,10425}, {249,648}, {850,4576}<br>
</p>
<p>
Let P=X(110), the focus of Kiepert parabola, and &ell;<sub>1</sub>. &ell;<sub>2</sub> perpendicular lines  through P, that intersect the sidelines BC, CA, AB at points A<sub>1</sub> and A<sub>2</sub>, B<sub>1</sub> and B<sub>2</sub>, C<sub>1</sub> and C<sub>2</sub>, respectively  Let  A<sub>bc</sub> = A<sub>1</sub>C<sub>2</sub> &cap; A<sub>2</sub>B<sub>1</sub> and A<sub>cb</sub> = A<sub>1</sub>B<sub>2</sub> &cap; A<sub>2</sub>C<sub>1</sub>.  When the lines &ell;<sub>1</sub> and &ell;<sub>2</sub> rotate around P, the points A<sub>bc</sub> and A<sub>cb</sub> lie on the same conic C(A), through B and C. Let A<sub>o</sub> be its center.  The conics C(B), C(C) and and their centers B<sub>o</sub> and C<sub>o</sub> are defined cyclically. Then the lines AA<sub>o </sub>, BB<sub>o</sub> and CC<sub>o</sub> concur in X(14221). (See <a href="https://amontes.webs.ull.es/otrashtm/HGT2021.htm#HG060721">HG060721</a>). (Angel Montesdeoca, July 13, 2021)
</p>
