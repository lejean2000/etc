<div class="preamble-sect">
<p>
Let P be an arbitrary point in the plane of a triangle ABC.  Let O<sub>A</sub> be the centere of the circumcircle of BPC.  Let L<sub>A</sub> be the line through O<sub>A</sub> parallel to line AP, and define L<sub>B</sub> and L<sub>C</sub> cyclically.  The lines L<sub>A</sub>, L<sub>B</sub>, L<sub>C</sub> concur in a point Q = Q(P), here named the <b>Dao image of P</b>.  (Dao Thanh Oai, November 20, 2017).
<p></p>
If P = p : q : r (barycentrics), then
<p></p>
Q = p (c^2 q^2 + (-a^2 + b^2 + c^2) q r + b^2 r^2) (2 a^2 q r + p (-(-a^2 + b^2 + c^2) p + (a^2 - b^2 + c^2) q + (a^2 + b^2 - c^2) r)) : :
<p></p>
If P is on the circumconic &Gamma; = {A, B, C, X(4), P}, then Q is on the complement of &Gamma;.  (Peter Moses, November 23, 2017)
<p></p>
The Dao image of P is the complement of the Kirikami-Euler image of P. (Randy Hutson, December 2, 2017)

<p>
The Dao image of P is the X(2)-Ceva conjugate of the 2nd Vu point of P.  (Randy Hutson, November 17, 2019)
</p>
