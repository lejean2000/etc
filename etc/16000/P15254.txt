<p>
Let ABC and A'B'C' be two perspective triangles such that neither is inscribed in the other. Let A<sub>b</sub> = BC&cap;A'B', A<sub>c</sub> = BC&cap;A'C', and likewise for B<sub>c</sub>, B<sub>a</sub>, C<sub>a</sub>, and C<sub>b</sub>. As ABC and A'B'C' are perspective, the pairs of lines {BC, B'C'}, {CA, C'A'} and {AB, A'B'} concur at three collinear points, and the lines A<sub>b</sub>A<sub>c</sub>, B<sub>c</sub>B<sub>a</sub>, C<sub>a</sub>C<sub>b</sub> join opposite vertices of an hexagon. By Pascal's theorem, the six points lie on a conic, here named the <b>perspeconic</b> of ABC and A'B'C'.
</p>
