Singular Foci of Cubics: X(14649)-X(14706)
</p>
<img src="rightri.svg" style="height:30px" alt="rightri">
</header>
<div class="preamble-sect">
<p>
Let O(P) denote the orthopivotal cubic of a point P, as defined at <a href="https://bernard-gibert.pagesperso-orange.fr/gloss/orthopivotalcubi.html"><b>Orthopivotal cubics</b></a>.  Five points on this cubic are A, B, C, X(13), and X(14).  The singular focus of O(P), denoted by Psi(P) defines an involutory mapping P &srarr; Psi(P), and Psi(P) is called the Psi-transform of P.  The centers X(14649)-X(14706) involve these orthopivotal cubics for these points:  X(14651), X(14656), X(14660), X(14704), X(14705), X(14706).  The others involve circular cubics.  See <a href="SingularFocusOfCubics.html"><b>Singular Focus of Circular Cubics in Bernard Gibert's CTC</b></a>.
