<h3 id="X15449">X(15449)&nbsp;=&nbsp; CENTER OF THE X(2)-ALTINTAS HYPERBOLA</h3>
Barycentrics &nbsp;&nbsp; (b<sup>4</sup> - c<sup>4</sup>)<sup>2</sup> : (c<sup>4</sup> - a<sup>4</sup>)<sup>2</sup> : (a<sup>4</sup> - b<sup>4</sup>)<sup>2</sup>
<p>
Let P be a point in the plane of a triangle ABC, and let DEF = cevian triangle of P, G(P) = X(2)-of-DEF, and K(P) = X(6)-of-DEF.  The circumconic {A, B, C, G(P), K(P)} is a hyperbola, here named the <b>P-Altintas hyperbola</b>.
</p>
<p>
A barycentric equation for the X(2)-Altintas hyperbola:
</p>
<p>
(b<sup>4</sup> - c<sup>4</sup>)<sup>2</sup>yz + (c<sup>4</sup> - a<sup>4</sup>)<sup>2</sup>zx + (a<sup>4</sup> - b<sup>4</sup>)<sup>2</sup>xy = 0.
</p>
<p>
This hyperbola passes through X(i) for i in {2, 66, 141, 427, 1031, 1502, 3613, 8024, 8801, 9076, 9483, 14617}.  The hyperbola is the isogonal conjugate of the line X(6)X(22) and is the isotomic conjugate of the line X(2)X(32).
</p>
<p>
See Kadir Altintas and César Lozada, <a href="http://www.hyacinthos.epizy.com/message.php?msg=26868"> <b>Hyacinthos 26868</b></a>.
</p>
<p>
X(15449) lies on the Steiner inellipse and lines: {2, 4577}, {4, 14718}, {32, 14378}, {66, 9233}, {115, 9479}, {125, 1084}, {570, 11672}, {1031, 9483}, {2482, 6292}, {3005, 8288}, {6697, 8265}, {14416, 14424}
</p>
<p>
X(15449) = midpoint of X(i) and X(j) for these {i,j}: {4, 14718}, {1031, 9483}<br>
X(15449) = complement of X(4577)<br>
