<p>
Let r be the Euler line of &Delta;ABC and P a variable point. Let r<sub>a</sub>, r<sub>b</sub>, r<sub>c</sub> be the Euler lines of the <b>triangulation</b> of P, here defined as ordered triple (&Delta;PBC, &Delta;PCA and &Delta;PAB) of triangles. Then, if P is on the Neuberg cubic, the four lines r, r<sub>a</sub>, r<sub>b</sub>, r<sub>c</sub> concur.
</p>
<p>
Under the same conditions, the Brocard axes of &Delta;ABC, &Delta;PBC, &Delta;PCA and &Delta;PAB concur.
</p>

Suppose that U, V, and X(399) are collinear points on the Neuberg cubic.  Let T(U), T(V), T(X(399)) be the corresponding triangulations.  The Euler lines of T(U), T(V), T(X(399)) concur in a point P on the Euler line of ABC.  Example: U=X(13), V=X(14), P= X(2).  Suppose that U,V,W on the Neuberg cubic and on the circle through X(110) and X(399).  The Brocard axes of their triangulations of U,V,W concur in a point Q on the Brocard axis of ABC.  Example: U=X(14), V=X(15), W=X(1337), Q=X(61). For proofs and related properties see <b>Bernard Gibert</b>, <a href="https://bernard-gibert.pagesperso-orange.fr/files/Resources/NeubergEuler.pdf">"Pairs and Triads of points on the Neuberg Cubic connected with Euler Lines and Brocard Axes Isometric Parallel Chords"</a>.
