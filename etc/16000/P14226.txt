<p>
This section is based on Nguyen Ngoc Giang's paper mentioned in the preamble just before X(14206).  The paper appears in the <a href="http://www.journal-1.eu/index.htm">International Journal of Computer Discovered Mathematics</a>, vol 2(2017), pp 135-140.
</p>
<p>
Let BB<sub>A</sub>C<sub>A</sub>C, CC<sub>B</sub>A<sub>B</sub>A, AA<sub>C</sub>B<sub>C</sub>B be three rectangles built on the sides of a triangle ABC, such that |BB<sub>A</sub>|/a = |CC<sub>B</sub>|/b = |AA<sub>C</sub>|/c = &lambda; = constant. Assume that &lambda; %gt; 0 means that rectangles are built outwards from ABC and &lambda; &lt; 0 means that rectangles are built inwards from ABC. Nguyen  proved that if N<sub>A</sub>, N<sub>B</sub>, N<sub>C</sub> are X(5) (i.e., the nine-point-center) of triangles AB<sub>A</sub>C<sub>A</sub>, BC<sub>B</sub>A<sub>B</sub> and CA<sub>C</sub>B<sub>C</sub>, respectively, then triangles N<sub>A</sub>N<sub>B</sub>N<sub>C</sub> and ABC are orthologic.
</p>
<p>
In addition, it can be proved that if the three nine-point-centers (the N's) are replaced by any other point P on the Euler line such that  (X(3)P)|/(X(3)X(4)) = (OP)/(OH) = t, a constant invariant of (a,b,c), then the triangles P<sub>A</sub>P<sub>B</sub>P<sub>C</sub> and ABC remain orthologic. In this case, the orthologic center P<sub>A</sub>P<sub>B</sub>P<sub>C</sub> to ABC is:
<br>
<br>&emsp;
X' = (2*a^6 - (b^2+c^2)*(a^4+(b^2-c^2)^2))*(3*t-1)*&lambda; - 2*S*(a^2+b^2-c^2)*(a^2-b^2+c^2) : : (barycentrics)
<br>
<br>
and the orthologic center ABC to P<sub>A</sub>P<sub>B</sub>P<sub>C</sub>:
<br>
<br>&emsp;
X" = 1/(((2*a^4 + 2*(b^2+c^2)*a^2 + 4*b^2*c^2 - 4*(b^2+c^2)^2)*t + (a^2+b^2+c^2)*(-a^2+b^2+c^2))*&lambda; + 2*S*(-a^2+b^2+c^2)) : : (barycentrics)
</p>
<p>
The point X' is here named the <b>Nguyen-Euler(&lambda;) point of P</b>, and the point X", the <b>Nguyen-Euler(&lambda;) adjoint point of P</b>.
</p>
<p>
For every &lambda; and P,:
<ul>
<li>X(4) = Nguyen-Euler(&lambda;) point of X(2);
<li>X(4) = Nguyen-Euler(&lambda;) adjoint point of X(3);
<li>X(4) = Nguyen-Euler(0) point of P = Nguyen-Euler(0) adjoint point of P.
</ul>
<p>
X' lies on the van Aubel line X(4)X(6), and |X(4)X'|/|X(4)X(6)| = &lambda;*(3*t - 1)*cot(&omega;), where &omega; is the Brocard angle of ABC; moreover, X" lies on the Kiepert hyperbola.
</p>
