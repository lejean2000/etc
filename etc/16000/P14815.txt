<p>
The following material is based on a construction by M. D. Ghoicas in 1934; see Antreas Hatzipolakis and CÃ©sar Lozada, <a href="http://www.hyacinthos.epizy.com/message.php?msg=26655"> <b>Hyacinthos 26655</b></a>.   Let P be a point in the plane of a triangle ABC, not on one of the three sidelines, and let P* be the isogonal conjugate P*.  Let
</p>
<p>
A<sub>B</sub> = AP&cap;BC, and define B<sub>C</sub> and C<sub>A</sub> cyclically.<br>
A<sub>C</sub> = AP*&cap;BC, and define B<sub>A</sub> and C<sub>B</sub> cyclically.<br>
A' = BB<sub>C</sub> /\ CC<sub>B</sub>,  and define B' and C' cyclically.<br>
A'' = BB<sub>A</sub> /\ CC<sub>A</sub>, and define B'' and C'' cyclically.<br>
</p>
<p>
Then the following pairs of triangles are perspective:  ABC and A'B'C', ABC and A''B''C'', and A'B'C' and A''B''C''.  Let
</p>
<p>
Q = ABC and A'B'C'<br>
Q* = perspector of ABC and A'B'C' <br>
GL(P) = perspector of A'B'C' and A''B''C''<br>
</p>
<p>
The point GL(P) is here named the <b>Ghoicas-Lozada image of P.</b>  Let
</p>
<p>
U = angle BAP = angle CAP*,  &nbsp;&nbsp; V = angle CBP = angle ACP*,  &nbsp;&nbsp; <p> W = angle ACP = angle BAP*
</p>
<p>
Homogeneous trilinear coordinates for Q, Q*, and G(L,P) are as follows:
</p>
<p>
Q = (sin A)/sin(A - U) : (sin B)/sin(B - U) : (sin C)/sin(C - U)   (cf. TCCT p.177, and much earlier: A. G. Burgess, "Concurrency of lines joining vertices of a triangle to opposite vewrtices of triangles on its side," <i>Proceedings of the Edinburgh Mathematical Society</i> 33 (1913-1914) 58-64; especialy p. 60.)
</p>
<p>
Q* = sin(A - U)/sin A : sin(B - U)/sin B : sin(C - U)/sin C
</p>
<p>
GL(P) = sin U sin(B - V) sin(C - W) + sin V sin W sin(A - U) : sin V sin(C - W) sin(A - U) + sin W sin U sin(B - U) :  sin W sin(A - U) sin(B - V) + sin U sin V sin(C - V)
</p>
<p>
If X is a triangle center, then GL(X) is a triangle center.
</p>
<p>
If P= p : q : r (barycentrics), then GL(P) = a<sup>2</sup>p(c<sup>2</sup>q<sup>2</sup> + b<sup>2</sup>r<sup>2</sup>)  : :
</p>
<p>
If X = x : y : z (trilinears), then GL(X) = x(y<sup>2</sup> + z<sup>2</sup>)  : :
</p>
<p>
GL(P) = crosspoint of P and the isogonal conjugate of P<br>
GL(P) = crosssum of P and the isogonal conjugate of P<br>
</p>
<p>

</p>
