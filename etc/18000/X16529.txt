Let F be the 1st Fermat point of ABC. Let A<sub>b</sub> be the point on CA and A<sub>c</sub> the point on AB such that FA<sub>b</sub>A<sub>c</sub> is an equilateral triangle with <u>the same orientation than ABC</u>. Build B<sub>c</sub>, B<sub>a</sub>, C<sub>a</sub>, C<sub>b</sub> cyclically. Then the Euler lines of triangles FB<sub>a</sub>C<sub>a</sub>, FC<sub>b</sub>A<sub>b</sub> and FA<sub>c</sub>B<sub>c</sub> are concurrent at X(16529). Also, the acute angle between each pair of these Euler lines is &pi;/3. (Dao Thanh Oai, March 14, 2018). Coordinates and properties provided by César Eliud Lozada, March 15, 2018.</p>
