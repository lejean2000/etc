<h3 id="X16840">X(16840)&nbsp;=&nbsp; PERSPECTOR OF THESE TRIANGLES:  ABC AND 2nd MORLEY-ADJUNCT-MIDPOINT</h3>
Barycentrics &nbsp;&nbsp;  sin 2A csc(2A/3 + &pi;/3) : :
<br>
Barycentrics &nbsp;&nbsp; Sin[A] (2 Sin[A]+Csc[C/3-Pi/6] Sin[B]+Csc[B/3-Pi/6] Sin[C]) : :
<p>
Let A&prime;B&prime;C&prime; be the 2nd Morley triangle.  Let Ba and Ca be points on BC such that A&prime;BaCa is an equilateral triangle having the same orientation as ABC; define Cb and Ac cyclically, and define Ab and Bc cyclically. Let A&Prime; be the midpoint of Ab and Ac, and define B&Prime; and C&Prime; cyclically.  Then A&Prime;B&Prime;C&Prime;, here named the <b>2nd Morley-adnjunct-midpoint triangle,</b> is perspective to ABC with perspector X(16840).  See X(3602).  (Thanh Oai Dao, barycentrics by Peter Moses, March 30, 2018)
</p>
