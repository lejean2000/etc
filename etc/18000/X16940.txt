<h3 id="X16940">X(16940)&nbsp;=&nbsp;PERSPECTOR OF THESE TRIANGLES: 1st LEMOINE-DAO AND ANTI-EULER</h3>
Barycentrics &nbsp;&nbsp; sqrt(3)*((SB+SC)*S^2+SW*SB*SC)-S*(S^2+2*SW*(SB+SC))  :  :  <p>
<p>
Like in the construction of the 2nd Lemoine circle of a triangle ABC, let A<sub>b</sub>, A<sub>c</sub> be the points where the antiparallel to BC through X(6) cuts the sides AC and AB, respectively. Define B<sub>c</sub>, B<sub>a</sub>, C<sub>a</sub>, C<sub>b</sub> cyclically. Build equilateral triangles B<sub>c</sub>C<sub>b</sub>A&prime;, C<sub>a</sub>A<sub>c</sub>B&prime; and A<sub>b</sub>B<sub>a</sub>C&prime; all <u>with the same orientation than ABC</u> and build equilateral triangles B<sub>c</sub>C<sub>b</sub>A&Prime;, C<sub>a</sub>A<sub>c</sub>B&Prime; and A<sub>b</sub>B<sub>a</sub>C&Prime;, all <u>with opposite orientation than ABC</u>. Then A&prime;B&prime;C&prime; and A&Prime;B&Prime;C&Prime; are both equilateral triangles. (Dao Thanh Oai, March 25, 2018)
</p>
<p>
A&prime;B&prime;C&prime; and A&Prime;B&Prime;C&Prime; will be named here the <b>1st Lemoine-Dao</b> and the <b>2nd Lemoine-Dao</b> equilateral triangles, respectively. Their respective centers are X(10654) and X(10653). The 1st vertices of each have barycentric coordinates:
<br>
<br>&emsp;&emsp;A&prime; = -(sqrt(3)*SA+2*S)*(SB+SC)/SA : sqrt(3)*SC-S : sqrt(3)*SB-S
<br>
<br>&emsp;&emsp;A&Prime; = -(sqrt(3)*SA-2*S)*(SB+SC)/SA : sqrt(3)*SC+S : sqrt(3)*SB+S
<br>
<br>and their sidelengths are a&prime;=(S/SW)*sqrt(|SW+sqrt(3)*S|) and a&Prime;=(S/SW)*sqrt(|SW-sqrt(3)*S|)
</p>
