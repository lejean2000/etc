<p>
Let F = X(14), the 2nd Fermat point.  Let Ab be the point on CA and Ac the point on AB such that FAbAc is an equilateral triangle.  Let A&prime; be the centroid of FAbAc, and define B&prime; and C&prime; cyclically.  Let A&Prime; be the centroid of AAbAc, and define B&Prime; and C&Prime; cyclically.  The triangle A&prime;B&prime;C&prime; is here named the <b>4th Fermat-Dao equilateral triangle.</b>  (The 3rd is defined at X(16267); the triangle A&Prime;B&Prime;C&Prime; is not generally equilateral.)  The triangles A&prime;B&prime;C&prime; and A&Prime;B&Prime;C&Prime; are perspective, and the perspector is X(16268), which is X(14)-of-A&prime;B&prime;C&prime; and X(14)-of-A&Prime;B&Prime;C&Prime;.  Moreover, the triangles ABC and A&prime;B&prime;C&prime; are perspective, with perspector X(18); and ABC and A&Prime;B&Prime;C&Prime; are perspective, with perspector X(2).
(Thanh Oai Dao, March 2, 2018)
