<p>
This section follows the discussion of collineations just before X(16286).  If A&prime;B&prime;C&prime; is a central triangle other than ABC and P and U are triangle centers, then (A,B,C,P; A&prime;,B&prime;,C&prime;,U) is a regular collineation, as is its inverse, given by (A&prime;,B&prime;,C&prime;,U; A,B,C,P).
</p>
<p>
The collineation images at X(16544)-X(16576) result from A&prime;B&prime;C&prime; = excentral triangle, P = X(2), and U = X(1).  We write the image of X as m(X); let m<sup>-1</sup> denote the inverse collineation.  Then centers X(16544)-X(16576) are examples of m(X), and X(16577)-X(16614) are examples of m<sup>-1</sup>(X). Other examples are given by the following list, in which the appearance of (i,j) means that m(X(i)) = X(j):
</p>
<p>
(1,9), (2,1), (3,19), 4,610), (5,48), (6,63), (7,165), (8,1743), (9,57), (10,6), (11,101), (12,572)
</p>
