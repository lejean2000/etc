<p>
<i>If the tangents at B and C to the circumcircle of a triangle ABC intersect at A&prime;, then the circle with center A&prime; passing through B and C is called A-excosine circle of ABC. This circle cuts AB and AC again at two points which are the extremities of a diameter of it.</i>. (Reference: <a href="http://mathworld.wolfram.com/ExcosineCircle.html">Weisstein, Eric W. "Excosine Circle." From MathWorld</a>)
</p>
<p>
Let A<sub>b</sub>, A<sub>c</sub> be the points where the A-excosine circle cuts again the sidelines AC and AB, respectively, and build {B<sub>c</sub>, B<sub>a</sub>}, {C<sub>a</sub>, C<sub>b</sub>} cyclically. The triangle A<sub>1</sub>B<sub>1</sub>C<sub>1</sub> bounded by the lines A<sub>b</sub>A<sub>c</sub>, B<sub>c</sub>B<sub>a</sub> and C<sub>a</sub>C<sub>b</sub> are here named here the <b>1st excosine triangle of ABC</b> and the triangle A<sub>2</sub>B<sub>2</sub>C<sub>2</sub> bounded by the lines B<sub>c</sub>C<sub>b</sub>, C<sub>a</sub>A<sub>c</sub> and A<sub>b</sub>B<sub>a</sub> are here named here the <b>2nd excosine triangle of ABC</b>. Their 1st vertices have trilinear coordinates:
<br>
<br>&emsp;&emsp;A<sub>1</sub> = -S^2*a : (S^2-2*SA*SB)*b : (S^2-2*SA*SC)*c
<br>
<br>&emsp;&emsp;A<sub>2</sub> = -S^2*SB*SC/(SA*a) : (S^2-2*SA*SB)*b : (S^2-2*SA*SC)*c
</p>
<p>
The 1st excosine triangle is the anticomplementary triangle of the tangential triangle. (Randy Hutson, June 27, 2018)
</p>
<p>
Points A<sub>b</sub>, A<sub>c</sub>, B<sub>c</sub>, B<sub>a</sub>, C<sub>a</sub>, C<sub>b</sub> lie on a conic, here named the <b>excosine conic</b>, with center X(17807) and perspector X(17808).
</p>
