<h3 id="X16839">X(16839)&nbsp;=&nbsp; PERSPECTOR OF THESE TRIANGLES:  ABC AND 1st MORLEY-ADJUNCT-MIDPOINT</h3>
Barycentrics &nbsp;&nbsp; sin 2A csc(2A/3 - &pi;/3) : :
<br>
Barycentrics &nbsp;&nbsp; Sin[A] (2 Sin[A]+Sec[C/3] Sin[B]+Sec[B/3] Sin[C]) : :
<p>
Let A&prime;B&prime;C&prime; be the 1st Morley.  Let Ba and Ca be points on BC such that A&prime;BaCa is an equilateral triangle having the same orientation as ABC; define Cb and Ac cyclically, and define Ab and Bc cyclically. Let A&Prime; be the midpoint of Ab and Ac, and define B&Prime; and C&Prime; cyclically.  Then A&Prime;B&Prime;C&Prime;, here named the <b>1st Morley-adjunct-midpoint triangle,</b>  is perspective to ABC, with perspector X(16839).  See X(3604).  (Thanh Oai Dao, barycentrics by Peter Moses, March 30, 2018)
</p>
