<h3 id="X16802">X(16802)&nbsp;=&nbsp;PERSPECTOR OF THESE TRIANGLES: 1st ISODYNAMIC-DAO AND ANTI-EXCENTERS-REFLECTIONS</h3>
Barycentrics &nbsp;&nbsp; (SB+SC)*(3*(30*R^2-5*SA-7*SW)*S^2-sqrt(3)*(2*SW^2-7*SB*SC+21*SA^2+18*S^2-2*(39*SA+4*SW)*R^2)*S+6*(SA^2-SB*SC)*(12*R^2-2*SA-SW))  :  :  <p>
<p>
Let P<sub>1</sub> = X(15) be the 1st isodynamic point of ABC. Circles (A, AP<sub>1</sub>), (B, BP<sub>1</sub>) and (C, CP<sub>1</sub>) intersect by pairs at P<sub>1</sub>, A&prime;, B&prime;, C&prime;. Then the triangle A&prime;B&prime;C&prime; is equilateral.
</p>
<p>
Let P<sub>2</sub> = X(16) be the 2nd isodynamic point of ABC. Circles (A, AP<sub>2</sub>), (B, BP<sub>2</sub>) and (C, CP<sub>2</sub>) intersect by pairs at P<sub>2</sub>, A&Prime;, B&Prime;, C&Prime;. Then the triangle A&Prime;B&Prime;C&Prime; is equilateral. (Both conclusions by Dao Thanh Oai, March 25, 2018)
</p>
<p>
Triangles A&prime;B&prime;C&prime; and A&Prime;B&Prime;C&Prime; are here named the <b>1st isodynamic-Dao equilateral triangle</b> and the <b>2nd isodynamic-Dao equilateral triangle</b>, respectively.
</p>
<p>
Barycentric coordinates of A&prime; and A&Prime; are:
</p>
<p>
&emsp;A&prime; = -(SB+SC)*(sqrt(3)*SA+S) : sqrt(3)*(S^2+SA*SC)+(SA+3*SC)*S : sqrt(3)*(S^2+SA*SB)+(SA+3*SB)*S
</p>
<p>
&emsp;A&Prime; = -(SB+SC)*(sqrt(3)*SA-S) : sqrt(3)*(S^2+SA*SC)-(SA+3*SC)*S : sqrt(3)*(S^2+SA*SB)-(SA+3*SB)*S
</p>
<p>
The squared sides of A&prime;B&prime;C&prime; and A&Prime;B&Prime;C&Prime; are a&prime;<sup>2</sup> = 4*S^2/(SW+sqrt(3)*S) and a&Prime;<sup>2</sup> = 4*S^2/(SW-sqrt(3)*S), respectively, and their centers are X(13) and X(14), respectively.
</p>
<p>
The appearance of (T,n) in the following list means that A&prime;B&prime;C&prime; and triangle T are perspective with perspector X(n):
<br>
(ABC, 13), (anti-excenters-reflections, 16802), (anti-inverse-in-incircle, 16804), (anti-orthocentroidal, 10657), (circumorthic, 16806), (outer-Fermat, 13), (3rd Fermat-Dao*, 11542), (7th Fermat-Dao*, 16267), (11th Fermat-Dao*, 16808), (outer-Napoleon*, 16), (orthic, 2902), (inner tri-equilateral, 15)
</p>
<p>
The appearance of (T,n) in the following list means that A&Prime;B&Prime;C&Prime; and triangle T are perspective with perspector X(n):
<br>
(ABC, 14), (anti-excenters-reflections, 16803), (anti-inverse-in-incircle, 16805), (anti-orthocentroidal, 10658), (circumorthic, 16807), (inner-Fermat, 14), (4th Fermat-Dao*, 11543), (8th Fermat-Dao*, 16268), (12th Fermat-Dao*, 16809), (inner-Napoleon*, 15), (orthic, 2903), (outer tri-equilateral, 16)
</p>
<p>
In both lists, an asterisk * means that the triangles are homothetic.
</p>
<p>
(This introduction and centers X(16802) to X(16809) were contributed by César Lozada, March 28, 2018)</p>

<p>
The 1st isodynamic-Dao triangle is also the 1st isogonal triangle of X(13).  The 2nd isodynamic-Dao triangle is also the 1st isogonal triangle of X(14). (see X(36))  (Randy Hutson, March 21, 2019)
</p>
