<p>
If A&prime;B&prime;C&prime; is a central triangle other than ABC and P and U are triangle centers, then (A,B,C,P; A&prime;,B&prime;,C&prime;,U) is a regular collineation, as is its inverse, given by (A&prime;,B&prime;,C&prime;,U; A,B,C,P).
</p>
<p>
The collineation images at X(17134)-X(17219) result from A&prime;B&prime;C&prime; = medial triangle, P = X(2), and U = X(1).  We write the image of X as m(X); let m<sup>-1</sup> denote the inverse collineation.  Then centers X(17134)-X(17166) are examples of m(X), and X(17167)-X(17219) are examples of m<sup>-1</sup>(X). Other examples are given by the following list, in which the appearance of (i,j) means that m(X(i)) = X(j):
</p>
<p>
(1,75), (2,1), (3,17220), (4,17134), (5,17221), (6,17135), (7,63), (8,3875), (9, 3873), (10,4360), (21,7), (58,69), (81,8),
</p>

</p>
