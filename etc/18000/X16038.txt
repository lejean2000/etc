<h3 id="X16038">X(16038)&nbsp;=&nbsp; CENTER OF 1st DAO EQUILATERAL TRIANGLE</h3>
Barycentrics &nbsp;&nbsp; a^3 + 4*a^2*b - 3*a*b^2 - 2*b^3 + 4*a^2*c + 6*a*b*c + 2*b^2*c - 3*a*c^2 + 2*b*c^2 - 2*c^3 + 6*Sqrt[3]*a*S : : &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; (Peter Moses, February 5, 2018)
<b><br>
X(16038) = ((a + b + c)^2 - 4 (b c + c a + a b) - 6 sqrt(3) S)*X(1) + 2 ((a + b + c)^2 - 4 (b c + c a + a b))*X(7)
X(16038) = X(1) + 2 X(3638) = 4 X(1323) - X(10651) = 4 X(3638) - X(10652) = 2 X(1) + X(10652) &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; (Peter Moses, February 5, 2018)
</b>
<p>
Let D be the circle with center X(1) and radius 2*3<sup>-1/2</sup>r.  Let {A<sub>B</sub>, A<sub>C</sub>} = D&cap;AB, where |AA<sub>B</sub>| &lt; |BA<sub>B</sub>|.  Define B<sub>C</sub> and C<sub>A</sub> cyclically, and define B<sub>A</sub> and C<sub>B</sub> cyclically.  Let O<sub>A</sub> be the circle {{X(1), C<sub>A</sub>, C<sub>B</sub>}}, and define O<sub>B</sub> and O<sub>C</sub> cyclically.
<br>
Let T<sub>A</sub> = O<sub>B</sub>&cap;O<sub>C</sub>, T<sub>B</sub> = O<sub>C</sub>&cap;O<sub>A</sub>, T<sub>C</sub> = O<sub>A</sub>&cap;O<sub>B</sub>.  The triangle T<sub>A</sub>T<sub>B</sub>T<sub>C</sub> is here named the <b>1st Dao equilateral triangle.</b>
</p>
<p>
See Dao Thanh Oai, <a href="http://www.adgeom.epizy.com/message.php?msg=2197">
<b>ADGEOM 2197</b></a>.
</p>
<p>
The A-vertex of the 1st Dao equilateral triangle is given by the following barycentrics:
</p>
<p>
T<sub>A</sub> = -a (3 (a+b-c) (a-b+c)+2 Sqrt[3] S)<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; : 3 (a+b-c) (a^2-a b-3 b c-c^2)-2 Sqrt[3] (a+4 b-c) S<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; : 3 (a-b+c) (a^2-b^2-a c-3 b c)-2 Sqrt[3] (a-b+4 c) S
</p>
<p>
Substituting - S for S yields another equilateral triangle, T*, congruent to T<sub>A</sub>T<sub>B</sub>T<sub>C</sub>.  The triangle T*, here named the <b>2nd Dao equilateral triangle, </b> is perspective to ABC, with perspector X(3639). &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; (Peter Moses, February 5, 2018)
</p>
