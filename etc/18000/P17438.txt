<p>
If A&prime;B&prime;C&prime; is a central triangle other than ABC and P and U are triangle centers, then (A,B,C,P; A&prime;,B&prime;,C&prime;,U) is a regular collineation, as is its inverse, given by (A&prime;,B&prime;,C&prime;,U; A,B,C,P).
</p>
<p>
The collineation images at X(17438)-X(17499) result from A&prime;B&prime;C&prime; = incentral triangle, P = X(2), and U = X(1).  We write the image of X as m(X); let m<sup>-1</sup> denote the inverse collineation.  Then centers X(17438)-X(17478) are examples of m(X), and X(17479)-X(17499) are examples of m<sup>-1</sup>(X). Other examples are given by the following list, in which the appearance of (i,j) means that m(X(i)) = X(j):
</p>
<p>
(1,37), (2,1), (3,1953), (4,48), (5,17437), (6,38), (7,55), (8,6), (9, 354), (10,1100), (63,65), (69,31), (75,42), (145,9), (192,2), (193,63), (194,75), (513,649)
</p>

</p>
