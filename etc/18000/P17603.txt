<p>
<i>Lemma: The internal tangents of two exterior circles touch them in four concyclic points. Also, the external tangents of two circles, none totally interior to the other, touch the circles in four concyclic points.</i>
</p>
<p>
In a triangle ABC, the common internal tangents of the incircle and the A-excircle touch them in four concyclic points. Let {o<sub>a</sub>} be the circle through these touchpoints and denote as a&prime; the radical axis of {o<sub>a</sub>} and the incircle. Build b&prime; and c&prime; cyclically. The triangle A&prime;B&prime;C&prime; bounded by a&prime;, b&prime; and c&prime; will be named here the <b>Ursa-minor triangle</b> of ABC.
</p>
<p>
Continuing with the previous construction, let a&Prime; be the radical axis of {o<sub>a</sub>} and the A-excircle and build b&Prime; and c&Prime; cyclically. The triangle A&Prime;B&Prime;C&Prime; bounded by a&Prime;, b&Prime; and c&Prime; will be named here the <b>Ursa-major triangle</b> of ABC.
</p>
<p>
A-vertices of both triangles have trilinear coordinates:
<br>
<br>&emsp;&emsp;A&prime; = (b+c)*a-(b-c)^2 : -(a-b+c)*(a-c) : -(a+b-c)*(a-b)
<br>
<br>&emsp;&emsp;A&Prime; = (b+c)*(a^2+b^2+c^2)-2*(b^2+c^2)*a : -((a-b)^2+(2*a-c)*c)*(a-c) : -((a-c)^2+(2*a-b)*b)*(a-b)
</p>
