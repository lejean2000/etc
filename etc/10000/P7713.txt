<p>
Antreas Hatzipolakis poses the following construction and related questions (June 10, 2015).  Let A'B'C' be the orthic triangle of a triangle ABC and H = X(4).  Let A<sub>B</sub> be the orthogonal projection of A' on line HB', and define Bc and Ca cyclically.  Let A<sub>C</sub> be the orthogonal projection of A' on line HC', and define B<sub>A</sub> and C<sub>B</sub> cyclically.  Let P be a point (in the usual sense of a function defined on a subset of the set of points in the plane of an abstract triangle with variable side-lengths a,b,c).  Let P<sub>A</sub> = P-of-A'A<sub>B</sub>A<sub>C</sub>, P<sub>B</sub> = P-of-B'B<sub>C</sub>B<sub>A</sub>, P<sub>C</sub> = P-of-C'C<sub>A</sub>C<sub>B</sub>.  Let P<sub>A</sub><sub>B</sub> be the reflection of P<sub>A</sub> in line HB', and define P<sub>B</sub><sub>C</sub> and P<sub>C</sub><sub>A</sub> cyclically.  Let P<sub>A</sub><sub>C</sub> be the reflection of P<sub>A</sub> in line HC', and define P<sub>B</sub><sub>C</sub> and P<sub>C</sub><sub>A</sub> cyclically.  Let M<sub>A</sub> be the midpoint of P<sub>A</sub><sub>B</sub> and P<sub>A</sub><sub>C</sub>, and define M<sub>B</sub> and M<sub>C</sub> cyclically.  Then M<sub>A</sub>M<sub>B</sub>M<sub>C</sub> is homothetic to ABC, and the Euler line of M<sub>A</sub>M<sub>B</sub>M<sub>C</sub> is parallel to the Euler line of ABC.
</p>
