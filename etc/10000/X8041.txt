<h3 id="X8041">X(8041)&nbsp;=&nbsp; DANNEELS POINT OF X(4576)</h3>
Barycentrics &nbsp;&nbsp; a<sup>2</sup>(b<sup>2</sup> + c<sup>2</sup>)<sup>2</sup> : :
<p>
In the plane of a triangle ABC, let<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp; G = centroid = X(2);<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp; DEF = cevian triangle of X(6);<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp; T<sub>A</sub> = line tangent to circumcircle at A;<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp; L<sub>AB</sub> = line through B parallel to AG;<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp; L<sub>AC</sub> = line through C parallel to AG;<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp; A<sub>2</sub> = T<sub>A</sub>&cap;L<sub>AB</sub>;<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp; A<sub>3</sub> = T<sub>A</sub>&cap;L<sub>AC</sub>;<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp; A<sub>b</sub> = DA<sub>2</sub>&cap;AG;<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp; A<sub>c</sub> = DA<sub>3</sub>&cap;AG;<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp; A' = {A<sub>b</sub>,A<sub>Cb</sub>}-harmonic conjugate of A, and define B' and C' cyclically.<br>
The finite fixed point of the affine transformation that carries ABC onto A'B'C' is X(8041). See
<a href="X(8041).png">X(8041)</a>. (Angel Montesdeoca, February 23, 2021.)
</p>
