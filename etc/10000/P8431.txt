<p>
Centers X(8431)-X(8533) are contributed by Peter Moses, November 4, 2015.  They lie on the Neuberg cubic, K001 in <a href=" https://bernard-gibert.pagesperso-orange.fr/Tables/table19.html">Bernard Gibert's <b>Cubics in the Triangle Plane</b></a>.  Each of these centers is the perspector of two triangles from a list of 25 triangles found in <a href="https://bernard-gibert.pagesperso-orange.fr/Tables/table19.html"><b>Table 19.1</b></a>.  That list is reproduced  in the following table, in which the isogonal conjugate of a point P is denoted by P*.  Triangles T5 and T6 are named in TCCT, p. 178.

<p>
Suppose that P and Q are points.  The cevian triangle of P is perspective to the anticevian triangle of Q.  If the perspector is written as &theta;<sub>P</sub>(Q) then &theta;<sub>P</sub>(&theta;<sub>P</sub>(Q)) = Q, so that the mapping &theta;<sub>P</sub> is a conjugacy.  The term <b>Ceva conjugate</b> appeared as early as 1998 in TCCT (p. 57-58); the term <b>cevian quotient</b> appeared in Hyacinthos in 2000.  See Francisco Javier Garcia Capit√°n, "Some Simple Results on Cevian Quotients", <i>Forum Geometricorum</i> 13 (2013) 227-231:   <a href="http://forumgeom.fau.edu/FG2013volume13/FG201324index.html"><b>Abstract</b> and access to a pdf</a>
</p>
