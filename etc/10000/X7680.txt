<p>
Let P be a point in the plane of a triangle ABC, and let A'B'C' be the circumcevian triangle of P.  Let A<sub>B</sub> be the reflection of A' is line AC, and define B<sub>C</sub> and C<sub>A</sub> cyclically.  Let A<sub>C</sub> be the reflection of A' is line AB, and define B<sub>A</sub> and C<sub>B</sub> cyclically.  Let M<sub>A</sub> be the midpoint of segment A<sub>B</sub>A<sub>C</sub>, and define M<sub>B</sub> and M<sub>C</sub> cyclically.  (The circumcircle of M<sub>A</sub>M<sub>B</sub>M<sub>C</sub> passes through X(4).)  Antreas Hatzipolakis calls attention to the circumcenter of M<sub>A</sub>M<sub>B</sub>M<sub>C</sub>, here named the <b>Hatzipolakis-Moses image</b> of P and denoted by HM(P).  If barycentrics for P are given by P = p : q : r, then
<p></p>
HM(P) = -2a<sup>2</sup>(b<sup>4</sup> + c<sup>4</sup> - a<sup>2</sup>b<sup>2</sup> - a<sup>2</sup>c<sup>2</sup> - 2b<sup>2</sup>c<sup>2</sup>)qr + b<sup>2</sup>(a<sup>2</sup> - b<sup>2</sup> + c<sup>2</sup>)(a<sup>2</sup> + b<sup>2</sup> - c<sup>2</sup>)pr + c<sup>2</sup>(a<sup>2</sup> - b<sup>2</sup> + c<sup>2</sup>)(a<sup>2</sup> + b<sup>2</sup> - c<sup>2</sup>)pq : :
