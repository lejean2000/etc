<p class="preamble-title">
Centers associated with extra-triangles:  X(7001)-X(7373)
</p>
<img src="rightri.svg" style="height:30px" alt="rightri">
</header>

<div class="preamble-sect">

<p class="textcenter">
This preamble and centers X(7001)-X(7373) were contributed by Richard Hilton, March 19, 2015.
</p>
<p>
Suppose that f(a,b,c) is a polynomial and that a triangle center X has barycentric coordinates f(a,b,c) : f(b,c,a) : f(c,a,b).  Then the A-extraversion of X is obtained by replacing a by -a in all three of its barycentric coordinates.  Likewise, the B-extraversion is obtained by the substitution b &#8594; -b, and the C-extraversion by c &#8594; -c.  The three extraversions are the vertices of a central triangle which we shall call the <b>extra-triangle</b> of X.  In the notation introduced in the preamble to X(3758), the extra-triangle of X is T(f(-a,b,c), f(b,c,-a)).
</p>
<p>
For centers that have no such polynomial representation, the three extraversions are defined "by construction".  Examples are centers based on the Morley triangles or the Malfatti circles, as, in both cases, figures can readily be constructed in the A-, B-, C- exterior regions of the triangle, following the same procedures as for the interior figures.  Since every center can be represented by barycentric coordinates in terms of A, B, C, the following <b>definition</b> is adopted here:
</p>
<p>
If the barycentric coordinates if triangle center X are f(A,B,C) : f(B,C,A) : f(C,A,B) then the coordinates of the A-extraversion of X are obtained by the substitutions A&#8594; -A, B &#8594; &pi; - B, C&#8594; &pi; - C.  Coordinates for the B- and C-extraversions are obtained by the corresponding cyclic substitutions.
</p>
<p>
Clearly, each of the substitutions a &#8594; -a, b &#8594; -b, c &#8594; -c yields &Delta; &#8594; -&Delta;, S &#8594; -S, and cot(&omega;) &#8594; -cot(&omega;).  Stipulating that R remains positive, then the substitutions a &#8594; -a, b &#8594; -b, c &#8594; -c, as in the first paragraph, give the three extraversions, and, equivalently, these subsitutions:  R &#8594; -R, a &#8594;, b &#8594; -b, c &#8594; -c.  The results of these substitutions on expressions involving the inradius, r, as well as exressions in Conway notation, follow from those just stated.
</p>
<p>
Centers with barycentric coordinates involving square roots of non-numeric expressions, and centers for which the transformations &omega; &#8594; - &omega; and &omega; &#8594; &pi; - &omega; lead to undesirable results, are excluded from consideration here.
</p>
<p>
If a pair of points (such as the Fermat points) have barycentric coordinates of the form f(a<sup>2</sup>,b<sup>2</sup>,c<sup>2</sup>,S) and f(a<sup>2</sup>,b<sup>2</sup>,c<sup>2</sup>,-S), they may be regarded as intermediate between strong and weak points, in that the extraversions of one of the pair coincide at the other.  A geometric interpretation is that for points on the Kiepert hyperbola, outward rotations from the edges of the reference triangle are equivalent to inward rotations from one of the exterior edges.
</p>
<p>
The focus here is on centers for which the extra-triangle is perspective with ABC.
</p>
<p>
Assuming that the barycentric coordinates of a center X are f(A,B,C) : f(B,C,A) : f(C,A,B), where f(A,C,B) = k*f(A,B,C) for k = Â±1, the extra-triangle of X is perspective to ABC if f(&pi; - A, &pi; - B, -C) can be expressed as u(A,B,C)*v(B,C,A) where u(A,C,B) = k*u(A,B,C) and v(A,C,B) = v(A,B,C).  The first barycentric coordinate of the perspector is then u(A,B,C) / v(A,B,C).
</p>
<p>
