Centers of apedal conics
</p>
<img src="rightri.svg" style="height:30px" alt="rightri">
</header>
<div class="preamble-sect">


<p>
This section was contributed by Clark Kimberling and Peter Moses, October 19, 2015.
</p>
<p>
Suppose that ABC is a triangle and P is a point not on a sideline of ABC.  Let P' denote the isogonal conjugate of P.  The vertices of the antipedal triangles of P and P' lie on a conic, here denoted by apedal(P).  The following table shows examples of P, P', and the center of apedal(P').
</p>

<table>
<tr>     <th>P</th>         <th>P'</th>        <th>center of apedal(P)</th>   </tr>
<tr>     <td>X(2)</td>    <td>X(6)</td>    <td>X(8268)</td>                   </tr>
<tr>     <td>X(3)</td>    <td>X(4)</td>    <td>X(4577)</td>                   </tr>
<tr>     <td>X(40)</td>  <td>X(80)</td>  <td>X(8269)</td>                   </tr>
</table>

<p>
Conjecture:  For every ABC and P, the conic apedal(P) is a hyperbola.  If you have The Geometer's Sketchpad, you can view <a href="Apedal.gsp"><b>an interactive sketch</b></a> supporting the conjecture.
</p>
