Let A'B'C' be the circumorthic triangle, and let A'' = {B,C}-reciprocal conjugate of A, with respect to A'B'C', and define B'' and C'' cyclically.  The lines AA'', BB'', CC'' concur in X(8795).  (Randy Hutson, December 26, 2015)  Here, the term "{X,Y}-reciprocal conjugate" designates the reciprocal conjugate that swaps X and Y, where X and Y are not on sidelines of ABC and not collinear with A or B or C.  Reciprocal conjugation is introduced in Keith Dean and Floor van Lamoen, 'Geometric Construction of Reciprocal Conjugations," <i>Forum Geometricorm</i> 1 (2001) 115-120.  Specifically,  if P = p : q : r, U = u : v : w, X = x : y: z are trilinears for points, none on a sideline of ABC, then the {P,U}-reciprocal conjugate of X is the point pu/x : qv/y : rw/z.  See "reciprocal conjugate" in <a href="glossary.html">Glossary</a>.
