<p>
Danneels points are introduced at X(3078).  The definition is restated here for a point U = u : v : w (barycentrics):  D(U) =  u<sup>2</sup>(v + w) : v<sup>2</sup>(w + u) : w<sup>2</sup>(u + v).
</p>
<p>
It is proved at X(3078) that if U is on the Euler line of a triangle ABC, then D(U) is also on the Euler line. Also,<br>
&bull; D(Steiner circumellipse) = X(2)<br>
&bull; D(P) is on the line P-to-X(2) for every point P<br>
&bull; D(line at infinity) = X(2)-of-T(X), where T(X) is the cevian triangle of X<br>
&bull; D(X) = X-Ceva conjugate of (X-crosspoint of X(2) (Peter Moses, July 29, 2015)
</p>
