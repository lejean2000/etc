<p>
Let ABC be a triangle, and suppose that C' is a point on side AB and that B' is a point on side AC.  Let r(B'C') be the rectangle whose vertices are B', C', and the orthogonal projections of B' and C' onto side BC.  Let  R<sub>A</sub> be the rectangle r(B'C') of maximal area,

which is obtained by taking A'B'C' to be the medial triangle of ABC.  Let O<sub>A</sub> be the center R<sub>A</sub>, and define O<sub>B</sub> and O<sub>C</sub> cyclically.  The central triangle O<sub>A</sub>O<sub>B</sub>O<sub>C</sub> is here named the

<b>submedial triangle</b>; c.f. the Calabi triangle (https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Calabi_triangle).
</p>
<p>
The A-vertex of O<sub>A</sub>O<sub>B</sub>O<sub>C</sub> is given by
</p>
<p>
 O<sub>A</sub> = 2*a*b*c : (3*a^2+b^2-c^2)*c : (3*a^2-b^2+c^2)*b (trilinears)
</p>
<p>
The vertices of the submedial triangle lie on the cubic K281, and
</p>
<p>
X(6688) = X(2)-of-O<sub>A</sub>O<sub>B</sub>O<sub>C</sub> <br>
X(3628) = X(3)-of-O<sub>A</sub>O<sub>B</sub>O<sub>C</sub> <br>
X(5462) = X(4)-of-O<sub>A</sub>O<sub>B</sub>O<sub>C</sub> <br>
</p>
<p>
The circumcircle of O<sub>A</sub>O<sub>B</sub>O<sub>C</sub> passes through these points:   X(6667), X(6721), X(6722), X(6723), and the X(5)  is the radical center of the circumcircles of the rectangles R<sub>A</sub>, R<sub>B</sub>, R<sub>C</sub>.
</p>

<p>
The triangle O<sub>A</sub>O<sub>B</sub>O<sub>C</sub> is parallelogic to the 1st and 2nd Parry triangles with centers X(5) and X(351) in both cases.
</p>
<p>
The locus of P such that O<sub>A</sub>O<sub>B</sub>O<sub>C</sub> and the cevian-triangle-of P are perspective is the circumcubic given by
</p>
<p>
&Sum;[SA*(2*SA+SW)*((2*SB+SC)*y-(2*SC+SB)*z)*x^2] + 2*(SB-SC)*(SC-SA)*(SA-SB)*x*y*z = 0 (barycentrics).
</p>
<p>
The curve passes through these points:  X(2), X(4), X(5395).
</p>
<p>
The locus of P such that O<sub>A</sub>O<sub>B</sub>O<sub>C</sub> and the anticevian-triangle-of P are perspective is the circumcubic  given by
</p>
<p>
&Sum;[a^2*SA*((SA+3*SB-SC)*y-(SA+3*SC-SB)*z)*y*z] + 2*(SB-SC)*(SC-SA)*(SA-SB)*x*y*z = 0 (barycentrics)
</p>
<p>
The curve passes through these points:  X(3), X(6), X(69), X(193), X(1351), X(2996).
</p>
