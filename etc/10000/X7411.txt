<p>
Let A<sub>B</sub>, A<sub>C</sub>, B<sub>C</sub>, B<sub>A</sub>, C<sub>A</sub>, C<sub>B</sub> be as
in the construction of the Conway circle; see http://mathworld.wolfram.com/ConwayCircle.html.  Let A' = B<sub>C</sub>B<sub>A</sub>&cap; C<sub>A</sub>C<sub>B</sub>, and define B' and C' cyclically.  Triangle A'B'C,' here named the Conway triangle, is the anticomplement of the 2nd extouch triangle.  Also, the Conway triangle is perspective to ABC, homothetic to the intouch triangle at X(7), and homothetic to the 1st circumperp triangle at X(7411).  (Randy Hutson, April 11, 2015)
