<h3 id="X7611">X(7611)&nbsp;=&nbsp; 1st HUTSON-McCAY POINT</h3>
Trilinears &nbsp;&nbsp; 2 a^4 (b + c) - a^3 b c - a^2 (b + c) (3 b^2 - b c + 3 c^2) - a b c (b^2 + c^2) + (b - c)^2 (b + c) (b^2 + 3 b c + c^2) : :
<br>
X(7611) = M<sub>A</sub>/R<sub>A</sub> + M<sub>B</sub>/R<sub>B</sub> + M<sub>A</sub>/R<sub>C</sub>, where R<sub>A</sub> = radius of A-McCay circle, and R<sub>B</sub> and R<sub>C</sub> are defined cyclically
<p></p>
Let M<sub>A</sub>M<sub>B</sub>M<sub>C</sub> be the McCay triangle, defined at X(7606).  Let A' be the insimilicenter of the B- and C- McCay circles, and define B' and C' cyclically.  The triangles A'B'C' and M<sub>A</sub>M<sub>B</sub>M<sub>C</sub> are perspective, and X(7611) is their perspector.  The excimilicenters of pairs of McCay circles lie on the sidelines of the excentral triangle.
<p></p>
See X(7606) and <a href="http://www.adgeom.epizy.com/message.php?msg=2541">ADGEOM #2541, Randy Hutson, May 19, 2015</a>.
<p></p>
X(7611) lies on these lines:  {1,576}, {2,2783}, {37,517}, {1001,2801}, {1284,5902}
<p></p>
X(7611) = {X(i),X(j)}-harmonic conjugate of X(k) for these (i,j,k):  (1,7609,576), (2,183,599), (2,1992,3815)
