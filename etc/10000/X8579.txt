<h3 id="X8579">X(8579)&nbsp;=&nbsp;COEFFICIENT POINT OF THE YIU CIRCLE</h3>
Trilinears &nbsp;&nbsp; (2*(cos(A)+cos(3*(B-C)))*cos(A)+cos(2*(B-C))-1/2)*sin(A) : :
<br>
<br><br>




&nbsp;&nbsp;<b>Perspectors associated with the Atik triangle:  X(8580)-X(8583)</b>
&nbsp;&nbsp;
<br>
<p>
This section is contributed by Clark Kimberling and Peter Moses, November 22, 2015.
</p>
<p>
Suppose that V is a point outside a circle (U,u).  Let (V,v) be the circle with center V that is orthogonal to (U,u), so that v<sup>2</sup> = |UV|<sup>2</sup> - u<sup>2</sup> = power of V with respect to (U,u).  Let U<sub>V,A</sub> be the circle (V,w) obtained from (U,r) = A-excircle and V = incenter, and let L<sub>A</sub> be the radical axis of U<sub>V,A</sub> and the A-excircle.  Define L<sub>B</sub> and L<sub>C</sub> cyclically.  Let A' = L<sub>B</sub>&cap;L<sub>C</sub>, B' = L<sub>C</sub>&cap;L<sub>A</sub>, C' = L<sub>A</sub>&cap;L<sub>B</sub>.  The triangle A'B'C' is here named the Atik triangle (Atik being the name of a star).  The Atik triangle is perspective to ABC at X(3062).
</p>
<p>
If you have The Geometer's Sketchpad, you can view <a href="X(3062).gsp"><b>X(3062) and the Atik triangle</b></a>.
</p>
<p>
The A-vertex of the Atik triangle is given by these barycentrics: <br>
a^2 b-2 a b^2+b^3+a^2 c+3 b^2 c-2 a c^2+3 b c^2+c^3 : -b (a^2-2 a b+b^2+2 a c+2 b c-3 c^2) : -c (a^2+2 a b-3 b^2-2 a c+2 b c+c^2)
</p>
<p>
Radius-squared of U<sub>V,A</sub>: &nbsp;&nbsp;w<sup>2</sup> = 4arR/s<sub>A</sub> - r<sup>2</sup><sub>A</sub> <br>
Power of A with respect to U<sub>V,A</sub>: &nbsp;&nbsp;(-a<sup>2</sup>  - b<sup>2</sup> - c<sup>2</sup> - 2ab - 2ac + 6bc)/4
<br>
Power of B with respect to U<sub>V,A</sub>: &nbsp;&nbsp;- (a + b - c )<sup>2</sup>/4<br>
Power of C with respect to U<sub>V,A</sub>: &nbsp;&nbsp;- (a + b - c )<sup>2</sup>/4
<p></p>
The Atik triangle is perpsective to the following triangles, with perspector X(8):  Fuhrmann, anticomplementary, outer Garcia.

<p>
Another construction of the Atik triangle, found near the end of the discussion at X(5927), is as follows.  Let P<sub>A</sub> be the polar of the incenter with respect to the A-excircle, and define P<sub>B</sub> and P<sub>C</sub> cyclically.  Then A' = P<sub>B</sub>&cap;P<sub>C</sub>, B' = P<sub>C</sub>&cap;P<sub>A</sub>, C' = P<sub>A</sub>&cap;P<sub>B</sub>.  The triangle A'B'C' is the Atik triangle, and its centroid is X(5927).  (Randy Hutson, July 11, 2014)
</p>
<p>
The inverse-in-incircle triangle defined at X(5571) is homothetic to the Atik triangle.  (Randy Hutson, November 24, 2015).
</p>
