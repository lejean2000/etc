<p>
The A-Artzt parabola of a triangle ABC is the parabola tangent at B and C to the sidelines AB and AC, respectively.  See William Gallatly, <i>The Modern Geometry of the Triangle,</i> 2nd ed. (London: Hodgson, 1913), p. 42.
</p>
<p>
The A-Artzt parabola has trilinear equation a<sup>2</sup>x<sup>2</sup> - 4bcyvz = 0.  Its axis is the line a(b<sup>2</sup> - c<sup>2</sup>)x + b(a<sup>2</sup> - b<sup>2</sup> - 3c<sup>2</sup>)y - (a<sup>2</sup> - 3b<sup>2</sup> - c<sup>2</sup>)cz = 0, and its directrix is the line (a<sup>2</sup> - b<sup>2</sup> - c<sup>2</sup>)ax + 2bc<sup>2</sup>y + 2b<sup>2</sup>cz = 0.
</p>
<p>
The B- and C-Artzt parabolas are defined cyclically.  The foci of the three parabolas are the vertices of the 2nd Brocard triangle.
</p>
<p>
The trilinear poles of the directrices of the Artzt parabolas lie on the cubics K675 and K676.
</p>
<p>
The triangle A'B'C' bounded by the directrices of the Artzt parabolas is here named the <b>Artzt triangle</b>.  Coordinates for A' follow:
</p>
<p>
A' = -(3a<sup>4</sup> + (b<sup>2</sup> - c<sup>2</sup>)<sup>2</sup>)/(2a) : (b<sup>2</sup>(c<sup>2</sup> + 2a<sup>2</sup>) + (a<sup>2</sup> - c<sup>2</sup>)c<sup>2</sup>)/b : (c<sup>2</sup>(b<sup>2</sup> + 2a<sup>2</sup>) + (a<sup>2</sup> - b<sup>2</sup>)b<sup>2</sup>)/c  (trilinears)
</p>
<p>
A' = SB*SC-(SB+SC)^2 : S^2+SC*SW : S^2+SB*SW (barycentrics)
</p>
<p>
Moreover, |B'C'| =SW*sqrt(3*SA+SW)/(3*S), and area(A'B'C') = SW^2/(6*S).
</p>

<p>
The Artzt triangle A'B'C' is directly similar to the circumsymmedial triangle and inversely similar to the 4th Brocard triangle, with center of inverse similitude X(9745). Also, A'B'C' is perspective to the 1st and 2nd Sharygin triangles, as well as all the Lucas(&plusmn;1) triangles, except these two: the antipodal and homothetic Lucas(&plusmn;1) triangles.
</p>
<p>
The locus of P such that the cevian triangle of P and A'B'C' are perspective is the cubic K677, and the locus of perspector is the cubic pK(X7735,X9756).
</p>
<p>
The locus of P such that the anticevian triangle of P and A'B'C' are perspective is the circumcubic pK(X7735,X6), which has barycentric equation &Sum;[ (SW SA+S^2) (SB y^2-SC z^2) x ]=0; this cubic passes through A,B,C, the vertices of the symmedial triangle and X(i) for these i:  2, 4, 6, 194, 262, 3168, 6776, 7735, 9307.
</p>
<p>
The locus of P such that the circumcevian triangle of P and A'B'C' are perspective is the quartic with trilinear equation &Sum;[ a*SA*(b*(SB*SW+S^2)*v-(SC*SW+S^2)*c*w)*u^3 ]=0; this quartic passes through A,B,C and X(i) for these i:  6, 262, 523, 1344, 1345, 5000, 5001.
</p>

</p>
<p>
An alternate construction for the Artzt triangle, A'B'C' follows.  Let Pa be the parabola with focus A and directrix BC.  Let La be the polar of X(3) wrt Pa, and define Lb and Lc cyclically.  Then A' = Lb&cap;Lc, B' = Lc&cap;La, C' = La&cap;Lb.  Also, the Artzt triangle is the 1st anti-Brocard triangle of the McCay triangle.  (Randy Hutson, April 9, 2016)
</p>
