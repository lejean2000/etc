<p class="preamble-title">
Gibert circumtangential conjugates:  X(8616)-X(8665)
</p>
<img src="rightri.svg" style="height:30px" alt="rightri">
</header>
<div class="preamble-sect">

<p>
This section is contributed by Peter Moses, November 26, 2015, based on notes from Bernard Gibert, November 2, 2015, in connection with the cubic <a href="https://bernard-gibert.pagesperso-orange.fr/Exemples/k024.html"><b>K024</b></a>.
</p>

<p>
The Gibert circumtangential conjugate of a point U = u : v : w (barycentrics) is defined as the point
</p>

<p>
a^2 (a^2 b^2 c^2 u v - b^2 c^4 u v + a^4 c^2 v^2 - a^2 c^4 v^2 + a^2 b^2 c^2 u w - b^4 c^2 u w - 3 a^2 b^2 c^2 v w + a^4 b^2 w^2 - a^2 b^4 w^2) : :
</p>


<p>
A related point is the circumtangential-isogonal conjugate of U, defined by
</p>

<p>
a^2 (a^2 c^2 u v - c^4 u v + a^2 c^2 v^2 - c^4 v^2 + a^2 b^2 u w - b^4 u w + a^4 v w - a^2 b^2 v w - a^2 c^2 v w - 2 b^2 c^2 v w + a^2 b^2 w^2 - b^4 w^2) : :
</p>

<p>
If U is on the circumcircle, then its Gibert circumtangential conjugate, denoted by M<sub>1</sub>(U), is on the Lemoine axis, X(187)X(237), and the circumtangential-isogonal conjugate, denoted by M<sub>2</sub>(U), is on the line at infinity, X(30)X(511).  Thus, if U is on X(187)X(237), then M<sub>1</sub>(U) is on the circumcircle, and if U is on X(30)X(511), then M<sub>2</sub>(U) is on the circumcircle.  For a selection of circumtangential-isogonal conjugates, see X(8666)-X(8714).
</p>
<p>
The appearance of (i,j) in the following list means that the Gibert circumtangential conjugate of X(i) is X(j):
</p>
<p>
(2,3), (74,1495), (98,237), (99,669), (100,667), (101,649), (105,2223), (106,902), (108,1946), (109,663), (110,512), (111,187), (112,647), (351,691), (665,919), (699,3229), (727,3009), (729,3231), (739,3230), (741,3747), (759,3724), (785,2978), (805,5027), (825,3250), (827,3005), (842,5191), (843,2502), (890,898), (901,1960), (907,3804), (1055,2291), (1291,6140), (1379,5638), (1380,5639), (1995,8182), (2701,5075), (2702,5029), (2703,5040), (2712,5168), (2715,3569), (4588,4775), (5092,5888), (5106,5970), (5994,6138), (5995,6137), (6287,6636)
</p>
<p>
Let U* denote the isogonal conjugate of a point U not on a sideline of ABC.  The points U*, M<sub>1</sub>(U), M<sub>2</sub>(U) are collinear.  If U lies on the cubic K024, then M<sub>1</sub>(U) = U*; if U is on the circumcircle or at infinity, then M<sub>2</sub>(U) = U*.  If U is on the circumcircle or the Lemoine axis, then M<sub>1</sub>(U) = X(32)/U (barycentric quotient),  The barycentric product U*M<sub>1</sub>(U) lies on the Brocard axis.  (Bernard Gibert, November 27, 2015)
</p>
