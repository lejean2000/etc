<h3 id="X7670">X(7670)&nbsp;=&nbsp; X(1)-OF-HONSBERGER TRIANGLE</h3>
Barycentrics &nbsp;&nbsp; a (a+b-c) (a-b+c) (b c (-a+b+c) Sin[A/2]+c (a^2+c (-b+c)-a (b+2 c)) Sin[B/2]+b (a^2+b (b-c)-a (2 b+c)) Sin[C/2]) : :
<p></p>
Let DEF be the cevian triangle of X(7); i.e., DEF = intouch triangle.  Let L<sub>A</sub> be the line through X(7) parallel to EF, and let A<sub>B</sub> = AB&cap;L<sub>A</sub> and A<sub>C</sub> = AC&cap;L<sub>A</sub>.  Define B<sub>C</sub> and C<sub>A</sub> cyclically, and define B<sub>A</sub> and C<sub>B</sub> cyclically.  (The six points A<sub>B</sub>,  B<sub>C</sub>, C<sub>A</sub>, A<sub>C</sub>,  B<sub>A</sub>, C<sub>B</sub> lie on the Adams circle.)  Let H<sub>A</sub> = A<sub>B</sub>C<sub>B</sub>&cap;A<sub>C</sub>B<sub>C</sub>, and define H<sub>B</sub> and H<sub>C</sub> cyclically.  The triangle Let H<sub>A</sub>H<sub>B</sub>H<sub>C</sub> is here named the <b>Honsberger triangle</b>, after the triangle labeled XYZ on page 98 of Ross Honsberger's <i>Episodes in Nineteenth and Twentieth Century Euclidean Geometry</i> (Mathematical Association of America, 1995).  Peter Moses found barycentrics for the A-vertex:
<p></p>
H<sub>A</sub> = a(a - b + c)(a + b - c) : (a + b - c)[(b - c)<sup>2</sup> - a(b + c)] : (a - b + c)[(b - c)<sup>2</sup> - a(b + c)]

The Honsberger triangle is perspective to the following triangles, with perspectors as indicated:  anticomplementary triangle, X(7674); hexyl, X(7675); 1st circumperp, X(7676), 2nd circumperp, X(7677); 3rd Euler, X(7678), 4th Euler, X(7679).  Indeed, the Honsberger triangle is homothetic to each of those triangles except the anticomplementary; it is also homothetic to the intouch triangle at X(7) and the excentral triangle at X(1445).
