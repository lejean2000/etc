<p>
Suppose that P = p : q : r and U = u : v : w (barycentrics) are distinct points on the line L at infinity; that is, p + q + r = 0 and u + v + w = 0.  As in the definition of orthopoint (copied below from Glossary), P may be regarded as a "direction" in the plane of the reference triangle ABC, and all the (parallel) lines in this direction meet in P, and likewise for U.  Let L(P) be any line that meets L in P, and let L(U) be any line that meets L in U.  Let W<sub>1</sub> and W<sub>2</sub> be the angle bisectors of the angles between L(P) and L(U) at L(P)&cap;L(U).  The midpoints of P and U are here defined as W<sub>1</sub>&cap;L and W<sub>2</sub>&cap;L.  Since L(P) and L(U) are perpendicular lines, the two midpoints are a pair of orthopoints, defined in the Glossary of ETC.
</p>

<p>
Let D(a,b,c) = [b<sup>2</sup>r<sup>2</sup> + c<sup>2</sup>q<sup>2</sup> + (b<sup>2</sup> + c<sup>2</sup> - a<sup>2</sup>)qr]<sup>1/2</sup>[b<sup>2</sup>w<sup>2</sup> + c<sup>2</sup>v<sup>2</sup> + (b<sup>2</sup> + c<sup>2</sup> - a<sup>2</sup>)vw]<sup>1/2</sup>.
</p>
<p>
Then the two midpoints are f(a,b,c) : f(b,c,a) : f(c,a,b) and g(a,b,c) : g(b,c,a) : g(c,a,b), where
</p>
<p>
f(a,b,c) = u[b<sup>2</sup>r<sup>2</sup> + c<sup>2</sup>q<sup>2</sup> + (b<sup>2</sup> + c<sup>2</sup> - a<sup>2</sup>)qr] + pD(a,b,c)
</p>
<p>
g(a,b,c) = u[b<sup>2</sup>r<sup>2</sup> + c<sup>2</sup>q<sup>2</sup> + (b<sup>2</sup> + c<sup>2</sup> - a<sup>2</sup>)qr] - pD(a,b,c)
</p>

<p>
Let P' = isogonal conjugate of P, and U' = isogonal conjugate of U.  Then P' and U' lie on the circumcircle of ABC.  Let M be the midpoint of P' and U', and assume that M is not the circumcenter, O.  Then the line OM intersects the circumcircle in two points, given by the combos M + (|OM|/R - 1)*O and M + ( - |OM|/R - 1)*O.  The isogonal conjugates of these two points are the midpoints of P and U.
</p>
