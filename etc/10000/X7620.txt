<h3 id="X7620">X(7620)&nbsp;=&nbsp; X(20)-OF-McCAY-TRIANGLE</h3>
Barycentrics &nbsp;&nbsp; 5 a^4-2 a^2 b^2-7 b^4-2 a^2 c^2+22 b^2 c^2-7 c^4 : :
<p></p>
See X(7617).
<p></p>
Let G<sub>A</sub> be the antipode of X(2) in the A-McCay circle, and define G<sub>B</sub> and G<sub>C</sub> cyclically; then G<sub>A</sub>G<sub>B</sub>G<sub>C</sub> is homothetic to the McCay triangle at X(2).           Let L<sub>A</sub> be the line tangent to the A-McCay circle at G<sub>A</sub>, and define L<sub>B</sub> and L<sub>C</sub> cyclically.  Let A" = L<sub>B</sub>&cap;L<sub>C</sub>, and define B" and C" cyclically.  Then          A"B"C" is the antipedal triangle of X(2) with respect to G<sub>A</sub>G<sub>B</sub>G<sub>C</sub>, and A"B"C" is inversely similar to ABC and homothetic to the 1st Brocard triangle.  Also, X(7620) = X(2)-of-A"B"C" = X(376)-of G<sub>A</sub>G<sub>B</sub>G<sub>C</sub>.  (Randy Hutson, May 27, 2015)
