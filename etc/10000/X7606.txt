<h3 id="X7606">X(7606)&nbsp;=&nbsp; X(182)-OF-McCAY TRIANGLE</h3>
Barycentrics &nbsp;&nbsp; 5a^6 + 2b^6 + 2c^6 - 6a^4(b^2 + c^2) - 18a^2b^2c^2 - 3b^4c^2 - 3b^2c^4 : :
<p></p>
The McCay triangle, here denoted by M, is the triangle whose vertices are the centers of the McCay circles.  Along with properties of M given at X(7606)-X(7615) are the following:  M is similar to the 4th Brocard triangle with X(111) as center of similitude.  The circumcircle of M is orthogonal to the Parry circle.  The inverse-in-circumcircle-of-M of X(2) is X(111).

Let L<sub>A</sub> be the radical axis of the A-McCay circle and the A-Neuberg circle, and define L<sub>B</sub> and L<sub>C</sub> cyclically.  Let A' = L<sub>B</sub>&cap;L<sub>C</sub>, and define B' and C' cyclically.  Then A'B'C' is the reflection of ABC in X(6).  Let T<sub>A</sub> be the radical trace of the A-McCay circle and the A-Neuberg circle, and define T<sub>B</sub> and T<sub>C</sub> cyclically.  The triangle T<sub>A</sub>T<sub>B</sub>T<sub>C</sub> is perspective to ABC at X(2996).
See <a href="http://www.adgeom.epizy.com/message.php?msg=2541">ADGEOM #2541, Randy Hutson, May 19, 2015</a>.

The A-vertex of the McCay triangle is given by barycentrics
-a^2 (a^2+b^2+c^2) : 2 a^4-2 a^2 b^2+2 b^4-3 a^2 c^2-3 b^2 c^2+c^4 : 2 a^4-3 a^2 b^2+b^4-2 a^2 c^2-3 b^2 c^2+2 c^4; moreover, (power of A in A-McCay circle) =  (b<sup>2</sup> + c<sup>2</sup> - a<sup>2</sup>)/3, (power of B in A-McCay circle) =  a<sup>2</sup>/3, (power of C in A-McCay circle) =  a<sup>2</sup>/3.  (Peter Moses, May 23, 2015)
