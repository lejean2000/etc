Centers associated with Van Lamoen circles:  X(8176)-X(8182)

<p>
Let A&prime;B&prime;C&prime; be the medial triangle of ABC, and let G = X(2), the centroid of ABC (and of A'B'C').  It is well known that the circumcenters of triangles GBC', GCA', GAB', GCB', GAC', GBA' lie on a circle, called the van Lamoen circle of ABC.  See <a href="http://mathworld.wolfram.com/vanLamoenCircle.html">van Lamoen Circle at MathWorld</a>.
<p></p>
More generally, let U be any triangle perspective to ABC with perspector P such that the six circumcenters all lie on a circle. That circle is introduced here as the U-van Lamoen circle.
<p></p>

The squared radius of the medial-van Lamoen circle is &rho;<sub>1</sub> = 2<sup>-1/2</sup>(S<sup>2</sup><sub>&omega;</sub> + 9S<sup>2</sup>)/[1296S<sup>4</sup>(3S<sub>A</sub> + S<sub>&omega;</sub>)(3S<sub>B</sub> + S<sub>&omega;</sub>)(3S<sub>C</sub> + S<sub>&omega;</sub>)]
<p>
The squared radius of the anticomplementary-van Lamoen circle is sqrt(2)&rho;<sub>1</sub>
</p>
<p>
The squared radius of the 1st Brocard-van Lamoen circle is 2<sup>1/2</sup>&rho; = -1/[64S<sup>2</sup><sub>&omega;</sub>S<sup>4</sup>(S<sup>2</sup><sub>&omega;</sub> + S<sup>2</sup>)]h(A,B,C)h(B,C,A)h(C,A,B), where h(A,B,C) = (S<sub>A</sub> + 3S<sub>&omega;</sub>)S<sup>2</sup> - (3S<sub>A</sub> + S<sub>&omega;</sub>)S<sup>2</sup><sub>&omega;</sub>
</p>
<p>
The squared radius of the 1st anti-Brocard-van Lamoen circle is
[(S<sup>2</sup><sub>&omega;</sub> + 9S<sup>2</sup>)/(5184S<sup>6</sup>(S<sup>2</sup><sub>&omega;</sub> + S<sup>2</sup>)<sup>2</sup>]j(A,B,C)j(B,C,A)j(C,A,B), where j(A,B,C) = (3S<sub>A</sub> + 5S<sub>&omega;</sub>)S<sup>2</sup> + (S<sub>&omega;</sub> - S<sub>A</sub>)S<sup>2</sup><sub>&omega;</sub>
</p>
<p>
The squared radius of the 1st anti-Brocard-van Lamoen circle is
[(S<sup>2</sup><sub>&omega;</sub> + 9S<sup>2</sup>)(81S<sup>4</sup> + 14S<sup>2</sup>S<sup>2</sup><sub>&omega;</sub> + S<sup>4</sup><sub>&omega;</sub>)/(576S<sup>6</sup>(5S<sup>2</sup><sub>&omega;</sub> - 27S<sup>2</sup>)<sup>4</sup>]k(A,B,C)k(B,C,A)k(C,A,B), where k(A,B,C) = 3S<sup>2</sup>(9S<sub>A</sub> - S<sub>&omega;</sub>) + (S<sub>&omega;</sub> - S<sub>A</sub>)S<sup>2</sup><sub>&omega;</sub>
</p>
