Perspectors involving the Ascella triangle:  X(8726)-X(8734)
</p>
<img src="rightri.svg" style="height:30px" alt="rightri">

<div class="preamble-sect">

<p>
This section is contributed by Clark Kimberling and Peter Moses, December 3, 2015.
</p>
<p>
Let A' = incircle-inverse of A, and define B' and C' cyclically.  Let O<sub>A</sub> be the circle {{B,C,B',C'}}, and define O<sub>B</sub> and O<sub>C</sub> cyclically.  The circles O<sub>A</sub>, O<sub>B</sub>, O<sub>C</sub> are orthogonal to the incircle.  Let V<sub>A</sub> be the center of O<sub>A</sub>, and define V<sub>B</sub> and V<sub>C</sub> cyclically.  The triangle V<sub>A</sub>V<sub>B</sub>V<sub>C</sub> is here named the Ascella triangle.  The Ascella triangle is also the mid-triangle of the excentral triangle and the intouch triangle.  Barycentrics are given by
</p>
<p>
V<sub>A</sub> = 2a<sup>2</sup> : a<sup>2</sup> - b<sup>2</sup> + c<sup>2</sup> - 2ab - 2ac : a<sup>2</sup> - c<sup>2</sup> + b<sup>2</sup> - 2ab - 2ac
</p>
