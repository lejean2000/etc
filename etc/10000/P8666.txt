This section is contributed by Peter Moses, November 26, 2015, based on notes from Bernard Gibert, November 2, 2015, in connection with the cubic <a href="https://bernard-gibert.pagesperso-orange.fr/Exemples/k024.html">K024</a>.

<p>
The circumtangential-isogonal conjugate of U, denoted by M<sub>2</sub>(U), is defined by
</p>
<p>
a^2 (a^2 c^2 u v - c^4 u v + a^2 c^2 v^2 - c^4 v^2 + a^2 b^2 u w - b^4 u w + a^4 v w - a^2 b^2 v w - a^2 c^2 v w - 2 b^2 c^2 v w + a^2 b^2 w^2 - b^4 w^2) : :
</p>
<p>
If U is on the circumcircle, then M<sub>2</sub>(U), is on the line at infinity, X(30)X(511), so that if U is on X(30)X(511), then M<sub>2</sub>(U) is on the circumcircle.  Related conjugates are M<sub>1</sub>(U) defined in the preamble to X(8616) and the circumnormal-isogonal conjugate M<sub>3</sub>(U) defined in the preamble to X(8715).
</p>
