<p>
For P on the nine-point circle, let E(P) be the isogonal conjugate of the trilinear pole of the tangent at P.  The locus of E(P) is an ellipse, E, with center X(9306).  Let F = X(11) (the Feuerbach point), and let A'B'C' be the Feuerbach triangle.  Let (O) denote the circumcircle.  Then E&cap;(O) = {F, A', B', C'}.  Also, E(X(11)) = X(101), E(X(125)) = X(1625), and E(X(115)) = X(1634).  (Randy Hutson, February 10, 2016)
</p>

</p>
