There is a unique equilateral triangle AA<sub>1</sub>A<sub>2</sub> inscribed in the circumcircle of triangle ABC, where, for concreteness, the labels are fixed so that AA<sub>1</sub>A<sub>2</sub> has the same orientation as ABC.  Let BB<sub>1</sub>B<sub>2</sub> and CC<sub>1</sub>C<sub>2</sub> be the corresponding equilateral triangles.  Let A' = B<sub>1</sub>B<sub>2</sub>&cap;C<sub>1</sub>C<sub>2</sub>, B' = C<sub>1</sub>C<sub>2</sub>&cap;A<sub>1</sub>A<sub>2</sub> and C' = A<sub>1</sub>A<sub>2</sub>&cap;B<sub>1</sub>B<sub>2</sub>.  The triangle A'B'C' is here named the <b>Trinh triangle</b> of ABC after Trinh Xuân Minh.  César Lozada found perspectivities and orthologies, as follows.

The Trinh triangle is the reflection in X(3) of the Kosnita triangle.  (Randy Hutson, Octover 13, 2015)
