<p class="preamble-title">
Points associated with tangential-midarc triangles:  X(8075) -X(8104)
</p>
<img src="rightri.svg" style="height:30px" alt="rightri">
</header>
<div class="preamble-sect">

<p class="textcenter">
This section was contributed by César Eliud Lozada, August 18, 2015.
</p>
<p>
Let ABC be a triangle with incenter I. Let D' and D'' be the points in which the line AI meets the incircle, where D&prime; is the closer of the two points to A.  Define E' and F' cyclically, and defined E'' and F'' cyclically.  Let A'B'C' be the triangle whose sidelines are the tangents to the incircle at D', E', F'.  The triangle A'B'C' has been called the tangential-midarc triangle of ABC (e.g., <a href="http://mathworld.wolfram.com/TangentialMid-ArcTriangle.html">MathWorld</a>), but here it is the <i>1st tangential-midarc triangle of ABC</i>.  Let A''B''C'' be the triangle whose sidelines are the tangents to the incircle at D'', E'', F''.  Triangle A''B''C'' is here introduced as the <i>2nd tangential-midarc triangle of ABC</i>.
</p>
<p>
Trilinears<br>
A' = -cos(B/2)*cos(C/2) : (cos(A/2)+cos(C/2))*cos(C/2) : (cos(A/2)+cos(B/2))*cos(B/2)<br>
A' = c*sin(B/2)+sin(C/2)*b+(a-s) : -c*sin(B/2)-sin(C/2)*(a-c)+(a-s) : -sin(C/2)*b -sin(B/2)*(a-b)+(a-s)<br>
A'' = -cos(B/2)*cos(C/2) : (-cos(A/2)+cos(C/2))*cos(C/2) : (-cos(A/2)+cos(B/2))*cos(B/2)<br>
A'' = c*sin(B/2)+sin(C/2)*b-(a-s) : -c*sin(B/2)-sin(C/2)*(a-c)-(a-s) : -sin(C/2)*b -sin(B/2)*(a-b)-(a-s)
</p>

<p>
The 1st tangential-midarc and Fuhrmann triangles are parallelogic with centers X(4) and X(8103).<br>
The 2nd tangential-midarc and Fuhrmann triangles are parallelogic with centers X(4) and X(8104).
</p>
<p>
Centers X(8075)-X(8104) occur in pairs with trilinears of the form: f(a,b,c)sin(A/2) + g(a,b,c)sin(B/2) + g(a,c,b)sin(C/2) ± h(a,b,c) : : .
</p>
