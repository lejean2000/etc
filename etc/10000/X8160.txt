<h3 id="X8160">X(8160)&nbsp;=&nbsp; CENTER OF THE OUTER MONTESDEOCA-LEMOINE CIRCLE </h3>
Barycentrics &nbsp;&nbsp; a^2(a^6(b^2+c^2)+2a^4(b^2+c^2)^2-a^2(4b^6+7b^4c^2+7b^2c^4+4c^6)+b^8-3b^6c^2-2b^4c^4-3b^2c^6+c^8 + 4S^3(2a^2-b^2-c^2) csc &omega;) : :
<br>
Barycentrics &nbsp;&nbsp; a^2 (SA + S Cot[&theta;]) : : , where &theta; = ArcTan[-(2 + Cos[w]) Csc[w]]
<br>
Barycentrics &nbsp;&nbsp; a^2 (SA - (S Sin[&omega;]) / (2 + Cos[&omega;])) : :
<b><br>
X(8160) = 3 X[3] - X[1671] = 3 X[1670] + X[1671] = 2 X[1671] - 3 X[8161] = 2 X[1670] + X[8161] = (1 + 2 Sec[&omega;]) X[3] - X[6]

</b>
Let ABC be a triangle, and let A'B'C' be the cevian triangle of X(6).  Let U be the line through A' parallel to AB, and let V be the line through A' parallel to AC.  Let U' = U&cap;AC and V' = V&cap;AB.  The 4 points B, C, U', V' lie on a circle, (O)<sub>A</sub>.  Define (O)<sub>B</sub> and (O)<sub>C</sub> cyclically.  Let M be the circle tangent to (O)<sub>A</sub>, (O)<sub>B</sub>, (O)<sub>C</sub> that encompasses them; call M the <b>outer Montesdeoca-Lemoine circle</b>.  Let M' be the circle tangent to (O)<sub>A</sub>, (O)<sub>B</sub>, (O)<sub>C</sub> that is encompassed by each of  them; call M' the <b>inner Montesdeoca-Lemoine circle</b>.  Then X(8160) is the center of M, and X(8161) is the center of M'.  The contact points of M with (O)<sub>A</sub>, (O)<sub>B</sub>, (O)<sub>C</sub> are the vertices of a triangle perspective to ABC, with perspector X(1343).  Likewise, the contact points of M' with (O)<sub>A</sub>, (O)<sub>B</sub>, (O)<sub>C</sub> are the vertices of a triangle perspective to ABC, with perspector X(1342).  (Based on notes from Angel Montesdeoca, October 2, 2015)

<p>
Properties of the two circles are given by Peter Moses, November 12, 2015: <br>
outer Montesdeoca-Lemoine circle:  radius = (1 + 2 cos &omega;)R/2; A-power = - b<sup>2</sup>c<sup>2</sup>(sin &omega;)/(2S)
<br>
inner Montesdeoca-Lemoine circle:  radius = (-1 + 2 cos &omega;)R/2; A-power =  b<sup>2</sup>c<sup>2</sup>(sin &omega;)/(2S)
</p>
