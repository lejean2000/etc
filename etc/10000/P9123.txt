Parry triangles and associated centers:  X(9123)-X(9214)
</p>
<img src="rightri.svg" style="height:30px" alt="rightri">

<div class="preamble-sect">

<p class="textcenter">
This preamble and centers X(9123)-X(9214) were contributed by Randy Hutson, January 15, 2016.
</p>
<p>
<p>
Let A<sub>1</sub> be the intersection, other than X(111), of the Parry circle and the line AX(111), and define B<sub>1</sub> and C<sub>1</sub> are cyclically.  The triangle A<sub>1</sub>B<sub>1</sub>C<sub>1</sub>, here named the <b>1st Parry triangle</b>, is similar to ABC and orthogonal to ABC, with similitude center X(110).
Let A<sub>2</sub> be the intersection, other than X(110), of the Parry circle and line AX(110), and define B<sub>2</sub> and C<sub>2</sub> cyclically.  The triangle A<sub>2</sub>B<sub>2</sub>C<sub>2</sub>, here named the <b>2nd Parry triangle</b>, is similar to ABC and orthogonal to ABC, with similitude center X(111).
On the Parry circle, A<sub>1</sub>B<sub>1</sub>C<sub>1</sub> and A<sub>2</sub>B<sub>2</sub>C<sub>2</sub> are antipodes.  The Euler line of A<sub>1</sub>B<sub>1</sub>C<sub>1</sub> is the Euler line of A<sub>2</sub>B<sub>2</sub>C<sub>2</sub>, and the Brocard axis of A<sub>1</sub>B<sub>1</sub>C<sub>1</sub> is the Brocard axis of A<sub>2</sub>B<sub>2</sub>C<sub>2</sub>.  The Brocard axis of A<sub>1</sub>B<sub>1</sub>C<sub>1</sub>, and also of A<sub>2</sub>B<sub>2</sub>C<sub>2</sub>, is the Lemoine axis of ABC.
</p>
<p>
Let A<sub>3</sub> be the intersection, other than X(2), of the Parry circle and the A-median, and define B<sub>3</sub> and C<sub>3</sub> cyclically.  The triangle A<sub>3</sub>B<sub>3</sub>C<sub>3</sub>, here named the <b>3rd Parry triangle</b>, is similar to the 4th Brocard triangle, with similitude center the intersection,
other than X(2), of the Parry circle and the orthocentroidal circle.  Also, A<sub>3</sub>B<sub>3</sub>C<sub>3</sub> is the reflection of the circumsymmedial triangle of A<sub>2</sub>B<sub>2</sub>C<sub>2</sub> in the common Brocard axis of A<sub>1</sub>B<sub>1</sub>C<sub>1</sub> (which is also the Brocard axis of A<sub>2</sub>B<sub>2</sub>C<sub>2</sub> and A<sub>3</sub>B<sub>3</sub>C<sub>3</sub>).  The triangles A<sub>2</sub>B<sub>2</sub>C<sub>2</sub> and
 A<sub>3</sub>B<sub>3</sub>C<sub>3</sub> are perspective at X(647).  Let A'B'C' be the reflection of the circumsymmedial triangle in the Brocard axis.  Then A<sub>3</sub>B<sub>3</sub>C<sub>3</sub> is similar to A'B'C', with similitude center X(111).
</p>

<p>
A-vertices of the three Parry triangles are given by Peter Moses (January 17, 2016):
</p>
<p>
A<sub>1</sub> = -3 a^4+2 a^2 b^2-b^4+2 a^2 c^2+b^2 c^2-c^4,b^2 (a^2+b^2-2 c^2),c^2 (a^2-2 b^2+c^2) <br>
A<sub>2</sub> = (b^2-c^2) (a^4-b^4+b^2 c^2-c^4), (a^2-b^2) b^2 (2 a^2-b^2-c^2),c^2 (c^2-a^2) (2 a^2-b^2-c^2) <br>
A<sub>3</sub> = a^2 (a^4-3 a^2 b^2+2 b^4-3 a^2 c^2+b^2 c^2+2 c^4),b^2 c^2 (2 a^2-b^2-c^2),b^2 c^2 (2 a^2-b^2-c^2)
</p>
