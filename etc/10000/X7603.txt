<h3 id="X7603">X(7603)&nbsp;=&nbsp; X(2)-HATZIPOLAKIS-LOZADA HOMOTHETIC CENTER</h3>
Trilinears &nbsp;&nbsp; bc[3a<sup>2</sup>(b<sup>2</sup> + c<sup>2</sup>) - 2(b<sup>2</sup> - c<sup>2</sup>)<sup>2</sup>] : :
<p></p>
Suppose that P is a point in the plane of a triangle ABC.  Let N<sub>A</sub> be the nine-point center of PBC, and define N<sub>B</sub> and N<sub>C</sub> cyclically.  Let N<sub>1</sub> be the nine-point center of NN<sub>B</sub>N<sub>C</sub>, and define N<sub>2</sub> and N<sub>3</sub> cyclically.  The triangle N<sub>1</sub>N<sub>2</sub>N<sub>3</sub> is homothetic to ABC.  Let H(P) denote the center of homothety, here named the P-Hatzipolakis-Lozada homothetic center.  If P = u : v : w, (trilinears), then
<p></p>
H(P) = (a^3*b*c*v*w + c*((2*b^2+c^2)*a^2-(b^2-c^2)^2)*u*w  + b*((2*c^2+b^2)*a^2-(b^2-c^2)^2)*u*v
- a*(a^4-3*(b^2+c^2)*a^2+2*(b^2-c^2)^2)*u^2)/(a*u) : :
<p></p>
The appearance of (i,j) in the following list indicates that H(X(i)) = X(j):  (1,5443), (2,7603), (3,6143), (4,5), (5,7604), (6,7605), (523,476), (1138,3258), (3459,5).
