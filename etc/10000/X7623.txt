<h3 id="X7623">X(7623)&nbsp;=&nbsp; CENTER OF BISECTING CIRCLE OF 3 CIRCLES:  CIRCUMCIRCLE, INCIRCLE, NINE-POINT</h3>
Barycentrics &nbsp;&nbsp; (b-c) (2 a^7+a^6 b-8 a^5 b^2-a^4 b^3+10 a^3 b^4-a^2 b^5-4 a b^6+b^7+a^6 c-8 a^5 b c+13 a^4 b^2 c-13 a^2 b^4 c+8 a b^5 c-b^6 c-8 a^5 c^2+13 a^4 b c^2-8 a^3 b^2 c^2+6 a^2 b^3 c^2+4 a b^4 c^2-3 b^5 c^2-a^4 c^3+6 a^2 b^2 c^3-16 a b^3 c^3+3 b^4 c^3+10 a^3 c^4-13 a^2 b c^4+4 a b^2 c^4+3 b^3 c^4-a^2 c^5+8 a b c^5-3 b^2 c^5-4 a c^6-b c^6+c^7) : :
<p></p>
If U, V, W are circles, there exists a unique circle B = B(U,V,W) which bisects each of the circles U, V, W.  Let P, Q, R be the centers of U,V,W, respectively, let O be the circumcenter of PQR, and let O' be the radical center of U, V, W.  Then B = reflection of O' in O.  For B = X(7623), the radical center O' is X(676).  (Peter Moses, May 26, 2015).  For properties of bisecting circles, see P. H. Daus's article in the <i>American Mathematical Monthly</i> 47 (1940) 519-529:
<a href="http://apollonius.math.nthu.edu.tw/d1/ne01/jyt/linkjstor/4%20circle/bisecting%20circle.pdf
">Bisecting Circles</a>.
<p></p>
Examples:
<p></p>
X(40) = center of B(Soddy circles)<br>
X(382) = center of B(power circles)<br>
X(550) = center of B(Stammler circles)<br>
X(6361) = center of B(Longuet-Higgins circles)<br>
X(5493) = center of B(excentral circles)<br>
X(7615) = center of B(McCay circles)<br>
X(7623) = center of B(circumcircle, incircle, nine-point circle)<br>
X(7624) = center of B(circumcircle, Brocard, nine-point circle)<br>
X(7625) = center of B(circumcircle, Brocard, orthocentroidal circle)<br>
X(7626) = center of B(circumcircle, nine-point circle, Apollonius circle)<br>
X(7627) = center of B(circumcircle, nine-point circle, Bevan circle)<br>
X(8547) = center of B(A-Ehrmann circle, B-Ehrmann circle, C-Ehrmann circle)
