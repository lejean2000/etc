<h3 id="X8054">X(8054)&nbsp;=&nbsp;  COMPLEMENT OF X(8050)</h3>
Barycentrics &nbsp;&nbsp;  a^2/((b-c) (a^2+a b+a c-b c)) : :

<p>
Let A'B'C' be the orthic triangle.  Let La be the Nagel line of AB'C', and define Lb and Lc cyclically.  Let A" = Lb&cap;Lc, B" = Lc&cap;La, C" = La&cap;Lb.  Triangle A"B"C" is inversely similar to ABC, with similitude center X(8054).  See also X(5510).  (Randy Hutson, August 13, 2015)
</p>
<p>
X(8054) lies on the bicevian conic of X(1) and X(2), and also on these lines: {1,3952}, {2,8050}, {11,3141}, {42,1386}, {244,659}, {1015,3124} et al
</p>
