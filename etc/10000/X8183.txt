<h3 id="X8183">X(8183)&nbsp;=&nbsp;MONTESDEOCA DEGENERATE CONICS POINT</h3>
Barycentrics a - a<sup>1/3</sup>b<sup>1/3</sup>c<sup>1/3</sup> : b - a<sup>1/3</sup>b<sup>1/3</sup>c<sup>1/3</sup> : c - a<sup>1/3</sup>b<sup>1/3</sup>c<sup>1/3</sup>
<p></p>
(Contributed by Angel Montesceoca, Ocftober 9, 2015)  Let ABC be a triangle and k a real number.  Let B<sub>A</sub> on line AB and C<sub>A</sub> on line AC be points such that B<sub>A</sub>C<sub>A</sub> is parallel to BC at distance |kr(A)|, where r(A) is the inradius of triangle AB<sub>A</sub>C<sub>A</sub>.  Points C<sub>B</sub>, A<sub>B</sub>, A<sub>C</sub>, B<sub>C</sub> are defined cyclically.  The six points B<sub>A</sub>, C<sub>A</sub>, C<sub>B</sub>, A<sub>B</sub>, A<sub>C</sub>, B<sub>C</sub> lie on a conic, with barycentric equation
<p></p>
0 = cyclic sum of kbc(a + b + c)x<sup>2</sup> - a(a<sup>2</sup> + b<sup>2</sup> + c<sup>2</sup> + 2bc + 2ca + 2ab + bck<sup>2</sup>)yz
<p></p>
The 4 degenerate real conics are given by these values of k: -(a + b + c)/a, -(a + b + c)/b, -(a + b + c)/c, and -(a + b + c)a<sup>-1/3</sup>b<sup>-1/3</sup>c<sup>-1/3</sup>.  The 4 singular points of degenerate conics (i.e., points of intersection of the pairs of lines comprising each degenerate conic) are X(8183) and
<p></p>
bc - a<sup>2</sup> : ba - bc : ca - cb <br>
ab - ac : ac - b<sup>2</sup> : cb - ca <br>
ac - ab : bc - ba : ba - c<sup>2</sup>
<p></p>
These last three points are collinear on the trilinear polar of X(86).
<p></p>
X(8183) lies on the line {1, 2}
