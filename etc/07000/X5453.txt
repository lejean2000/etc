<h3 id="X5453">X(5453)&nbsp;=&nbsp; CENTER OF HATZIPOLAKIS CIRCLE</h3>
Trilinears&nbsp;&nbsp; f(A,B,C) : f(B,C,A) : f(C,A,B), where
f(A,B,C) = 2 + 2 cos A + cos(B - C) + 4 sin(3A/2) cos(B/2 -
C/2)&nbsp;&nbsp; (C&eacute;sar Lozada, April 17, 2013)

<p>Let A&prime;B&prime;C&prime; be the cevian triangle of I (the incenter, X(1)). Let
N<sub>A</sub> be the nine-point center of triangle IB&prime;C&prime;, and define
N<sub>B</sub> and N<sub>C</sub> cyclically. The points I,
N<sub>A</sub>, N<sub>B</sub>, N<sub>C</sub> are concyclic, and their
circle, described by Antreas Hatzipolakis, April 17, 2013.</p>
