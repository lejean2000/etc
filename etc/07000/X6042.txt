<h3 id="X6042">X(6042)&nbsp;=&nbsp; PERSPECTOR OF MONTESDEOCA-HUNG
TRIANGLE AND ABC</h3>
Barycentrics&nbsp;&nbsp; f(a,b,c) : f(b,c,a) :
f(c,a,b), where f(a,b,c) = a(b + c)<sup>2</sup>(b<sup>2</sup> +
c<sup>2</sup> + ab + ac)<sup>2</sup>

<p>Let (Ap) be the Apollonius circle, and let (K<sub>A</sub>),
(K<sub>B</sub>), (K<sub>C</sub>) be the circles described at X(5973) in
association with the Hung-Feuerbach circle at X(5974). Let
L<sub>A</sub> be the radical axis of (Ap) and (K<sub>A</sub>), and
define L<sub>B</sub> and L<sub>C</sub> cyclically. The lines
L<sub>A</sub>, L<sub>B</sub>, (L<sub>C</sub> form a triangle T (here
named the Montesdeoca-Hung triangle) that is perspective to ABC, and
the perspector is X(6042). (Tran Quang Hung, ADGEOM #1506; Angel
Montesdeoca, ADGEOM #1525, August 24, 2014)</p>

<p>The triangle T is a central triangle of type 1, perpsective to each
of the following triangles: ABC, excentral (see X(6043), Apollonius,
incentral, Feuerbach, and extangents. (Peter Moses, August 24,
2014).</p>

<p>The A-vertex of the central triangle T has these barycentrics:
g(a,b,c) : b (a+c)^2 (a^2+a b+b c+c^2)^2 : (a+b)^2 c (a^2+b^2+a c+b
c)^2, where<br>
g(a,b,c) = -a (2 a^6+4 a^5 b+4 a^4 b^2+4 a^3 b^3+3 a^2 b^4+2 a
b^5+b^6+4 a^5 c+12 a^4 b c+16 a^3 b^2 c+12 a^2 b^3 c+6 a b^4 c+2 b^5
c+4 a^4 c^2+16 a^3 b c^2+20 a^2 b^2 c^2+8 a b^3 c^2+b^4 c^2+4 a^3
c^3+12 a^2 b c^3+8 a b^2 c^3+3 a^2 c^4+6 a b c^4+b^2 c^4+2 a c^5+2 b
c^5+c^6) &nbsp;&nbsp; (Peter Moses, August 24, 2014)</p>
