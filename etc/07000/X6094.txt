<h3 id="X6094">X(6094)&nbsp;=&nbsp; 11th HATZIPOLAKIS-MONTESDEOCA
POINT</h3>
Barycentrics &nbsp;&nbsp; f(a,b,c) : f(b,c,a) : f(c,a,b),
where f(a,b,c) = (a^4+5 a^2 b^2+b^4-4 a^2 c^2-4 b^2 c^2+c^4) (a^4-4 a^2
b^2+b^4+5 a^2 c^2-4 b^2 c^2+c^4)

<p>Suppose that P = p : q : r (barycentrics) and P* are a pair of
isogonal conjugate points in the plane of a triangle ABC. Let A&prime;B&prime;C&prime; be
the pedal triangle of P and A&Prime;B&Prime;C&Prime; the pedal triangle of P*. Let A*
be the reflection of A&prime; in line B&Prime;C&Prime;, and define B* and C*
cyclically. In
<a href="http://www.hyacinthos.epizy.com/message.php?msg=22600"
class="bold">Hyacinthos</a> (October 3, 2014), Antreas
Hatzipolakis asks for the locus of P for which the circumcircles of
A*BC, B*CA, C*AB concur. Angel Montesceoca responds that the three
circumcircles concur for all choices of P. He further notes that if P
is not on the circumcircle and not on the line at infinity, then the
point Q of conurrence of the three circles has barycentrics
Q(a,b,c,p,q,r) : Q(b,c,a,q,r,p) : Q(c,a,b,r,p,q) given by</p>

<p>Q(a,b,c,p,q,r) = (q + r)/[a<sup>4</sup>qr(p + q)(p + r) -
2a<sup>2</sup>(q + r)(r + p)(p + q)(c<sup>2</sup>q + b<sup>2</sup>r) +
p(q + r)(b<sup>4</sup>r(p + q) + c<sup>4</sup>q(q + r) +
2b<sup>2</sup>c<sup>2</sup>(q<sup>2</sup> + r<sup>2</sup> + pq + qr +
rp))]</p>

<p>Writing Q as Q(P), the occurrence of (i,j) in the following list
means that Q(X(i)) = X(j): (1,5620), (2,6094), (3, 1263), (8, 6095),
(20, 265), (69, 6096), (1138, 1138). In particular, Q(X(2)) =
X(6094).</p>

<p>X(6094) lies on these lines: {6,543}, {263,2854}</p>

<p>X(6094) = isogonal conjugate of X(352)<br>
X(6094) = trilinear pole of the line X(373)X(512)</p>
