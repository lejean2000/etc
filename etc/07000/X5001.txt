<h3 id="X5001">X(5001)&nbsp;=&nbsp;INVERSE-IN-CIRCUMCIRCLE OF X(5000)</h3>
Barycentrics &nbsp;&nbsp; (SB+SC)*(S*SB*SC + SA*sqrt(SA*SB*SC*SW))  : :
<br>
Barycentrics &nbsp;&nbsp; S<sup>2</sup> - S<sub>B</sub>S<sub>C</sub> - k<sup>2</sup>(S<sup>2</sup> - 3S<sub>B</sub>S<sub>C</sub>) : : ,
<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;where k<sup>2</sup> = (3S<sub>A</sub>S<sub>B</sub>S<sub>C</sub> - S<sup>2</sup>S<sub>&omega;</sub> -  2(S<sub>A</sub>S<sub>B</sub>S<sub>C</sub>S<sup>2</sup>S<sub>&omega;</sub>)<sup>1/2</sup>)/(9S<sub>A</sub>S<sub>B</sub>S<sub>C</sub> - S<sup>2</sup>S<sub>&omega;</sub>)
<br>
Barycentrics&nbsp;&nbsp; SS<sub>B</sub>S<sub>C</sub>(3S<sub>A</sub> - S<sub>&omega;</sub>) + (S<sup>2</sup> - 3S<sub>B</sub>S<sub>C</sub>)(S<sub>A</sub>S<sub>B</sub>S<sub>C</sub>S<sub>&omega;</sub>)<sup>1/2</sup> : : &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;  (Peter Moses, December 8, 2014)
<br>
Tripolars &nbsp;&nbsp; Sqrt[SA] : :
<br>
<b>
X(5001) = (1 - k<sup>2</sup>)X(3) +  k<sup>2</sup>X(4)<br>
X(5001) = 3k<sup>2</sup>X(2) + (1 - 3k<sup>2</sup>)X(3)  </b>
<p>
As a point on the Euler line, X(5001) has Shinagawa coefficients (1 -k<sup>2</sup>, -1 + 3k<sup>2</sup>).
</p>
<p>
</p>
<p>
X(5000) and X(5001) are the antiorthocorrespondents of X(6); i.e. they share the same orthocorrespondent, X(6). See Bernard Gibert,
<a href="https://bernard-gibert.pagesperso-orange.fr/Tables/table55.html"><b>Table 55: X(5000, X(5001) and related curves</b></a>.
</p>
