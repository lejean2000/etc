<h3 id="X6791">X(6791)&nbsp;=&nbsp;CENTROID OF PEDAL TRIANGLE OF X(111)</h3>
Barycentrics &nbsp;&nbsp; (b<sup>2</sup> - c<sup>2</sup>)<sup>2</sup>(5a<sup>2</sup> - b<sup>2</sup> - c<sup>2</sup>) : :

<p>
Let L<sub>A</sub>, L<sub>B</sub>, L<sub>C</sub> be the lines through A, B, C, resp. parallel to line X(2)X(6).  Let M<sub>A</sub>, M<sub>B</sub>, M<sub>C</sub> be the reflections of lines BC, CA, AB in L<sub>A</sub>, L<sub>B</sub>, L<sub>C</sub>, resp.  Let A&prime; = M<sub>B</sub>&cap;M<sub>C</sub>, and define B&prime;, C&prime; cyclically.  Triangle A&prime;B&prime;C&prime; is inversely similar to, and 3 times the size of, ABC.  Let A&Prime;B&Prime;C&Prime; be the reflection of A&prime;B&prime;C&prime; in line X(2)X(6).  The triangle A&Prime;B&Prime;C&Prime; is homothetic to ABC, with center of homothety X(6791).  (See Hyacinthos #16741/16782, Sep 2008.)
</p>
<p>
X(6791) lies on the ellipse described at X(6784) and these lines: {2,5503), {6,5642}, {111,542}, {115,125}, {126,5969}, {373,3815}, {1499,2686}
</p>
<p>
X(6791) = midpoint of X(111) and X(6792)<br>
X(6791) = crossdifference of every pair of points on line X(110)X(1296)
</p>
