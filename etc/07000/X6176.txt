<p>
Let ABC be an acute triangle, and let L<sub>A</sub> be the circle that is externally tangent to the nine-point circle and to the sidelines AB and AC.  Define L<sub>B</sub> and L<sub>C</sub> cyclically.  The three circles are named the Odehnal tritangent circles at X(3822).  The center of their outer Apollonian circle is X(6176).  (Boris Odehnal, "A Triad of Tritangent Circles, <I>Journal for Geometry and Graphics</I> 18 (2014) no. 1, 61-71)
