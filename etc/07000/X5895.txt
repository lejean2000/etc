<br>

<p>
Let H be the hyperbola {A,B,C,X(4),X(20)}. Let L(X) denote the line
tangent to H at a point X on H. Then X(5895) is the point of
intersection of L(X(4)) and L(X(20)). (Randy Hutson, July 7, 2014)
</p>
<p>
Let A&prime; be the trilinear pole of the perpendicular bisector of BC, and define B&prime; and C&prime; cyclically.  A&prime;B&prime;C&prime; is also the anticomplement of the anticomplement of the midheight triangle.  X(5895) = X(3)-of-A&prime;B&prime;C&prime;. (Randy Hutson, January 29, 2018)
</p>
