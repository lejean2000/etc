<h3 id="X6199">X(6199)&nbsp;=&nbsp; PERSPECTOR OF CIRCUMSYMMEDIAL TRIANGLE AND LUCAS CENTRAL TRIANGLE</h3>
Trilinears&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; 3 cos A + 4 sin A : 3 cos B + 4 sin B : 3 cos C + 4 sin C
<br>
Trilinears&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; a(3S<sub>A</sub> + 4S) : b(3S<sub>B</sub> + 4S) : c(3S<sub>C</sub> + 4S)
<br>
Barycentrics&nbsp;&nbsp; g(a,b,c) : g(b,c,a) : g(c,a,b), where g(a,b,c) = a^2 (3 a^2-3 b^2-3 c^2-8 S)
<p>
See C&eacute;sar Lozada,
<a href="https://docs.google.com/uc?export=download&id=0BxTBrium3DhVbGR0X0pDNEFGTzQ" class="bold">Perspective-Orthologic-Parallelogic</a>.
</p>
<p>
Let (L<sub>A</sub>), (L<sub>B</sub>), (L<sub>C</sub>) be the Lucas circles.  Let (L&prime;<sub>A</sub>) be the circle, other than (L<sub>A</sub>), tangent to (L<sub>B</sub>), (L<sub>C</sub>), and the circumcircle.  Define (L&prime;<sub>B</sub>), (L&prime;<sub>C</sub>) cyclically.  The circles (L&prime;<sub>A</sub>), (L&prime;<sub>B</sub>), (L&prime;<sub>C</sub>) are here introduced as the <b>Lucas secondary circles</b>, and the triangle formed by their centers as the <b>Lucas secondary central triangle</b>.  Let M<sub>A</sub> be the line through the touch points of (L<sub>A</sub>) and the adjacent Lucas secondary circles ((L&prime;<sub>B</sub>) and (L&prime;<sub>C</sub>)).  Define M<sub>B</sub> and M<sub>C</sub> cyclically.  The <b>1st Lucas secondary tangents triangle</b> is the triangle having sidelines M<sub>A</sub>, M<sub>B</sub>, M<sub>C</sub>.  Let N<sub>A</sub> be the line through the touch points of (L&prime;<sub>A</sub>) and the adjacent Lucas circles ((L<sub>B</sub>) and (L<sub>C</sub>)).  Define N<sub>B</sub> and N<sub>C</sub> cyclically.  The <b>2nd Lucas secondary tangents triangle</b> is the triangle having sidelines N<sub>A</sub>, N<sub>B</sub>, N<sub>C</sub>.  The 1st and 2nd Lucas secondary tangents triangles are perspective, and their perspector is X(6199).  Moreover, the six touch points of the Lucas circles and Lucas secondary circles lie on a circle, here introduced as the <b>Lucas secondary tangents circle</b>, with center X(6409).  X(6199) is the inverse-in-Lucas-secondary-tangents-circle of X(187).  (Randy Hutson, January 29, 2015)
</p>
