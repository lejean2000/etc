<h3 id="X5655">X(5655)&nbsp;=&nbsp; INTERSECTION OF LINES X(6)X(13) AND X(30)X(110)</h3>
Barycentrics &nbsp;&nbsp; f(a,b,c) : f(b,c,a) :
f(c,a,b), where f(a,b,c) = a^10 - 6a^8(b^2 + c^2) + a^6(11b^4 - b^2c^2 + 11c^4) - a^4(7b^6 - 2b^4c^2 - 2b^2c^4 + 7c^6) + 8a^2b^2c^2(b^2 - c^2)^2 + (b^2 - c^2)^4(b^2 + c^2) : :
<p>
Let A&prime;B&prime;C&prime; be the orthocentroidal triangle. Let L be the lines through A&prime; parallel to the Euler line, and define M and N cyclically. Let L&prime; be the reflection of L in sideline BC, and define M&prime; and L&prime; cyclically. The lines L&prime;,M&prime;,N&prime; concur in X(5655); c.f. X(i) for i = 74, 113, 265, 399, 1147, 1511, 5504, 5609. (Randy Hutson, August 26, 2014)
</p>
<p>
X(5655) is the center of the perspeconic of the orthocentroidal and anti-orthocentroidal triangles (see preamble before X(15254)).  This conic is a circle. (Randy Hutson, December 2, 2017)
</p>
<p>

{3,541}, {4,5609}, {5,5643}, {6,13}, {30,110}, {74,549}, {125,5055}, {146,376}, {154,2777}, {354,2771}, {1539,3543}, {3167,3830}, {3448,3545}, {5054,5646}
</p>
