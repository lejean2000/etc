&Lambda;(P, X) is defined (TCCT, p. 80) as the isogonal conjugate of
the point in which the line PX meets the infinity line. X(5897) =
&Lambda;(X(3), X(1661) = &Lambda;(X(4), X(64) = &Lambda;(X(5), X(5893))
= &Lambda;(X(20), X(394) = &Lambda;(X(146), X(2071). X(5897) is the
point of intersection, other than A,B,C, of the circumcircle and the
hyperbola {A,B,C,X(3),X(20)}; also, X(5897) is the antipode of X(1301)
on the circumcircle. (Randy Hutson, July 7, 2014)</p>

<p>X(5897) lies on the circumcircle and these lines:
{3,1301}, {20,107}, {110,1498}, {112,1033}, {376,1289}, {393,3344}, {1302,1370}, {1304,2071}</p>
