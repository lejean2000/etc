<h3 id="X6801">X(6801)&nbsp;=&nbsp;1st AVIOR CYCLOLOGIC CENTER</h3>
Barycentrics &nbsp;&nbsp;  (a^2+b^2-c^2) (a^2-b^2+c^2) (a^10-3 a^8 b^2+2 a^6 b^4+2 a^4 b^6-3 a^2 b^8+b^10-3 a^8 c^2+5 a^6 b^2 c^2-4 a^4 b^4 c^2+5 a^2 b^6 c^2-3 b^8 c^2+4 a^6 c^4+4 b^6 c^4-4 a^4 c^6-5 a^2 b^2 c^6-4 b^4 c^6+3 a^2 c^8+3 b^2 c^8-c^10) (a^10-3 a^8 b^2+4 a^6 b^4-4 a^4 b^6+3 a^2 b^8-b^10-3 a^8 c^2+5 a^6 b^2 c^2-2 a^4 b^4 c^2-3 a^2 b^6 c^2+3 b^8 c^2+4 a^6 c^4-2 a^4 b^2 c^4-2 b^6 c^4-4 a^4 c^6-3 a^2 b^2 c^6-2 b^4 c^6+3 a^2 c^8+3 b^2 c^8-c^10) (a^10-3 a^8 b^2+4 a^6 b^4-4 a^4 b^6+3 a^2 b^8-b^10-3 a^8 c^2+5 a^6 b^2 c^2-5 a^2 b^6 c^2+3 b^8 c^2+2 a^6 c^4-4 a^4 b^2 c^4-4 b^6 c^4+2 a^4 c^6+5 a^2 b^2 c^6+4 b^4 c^6-3 a^2 c^8-3 b^2 c^8+c^10) : :  &nbsp;&nbsp; (Peter Moses, June 13, 2018)
<p>
Let A&prime;B&prime;C&prime; be the anticevian triangle of X(54).  Let A&Prime; be the reflection of A&prime; in BC, and define B&Prime; and C&Prime; cyclically.  The triangles ABC and A&Prime;B&Prime;C&Prime; are cyclologic.  The (ABC, A&Prime;B&Prime;C&Prime;)-cyclologic center is X(6801), and the (A&Prime;B&Prime;C&Prime;, ABC)-cyclologic center is X(6802).  See
<a href="http://www.hyacinthos.epizy.com/message.php?msg=23162" class="bold">Hyacinthos 23162</a> (March 19, 2015, Randy Hutson, C&eacute;sar Lozada)
</p>
