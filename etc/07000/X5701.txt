<h3 id="X5701">X(5701)&nbsp;=&nbsp; MINIMIZER ON LINE X(1)X(6) OF
x<sup>2</sup> + y<sup>2</sup> + z<sup>2</sup></h3>
Barycentrics&nbsp;&nbsp; f(a,b,c) : f(b,c,a) :
f(c,a,b), where f(a,b,c) = a(a<sup>3</sup>b<sup>2</sup> +
a<sup>3</sup>c<sup>2</sup> - 2a<sup>2</sup>b<sup>3</sup> -
2a<sup>2</sup>c<sup>3</sup> + ab<sup>4</sup> + ac<sup>4</sup> -
ab<sup>3</sup>c - abc<sup>3</sup> + 2a<sup>2</sup>b<sup>2</sup> +
b<sup>4</sup>c + bc<sup>4</sup> - b<sup>3</sup>c<sup>2</sup> -
b<sup>2</sup>c<sup>3</sup>)

<p>Suppose that P = p : q : r and U = u : v : w (homogeneous
barycentric coordinates). Let (x,y,z) be normalized barycentric
coordinates for an arbitrary point X. The point T on the line PU that
minimizes x<sup>2</sup> + y<sup>2</sup> + z<sup>2</sup> is given by</p>

<p>T = (qu - pv)(pv + rv - qu - qw) + (ru - pw)(pw + qw - ru -
rv)&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; (Peter Moses, June 23, 2014)</p>

<p>X(5701) is the minimizer T when P = X(1) and Q = X(6). See also
X(5661) and X(5662).</p>

<p>X(5701) lies on these lines:
{1,6}, {2,650}, {1252,1621}, {3693,4702}, {5284,5375}</p>
