<h3 id="X6097">X(6097)&nbsp;=&nbsp; 14th HATZIPOLAKIS-MONTESDEOCA POINT</h3>
Barycentrics &nbsp;&nbsp; f(a,b,c) : f(b,c,a) : f(c,a,b),
where f(a,b,c) = a^2 (a^2-b^2-b c-c^2) (a^5 b-2 a^3 b^3+a b^5+a^5 c-a^2 b^3 c-a b^4 c+b^5 c-2 a^3 c^3-a^2 b c^3-2 b^3 c^3-a b c^4+a c^5+b c^5)
<br>
<b>X(6097) = (r<sup>2</sup> + 2rR - R<sup>2</sup> + s<sup>2</sup>)*X(3)
+ R<sup>2</sup>*X(4)</b>  &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; barycentrics, Peter
Moses, October 3, 2014; combo, Angel Montesdoca, October 3, 2014

<p>Let ABC be a triangle and A&prime;B&prime;C&prime; the cevian triangle of X(1). Let
O<sub>AB</sub> be the circumcenter of ABA&prime;, and define O<sub>BC</sub>
and O<sub>CA</sub> cyclically; let O<sub>AC</sub> be the circumcenter
of ACA&prime;, and define O<sub>BA</sub> and O<sub>CB</sub> cyclically. Let
O<sub>A</sub> be the circumcenter of triangle
AO<sub>AB</sub>O<sub>AC</sub>, and define O<sub>B</sub> and
O<sub>C</sub> cyclically. Hatzipolakis proposed, and Montesdeoca
proved, that the Euler lines concur, in X(186), and that the
orthocenter of triangle O<sub>A</sub>O<sub>B</sub>O<sub>C</sub>, which
is X(6097), lies on the Euler line. See
<a href="http://anthrakitis.blogspot.gr/2014/10/i-concurrent-euler-lines.html"
class="bold">Anthrakitis</a> (October 3, 2014)</p>

<p>X(6097) lies on these lines: {2,3}, {35,500}, {55,5453}, {511,5495}, {3724,5492}</p>
