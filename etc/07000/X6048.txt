<h3 id="X6048">X(6048)&nbsp;=&nbsp; CENTER OF MOSES HULL CIRCLE</h3>
Barycentrics&nbsp;&nbsp; f(a,b,c) : f(b,c,a) : f(c,a,b),
where f(a,b,c) = a(a<sup>2</sup>b + a<sup>2</sup>c + ab<sup>2</sup> +
ac<sup>2</sup> + abc - 3b<sup>2</sup>c - 3bc<sup>2</sup>)
<br>
<b>
X(6048) = (r<sup>2</sup> + s<sup>2</sup>)*X(1) - 16rR*X(10)
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; (barycentrics and
combo, Peter Moses, August 26, 2014)
</b>
<p>
As at X(3596), let A&prime;, B&prime;, C&prime; denote the respective excircles of a
triangle ABC. Let A&Prime; be the circle tangent to A&prime;, B&prime;, C&prime; whose
interior includes A&prime;, and define B&Prime; and C&Prime; cyclically. Let (M) be the
circle externally tangent to all three of the circles A&prime;, B&prime;, C&prime;; that
is, the circular hull of A&prime;, B&prime;, C&prime;, of which the center is X(6048).
Also, (M) is tangent to the Apollonius circle at X(3030), and the
tangent line through X(3030) passes through X(2254). The radius of (M)
is r(r<sup>2</sup> + s<sup>2</sup>)/(16rR - r<sup>2</sup> -
s<sup>2</sup>). (Peter Moses, August 26, 2014) The circle (M) is here
named the Moses hull circle.
</p>
<p>
The Moses hull circle is the inverse of the incircle in the
excircles-radical circle. (Randy Hutson, September 5, 2014)
</p>
<p>
Let (Ia), (Ib), (Ic) be the excircles of ABC, and let (AP) be the classical Apollonius circle (e.g., TCCT, p. 102).  Let (Ja) be the circle internally tangent to (Ia) and externally tangent to (Ib) and (Ic), and define (Jb) and Jc) cyclically.  Let (J) be the circle internally tangent to (Ja), (Jb), (Jc).  Then X(6048) = center of (J).
