<h3 id="X5499">X(5499)&nbsp;=&nbsp; 7th HATZIPOLAKIS-MONTESDEOCA POINT</h3>
Barycentrics &nbsp;&nbsp; (b^2+4*b*c+c^2)*a^5-(b+c)*(b^2+c^2)*a^4-(2*b^4+2*c^4+3*b*c*(b^2+c^2))*a^3+2*(b^3-c^3)*(b^2-c^2)*a^2+(b^2-c^2)*(b-c)*(b^3+c^3)*a-(b^2-c^2)^3*(b-c) : :
<p>
Let I<sub>A</sub> be the A-excenter of a triangle ABC and let
N<sub>A</sub> be the nine-point center of I<sub>A</sub>BC. Define
N<sub>B</sub> and N<sub>C</sub> cyclically. The circumcenter of
N<sub>A</sub>N<sub>B</sub>N<sub>C</sub> is X(5499), which lies on the
Euler line of ABC. &nbsp;&nbsp; (Antreas Hatzipolakis, May 30,
2013)</p>

<p>Let A&prime;B&prime;C&prime; be the Feuerbach triangle, and let A&Prime; be the reflection
of X(11) in line B&prime;C&prime;; define B&Prime; and C&Prime; cyclically. Then A&Prime;, B&Prime;,
C&Prime; are collinear, and their line, X(12)X(79) is here named the
Feuerbach line. X(5499) is the point of intersection of the Feuerbach
line and the Euler line.&nbsp;&nbsp; (Randy Hutson, August 26,
2014)</p>
