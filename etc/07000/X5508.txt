<h3 id="X5508">X(5508)&nbsp;=&nbsp; KIRIKAMI CONCURRENT CIRCLES IMAGE
(2nd KIND) OF X(31)</h3>
Barycentrics&nbsp;&nbsp; f(a,b,c) : f(b,c,a) :
f(c,a,b), where f(a,b,c) = a/[a<sup>6</sup> -
a<sup>4</sup>(b<sup>2</sup> + c<sup>2</sup>) +
a<sup>3</sup>(b<sup>3</sup> + c<sup>3</sup>) - a(b<sup>5</sup> +
c<sup>5</sup>) + b<sup>3</sup>c<sup>3</sup>] &nbsp;&nbsp; (Seichii
Kirikami, July 2, 2013)

<p>Let P be a point in the plane of triangle ABC. Let H<sub>A</sub> be
the orthocenter of triangle PBC, and define H<sub>B</sub> and
H<sub>C</sub> cyclically. The circumcircles of H<sub>A</sub>BC,
H<sub>B</sub>CA, H<sub>C</sub>AB concur in a point Q, the Kirikami
concurrent circles image (2nd kind) of P; see X(5503). Let P be given
by barycentrics p : q : r. Then Q is given by</p>

<p>Q = g(a,b,c,p,q,r) : g(b,c,a,q,r,p) : g(c,a,b,r,p,q), where
g(a,b,c,p,q,r) = p/[(a<sup>2</sup> - b<sup>2</sup> -
c<sup>2</sup>)p<sup>2</sup> + a<sup>2</sup>qr + (a<sup>2</sup> -
b<sup>2</sup>)pq + (a<sup>2</sup> - c<sup>2</sup>)pr].</p>

<p>If P = X(31), then Q = X(5508).&nbsp;&nbsp; (Seichii Kirikami, July
2, 2013)</p>

<p>The barycentrics for Q show that "concurrent circles image (2nd
kind)" is the same as "antigonal image". &nbsp;&nbsp; (Randy Hutson,
July 15, 2013)</p>
