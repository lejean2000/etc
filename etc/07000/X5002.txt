<h3 id="X5002">X(5002)&nbsp;=&nbsp;1st WALSMITH-MOSES POINT</h3>
Barycentrics &nbsp;&nbsp; S<sup>2</sup> - S<sub>B</sub>S<sub>C</sub> +
k<sup>2</sup>(S<sup>2</sup> - 3S<sub>B</sub>S<sub>C</sub>) : : ,
<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;where
k<sup>2</sup> = (3S<sub>A</sub>S<sub>B</sub>S<sub>C</sub> - S<sup>2</sup>S<sub>&omega;</sub> -
4(S<sub>A</sub>S<sub>B</sub>S<sub>C</sub>S<sup>2</sup>S<sub>&omega;</sub><sup>)1/2</sup>)/(-9S<sub>A</sub>S<sub>B</sub>S<sub>C</sub> + S<sup>2</sup>S<sub>&omega;</sub>)
<br>
Tripolars &nbsp;&nbsp; a : b : c
<br>
<b>
X(5002) = -3k<sup>2</sup>X(2) + (1 + 3k<sup>2</sup>)X(3)<br>
X(5002) = (1 + k<sup>2</sup>)X(3) - k<sup>2</sup>X(4)  </b>
<p>
As a point on the Euler line, X(5002) has Shinagawa coefficients (1 +k<sup>2</sup>, -1 - 3k<sup>2</sup>).
</p>
<p>
X(5002) is the point whose tripolar distances in the plane of
triangle ABC are proportional to (a, b, c); the actual tripolar
distances in case ABC is acute are ka, kb, kc.</p>
<p>If the reference triangle ABC is obtuse, then the barycentrics of
X(5002) are a nonreal complex numbers. Nevertheless, for all triangles
ABC, the midpoint of X(5002) and X(5003) is X(858).</p>
<p>A method for converting from homogeneous tripolar coordinates (henceforth
simply "tripolars") to homogeneous barycentrics, found by Peter Moses (March, 2012), depends on finding the point of intersection of the radical axes of radical circles centered at A, B, C. Write the tripolars for a point U as u : v : w, and let</p>

<p>d<sub>a</sub> = b<sup>2</sup> + c<sup>2</sup> - a<sup>2</sup>,
d<sub>b</sub> = c<sup>2</sup> + a<sup>2</sup> - c<sup>2</sup>,
d<sub>c</sub> = a<sup>2</sup> + b<sup>2</sup> - c<sup>2</sup>.</p>

<p>Then barycentrics x : y : z for U are given by</p>

<p>x = a<sup>2</sup>d<sub>a</sub> +
k<sup>2</sup>[d<sub>c</sub>v<sup>2</sup> + d<sub>b</sub>w<sup>2</sup> -
2a<sup>2</sup>u<sup>2</sup>]<br>
y = b<sup>2</sup>d<sub>b</sub> +
k<sup>2</sup>[d<sub>a</sub>w<sup>2</sup> + d<sub>c</sub>u<sup>2</sup> -
2b<sup>2</sup>v<sup>2</sup>]<br>
z = c<sup>2</sup>d<sub>c</sub> +
k<sup>2</sup>[d<sub>b</sub>u<sup>2</sup> + d<sub>a</sub>v<sup>2</sup> -
2c<sup>2</sup>w<sup>2</sup>],</p>

<p>where k<sup>2</sup> has two values (as in the quadratic formula):
(-f - g)/h or (-f + g)/h, where</p>

<p>f = - a<sup>2</sup>u<sup>2</sup>d<sub>a</sub> -
b<sup>2</sup>v<sup>2</sup>d<sub>b</sub> -
c<sup>2</sup>w<sup>2</sup>d<sub>c</sub><br>
g = 2S[(-au + bv + cw)(au - bv + cw)(au + bv - cw)(au + bv +
cw)]<sup>1/2</sup><br>
h = 2[a<sup>2</sup>(u<sup>2</sup> - v<sup>2</sup>)(u<sup>2</sup> -
w<sup>2</sup>) + b<sup>2</sup>(v<sup>2</sup> -
w<sup>2</sup>)(v<sup>2</sup> - u<sup>2</sup>) +
c<sup>2</sup>(w<sup>2</sup> - u<sup>2</sup>)(w<sup>2</sup> -
v<sup>2</sup>)]
</p>
<p>
The meaning of k can be stated thus: starting with tripolars u : v :
w, the actual tripolar distances are ku, kv, kw. That is, |UA| = ku,
|UB| = kv, |UC| = kw.</p>
<p>
X(5002) lies on this line: {2,3}<br>
</p>

X(5002) = antigonal conjugate of X(34240)<br>
X(5002) = circumcircle-inverse of X(5003)<br>
</p>
