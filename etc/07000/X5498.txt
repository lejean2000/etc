<p>Let ABC be a triangle, let N<sub>A</sub> be the nine-point center of
the triangle BCO, where O = X(3), and define N<sub>B</sub> and
N<sub>C</sub> cyclically. The nine-point center of the triangle
N<sub>A</sub>N<sub>B</sub>N<sub>C</sub> is X(5498), which lies on the
Euler line of ABC.&nbsp;&nbsp; (Antreas Hatzipolakis, May 30, 2013)</p>
