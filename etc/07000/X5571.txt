<h3 id="X5571">X(5571)&nbsp;=&nbsp; X(1) OF INVERSE-IN-INCIRCLE TRIANGLE</h3>
Barycentrics a[(b + c - a)(ab + ac - (b - c)<sup>2</sup>)sin(A/2) + b(c + a - b)<sup>2</sup>sin(B/2) + c(a + b - c)<sup>2</sup>Sin(C/2)] : :
<p>
Let ABC be a triangle. Let A&prime; = (inverse-in-incircle) of A, and define B&prime; and C&prime; cyclically. The triangle with vertices A,&prime; B, C&prime; is
introduced here as the inverse-in-incircle triangle. A&prime;B&prime;C&prime; is a central triangle of type 2. The following properties were communicated
by Peter Moses, November 9, 2013:
</p>
<p>
Barycentric coordinates for vertices:<br>
A&prime; = (b - c)<sup>2</sup> - ab - ac : b(a - b - c) : c(a - b - c)<br>
B&prime; = a(b - c - a) : (c - a)<sup>2</sup> - bc - ba : c(b - c - a)<br>
C&prime; = a(c - a - b) : b(c - a - b) : (a - b)<sup>2</sup> : a(c - a - b).
</p>
<p>
|B&prime;C&prime;|<sup>2</sup> = (a - b - c)<sup>2</sup>[a<sup>2</sup> - (b -
c)<sup>2</sup>]/(16bc)
<br>
area(A&prime;B&prime;C&prime;)/area(ABC) = (b + c - a)(c + a - b)(a + b - c)/(16abc)
</p>
<p>
X(354) = centroid of A&prime;B&prime;C&prime;<br>
X(942) = circumcenter of A&prime;B&prime;C&prime;<br>
X(1) = orthocenter of A&prime;B&prime;C&prime;<br>
X(5045) = nine-point center of A&prime;B&prime;C&prime;
</p>
<p>
The following triangles are perspective to A&prime;B&prime;C&prime;, with perspector X(1): reflection of T in X(3), excentral, incentral, mid-arc,
2nd circumperp, mixtilinear, 1st circumperp. Also, A&prime;B&prime;C&prime; is perspective to other central triangles, with perspectors as shown:
</p>
<p>
medial, X(142)<br>
intouch, X(354)<br>
hexyl, X(3333)<br>

</p>
