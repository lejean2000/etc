<h3 id="X6120">X(6120)&nbsp;=&nbsp; 1st HATZIPOLAKIS-VAN TIENHOVEN POINT</h3>
Trilinears &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; cos A - cos(A/3) : cos B - cos(B/3) : cos C - cos(C/3)
<br>
Trilinears &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; sin(A/3) sin(2A/3) : sin(B/3) sin(2B/3) : sin(C/3) sin(2C/3)
<p>
In the plane of ABC, let A<sub>B</sub> be the internal trisector at A that is closer to B, and define B<sub>C</sub> and C<sub>A</sub> cyclically.  Let A<sub>C</sub> be the internal trisector of A that is closer to C, and define B<sub>A</sub> and C<sub>B</sub> cyclically.  Let P<sub>A</sub> be the Miquel point (QL-P1) of the quadrilateral having vertices B<sub>C</sub>, B<sub>A</sub>, C<sub>A</sub>, C<sub>B</sub>, and define P<sub>B</sub> and P<sub>C</sub> cyclically.  The points P<sub>A</sub>, P<sub>B</sub>, P<sub>C</sub> lie on the circumcircle of ABC, and the triangle P<sub>A</sub>, P<sub>B</sub>, P<sub>C</sub>, here named the 1st Hatzipolakis-Van Tienhoven triangle, is perspective to ABC.  The perspector is X(6120).  There are 3 sets of trisectors, so that there are 2nd and 3rd triangles and 2nd and 3rd points, as found at X(6121) and X(6122).  (Antreas Hatzipolakis and Chris van Tienhoven, Hyacinthos, November 8-9, 2014:
<a href="http://www.hyacinthos.epizy.com/message.php?msg=22717" class="bold">22717</a>,
<a href="http://www.hyacinthos.epizy.com/message.php?msg=22719" class="bold">22719</a>)
</p>
<p> Let L<sub>A</sub> be the line through the circumcircle intercepts of the interior trisector of angle A, and define L<sub>B</sub> and L<sub>C</sub> cyclically.  Let A&prime; = L<sub>B</sub>&cap;L<sub>C</sub>, and define B&prime; and C&prime; cyclically.  Then A&prime;B&prime;C&prime; is homothetic to ABC at X(6120).  (Randy Hutson, September 18, 2014; see
<a href="http://www.adgeom.epizy.com/message.php?msg=1733" class="bold">ADGEOM 1733</a>
</p>
<p>

</p>
