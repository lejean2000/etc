<h3 id="X5503">X(5503)&nbsp;=&nbsp; KIRIKAMI CONCURRENT CIRCLES IMAGE OF THE CENTROID</h3>
Barycentrics &nbsp;&nbsp; 1/(4a<sup>4</sup> + b<sup>4</sup> + c<sup>4</sup> - 4b<sup>2</sup>c<sup>2</sup> -
a<sup>2</sup>b<sup>2</sup> - a<sup>2</sup>c<sup>2</sup>) &nbsp;&nbsp;
(Seichii Kirikami, June 2, 2013)

<p>Let P be a point in the plane of triangle ABC. Let H<sub>A</sub> be
the orthocenter of triangle PBC, and define H<sub>B</sub> and
H<sub>C</sub> cyclically. Let O<sub>A</sub> be the circle of the points
A, H<sub>B</sub>, H<sub>C</sub>, and define O<sub>B</sub> and
O<sub>C</sub> cyclically. The circles O<sub>A</sub>, O<sub>B</sub>,
O<sub>C</sub> concur in a point Q, the Kirikami concurrent circles
image of P. Let P be given by barycentrics p : q : r. Then Q given
by</p>

<p>Q = g(a,b,c,p,q,r) : g(b,c,a,q,r,p) : g(c,a,b,r,p,q), where
g(a,b,c,p,q,r) =  p/(2 a^4 q r(p+q) (p+r)+a^2 p (c^2 q (p+q-3 r) (p+r)+b^2 (p+q) r (p-3 q+r))-p (b^4 (p+q) (p-q-r) r-c^4 q (p+r) (-p+q+r)+b^2 c^2 (p^2 (q+r)+2 q r (q+r)+p (q^2+r^2)))) .
</p>
<p>
If P = X(2), then Q = X(5503).&nbsp;&nbsp; (Seichii Kirikami, June
2, 2013)</p>

<p>If P is on the circumcircle, then Q(P) = P. This follows from the
fact that the denominators of g(a,b,c,p,q,r) and g(b,c,a,q,r,p} are
polynomial multiples of a<sup>2</sup>qr +
b<sup>2</sup>rp+c<sup>2</sup>pq. &nbsp;&nbsp; (Seichii Kirikami, July
27, 2013)</p>
