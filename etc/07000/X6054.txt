<h3 id="X6054">X(6054)&nbsp;=&nbsp; REFLECTION OF X(98) IN X(2)</h3>
Barycentrics&nbsp;&nbsp; f(A,B,C) : f(B,C,A) : f(C, A, B),
where f(A,B,C) = 2 sin B sec(B + &omega;) + 2 sin C sec(C + &omega;) -
sin A sec(A + &omega;)
<p>
Let P be a point on the Steiner circumellipse.  Let A&prime; be the orthocenter of BCP, and define B&prime;, C&prime; cyclically.  Let Q be the centroid of A&prime;B&prime;C&prime;.  The locus of Q as P varies is an ellipse similar and orthogonal to the Steiner circumellipse, and also centered at X(2).  When P = X(99), Q = X(6054).  See also X(98) and X(24808).  (Randy Hutson, October 15, 2018)
</p>
