<p>
Let A&prime;B&prime;C&prime; be the reflection triangle.  Let O<sub>A</sub> be the circle with center A and pass-through point A&prime;, and define O<sub>B</sub> and O<sub>C</sub> cyclically.  The radical center of the circles O<sub>A</sub>, O<sub>B</sub>, O<sub>C</sub> is X(6243).  If O&prime;<sub>A</sub> has center A&prime; and pass-through point A, and cyclically for O&prime;<sub>B</sub>, O&prime;<sub>C</sub>, the radical center is X(3519).  (Randy Hutson, January 29, 2015)
</p>
<p>
Let A&prime;B&prime;C&prime; be the reflection triangle.  Let A<sub>B</sub>, A<sub>C</sub> be the orthogonal projections of A&prime; on CA, AB, resp. The lines A&prime;A&Prime;, B&prime;B&Prime;, C&prime;C&Prime; concur in X(6243), which is also X(3)-of-A&Prime;B&Prime;C&Prime;.  (Randy Hutson, March 29, 2020)
