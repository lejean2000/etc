<h3 id="X5502">X(5502)&nbsp;=&nbsp; 10th HATZIPOLAKIS-MONTESDEOCA POINT</h3>
Barycentrics &nbsp;&nbsp; f(a,b,c) : f(b,c,a) : f(c,a,b),
where f(a,b,c) = a<sup>2</sup>(a<sup>2</sup> -
b<sup>2</sup>)[a<sup>2</sup> - c<sup>2</sup>)(a<sup>6</sup> -
a<sup>4</sup>(b<sup>2</sup> + c<sup>2</sup>) +
a<sup>2</sup>(a<sup>2</sup> -b<sup>2</sup>)(a<sup>2</sup> -
c<sup>2</sup>) + 3(b<sup>2</sup> -
c<sup>2</sup>)<sup>2</sup>(b<sup>2</sup> + c<sup>2</sup>)] &nbsp;&nbsp;
(Angel Montesdeoca, June 3, 2013)

<p>Let L be the Euler line of a triangle ABC. Let L<sub>A</sub> be the
reflection of L in line BC, and define L<sub>B</sub> and L<sub>C</sub>
cyclically. Let A&prime; = L&cap;BC, and define B&prime; and C&prime; cyclically. The
circles whose diameters are the segments AA&prime;, BB&prime;, CC&prime; are coaxial. Let
D be their coaxial axis (the line X(4)X(74)); let D<sub>A</sub> be the
reflection of D in line BC, and define D<sub>B</sub> and D<sub>C</sub>
cyclically. Let H<sub>A</sub> = L<sub>B</sub>&cap;D<sub>C</sub>, and
define H<sub>B</sub> and H<sub>C</sub> cyclically. Let M<sub>A</sub> =
L<sub>C</sub>&cap;D<sub>B</sub>, and define M<sub>B</sub> and
M<sub>C</sub> cyclically. The triangles
H<sub>A</sub>H<sub>B</sub>H<sub>C</sub> and
M<sub>A</sub>M<sub>B</sub>M<sub>C</sub> are perspective, and their
perspector is X(5502). &nbsp;&nbsp; (Antreas Hatzipolakis, June 3,
2013)</p>

<p>For a discussion, see
<a href="http://amontes.webs.ull.es/otrashtm/HechosGeometricos.htm#HG050613">Hechos
Geom&eacute;tricos en el Tri&aacute;ngulo</a>.</p>
