<h3 id="X5587">X(5587)&nbsp;=&nbsp; PERSPECTOR OF AQUILA AND CARNOT TRIANGLES</h3>
Barycentrics&nbsp;&nbsp; f(a,b,c) : f(b,c,a) : f(c,a,b),
where f(a,b,c) = 2b<sup>4</sup> + 2c<sup>4</sup> - a<sup>4</sup> +
a<sup>3</sup>b + a<sup>3</sup>c - a<sup>2</sup>b<sup>2</sup> -

<p>The Aquila triangle is introduced at X(5586). Not only is X(5587)
the perspector of the Aquila and Carnot triangles, but also, X(5587) is
also the perspector of the Euler triangle and the outer Garcia
triangle, defined as follows. Let
T<sub>A</sub>T<sub>B</sub>T<sub>C</sub> be the extouch triangle of a
triangle ABC, and let L<sub>A</sub> be the line perpendicular to line
BC at T<sub>A</sub>. Of the two points on L<sub>A</sub> at distance r
from T<sub>A</sub>, let A&prime; be the one farther from A and let A&Prime; be the
closer. Define B&prime;, C&prime; and B&Prime;, C&Prime; cyclically. We call A&prime;B&prime;C&prime; the outer
Garcia triangle and A&Prime;B&Prime;C&Prime; the inner Garcia triangle. The outer
triangle is introduced by Emmanuel Garcia in ADGEOM #1205 (April 2,
2014), and the inner by Garcia in ADGEOM #1212 (April 3, 2014). In
subsequent postings, Paul Yiu reports that A&prime;B&prime;C&prime; is oppositely
congruent to ABC at X(10), the Euler lines of the four triangles AB&prime;C&prime;,
BC&prime;A&prime;, CA&prime;B&prime;, ABC concur in X(2475), and the circles (BCA&prime;), (CAB&prime;),
(ABC&prime;) concur in X(80). Peter Moses reports that</p>

<p>A&prime; = -a : a + c : a + b,&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; B&prime; = b + c : -b : b
+ a,&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; C&prime; = c + b : c + a : -c
</p>
<p>
A&Prime; = a<sup>2</sup> : ca + c<sup>2</sup> - b<sup>2</sup> : ab + b<sup>2</sup> - c<sup>2</sup>,&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;
B&Prime; = bc + c<sup>2</sup> - a<sup>2</sup> : b<sup>2</sup> : ab + a<sup>2</sup> - c<sup>2</sup> ,&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;
C&Prime; = bc + b<sup>2</sup> - a<sup>2</sup> : ca + a<sup>2</sup> - b<sup>2</sup> : c<sup>2</sup> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;
</p>
<p>
Randy Hutson notes that the inner Garcia triangle is the anticomplement of orthic-triangle-of-Fuhrmann-triangle, that X(5587) = X(2)-of-Fuhrmann triangle, and X(5587) = {X(355), X(5)}-harmonic conjugate of X(1).
</p>
<p>

<a href="http://forumgeom.fau.edu/FG2014volume14/FG201414.pdf" class="bold">A Note on Reflections</a>.
