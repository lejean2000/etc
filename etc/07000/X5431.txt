<h3 id="X5431">X(5431)&nbsp;=&nbsp;CENTER OF THE 2nd GRIGORIEV
CONIC</h3>
Barycentrics&nbsp;&nbsp; (1 + sec A/2)(sec B/2 + sec C/2) -
tan<sup>2</sup>A/2

<p>Let L<sub>A</sub> be the line parallel to line BC and tangent to the
circumcircle of triangle ABC on the positive side of BC (the region
that includes A), and define L<sub>B</sub> and L<sub>C</sub>
cyclically. Let A&prime; = L<sub>B</sub>&cap;L<sub>C</sub>, and define B&prime; and
C&prime; cyclically. Let A<sub>B</sub> be the reflection of A in line A&prime;B&prime;,
and let A<sub>C</sub> be the reflection of A in line A&prime;C&prime;. Let
C<sub>1</sub> be the touchpoint of the incircle of triangle
A<sub>B</sub>AB and line AB, and let B<sub>2</sub> be the touchpoint of
the incircle of A<sub>C</sub>AC and line AC. Define A<sub>1</sub>,
B<sub>1</sub> and C<sub>2</sub>, A<sub>2</sub> cyclically. Then
|AB<sub>2</sub>| = |CB<sub>1</sub>|, |BA<sub>1</sub>| =
|CA<sub>2</sub>|, |AC<sub>1</sub>| = |BC<sub>2</sub>|, so that by
Carnot's theorem, the six points A<sub>1</sub>, A<sub>2</sub>,
B<sub>1</sub>, B<sub>2</sub>, C<sub>1</sub>, C<sub>2</sub> lie on a
conic. A barycentric equation for this 2nd Grigoriev conic follows:</p>

<p></p>
x<sup>2</sup> + y<sup>2</sup> + z<sup>2</sup> - 2 sec(A/2) yz -
2 sec(B/2) zx - 2 sec(C/2) xy = 0

<p></p>
<br>

<p>The perspector of the 2nd Grigoriev conic is X(5451).&nbsp;&nbsp;
(Randy Hutson, April 8, 2013)<br>
See also X(5452).</p>
