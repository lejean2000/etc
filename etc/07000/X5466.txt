<h3 id="X5466">X(5466)&nbsp;=&nbsp; TRILINEAR POLE OF LINE X(115)X(523)</h3>
Trilinears&nbsp;&nbsp; f(a,b,c) : f(b,c,a) : f(c,a,b),
where f(a,b,c) = bc(b<sup>2</sup> - c<sup>2</sup>)/(b<sup>2</sup> +
c<sup>2</sup> - 2a<sup>2</sup>)

<p>Let L be the line tangent at X(13) to the circle defined at X(5459),
and let L&prime; be the line tangent to X(14) to the circle defined at
X(5460). Then X(5466) = L&cap;L&prime;. Also, on the circle passing through
X(2), X(13), X(14), X(111), and X(476), the antipode of X(2) is
X(5466). &nbsp;&nbsp; (Randy Hutson, May 3 2013)
</p><p>
The circle just described is here given the name <b>Hutson-Parry circle</b>.  (CK, October 31, 2015).  The circle is mentioned briefly in TCCT, page 228.
</p><p>
In the plane of a triangle ABC, let<br>
&Gamma; = circumcircle;<br>
D = line tangent to $Gamma; at A;<br>
Ta = D&cap;BC;<br>
Oab = center of the circle through Ta that is tangent to &Gamma; at B;<br>
Oac = center of the circle through Ta that is tangent to &Gamma; at C;<br>
Ab = orthogonal projection of Oab onto AC;<br>
Ac = orthogonal projection of Oac onto AB;<br>
A' = AbAc&cap;BC, and define B' and C' cyclically.<br>
The lines AA', BB', CC' concur in X(5466).  See <a href="X(5466).png"><b>X(5466)</b></a>  (Angel Montesdeoca, August 21, 2022)
</p><p>
