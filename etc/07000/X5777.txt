Barycentrics&nbsp;&nbsp; f(a,b,c) : f(b,c,a) : f(c,a,b),
where f(a,b,c) = a*(a^5*b - a^4*b^2 - 2*a^3*b^3 + 2*a^2*b^4 + a*b^5 -
b^6 + a^5*c + 2*a^2*b^3*c - a*b^4*c - 2*b^5*c - a^4*c^2 + b^4*c^2 -
2*a^3*c^3 + 2*a^2*b*c^3 + 4*b^3*c^3 + 2*a^2*c^4 - a*b*c^4 + b^2*c^4 +
a*c^5 - 2*b*c^5 - c^6)

<p>Let P be a point in the plane of a triangle ABC and let A&prime;B&prime;C&prime; be
the cevian triangle of P. Let H<sub>A</sub> be the orthocenter of
triangle AB&prime;C&prime;, and define H<sub>B</sub> and H<sub>C</sub> cyclically.
Then the points A&prime;, B&prime;, C&prime;, H<sub>A</sub>, H<sub>B</sub>, H<sub>C</sub>
lie on a conic. (Dominik Burek (ADGEOM #424, Aug 2, 2013)</p>

<p>The conic {A&prime;, B&prime;, C&prime;, H<sub>A</sub>, H<sub>B</sub>, H<sub>C</sub>}
is here named the Burek-cevian conic of P. If P = X(8), the conic has
center X(5777). More generally, if P is on the Lucas cubic, then the
triangles A&prime;B&prime;C&prime; and H<sub>A</sub>H<sub>B</sub>H<sub>C</sub> are
homothetic, and H<sub>A</sub>H<sub>B</sub>H<sub>C</sub> is perspective
to ABC at a point on the Darboux cubic. (ADGEOM #431, August 3, 2013,
and related postings)</p>

<p>X(5777) = (X(5) of 2nd extouch triangle); see X(5776). Also, X(5777)

<a href="https://bernard-gibert.pagesperso-orange.fr/Exemples/k645.html" style=
"font-weight: bold">K645</a>.</p>
