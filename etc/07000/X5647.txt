<h3 id="X5647">X(5647)&nbsp;=&nbsp; HATZIPOLAKIS-EULER POINT</h3>
Barycentrics&nbsp;&nbsp; f(A,B,C) : f(B,C,A) : f(C,A,B),
where f(A,B,C) = (sin A cos(B - C))(4 cos<sup>4</sup>A + (-1 + 4
cos<sup>2</sup>(B - C)) cos<sup>2</sup>A + cos(B - C)(4 cos A + cos(B -
C)) &nbsp;&nbsp; (C&eacute;sar Lozada, May 18, 2014)

<p>Let A&prime;B&prime;C&prime; be the cevian triangle of the circumcenter. Let</p>

<p>L<sub>AB</sub> = reflection of AA&prime; in AB, L<sub>AC</sub> =
reflection of AA&prime; in AC; H<sub>AB</sub> = orthogonal projection of A&prime;
on L<sub>AB</sub>, H<sub>AC</sub> = orthogonal projection of A&prime; on
L<sub>AC</sub>, and define L<sub>BC</sub>, L<sub>CA</sub>,
L<sub>BA</sub>, L<sub>CB</sub>, and H<sub>BC</sub>, H<sub>CA</sub>,
H<sub>BA</sub>, H<sub>CB</sub> cyclically,</p>

<p>M<sub>11</sub> = midpoint(H<sub>AB</sub>, H<sub>AC</sub>),
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; M<sub>12</sub> = midpoint(H<sub>BC</sub>,
H<sub>BA</sub>), &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; M<sub>13</sub> =
midpoint(H<sub>CA</sub>, H<sub>CB</sub>)<br>
M<sub>21</sub> = midpoint(H<sub>BA</sub>, H<sub>CA</sub>),
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; M<sub>22</sub> = midpoint(H<sub>CB</sub>,
H<sub>AB</sub>), &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; M<sub>23</sub> =
midpoint(H<sub>AC</sub>, H<sub>BC</sub>)<br>
M<sub>31</sub> = midpoint(H<sub>BC</sub>, H<sub>CB</sub>),
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; M<sub>32</sub> = midpoint(H<sub>CA</sub>,
H<sub>AC</sub>), &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; M<sub>33</sub> =
midpoint(H<sub>AB</sub>, H<sub>BA</sub>).</p>
<p>Antreas Hatzipolakis (<a href="http://www.hyacinthos.epizy.com/message.php?msg=22297"
class="bold">Anopolis</a> , May 17, 2014) asked if the
Euler lines of the triangles
M<sub>11</sub>M<sub>12</sub>M<sub>13</sub>,
M<sub>21</sub>M<sub>22</sub>M<sub>23</sub>,
M<sub>31</sub>M<sub>32</sub>M<sub>33</sub> concur, and C&eacute;sar Lozada
established that they do. The point of concurrence is X(5647).</p>
