<h3 id="X6596">X(6596)&nbsp;=&nbsp; (1st SCHIFFLER, 2nd SCHIFFLER) CYCLOLOGIC CENTER</h3>
Barycentrics &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;  a (a^9 + 10 a^7 b c - 3 a^8 (b + c) + (b - c)^6 (b + c)^3 - a (b - c)^4 (b + c)^2 (3 b^2 - 2 b c + 3 c^2) + a^6 (8 b^3 - 6 b^2 c - 6 b c^2 + 8 c^3) - a^5 (6 b^4 + 12 b^3 c - 13 b^2 c^2 + 12 b c^3 + 6 c^4) - a^4 (6 b^5 - 18 b^4 c + 7 b^3 c^2 + 7 b^2 c^3 - 18 b c^4 + 6 c^5) - a^2 b c (6 b^5 - 13 b^4 c + 11 b^3 c^2 + 11 b^2 c^3 - 13 b c^4 + 6 c^5) + a^3 (8 b^6 - 6 b^5 c - 9 b^4 c^2 + 26 b^3 c^3 - 9 b^2 c^4 - 6 b c^5 + 8 c^6)) : :
<p>
Continuing from X(6595), let W = X(3065).  Let C&prime;<sub>2</sub> be the point, other than S, of intersection of the Euler line of the triangle WCA and the circle (C, |CS|).   Let B&prime;<sub>3</sub> be the point, other than S, of intersection of the Euler line of the triangle WAB and the circle (B, |BS|).  Let O&prime;<sub>1</sub> be the circumcenter of SC<sub>2</sub>B<sub>3</sub>, and define O&prime;<sub>2</sub> and O&prime;<sub>3</sub> cyclically.  The triangle O&prime;<sub>1</sub>O&prime;<sub>2</sub>O&prime;<sub>3</sub> is here named the 2nd Schiffler triangle.The triangles ABC,
O<sub>1</sub>O<sub>2</sub>O<sub>3</sub>, and O&prime;<sub>1</sub>O&prime;<sub>2</sub>O&prime;<sub>3</sub> and pairwise cyclologic:
</p>
<p>
X(6595) = (ABC, O<sub>1</sub>O<sub>2</sub>O<sub>3</sub>)-cyclologic center<br>
X(3065) = (O<sub>1</sub>O<sub>2</sub>O<sub>3</sub>, ABC)-cyclologic center<br>
X(1320) = (ABC, O&prime;<sub>1</sub>O&prime;<sub>2</sub>O&prime;<sub>3</sub>)-cyclologic center<br>
X(1) = (O&prime;<sub>1</sub>O&prime;<sub>2</sub>O&prime;<sub>3</sub>, ABC)-cyclologic center<br>

</p>
