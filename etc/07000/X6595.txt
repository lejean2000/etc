<h3 id="X6595">X(6595)&nbsp;=&nbsp; (ABC-1st-SCHIFFLER)  CYCLOLOGIC CENTER</h3>
Barycentrics &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;  a (a^9+a^7 (-6 b^2+7 b c-6 c^2)-(b-c)^4 (b+c)^3 (2 b^2+5 b c+2 c^2)+a^6 (2 b^3-3 b^2 c-3 b c^2+2 c^3)+a (b^2-c^2)^2 (3 b^4-b^3 c+2 b^2 c^2-b c^3+3 c^4)+a^5 (12 b^4-15 b^3 c+13 b^2 c^2-15 b c^3+12 c^4)+a^4 (-6 b^5+3 b^4 c+5 b^3 c^2+5 b^2 c^3+3 b c^4-6 c^5)+a^3 (-10 b^6+9 b^5 c-3 b^4 c^2+5 b^3 c^3-3 b^2 c^4+9 b c^5-10 c^6)+a^2 (6 b^7+3 b^6 c-11 b^5 c^2+b^4 c^3+b^3 c^4-11 b^2 c^5+3 b c^6+6 c^7)) : :
<p>
Let I = X(1), the incenter of a triangle ABC.  Let L<sub>A</sub> be the Euler line of triangle IBC, and define L<sub>B</sub> and L<sub>C</sub> cyclically.  These lines concur in S = X(21), the Schiffler point of ABC.  Let C<sub>2</sub> be the point, other than S, of intersection of the line L<sub>B</sub> and the circle (C, |CS|).   Let B<sub>3</sub> be the point, other than S, of intersection of the line L<sub>C</sub> and the circle (B, |BS|).  Let O<sub>1</sub> be the circumcenter of SC<sub>2</sub>B<sub>3</sub>, and define O<sub>2</sub> and O<sub>3</sub> cyclically.  The triangle O<sub>1</sub>O<sub>2</sub>O<sub>3</sub> is here named the 1st Schiffler triangle.  The triangles ABC and O<sub>1</sub>O<sub>2</sub>O<sub>3</sub> are cyclologic, and X(6595) is the (ABC, O<sub>1</sub>O<sub>2</sub>O<sub>3</sub>)-cyclologic center.  See X(6596)-X(6599) and
<a href="http://www.hyacinthos.epizy.com/message.php?msg=23098">Hyacinthos 23098</a> and
<a href="http://www.hyacinthos.epizy.com/message.php?msg=23101">Hyacinthos 23101</a>.   (S. Kirikami, A. Hatzipolakis and A.Montesdeoca, February 4-5, 2015)
</p>
<p>
The points O<sub>1</sub>, O<sub>2</sub>, O<sub>3</sub> lie on the Feuerbach hyperbola.  (Randy Hutson, September 14, 2016)
</p>
<p>
