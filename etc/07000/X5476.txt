<h3 id="X5476">X(5476)&nbsp;=&nbsp; MIDPOINT OF X(6) AND X(381)</h3>
Barycentrics&nbsp;&nbsp; f(a,b,c) : f(b,c,a) : f(c,a,b),
where f(a,b,c) = a<sup>6</sup> + b<sup>6</sup> + c<sup>6</sup> -
4a<sup>4</sup>b<sup>2</sup> - 4a<sup>4</sup>c<sup>2</sup> +
2a<sup>2</sup>b<sup>4</sup> + 2a<sup>2</sup>c<sup>4</sup> -
6a<sup>2</sup>b<sup>2</sup>c<sup>2</sup> - b<sup>4</sup>c<sup>2</sup> -
b<sup>2</sup>c<sup>4</sup> &nbsp;&nbsp; (Peter Moses, May 12, 2013)

<p>Let A&prime; be the intersection, other than X(4), of the A-altitude and
the orthocentroidal circle, and define B&prime; and C&prime; cyclically. The
triangle A&prime;B&prime;C&prime;, introduced here as the orthocentroidal triangle, is
inversely similar to ABC, with center X(6) of similitude. If, in that
definition, X(4) replaced by X(2) and A-altitude by A-median, the
resulting triangle is the 4th Brocard triangle. Regarding a point X as
a function of a triangle, X(A&prime;B&prime;C&prime;) - that is, X of A&prime;B&prime;C&prime; - is the
reflection of X(ABC) in the centroid of the pedal triangle of X.
X(5476) = X(182) of the orthocentroidal triangle, and X(5476) = X(182)
of the 4th Brocard triangle. Also, X(5476) =
inverse-in-Kiepert-hyperbola of X(5475), and X(5476) =
{X(13),X(14)}-harmonic conjugate of X(5475). &nbsp;&nbsp; (Randy
Hutson, May 7, 2013)</p>
<p>The vertices of the orthocentroidal triangle are given by Peter
Moses (June 17, 2014): A&prime; = a<sup>2</sup> : a<sup>2</sup> +
b<sup>2</sup> - c<sup>2</sup> : a<sup>2</sup> - b<sup>2</sup> +
c<sup>2</sup><br>
B&prime; = b<sup>2</sup> - c<sup>2</sup> + a<sup>2</sup> : b<sup>2</sup> :
b<sup>2</sup> + c<sup>2</sup> - a<sup>2</sup><br>
C&prime; = c<sup>2</sup> + a<sup>2</sup> - b<sup>2</sup> : c<sup>2</sup> -
a<sup>2</sup> + b<sup>2</sup> : c<sup>2</sup></p>
<p>
The orthocentroidal triangle is the circumsymmedial triangle of the 4th Brocard triangle.  (Randy Hutson, November 22, 2014)
</p>
Another construction of the orthocentroidal triangle:  Let NaNbNc and Na'Nb'Nc' be the inner and outer Napoleon triangles, resp.  Then A&prime; = NbNc'&cap;NcNb', and B&prime; and C&prime; are constructed cyclically; i.e, A&prime;B&prime;C&prime; is the cross-triangle of the inner and outer Napoleon triangles.  Also, A&prime; is the center of inverse similitude of ABC and the A-altimedial triangle, and cyclically for B&prime; and C&prime;.  (Randy Hutson, December 10, 2016)
