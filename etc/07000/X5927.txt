<h3 id="X5927">X(5927)&nbsp;=&nbsp; CENTROID OF 2nd EXTOUCH TRIANGLE</h3>
Barycentrics&nbsp;&nbsp; a{(b + c)[a<sup>4</sup> + 2a<sup>2</sup>bc - (b - c)<sup>2</sup>(b<sup>2</sup> + 4bc + c<sup>2</sup>)] - 2a[a<sup>2</sup>(b<sup>2</sup> + c<sup>2</sup>) - (b<sup>2</sup> - c<sup>2</sup>)<sup>2</sup>]} : : &nbsp;&nbsp; (Richard Hilton, March 2, 2015)

<p>The classical extouch triangle is regarded as the 1st extouch
triangle. The 2nd, 3rd, 4th, and 5th extouch triangles are defined by
Randy Hutson (July 11, 2014) as follows. Let A<sub>A</sub>,
A<sub>B</sub>, A<sub>C</sub> be the touchpoints of the A-excircle and
the lines BC, CA, AB, respectively, and define B<sub>B</sub>,
B<sub>C</sub>, B<sub>A</sub> and C<sub>C</sub>, C<sub>A</sub>,
C<sub>B</sub> cyclically.</p>

<p>Let A<sub>1</sub> = A<sub>A</sub>, B<sub>1</sub> = B<sub>B</sub>,
C<sub>1</sub> = C<sub>C</sub>; 1st extouch triangle =
A<sub>1</sub>B<sub>1</sub>C<sub>1</sub></p>

<p>Let A<sub>2</sub> =
B<sub>C</sub>B<sub>A</sub>&cap;C<sub>A</sub>C<sub>B</sub>, and define
B<sub>2</sub> and C<sub>2</sub> cyclically; 2nd extouch triangle =
A<sub>2</sub>B<sub>2</sub>C<sub>2</sub></p>

<p>Let A<sub>3</sub> =
C<sub>A</sub>A<sub>C</sub>&cap;A<sub>B</sub>B<sub>A</sub>, and define
B<sub>3</sub> and C<sub>3</sub> cyclically; 3rd extouch triangle =
A<sub>3</sub>B<sub>3</sub>C<sub>3</sub></p>

<p>Let A<sub>4</sub> =
B<sub>C</sub>C<sub>A</sub>&cap;B<sub>A</sub>C<sub>B</sub>, and define
B<sub>4</sub> and C<sub>4</sub> cyclically; 4th extouch triangle =
A<sub>4</sub>B<sub>4</sub>C<sub>4</sub></p>

<p>Let D<sub>1</sub> =
B<sub>B</sub>B<sub>C</sub>&cap;C<sub>C</sub>C<sub>A</sub>, and define
D<sub>2</sub> and D<sub>3</sub> cyclically. Let E<sub>1</sub> =
B<sub>B</sub>B<sub>A</sub>&cap;C<sub>C</sub>C<sub>B</sub>, and define
E<sub>2</sub> and E<sub>3</sub> cyclically. Define A<sub>5</sub> =
D<sub>2</sub>E<sub>2</sub>&cap;D<sub>3</sub>E<sub>3</sub>, and define
B<sub>5</sub> and C<sub>5</sub> cyclically; 5th extouch triangle =
A<sub>5</sub>B<sub>5</sub>C<sub>5</sub></p>

<p>Barycentric coordinates for A-vertices of the the five
triangles:<br>
A<sub>1</sub> = 0 : a - b + c : a + b - c<br>
A<sub>2</sub> = 2a(b + c) : - a<sup>2</sup> - b<sup>2</sup> +
c<sup>2</sup> : - a<sup>2</sup> + b<sup>2</sup> - c<sup>2</sup><br>
A<sub>3</sub> = 2a(b + c)(a - b + c)(a + b - c) : (a + b + c)(a - b -
c)(a<sup>2</sup> + b<sup>2</sup> - c<sup>2</sup>) : (a + b + c)(a - b -
c)(a<sup>2</sup> - b<sup>2</sup> + c<sup>2</sup>)<br>
A<sub>4</sub> = 2a(b + c)(a + b + c) : (a - b - c)(a<sup>2</sup> -
b<sup>2</sup> + c<sup>2</sup>) : (a - b - c)(a<sup>2</sup> +
b<sup>2</sup> - c<sup>2</sup>)<br>
A<sub>5</sub> = 2a(b + c)(a - b + c)(a + b - c) : (a - b - c)(a + b -
c)[b<sup>2</sup> + (a + c)<sup>2</sup>] : (a - b - c)(a - b +
c)[c<sup>2</sup> + (a + b)<sup>2</sup>]</p>

<p>A<sub>2</sub>B<sub>2</sub>C<sub>2</sub> is perspective to ABC and
A<sub>3</sub>B<sub>3</sub>C<sub>3</sub> at X(4).<br>
A<sub>2</sub>B<sub>2</sub>C<sub>2</sub> is homothetic to the excentral
triangle at X(9).<br>
A<sub>2</sub>B<sub>2</sub>C<sub>2</sub> is homothetic to the intouch
triangle at X(226).<br>
A<sub>2</sub>B<sub>2</sub>C<sub>2</sub> is perspective to the extouch
triangle and (extraversion triangle of X(65)) at X(72).<br>
A<sub>2</sub>B<sub>2</sub>C<sub>2</sub> is perspective to the
anticevian triangle of X(8) at X(329).<br>
A<sub>2</sub>B<sub>2</sub>C<sub>2</sub> is homothetic to the hexyl
triangle at X(1490).<br>
A<sub>2</sub>B<sub>2</sub>C<sub>2</sub> is perspective to the Feuerbach
triangle at X(442).<br>
A<sub>2</sub>B<sub>2</sub>C<sub>2</sub> is perspective to
A<sub>4</sub>B<sub>4</sub>C<sub>4</sub> at X(5928).<br>
A<sub>2</sub>B<sub>2</sub>C<sub>2</sub> is homothetic to the inner
Hutson triangle at X(5934).<br>
A<sub>2</sub>B<sub>2</sub>C<sub>2</sub> is homothetic to the outer
Hutson triangle at X(5935).<br>
A<sub>2</sub>B<sub>2</sub>C<sub>2</sub> is homothetic to the 2nd
circumperp triangle at X(405).<br>
A<sub>2</sub>B<sub>2</sub>C<sub>2</sub> is homothetic to the
inverse-in-incircle triangle (see X(5571) at X(5728).<br>
A<sub>2</sub>B<sub>2</sub>C<sub>2</sub> is homothetic to the
Hutson-intouch triangle at X(950).<br>
A<sub>2</sub>B<sub>2</sub>C<sub>2</sub> is perspective to the
Hutson-extouch triangle at X(442).</p>

<p>In the following list, the appearance of (i,j) means that (X(i) of
the 2nd extouch triangle) = X(j):<br>
(3,4), (4,72), (5,5777), (6,9), (25,329), (26,5812), (54, 442),
(184,226), (185,950), (195,3651), (578, 10), (647,1635), (1181,1)</p>

<p>A<sub>3</sub>B<sub>3</sub>C<sub>3</sub> is perspective to the
intouch triangle and (extraversion triangle of X(65)) at X(1439).<br>
A<sub>3</sub>B<sub>3</sub>C<sub>3</sub> is perspective to the extouch
triangle and A<sub>5</sub>B<sub>5</sub>C<sub>5</sub> at X(5930).<br>
A<sub>3</sub>B<sub>3</sub>C<sub>3</sub> is perspective to
A<sub>4</sub>B<sub>4</sub>C<sub>4</sub> at X(5929).<br>
A<sub>3</sub>B<sub>3</sub>C<sub>3</sub> is perspective to the
anticevian triangle of X(7) at X(5932).</p>

<p>A<sub>4</sub>B<sub>4</sub>C<sub>4</sub> is perspective to ABC at
X(69).<br>
A<sub>4</sub>B<sub>4</sub>C<sub>4</sub> is perspective to the intouch
triangle and A<sub>5</sub>B<sub>5</sub>C<sub>5</sub> at X(65).<br>
A<sub>4</sub>B<sub>4</sub>C<sub>4</sub> is perspective to the
anticevian triangle of X(7) at X(5933).</p>

<p>A<sub>5</sub>B<sub>5</sub>C<sub>5</sub> is perspective to ABC at
X(388).<br>
A<sub>5</sub>B<sub>5</sub>C<sub>5</sub> is perspective to the
anticevian triangle of X(7) at X(8).</p>

<p>X(5927) is also the centroid of the triangle formed by the polars of the incenter with respect to the excircles. (Randy Hutson, July 11, 2014)  Note added (11/25/2015):  for more about this triangle, named the Atik triangle, see the preamble to X(8580).
