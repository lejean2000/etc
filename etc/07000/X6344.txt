<h3 id="X6344">X(6344)&nbsp;=&nbsp;ABC-ORTHOLOGIC CENTER OF X(3)-HATZIPOLAKIS-LOZADA TRIANGLE</h3>
Trilinears &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; f(A,B,C) : f(B,C,A) : f(C,A,B), where f(A,B,C) = (sec A)[1 - 4 cos<sup>2</sup>B][1 - 4 cos<sup>2</sup>C]
<br>
Trilinears &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; tan A csc 3A : tan B csc 3B : tan C csc 3C
<br>
Barycentrics &nbsp;&nbsp; sin A tan A csc 3A : sin B tan B csc 3B : sin C tan C csc 3C
<br>
Barycentrics &nbsp;&nbsp; (sec A)/(4 sin A - 3 csc A) : (sec B)(4 sin B - 3 csc B) : (sec C)(4 sin C = 3 csc C)
<br>
Barycentrics &nbsp;&nbsp; (tan A)/(3 - 4 sin<sup>2</sup>A) : (tan B)/(3 - 4 sin<sup>2</sup>B) : (tan C)/(3 - 4 sin<sup>2</sup>C)

<p>
Suppose that P is a point in the plane of a triangle ABC.  Let P<sub>A</sub> be the reflection of P in line BC, and define Let P<sub>B</sub> and Let P<sub>C</sub> cyclically.  Let P<sub>A</sub><sub>B</sub> be the reflection of P<sub>A</sub> in P<sub>B</sub>, and let P<sub>A</sub><sub>C</sub> be the reflection of P<sub>A</sub> in P<sub>C</sub>.  Let N<sub>A</sub> be the nine-point center of the triangle P<sub>A</sub>P<sub>A</sub><sub>B</sub>P<sub>A</sub><sub>C</sub>, and define N<sub>B</sub> and N<sub>C</sub> cyclically.  The triangle N<sub>A</sub>N<sub>B</sub>N<sub>C</sub> is the P-Hatzipolakis-Lozada triangle.  For P = X(3), the ABC-orthologic center of this triangle is X(6344).  See also X(6345) and X(5346).  These four orthologic centers, and also X(1), X(4), X(5), X(1113), and X(1114) lie on the excentral-circum-septic curve described at
<a href="http://www.hyacinthos.epizy.com/message.php?msg=23055" class="bold">Hyacinthos 23055</a>.  (Antreas Hatzipolakis and C&eacute;sar Lozada, January 23, 2015)
</p>
