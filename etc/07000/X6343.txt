<h3 id="X6343">X(6343)&nbsp;=&nbsp;HATZIPOLAKIS-LOZADA-EULER REFLECTIONS POINT</h3>
Trilinears &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; f(A,B,C) : f(B,C,A) : f(C,A,B), where f(A,B,C) = 4 cos A - 4 cos 3A + 2 cos 5A - 4 cos 7A
<br>
+ cos(B - C) (7 + 10 cos 2A + 4 cos 4A - 2 cos 6A)
<br>
+ 2 cos(2B - 2C) (cos A + 3 cos 3A - 3 cos 5A)
<br>
+ 2 cos(3B - 3C) (2 + cos 2A + cos 4A)
<br>
+ 2 cos(4B - 4C) (cos A - cos 3A)

<p>
Let N the the nine-point center of a triangle ABC, and let N<sub>A</sub> the reflection of N in line BC.  Define N<sub>B</sub> and N<sub>C</sub> cyclically.  Let N<sub>AB</sub> be the reflection of N<sub>A</sub> in line NB, and define N<sub>BC</sub> and N<sub>CA</sub> cyclically.  Let N<sub>AC</sub> be the reflection of N<sub>A</sub> in line NC, and define N<sub>BA</sub> and N<sub>CB</sub> cyclically.  Let L<sub>A</sub> be the Euler line of triangle N<sub>A</sub>N<sub>AB</sub>N<sub>AC</sub>, and define L<sub>B</sub> and L<sub>C</sub> cyclically.  Let L&prime;<sub>A</sub> be the reflection of L<sub>A</sub> in line BC, and define L&prime;<sub>B</sub> and L&prime;<sub>C</sub> cyclically.  The lines L&prime;<sub>A</sub>, L&prime;<sub>B</sub>, L&prime;<sub>C</sub> concur in X(6343).  (Antreas Hatzipolakis and C&eacute;sar Lozada, January 22, 2015; see
<a href="http://www.hyacinthos.epizy.com/message.php?msg=23050" class="bold">Hyacinthos 23050</a>)
</p>
