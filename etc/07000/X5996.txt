<h3 id="X5996">X(5996)&nbsp;=&nbsp; INTERSECTION OF LINES X(2)X(512) AND X(325)X(523)</h3>
Barycentrics &nbsp;&nbsp; b<sup>2</sup>c<sup>2</sup>(4a<sup>2</sup> - 3b<sup>2</sup> - 3c<sup>2</sup>) : :
<p>
Let L be the de Longchamps line of ABC, and let L&prime; be the de
Longchamps line of the 1st anti-Brocard triangle. Then X(5996) =
L&cap;L&prime;. Let O&prime; be the inverter of the circumcircle and the nine-point
circle; then X(5996) is the pole of the Brocard axis with respect to
O&prime;. (Randy Hutson, August 22, 2014)
</p>
<p>
X(5996) lies on these lines: {2,512}, {262,5466}, {325,523}, {1180,2524}, {1499,1513}
</p>
