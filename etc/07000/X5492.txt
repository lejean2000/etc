<p>Let T be the orthocentric triangle
H<sub>A</sub>H<sub>B</sub>H<sub>C</sub> of X(1), as defined at X(5484).
X(5492) is the orthocenter of T, and T is perspective to the Fuhrmann
triangle with perspector X(1), and T is perspective to the
anticomplementary triangle, with perspector X(5484). &nbsp;&nbsp;
(Peter Moses, May 17, 2013)</p>

<p>T is similar to the incentral triangle, with center of similitude I,
the incenter. Let A&Prime;B&Prime;C&Prime; be the antipedal triangle of X(1) with
repect to the incentral triangle. X(5492) is the nine-point center of
A&Prime;B&Prime;C&Prime;. (The triangle A&Prime;B&Prime;C&Prime; is also the triangle formed by the
lines L<sub>A</sub>, L<sub>B</sub>, L<sub>C</sub>, where L<sub>A</sub>
is the polar of A with respect to the circle BCI, and L<sub>B</sub> and
L<sub>C</sub> are defined cyclically.) &nbsp;&nbsp; (Randy Hutson, May
18, 2013)</p>
