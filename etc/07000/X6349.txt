<h3 id="X6349">X(6349)&nbsp;=&nbsp; 1st SODDY HOMOTHETIC CENTER</h3>
Barycentrics &nbsp;&nbsp; 1 + sec B + sec C : 1 + sec C + sec A : 1 + sec A + sec B
<br>
Barycentrics &nbsp;&nbsp; (a^2 - b^2 - c^2) (a^4 + 2 a^3 (b + c) - 2 a (b - c)^2 (b + c) - (b^2 - c^2)^2) : :

<p>
Let E<sub>A</sub> be the ellipse that passes through A and has foci B and C.  Call E<sub>A</sub> the A-Soddy ellipse.  Let L<sub>A</sub> be the polar of X(3) with respect to E<sub>A</sub>.  Define L<sub>B</sub> and L<sub>B</sub> cyclically.  Let A&prime; = L<sub>B</sub>&cap;L<sub>C</sub>, B&prime; = L<sub>C</sub>&cap;L<sub>A</sub>, C&prime; = L<sub>A</sub>&cap;L<sub>B</sub>.  Then triangle A&prime;B&prime;C&prime; is homothetic to the medial triangle, with homothethic center X(6349).  Also, A&prime;B&prime;C&prime; is homothetic to ABC, with homthetic center X(281).  The orthocenter of A&prime;B&prime;C&prime; is X(6361).  (Randy Hutson, January 30, 2015)
</p>
