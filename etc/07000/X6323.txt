<h3 id="X6323">X(6323)&nbsp;=&nbsp; CIRCUMMEDIAL-TRIANGLE-PARALLELOGIC CENTER OF 2nd BROCARD TRIANGLE</h3>
Trilinears&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; f(a,b,c) : f(b,c,a) : f(c,a,b), where f(a,b,c) =
a/(4*a^4-(b^2+c^2)*(a^2)-2*(b^4+c^4-b^2*c^2))
<br>
Barycentrics&nbsp;&nbsp; g(a,b,c) : g(b,c,a) : g(c,a,b), where g(a,b,c) = a^2 (2 a^4-2 a^2 b^2+2 b^4+a^2 c^2+b^2 c^2-4 c^4) (2 a^4+a^2 b^2-4 b^4-2 a^2 c^2+b^2 c^2+2 c^4)
<p>
See C&eacute;sar Lozada,
<a href="https://docs.google.com/uc?export=download&id=0BxTBrium3DhVbGR0X0pDNEFGTzQ" class="bold">Perspective-Orthologic-Parallelogic</a>.
</p>
<p>
Let A&prime;B&prime;C&prime; be the circumsymmedial triangle.  Let L<sub>A</sub> be the reflection of the Brocard axis in line B&prime;C&prime;, and define L<sub>B</sub> and L<sub>C</sub> cyclically.   Let A&Prime; = L<sub>B</sub>&cap;L<sub>C</sub>, B&Prime; = L<sub>C</sub>&cap;L<sub>A</sub>, C&Prime; = L<sub>A</sub>&cap;L<sub>B</sub>.  The lines A&prime;A&Prime;, B&prime;B&Prime;, C&prime;C&Prime; concur in X(6323).  Also, X(6323)-of-orthocentroidal-triangle = X(99)-of-4th-Brocard-triangle.  (Randy Hutson, January 29, 2015)
</p>
<p>

</p>
