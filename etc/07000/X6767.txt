<h3 id="X6767">X(6767)&nbsp;=&nbsp;CENTER OF THE APOLLONIAN CIRCLE OF THE MIXTILINEAR CIRCLES</h3>
Trilinears &nbsp;&nbsp; 4 + cos A : 4 + cos B : 4 + cos C
<b><br>
X(6767) = 4X(1) - X(1159) = 4R*X(1) + r*X(3)
</b>
<p>
Let A&prime;B&prime;C&prime; be a triangle in the poristic system of triangles (triangles that share the same incircle and circumcircle with ABC).  Let X be the trilinear pole of line X(1)X(3) wrt A&prime;B&prime;C&prime;.  The locus of X as A&prime;B&prime;C&prime; varies is an ellipse, E, centered at X(3) with vertices X(1381) and X(1382).  E is the poristic locus of X(651).  Let F<sub>1</sub> and F<sub>2</sub> be the foci of E.  Then X(6767) is the {F<sub>1</sub>,F<sub>2</sub>}-harmonic conjugate of X(1).
</p>
<p>
Let L be a line through X(1), and let P and Q be the circumcircle intercepts of L.  Let U be the {X(1),P}-harmonic conjugate of Q.  The locus of U as L varies is an ellipse centered at X(6767).
</p>
<p>
Let (Ap) denote the Apollonian circle of the mixtilinear circles.  The touch point of (Ap) and the A-mixtilinear circle is the point with barycentrics a(a^2+2 a b-3 b^2+2 a c+6 b c-3 c^2) : -2 b^2 (-a+b-c) : 2 c^2 (a+b-c)}.  (Peter Moses, October 4, 2015)
</p>
<p>
The vertices of the <b>8th mixtilinear triangle</b> are the touchpoints of the inner Apollonian circle of the mixtilinear incircles, with A-vertex, A&prime;, given by these trilinears:
</p>
<p>
A&prime; = a^2 + 2*a*b - 3*b^2 + 2*a*c + 6*b*c - 3*c^2 : -2*b*(-a + b - c) : 2*(a + b - c)*c (Dan Reznik and Peter Moses, September 10, 2021).
</p>
