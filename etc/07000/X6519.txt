<h3 id="X6519">X(6519)&nbsp;=&nbsp; CENTER OF MOSES-LUCAS CIRCLE</h3>
Trilinears &nbsp;&nbsp;  &nbsp;&nbsp;11 cos A + 6 sin A : :
<br>
Barycentrics &nbsp;&nbsp;  a^2 (11 S<sub>A</sub> + 6 S) : :
<p>
X(6519) lies on these lines: {3,6}, {3628,6459}, {5072,6561}, {5079,5418}
</p>
<p>
Let (R) be the radical circle of the Lucas circles, and let (L) = inverse in (R) of the circumcircle.  Then (L) tangent to each of the 3 Lucas circles.  Let A&prime; be the touchpoint of (L) and the A-Lucas circle, and define B&prime; and C&prime; cyclically.
Then
</p>
<p>
A&prime; = a<sup>2</sup>(3S + 4S<sub>A</sub>) : 2b<sup>2</sup> (S + 2S<sub>B</sub>) : 2c<sup>2</sup> (S + 2S<sub>C</sub>)  <br>
B&prime; = 2a<sup>2</sup> (S + 2S<sub>A</sub>) : b<sup>2</sup>(3S + 4S<sub>B</sub>) : 2c<sup>2</sup> (S + 2S<sub>C</sub> )  <br>
C&prime; = 2a<sup>2</sup> (S + 2S<sub>A</sub>) : 2b<sup>2</sup> (S + 2S<sub>B</sub>) : c<sup>2</sup> (3S + 4S<sub>C</sub>).
</p>
<p>
Let A&prime;<sub>B</sub> = inverse-in-A-Lucas-circle of B&prime;, and define B&prime;<sub>C</sub> and C&prime;<sub>A</sub> cyclically.  Let A&prime;<sub>C</sub> = inverse-in-A-Lucas-circle of C&prime;, and define B&prime;<sub>A</sub> and C&prime;<sub>B</sub> cyclically.  The six points A&prime;<sub>B</sub>, B&prime;<sub>C</sub>, C&prime;<sub>A</sub>, A&prime;<sub>C</sub>, B&prime;<sub>A</sub>, C&prime;<sub>B</sub> lie on a circle with center X(6519).  The circle is here called the Moses-Lucas Circle.  It and the circle constructed from 6 points at X(6449) are members of the Schoute coaxal system; the Schoute circle with center
</p>
<p>
a<sup>2</sup>(pS<sub>A</sub> + qS) : b<sup>2</sup>(pS<sub>B</sub> + qS) : c<sup>2</sup>(pS<sub>C</sub> + qS) = pS*X(3) + qS<sub>&omega;</sub>*X(6)
</p>
<p>
has radius R(S(p<sup>2</sup> - 3q<sup>2</sup>)<sup>1/2</sup>) / (pS + qS<sub>&omega;</sub>).    See X(6449) and X(6522).   (Peter Moses, February 11, 2015)
</p>
<p>
A combo for X(6519) is found as follows:  Let L<sub>A</sub> and R<sub>A</sub> be the center and radius of the A-Lucas circle, and define  L<sub>B</sub> and L<sub>C</sub>, and also
R<sub>B</sub> and R<sub>C</sub>, cyclically.  Let L* be the center of the Lucas inner circle and R* its radius.  Then X(6519) = L<sub>A</sub>/R<sub>A</sub> +
L<sub>B</sub>/R<sub>B</sub> + L<sub>C</sub>/R<sub>C</sub> + L*/R* + X(3)/R.  (Circle (L) is the Lucas inner circle, and A&prime;B&prime;C&prime; is the Lucas inner triangle.)  (Randy Hutson, February 12, 2015)
</p>
