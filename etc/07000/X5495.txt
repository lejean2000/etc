<h3 id="X5495">X(5495)&nbsp;=&nbsp; 3rd HATZIPOLAKIS-MONTESDEOCA POINT</h3>
Barycentrics &nbsp;&nbsp; f(a,b,c) : f(b,c,a) : f(c,a,b),
where f(a,b,c) = a<sup>2</sup>[a<sup>7</sup>(b + c) -
a<sup>6</sup>(b<sup>2</sup> + c<sup>2</sup>) -
a<sup>5</sup>(3b<sup>3</sup> + 2b<sup>2</sup>c + 2bc<sup>2</sup> +
3c<sup>3</sup>) + a<sup>4</sup>(3b<sup>4</sup> - b<sup>3</sup>c +
4b<sup>2</sup>c<sup>2</sup> - bc<sup>3</sup> + 3c<sup>4</sup>) +
a<sup>3</sup>(3b<sup>5</sup> + b<sup>4</sup>c +
2b<sup>3</sup>c<sup>2</sup> + 2b<sup>2</sup>c<sup>3</sup> +
bc<sup>4</sup> + 3c<sup>5</sup>) - a<sup>2</sup>(3b<sup>6</sup> -
2b<sup>5</sup>c - 2bc<sup>5</sup> + 3c<sup>6</sup>) - a(b<sup>7</sup> -
b<sup>4</sup>c<sup>3</sup> - b<sup>3</sup>c<sup>4</sup> +
c<sup>7</sup>) + (b<sup>2</sup> -
c<sup>2</sup>)<sup>2</sup>(b<sup>4</sup> - b<sup>3</sup>c -
bc<sup>3</sup> - b<sup>2</sup>c<sup>2</sup> + c<sup>4</sup>)]
&nbsp;&nbsp; (Angel Montesdeoca, May 28, 2013)

<p>Let ABC be a triangle and let A&prime;B&prime;C&prime; be the cevian triangle of the
incenter, X(1). Let L<sub>A</sub> be the line through A&prime; perpendicular
to line AA&prime;, and define L<sub>B</sub> add L<sub>C</sub> cyclically.
Let</p>

<p>U<sub>A</sub> = reflection of L<sub>A</sub> in AA&prime;<br>
U<sub>B</sub> = reflection of L<sub>A</sub> in BB&prime;<br>
U<sub>C</sub> = reflection of L<sub>A</sub> in CC&prime;<br>
</p>

<p>V<sub>A</sub> = reflection of L<sub>B</sub> in AA&prime;<br>
V<sub>B</sub> = reflection of L<sub>B</sub> in BB&prime;<br>
V<sub>C</sub> = reflection of L<sub>B</sub> in CC&prime;<br>
</p>

<p>W<sub>A</sub> = reflection of L<sub>C</sub> in AA&prime;<br>
W<sub>B</sub> = reflection of L<sub>C</sub> in BB&prime;<br>
W<sub>C</sub> = reflection of L<sub>C</sub> in CC&prime;<br>
</p>

<p>T<sub>A</sub> = triangle formed by the lines in U<sub>A</sub>,
U<sub>B</sub>, U<sub>C</sub><br>
T<sub>B</sub> = triangle formed by the lines in V<sub>A</sub>,
V<sub>B</sub>, V<sub>C</sub><br>
T<sub>C</sub> = triangle formed by the lines in W<sub>A</sub>,
W<sub>B</sub>, W<sub>C</sub><br>
</p>

<p>O<sub>A</sub> = circumcenter of T<sub>A</sub>, O<sub>B</sub> =
circumcenter of T<sub>A</sub>, O<sub>C</sub> = circumcenter of
T<sub>A</sub>, O = X(3) = circumcenter of ABC. The points O,
O<sub>A</sub>, O<sub>B</sub>, O<sub>C</sub> are concyclic. The center
of their circle is X(5495). &nbsp;&nbsp; (Antreas Hatzipolakis, May 28,
2013)</p>

<p>For the construction and discussion, see
<a href="http://anthrakitis.blogspot.gr/2013/05/concyclic-circumcenters.html">Concyclic
Circumcenters</a>.</p>

<p>X(5495) lies on these lines:  {1, 30}, {3, 143}, {511, 6097}, {6102, 7416}
</p>
