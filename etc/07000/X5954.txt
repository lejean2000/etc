<h3 id="X5954">X(5954)&nbsp;=&nbsp; 3rd HUTSON-FEUERBACH
POINT</h3>
Barycentrics&nbsp;&nbsp; f(a,b,c) : f(b,c,a) : f(c,a,b),
where f(a,b,c) = (b-c)^2 (-a^6+a^5 b+a^4 b^2-a^2 b^4-a b^5+b^6+a^5 c-2
a^2 b^3 c+b^5 c+a^4 c^2-3 a^2 b^2 c^2+4 a b^3 c^2-b^4 c^2-2 a^2 b c^3+4
a b^2 c^3-2 b^3 c^3-a^2 c^4-b^2 c^4-a c^5+b c^5+c^6)
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; (Peter Moses, August 10, 2014)

<p>Let A&prime;B&prime;C&prime; be the Feuerbach triangle, L the line through A and
X(11), and A&Prime; = L&cap;B&prime;C&prime;; and define B&Prime; and C&Prime; cyclically. The
lines A&prime;A&Prime;, B&prime;B&Prime;, C&prime;C&Prime; concur in X(5954). See also X(3614) and
X(3615). (Randy Hutson, August 5, 2014)</p>

<p>Continuing, let A* be the trilinear pole, with respect to A&prime;B&prime;C&prime;, of
line BC, and define B* and C* cyclically. Let A** be the trilinear
pole, with respect to A&prime;B&prime;C&prime;, of line B*C*, and define B** and C**
cyclically. The lines A&prime;A**, B&prime;B**, C&prime;C** concur in X(5954). (Randy
Hutson, August 5, 2014)</p>

<p>X(5954) lies on these lines: {5,2607}, {11,523}, {12,59}</p>
