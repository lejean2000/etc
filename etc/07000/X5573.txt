Barycentrics&nbsp;&nbsp; f(a,b,c) : f(b,c,a) : f(c,a,b),
where f(a,b,c) = a(a<sup>2</sup> + 3b<sup>2</sup> + 3c<sup>2</sup> -
6bc)

<p>Let A&prime; be the center of the inverse-in-incircle of the A-excircle,
and define B&prime; and C&prime; cyclically. The triangle with vertices A,&prime; B, C&prime;
is introduced here as the Andromeda triangle. A&prime;B&prime;C&prime; is a central
triangle of type 2. The following properties were communicated by Peter
Moses, November 9, 2013:</p>

<p>Barycentric coordinates for vertices:<br>
A&prime; = a[a<sup>2</sup> + 3(b - c)<sup>2</sup>] : b[3a<sup>2</sup> + (b -
c)<sup>2</sup>] : c[3a<sup>2</sup> + (b - c)<sup>2</sup>]<br>
B&prime; = a[3b<sup>2</sup> + (c - a)<sup>2</sup>] : b[b<sup>2</sup> + 3(c -

C&prime; = a[3c<sup>2</sup> + (a - b)<sup>2</sup>] : b[3c<sup>2</sup> + (a -
b)<sup>2</sup>] : c[c<sup>2</sup> + 3(a - b)<sup>2</sup>]</p>
