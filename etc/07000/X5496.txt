<h3 id="X5496">X(5496)&nbsp;=&nbsp; 4th HATZIPOLAKIS-MONTESDEOCA POINT</h3>
Barycentrics &nbsp;&nbsp; f(a,b,c) : f(b,c,a) : f(c,a,b),
where f(a,b,c) = a(b + c)[a<sup>5</sup> - 2a<sup>3</sup>(b<sup>2</sup>
+ c<sup>2</sup>) - a<sup>2</sup>bc(b + c) + a(b<sup>4</sup> -
b<sup>2</sup>c<sup>2</sup> + c<sup>4</sup>) + bc(b + c)(b -
c)<sup>2</sup>] &nbsp;&nbsp; (Angel Montesdeoca, May 29, 2013)

<p>Let ABC be a triangle and let A&prime;B&prime;C&prime; be the cevian triangle of the
incenter, X(1). Let L<sub>A</sub> be the line through A&prime; perpendicular
to line AA&prime;, and define L<sub>B</sub> add L<sub>C</sub> cyclically.
Using the notation at X(5495), let M<sub>AB</sub> be the line parallel
to U<sub>B</sub> through B&prime;, and let M<sub>AC</sub> be the line parallel
to U<sub>C</sub> through C&prime;.  Let A&Prime; = M<sub>AB</sub>&cap;M<sub>AC</sub>, and define B&Prime;
and C&Prime; cyclically. Let O<sub>A</sub> = circumcenter of A&Prime;B&prime;C&prime;, and
define O<sub>B</sub> and O<sub>C</sub> cyclically. Then the points
X(1), O<sub>A</sub>, O<sub>B</sub>, O<sub>C</sub> are concyclic, and
the center of their circle is X(5496). &nbsp;&nbsp; (Antreas
Hatzipolakis, May 29, 2013)</p>

Circles</a>.
