<h3 id="X6600">X(6600)&nbsp;=&nbsp; SS(a &#8594; 1 + cos A) OF X(3)</h3>
Barycentrics &nbsp;&nbsp; (1 + Cos[A])^2*(-1 + 2*Cos[A] + Cos[A]^2 - 2*Cos[B] - Cos[B]^2 - 2*Cos[C] - Cos[C]^2) : :
<p></p>
Suppose that X = f(a,b,c) : f(b,c,a) : f(c,a,b) (barycentrics).  Then "SS(a &#8594; 1 + cos A) of X" is the point f(1 + cos A, 1 + cos B, 1 + cos C) : f(1 + cos B, 1 + cos C, 1 + cos A) : f(1 + cos C, 1 + cos A, 1 + cos B).  See X(6337).

<p>
Let V = X(9), and let V<sub>A</sub>,V<sub>B</sub>,V<sub>C</sub> be the vertices of the anticevian triangle of V.  Then X(6600) is the centroid of {V,  V<sub>A</sub>, V<sub>B</sub>, V<sub>C</sub>}.  (Randy Hutson, February 20, 2015)
</p>
