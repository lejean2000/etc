<h3 id="X5484">X(5484)&nbsp;=&nbsp; INTERSECTION OF LINES X(2)X(12) AND X(8)X(38)</h3>
Barycentrics &nbsp;&nbsp; f(a,b,c) : f(b,c,a) : f(c,a,b),
where f(a,b,c) = a<sup>4</sup> - b<sup>4</sup> - c<sup>4</sup> -
a<sup>3</sup>b - a<sup>3</sup>c - 2a<sup>2</sup>b<sup>2</sup> -
2a<sup>2</sup>c<sup>2</sup> + a<sup>2</sup>bc - ab<sup>3</sup> -
ac<sup>3</sup> - 3a<sup>2</sup>bc - 3abc<sup>2</sup> - b<sup>3</sup>c -
bc<sup>3</sup> &nbsp;&nbsp; (Peter Moses, May 18, 2013)<br>
<b>X(5484) = 3X(2) - 2*X(1220)  </b>

<p>Let A&prime;B&prime;C&prime; be the cevian triangle of point X. Let A<sub>B</sub> =
(reflection of A&prime; in BB&prime;), and define B<sub>C</sub> and C<sub>A</sub>
cyclically. Let A<sub>C</sub> = (reflection of A&prime; in CC&prime;), and define
B<sub>A</sub> and C<sub>B</sub> cyclically. Let H<sub>A</sub> =
(orthocenter of A&prime;A<sub>B</sub>A<sub>C</sub>), and define H<sub>B</sub>
and H<sub>C</sub> cyclically. The orthocentric triangle of X is here
introduced as the (central) triangle
H<sub>A</sub>H<sub>B</sub>H<sub>C</sub>.&nbsp;&nbsp; (Antreas
Hatzipolakis, May 17, 2013)</p>

<p>For X = X(1), the orthocentric triangle
H<sub>A</sub>H<sub>B</sub>H<sub>C</sub> is perspective to the
anticomplementary triangle, and X(5484) is the perspector. &nbsp;&nbsp;
(Peter Moses, May 17, 2013)</p>

<p>Also, H<sub>A</sub>H<sub>B</sub>H<sub>C</sub> is perspective to ABC
at X(10). &nbsp;&nbsp; (Randy Hutson, May 18, 2013)</p>

<p>X(5484) is the crosspoint of X(1) and X(8) with respect to the
extraversion triangle of X(8). &nbsp;&nbsp; (Randy Hutson, August 26,
2014)</p>
