<h3 id="X6143">X(6143)&nbsp;=&nbsp; HATZIPOLAKIS-MOSES-EULER POINT</h3>
Barycentrics &nbsp;&nbsp; f(a,b,c) : f(b,c,a) : f(c,a,b), where f(a,b,c) = (a^2+b^2-c^2) (a^2-b^2+c^2) (a^6-3 a^4 b^2+3 a^2 b^4-b^6-3 a^4 c^2+3 a^2 b^2 c^2+b^4 c^2+3 a^2 c^4+b^2 c^4-c^6)
<br>
<b>
X(6143) = 3(J<sup>2</sup> - 2)*X(2) + 2 X(3), where J = |OH|/R<br>
X(6143) = 3(J<sup>2</sup> - 1)*X(2) - X(4)<br>
X(6143) = 2(J<sup>2</sup> - 1)*X(3) + (J^2 - 2)*X(4)
</b>
<p>
As a point on the Euler line, X(6143) has Shinagawa coefficients (8F, E).
</p>
<p>
Let A&prime;B&prime;C&prime; be the orthic triangle of ABC.  Let A&prime; be the nine-point center of OBC, where O = X(3), and define B&prime; and C&prime; cyclically.  Let A&Prime; be the orthogonal projection of A&prime; on the altitude A&prime;, and define B&Prime; and C&Prime; cyclically.  The Euler lines of the jfour triangles ABC, A&Prime;BC, AB&Prime;C, ABC&Prime; concur in X(6143).
(Antreas Hatzipolakis and Peter Moses, November 18, 2014; see
<a href="http://www.hyacinthos.epizy.com/message.php?msg=22767" class="bold">22767</a>)
</p>
