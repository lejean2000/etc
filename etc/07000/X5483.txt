<h3 id="X5483">X(5483)&nbsp;=&nbsp; CENTER OF HUTSON ELLIPSE</h3>
Barycentrics &nbsp;&nbsp; f(a,b,c) : f(b,c,a) : f(c,a,b),
where f(a,b,c) = a(a<sup>5</sup> + b<sup>5</sup> + c<sup>5</sup> +
a<sup>4</sup>b + a<sup>4</sup>c - 2a<sup>3</sup>b<sup>2</sup> -
2a<sup>3</sup>c<sup>2</sup> - 2a<sup>2</sup>b<sup>3</sup> -
2a<sup>2</sup>c<sup>3</sup> - 3a<sup>2</sup>b<sup>2</sup>c -
3a<sup>2</sup>bc<sup>2</sup> + ab<sup>4</sup> + ac<sup>4</sup> -
2ab<sup>3</sup>c -2abc<sup>3</sup> - 3ab<sup>2</sup>c<sup>2</sup> -
b<sup>3</sup>c<sup>2</sup> - b<sup>2</sup>c<sup>3</sup>) &nbsp;&nbsp;
(Peter Moses, May 17, 2013)<br>
<b>X(5483) = 4(r + R)*X(226) - (2r + R)*X(1029)  </b>

<p>Let A&prime;B&prime;C&prime; be the cevian triangle of the incenter. Let A<sub>B</sub>
= (reflection of A&prime; in BB&prime;), and define B<sub>C</sub> and C<sub>A</sub>
cyclically. Let A<sub>C</sub> = (reflection of A&prime; in CC&prime;), and define
B<sub>A</sub> and C<sub>B</sub> cyclically. The ellipse passing through
the points A<sub>B</sub>, A<sub>C</sub>, B<sub>C</sub>, B<sub>A</sub>,
C<sub>A</sub>, C<sub>B</sub> is here introduced as the Hutson Ellipse,
and X(5483) is its center. (Antreas Hatzipolakis, May 17, 2013)</p>

<p>X(5483) lies on these lines: {1, 5180}, {81, 593}, {226, 1029}, {3920, 5988}</p>
