<p>Suppose that P is a point in the plane of triangle ABC. Let A&prime;B&prime;C&prime;
be the anticevian triangle of P and let A&Prime;B&Prime;C&Prime; be the 1st circumperp
triangle. The locus of P for which the lines A&prime;A&Prime;, B&prime;B&Prime;, C&prime;C&Prime; concur
is the union of the line X(1)X(6) and the conic U = {A, B, C, X(66),
X(101), X(294), X(651)}; i.e., the isogonal conjugate of the Gergonne
line. The conic U has center X5452) and is given by the trilinear
equation</p>

<p>a(b + c - a)yz + b(c + a - b)zx + c(a + b - c)xy = 0.</p>
