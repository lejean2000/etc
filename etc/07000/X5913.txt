<h3 id="X5913">X(5913)&nbsp;=&nbsp; GERGONNE-STEINER POINT OF
QUADRANGLE X(13)X(14)X(15)X(16)</h3>
Barycentrics&nbsp;&nbsp; f(a,b,c) :
f(b,c,a) : f(c,a,b), where f(a,b,c) = 3 a^4 b^2 + 2 a^2 b^4 - b^6 + 3
a^4 c^2 - 10 a^2 b^2 c^2 + b^4 c^2 + 2 a^2 c^4 + b^2 c^4 - c^6

<p>X(5913) is the QA-P3 center of the quadrangle X(13)X(14)X(15)X(16);
see
<a href="http://www.chrisvantienhoven.nl/quadrangle-objects/10-mathematics/quadrangle-objects/18-qa-p3.html" style="font-style: italic; font-weight: bold">Gergonne-Steiner
Point</a>.</p>

<p>X(5913) = inverse-in-{circumcircle, nine-point circle}-inverter of
X(6); see X(5577) for the definition of inverter.</p>

<p>The {circumcircle, nine-point circle}-inverter is the orthopic
circle of the Steiner inscribed ellipse; its center is X(2), its radius
is [(a<sup>2</sup> + b<sup>2</sup> + c<sup>2</sup>)/18]<sup>1/2</sup>,
and the powers of A,B,C with respect to this circle are (-a<sup>2</sup>
+ b<sup>2</sup> + c<sup>2</sup>)/6, (a<sup>2</sup> - b<sup>2</sup> +
c<sup>2</sup>)/6, (a<sup>2</sup> + b<sup>2</sup> - c<sup>2</sup>)/6.
(Peter Moses, July 16, 2014)T</p>

<p>X(5913) lies on these lines:
{2,6}, {23,2079}, {30,111}, {112,468}, {115,858}, {403,1560}, {843,1302}, {1499,1513}, {2030,5642}</p>
