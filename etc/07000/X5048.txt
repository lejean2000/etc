<h3 id="X5048">X(5048)&nbsp;=&nbsp;INVERSE-IN-INCIRCLE OF X(3057)</h3>
Barycentrics&nbsp;&nbsp; a(b + c - a)(3b<sup>2</sup> + 3c<sup>2</sup> - 2a<sup>2</sup> + ab + ac - 6bc) : :
       <br>
X(5048) = (R - 3r)*X(1) + r*X(3)

<p>
Let I be the incenter of a triangle ABC, let N<sub>A</sub> be the
nine-point circle of the triangle IBC, and define N<sub>B</sub> and
N<sub>C</sub> cyclically. Let R<sub>A</sub> be the reflection of
N<sub>A</sub> in the line AI, and define R<sub>B</sub> and
R<sub>C</sub> cyclically. Then X(5048) is the radical center of the
circles R<sub>A</sub>, R<sub>B</sub>, R<sub>C</sub>.  See Antreas Hatzipolakis and Angel Montesdeoca,
<a href="http://www.hyacinthos.epizy.com/message.php?msg=22502"
class="bold">Hyacinthos #22502</a>, July 7, 2014. For another construction see Antreas Hatzipolakis and Peter Moses,
<a href="http://www.hyacinthos.epizy.com/message.php?msg=28076"
class="bold">Hyacinthos #28076</a>.
</p>
