<h3 id="X5507">X(5507)&nbsp;=&nbsp;5th HATZIPOLAKIS-YIU POINT</h3>
Barycentrics&nbsp;&nbsp; f(a,b,c) : f(b,c,a) : f(c,a,b),
where f(a,b,c) = a<sup>2</sup>(bc - 2S)[abc(b + c - a) +
2(b<sup>2</sup> + c<sup>2</sup> - a<sup>2</sup>)S]

<p>Let O<sub>A</sub> be the circle with center A and radius R, the
circumradius of triangle ABC. Let B<sub>A</sub> be the point where
O<sub>A</sub> meets line AB nearest to B. Define C<sub>B</sub> and
A<sub>C</sub> cyclically. Let C<sub>A</sub> be the point where
O<sub>A</sub> meets line AC nearest to C. Define A<sub>B</sub> and
B<sub>C</sub> cyclically. X(5507) is the radical center of the circles
AB<sub>A</sub>C<sub>A</sub>, BC<sub>B</sub>A<sub>B</sub>,
CA<sub>C</sub>B<sub>C</sub>. If "nearest to" is changed to "farthest
from" in the construction, the resulting point is X(600). See also
X(600). (Peter Moses, June 19, 2013)</p>

<a href="X(5507).gsp" class="bold">X(5507)</a>.</p>
