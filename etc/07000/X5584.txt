<h3 id="X5584">X(5584)&nbsp;=&nbsp; PERSPECTOR OF EXTANGENTS AND APUS TRIANGLES</h3>
Barycentrics &nbsp; a<sup>2</sup>(b<sup>5</sup> + c<sup>5</sup> - a<sup>5</sup> + a<sup>4</sup>b + a<sup>4</sup>c + 2a<sup>3</sup>b<sup>2</sup> + 2a<sup>3</sup>c<sup>2</sup> + 4a<sup>3</sup>bc - 2a<sup>2</sup>b<sup>3</sup> - 2a<sup>2</sup>c<sup>3</sup> + 2a<sup>2</sup>b<sup>2</sup>c + 2a<sup>2</sup>bc<sup>2</sup> - ab<sup>4</sup> - ac<sup>4</sup> - 4ab<sup>3</sup>c - 4abc<sup>3</sup> -  6ab<sup>2</sup>c<sup>2</sup> - 3b<sup>4</sup>c - 3bc<sup>4</sup> + 2b<sup>3</sup>c<sup>2</sup> +
2b<sup>2</sup>c<sup>3</sup>)
<p>
Let A&prime; be the insimilicenter of the circumcircle and A-excircle, and define B&prime; and C&prime; cyclically.  The triangle with vertices A,&prime; B, C&prime; is introduced here as the Apus triangle. A&prime;B&prime;C&prime; is a central triangle of type 2. The following properties were communicated by Peter Moses, November 12, 2013:
</p>
<p>
Barycentric coordinates for vertices:<br>
A&prime; = a<sup>2</sup>(a - b + c)(a + b - c) : b<sup>2</sup>(b - c - a)(a +
b + c) : c<sup>2</sup>(c - b - a)(a + b + c)<br>
B&prime; = a<sup>2</sup>(a - c - b)(a + b + c) : b<sup>2</sup>(b - c + a)(b +
c - a) : c<sup>2</sup>(c - a - b)(a + b + c)<br>
C&prime; = a<sup>2</sup>(a - b - c)(a + b + c) : b<sup>2</sup>(b - a - c)(a +
b + c) : c<sup>2</sup>(c - a + b)(c + a - b)
</p>
<p>
The triangle A&prime;B&prime;C&prime; is perspective to ABC at X(55), the excentral and hexyl triangles at X(3), the incentral triangle at X(56), the tangential triangle at X(198), the Feuerbach triangle at X(4), and the Apollonius triangle at X(573).
</p>
<p>
The Apus triangle is the extraversion triangle of X(56). (Randy Hutson, July 18, 2014)
</p>
<p>
Let A&prime;B&prime;C&prime; be the intouch triangle of the extangents triangle, if ABC is acute.  Then A&prime;B&prime;C&prime; is homothetic to the cevian triangle of X(3) at X(5584). (Randy Hutson, December 2, 2017)
</p>
