<h3 id="X5597">X(5597)&nbsp;=&nbsp; PERSPECTOR OF ABC AND 1st AURIGA TRIANGLE</h3>
Barycentrics&nbsp;&nbsp; f(a,b,c) : f(b,c,a) : f(c,a,b),
where f(a,b,c) = a<sup>4</sup> - a<sup>2</sup>(b + c)<sup>2</sup> +
4aS(rR + 4R<sup>2</sup>)<sup>1/2</sup>

<p>Let U be the inverter of the circumcircle and incircle, as described
at X(5577). There are two triangles that circumscribe U and are
homothetic to triangle ABC, one of which has A-vertex on the same side
of line BC as A. This triangle, A&prime;B&prime;C&prime;, is introduced here as the 1st
Auriga triangle, and the other, as the 2nd Auriga triangle. Each is the
reflection of the other in the center, X(55), of the inverter. The six
points A&prime;, B&prime;, C&prime;, A&Prime;, B&Prime;, C&Prime; lie on a conic introduced here as the
Auriga conic. Let D = (rR + 4R<sup>2</sup>)<sup>1/2</sup>; barycentrics
for the six points and conic were found by Peter Moses (Nov. 21,
2013):</p>

<p>A&prime; = a<sup>4</sup> - a<sup>2</sup>(b + c)<sup>2</sup> - 4(b + c)SD :
b<sup>4</sup> - b<sup>2</sup>(c + a)<sup>2</sup> + 4bSD : c<sup>4</sup>
- c<sup>2</sup>(a + b)<sup>2</sup> + 4cSD</p>

<p>A&Prime; = a<sup>4</sup> - a<sup>2</sup>(b + c)<sup>2</sup> + 4(b + c)SD
: b<sup>4</sup> - b<sup>2</sup>(c + a)<sup>2</sup> - 4bSD :
c<sup>4</sup> - c<sup>2</sup>(a + b)<sup>2</sup> - 4cSD</p>

<p>where B&prime;, C&prime;, B&Prime;, C&Prime; are determined cyclically.</p>

<p>The Auriga conic is given by {cyclic sum[g(a,b,c)x<sup>2</sup> +
h(a,b,c)yz} = 0, where</p>

<p>g(a,b,c) = bc(b + c - a)(b<sup>5</sup> + c<sup>5</sup> +
3a<sup>3</sup>bc + a<sup>2</sup>b<sup>3</sup> +
a<sup>2</sup>c<sup>3</sup> - a<sup>2</sup>b<sup>2</sup>c -
a<sup>2</sup>bc<sup>2</sup> + ab<sup>3</sup>c + abc<sup>3</sup> -
2ab<sup>2</sup>c<sup>2</sup> + 3b<sup>4</sup>c + 3bc<sup>4</sup> -
2b<sup>3</sup>c<sup>2</sup> - 2b<sup>2</sup>c<sup>3</sup>)</p>

<p>h(a,b,c) = a[a<sup>7</sup> - 2a<sup>6</sup>(b + c) -
a<sup>5</sup>(b<sup>2</sup> + c<sup>2</sup> - 4a<sup>5</sup>bc) +
4a<sup>4</sup>(b<sup>3</sup> + c<sup>3</sup>)<br>
- a<sup>3</sup>(b<sup>4</sup> + c<sup>4</sup> + 4b<sup>3</sup>c +
4bc<sup>3</sup> - 8b<sup>2</sup>c<sup>2</sup>)<br>
- 2a<sup>2</sup>(b<sup>5</sup> + c<sup>5</sup> - b<sup>4</sup>c -
bc<sup>4</sup> + 5b<sup>3</sup>c<sup>2</sup> +
5b<sup>2</sup>c<sup>3</sup>)<br>
+ a(b<sup>6</sup> + c<sup>6</sup> + b<sup>4</sup>c<sup>2</sup> +
b<sup>2</sup>c<sup>4</sup> - 4b<sup>3</sup>c<sup>3</sup>)<br>
+ 2(b<sup>5</sup>c<sup>2</sup> + b<sup>2</sup>c<sup>5</sup> -
b<sup>4</sup>c<sup>3</sup> - b<sup>3</sup>c<sup>4</sup>)</p>
<p>
The two Auriga triangles are perspective with perpsector X(55), which is the center of the Auriga conic.</p>
