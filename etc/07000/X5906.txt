<h3 id="X5906">X(5906)&nbsp;=&nbsp; ANTICOMPLEMENT OF X(255)</h3>
Barycentrics&nbsp;&nbsp; f(A,B,C) : f(B,C,A) : f(C,A,B),
where f(A,B,C) = sin B cos<sup>2</sup>B + sin C cos<sup>2</sup>C - sin
A cos<sup>2</sup>A

<p>Let A&prime;B&prime;C&prime; be the circumorthic triangle, and let L<sub>A</sub> be
the reflection of line B&prime;C&prime; in the internal bisector of angle A, and
define L<sub>B</sub> and L<sub>C</sub> cyclically. Let A&Prime; =
L<sub>B</sub>&cap;L<sub>C</sub>, and define B&Prime; and C&Prime; cyclically. The
triangle A&Prime;B&Prime;C&Prime; is homothetic to ABC at X(3075) and homothetic to
the anticomplementary triangle at X(5906). Let M<sub>A</sub> be the
reflection of the line B&prime;C&prime; in the external bisector of angle A, and
define M<sub>B</sub> and M<sub>C</sub> cyclically. Let A* =
M<sub>B</sub>&cap;M<sub>C</sub>, and define B* and C* cyclically. The
triangle A*B*C*' is homothetic to ABC at X(3074) and to homothetic to
the anticomplementary triangle at X(5906). (Randy Hutson, July 7,
2014)</p>

{2,255}, {8,79}, {69,349}, {78,1448}, {651,5125}, {860,3157}, {962,2817}, {1259,3936}, {1788,2406}, {3868,5081}</p>
