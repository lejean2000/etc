<h3 id="X5585">X(5585)&nbsp;=&nbsp; CENTER OF AQUARIUS CONIC</h3>
Barycentrics&nbsp;&nbsp; a<sup>2</sup>(11b<sup>2</sup> +
11c<sup>2</sup> - 13a<sup>2</sup>) : b<sup>2</sup>(11c<sup>2</sup> +
11a<sup>2</sup> - 13b<sup>2</sup>) : c<sup>2</sup>(11a<sup>2</sup> +
11b<sup>2</sup> - 13c<sup>2</sup>)

<p>Let A&prime;B&prime;C&prime; be the tangential triangle, so that A&prime; is the center of
the circle O<sub>A</sub> through B and C that is orthogonal to the
circumcircle (whence O<sub>A</sub> is self-inverse with respect to the
circumcircle). Define O<sub>B</sub> and O<sub>C</sub> cyclically. Let
O(A,B) be the circle which is the inverse-in-O<sub>A</sub> of
O<sub>B</sub>; define O(B,C) and O(C,A) cyclically. Let O(A,C) be the
circle which is the inverse-in-O<sub>A</sub> of O<sub>C</sub>; define
O(B,A) and O(C,B) cyclically. The centers of these six circles lie on a
conic, introduced here as the Aquarius conic, of which X(5585) is the
center. The following properties were found by Peter Moses (Nov. 18,
2013).</p>

<p>The centers of the 6 circles are given by the following
barycentrics:</p>

<p>- a<sup>2</sup> : b<sup>2</sup> :
3c<sup>2</sup>,&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; 3a<sup>2</sup> : -
b<sup>2</sup> : c<sup>2</sup> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;
a<sup>2</sup> : 3b<sup>2</sup> : - c<sup>2</sup>;<br>
- a<sup>2</sup> : 3b<sup>2</sup> :
c<sup>2</sup>,&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; a<sup>2</sup> : -
b<sup>2</sup> : 3c<sup>2</sup> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;
3a<sup>2</sup> : b<sup>2</sup> : - c<sup>2</sup></p>

<p>The radius of O(A,B) is abc/(-a<sup>2</sup> + b<sup>2</sup> +
3c<sup>2</sup>); the remaining 5 radii are found by cyclical and
bicentric modifications. The Aquarius conic has equation</p>

<p>b<sup>4</sup>c<sup>4</sup>x<sup>2</sup> +
c<sup>4</sup>a<sup>4</sup>y<sup>2</sup> +
a<sup>4</sup>b<sup>4</sup>z<sup>2</sup> +
11a<sup>2</sup>b<sup>2</sup>c<sup>2</sup>(a<sup>2</sup>yz +
b<sup>2</sup>zx + c<sup>2</sup>xy) = 0</p>

<p>The major axis of the Aquarius conic is the Brocard axis, and the
perspector is X(6). &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; (Randy Hutson, November
30, 2013)</p>

<p>X(5585) lies on these lines: {3,6}, {20,3054}, {439,3619},
{3055,3523}
</p>
