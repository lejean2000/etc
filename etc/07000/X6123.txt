<h3 id="X6123">X(6123)&nbsp;=&nbsp; 4th HATZIPOLAKIS-VAN TIENHOVEN POINT</h3>
Trilinears &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; cos A + cos(A/3) : cos B + cos(B/3) : cos C + cos(C/3)
<br>
Trilinears &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; sin(4A/3) csc(A/3) : sin(4B/3) csc(B/3) : sin(4C/3) csc(C/3)
<br>
Trilinears &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; cos(A/3) cos(2A/3) : cos(B/3) cos(2B/3) : cos(C/3) cos(2C/3)
<p>
In the plane of ABC, let A<sub>B</sub> be the line perpendicular to the internal trisector at A that is closer to B, and define B<sub>C</sub> and C<sub>A</sub> cyclically.  Let A<sub>C</sub> be the line perpendicular to the internal trisector of A that is closer to C, and define B<sub>A</sub> and C<sub>B</sub> cyclically.  Let Q<sub>A</sub> be the Miquel point (QL-P1) of the quadrilateral having vertices B<sub>C</sub>, B<sub>A</sub>, C<sub>A</sub>, C<sub>B</sub>, and define Q<sub>B</sub> and Q<sub>C</sub> cyclically.  The points Q<sub>A</sub>, Q<sub>B</sub>, Q<sub>C</sub> lie on the circumcircle of ABC, and the triangle Q<sub>A</sub>, Q<sub>B</sub>, Q<sub>C</sub>, here named the 4th Hatzipolakis-Van Tienhoven triangle, is perspective to ABC.  The perspector is X(6123).  There are 3 sets of trisectors, so that there are 5th and 6th triangles and 5th and 6th points, as found at X(6124) and X(6125).  (Antreas Hatzipolakis and Chris van Tienhoven, Hyacinthos, November 9, 2014; see
<a href="http://www.hyacinthos.epizy.com/message.php?msg=22720" class="bold">22720</a>)
</p>
<p>
X(6123) lies on these lines:  {3,358}, {357,3278}, {1135,6122}, {1136,3334}, {1137,6121}, {3273,6124}, {3274,6125}, {3281,3702}, {3335,3604}
<p>
X(6123) = isogonal conjugate of X(5457)<br>
X(6123) = Hofstadter 4/3 point<br>
X(6123) = {X(3),X(358)}-harmonic conjugate of X(6120)
</p>
