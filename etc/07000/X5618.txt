<h3 id="X5618">X(5618)&nbsp;=&nbsp; 1st MONTESDEOCA EQUILATERAL TRIANGLES POINT</h3>
Barycentrics &nbsp;&nbsp; f(a,b,c) : f(b,c,a) :
f(c,a,b), where f(a,b,c) = 1/[(b<sup>2</sup> -
c<sup>2</sup>)(27<sup>1/2</sup>b<sup>2</sup>c<sup>2</sup>S<sub>A</sub>
+ S(S<sup>2</sup> + 9S<sub>A</sub>S<sub>A</sub>))]

<p>Let AP, BP, CP be the cevians of a point P in the plane of a
triangle ABC. The A-positive Montesdeoca equilateral triangle is
constructed as follows: let L<sub>A</sub> be the line through A
perpendicular to CP; let U<sub>A</sub> be the 30-degree rotation of
L<sub>A</sub>, where the angle BAC, for present purposes, defines the
positive direction of rotation, and angle CAB, the negative (used in
X(5619)). Let A<sub>1</sub> = U<sub>A</sub>&cap;CP, let V<sub>A</sub>
be the - 60 degree rotation of CP about A<sub>1</sub>, let
A<sub>B</sub> = V<sub>A</sub>&cap;BP, let A<sub>C</sub> be the - 60
degree rotation of segment AA<sub>B</sub> about A. Then
AA<sub>B</sub>A<sub>C</sub> is an equilateral triangle. Define
BB<sub>C</sub>B<sub>A</sub> and CC<sub>A</sub>C<sub>B</sub> cyclically.
These are the positive Montesdeoca equilateral triangles. X(5618) is
the unique choice of P on the circumcircle of ABC for which the lines
A<sub>B</sub>A<sub>C</sub>, B<sub>C</sub>B<sub>A</sub>,
C<sub>B</sub>C<sub>A</sub> concur. For arbitrary P, the centers of the
three equilateral triangles are collinear with P; denote their line by
L(P). If P is on the circumcircle of ABC, then L(P) passes through
X(110). &nbsp;&nbsp; (Angel Montesdeoca, November 3, 2013)</p>

<p>For details, see
<a href="http://amontes.webs.ull.es/otrashtm/HechosGeometricos.htm#HG031113">Hechos
Geom&eacute;tricos en el Tri&aacute;ngulo</a>.</p>
