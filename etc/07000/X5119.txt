<h3 id="X5119">X(5119)&nbsp;=&nbsp;INVERSE-IN-BEVAN-CIRCLE OF X(3245)</h3>
Barycentrics&nbsp;&nbsp; f(a,b,c) : f(b,c,a) : f(c,a,b),
where f(a,b,c) = a(b<sup>3</sup> + c<sup>3</sup> - a<sup>3</sup> -

<p class="bold">X(5119) = (R - r)X(1) + 2r*X(3)<br>
X(5119) = r(r + 4R)*X(9) - R(2r - R)X(80)<br>

<p>
Let A&prime;B&prime;C&prime; be the orthic triangle of ABC.  Let L<sub>A</sub> be the antiorthic axis of AB&prime;C&prime;, and define L<sub>B</sub> and L<sub>C</sub> cyclically.  Let A&Prime; = L<sub>B</sub>&cap;L<sub>C</sub>, and define B&Prime; and C&Prime; cyclically.  Then A&Prime;B&Prime;C&Prime; is inversely similar to ABC, with similitude center X(9), and X(5119) = A&Prime;B&Prime;C&Prime;-to-ABC similarity image of X(1).  (Randy Hutson, December 4, 2014)
</p>
