<h3 id="X5494">X(5494)&nbsp;=&nbsp; 2nd HATZIPOLAKIS-MONTESDEOCA POINT</h3>
Barycentrics &nbsp;&nbsp; f(a,b,c) : f(b,c,a) : f(c,a,b),
where f(a,b,c) = a[a<sup>9</sup> - a<sup>8</sup>(b + c) -
a<sup>7</sup>( b - c)<sup>2</sup> + a<sup>6</sup>(2b<sup>3</sup> -

a<sup>5</sup>(3b<sup>4</sup> + b<sup>3</sup>c -
7b<sup>2</sup>c<sup>2</sup> + bc<sup>3</sup> + 3c<sup>4</sup>) +
4a<sup>4</sup>bc(b - c)<sup>2</sup>(b + c) +

- 4bc + 5c<sup>2</sup>) - a<sup>2</sup>(b -

&nbsp;&nbsp; (Angel Montesdeoca, May 25, 2013)<br>
<b>X(5494) = (2r + R)*X(110) - 4(r + R)X(1385)<br>
X(5494) = 2R*X(65) + (2r + R)*X(74)  </b>

<p>Let ABC be a triangle and let A&prime;B&prime;C&prime; be the cevian triangle of the
incenter, X(1). Let A<sub>B</sub> be the reflection of A&prime; in line BB&prime;,
and define B<sub>C</sub> and C<sub>A</sub> cyclically. Let
A<sub>C</sub> be the reflection of A&prime; in line CC&prime;, and define
B<sub>A</sub> and C<sub>B</sub> cyclically. Let L be the Euler line of
ABC, let L<sub>A</sub> be the Euler line of
AA<sub>B</sub>A<sub>C</sub>, and define L<sub>B</sub> and L<sub>C</sub>
cyclically. Let M<sub>A</sub> be the reflection of L<sub>A</sub> in
AA&prime;, and define M<sub>B</sub> and M<sub>C</sub> cyclically. The lines
M<sub>A</sub>, M<sub>B</sub>, M<sub>C</sub> concur in X(5494).
Moreover, the four Euler lines L, L<sub>A</sub>, L<sub>B</sub>,
L<sub>C</sub> are parallel, concurring in X(30). &nbsp;&nbsp; (Antreas
Hatzipolakis, May 25, 2013)</p>
