<h3 id="X5665">X(5665)&nbsp;=&nbsp; ISOGONAL CONJUGATE OF
X(3601)</h3>
Trilinears&nbsp;&nbsp; f(a,b,c) : f(b,c,a) : f(c,a,b),
where f(a,b,c) = 1/[(s - a)(s<sup>2</sup> - S<sub>A</sub>)]

<p>Let A&prime;B&prime;C&prime; be the intouch triangle of a triangle ABC. Let (BA&prime;) be
the circle having diameter BA&prime;, and likewise for (A&prime;C); define circles
(CB&prime;) and (AC&prime;) cyclically, and define circles (B&prime;A) and (C&prime;B)
cyclically. Let U be the point, other than A&prime;, in which (BA&prime;) meets the
incircle, and define V and W cyclically. Let U&prime; be the point, other
than A&prime;, in which (A&prime;C) meets the incircle, and define V&prime; and W&prime;
cyclically. Let A&Prime;B&Prime;C&Prime; be the triangle formed by the lines UU&prime;, VV&prime;,
WW&prime;. Let A&Prime;' be the point, other than A, in which the circles (AB&prime;)
and (AC&prime;) meet, and define B&tprime; and C&tprime; cyclically. Then A&Prime;B&Prime;C&Prime; is
perspective to ABC, and the perspector is X(5665). Moreover, A&Prime;B&Prime;C&Prime;
is perspective to A&Prime;B&Prime;C&Prime;, and the perspector is X(5666). (C&eacute;sar
Lozada ADGEOM #1155, March 8, 2014)</p>
