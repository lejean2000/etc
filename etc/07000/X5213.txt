<h3 id="X5213">X(5213)&nbsp;=&nbsp;INVERSE-IN-SPIEKER-RADICAL-CIRCLE OF
X(115)</h3>
Barycentrics&nbsp;&nbsp; f(a,b,c) : f(b,c,a) : f(c,a,b),
where f(a,b,c) = a(b + c)<sup>2</sup>(a<sup>2</sup> - ab + ac -
b<sup>2</sup>)(a<sup>2</sup> + ab - ac - c<sup>2</sup>)

<p>Let (N) be the nine-point circle of a triangle ABC. Let
(I<sub>A</sub>) be the A-excircle of ABC, and define (I<sub>B</sub>)
and (I<sub>C</sub>) cyclically. Let A&prime; be the midpoint of side BC, and
define B&prime; and C&prime; cyclically. Let (K<sub>A</sub>) be the circle, other
than (N), that passes through B&prime; and C&prime; and touches (I<sub>A</sub>),
and define (K<sub>B</sub>) and (K<sub>C</sub>) cyclically. Let (K) be
the circle externally tangent to (K<sub>A</sub>), (K<sub>B</sub>),
(K<sub>C</sub>), and let (L) be the circle externally tangent to
(I<sub>A</sub>), (I<sub>B</sub>), (I<sub>C</sub>). Then X(5213) =
(K)&cap;(L). (Tran Quang Hung, July 16, 2014)</p>
