<h3 id="X5485">X(5485)&nbsp;=&nbsp; KIRIKAMI-EULER IMAGE OF THE CENTROID</h3>
Barycentrics&nbsp;&nbsp; 1/(5a<sup>2</sup> - b<sup>2</sup>
- c<sup>2</sup>) : 1/(5b<sup>2</sup> - c<sup>2</sup> - a<sup>2</sup>) :
1/(5c<sup>2</sup> - a<sup>2</sup> - b<sup>2</sup>) &nbsp;&nbsp;
(Seichii Kirikami, May 21, 2013)

<p>Let P be a point in the plane of triangle ABC. Let H<sub>A</sub> be
the orthocenter of triangle PBC, and define H<sub>B</sub> and
H<sub>C</sub> cyclically. The Euler lines of the triangles
AH<sub>B</sub>H<sub>C</sub>, BH<sub>C</sub>H<sub>A</sub>,
CH<sub>A</sub>H<sub>B</sub> concur in the Kirikami-Euler image of P.
Let P be given by barycentrics p : q : r. Then Q is given by</p>

<p>Q = g(a,b,c,p,q,r) : g(b,c,a,q,r,p) : g(c,a,b,r,p,q), where
g(a,b,c,p,q,r) = p/[(a<sup>2</sup> - b<sup>2</sup> -
c<sup>2</sup>)p<sup>2</sup> + (a<sup>2</sup> - b<sup>2</sup> +
c<sup>2</sup>)pq + (a<sup>2</sup> + b<sup>2</sup> - c<sup>2</sup>)pr +
2a<sup>2</sup>qr]</p>

<p>If P = X(2), then Q = X(5485).&nbsp;&nbsp; (Seichii Kirikami, May
20, 2013)</p>

<p>The Kirikami-Euler image K(P) of a point P is related to the mapping
H(P) called "pedal antipodal perspector", defined in Hyacinthos #20403
and #20405, November 2011, by Randy Hutson, with general coordinates
given in #20404 by Francisco Javier. X(5485) = H(X(i)) for I = 6 and I
= 187. In general, K(P) = H(P&prime;) = H(P*), where P&prime; denotes the isogonal
conjugate of P, and P* = (inverse-in-circumcircle of P&prime;); for example,
K(X(1)) = X(8), K(X(3)) = X(68), and K(X(6)) = X(5486). &nbsp;&nbsp;
(Randy Hutson, May 22, 2013)</p>
