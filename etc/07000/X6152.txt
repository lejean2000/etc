<p>
X(6152) is the orthologic center of the pedal triangles of X(4) and X(5). This point is one of several found in connection with the following configuration:  Let ABC be a triangle, and let T<sub>1</sub>, T<sub>2</sub>, T<sub>3</sub> be the pedal triangles of X(3), X(4), X(5), respectively.  The three triangles are pairwise orthologic.   Let P<sub>i,j</sub> be the orthologic center of T<sub>i</sub>, T<sub>j</sub>.  Then the orthocenter of T<sub>3</sub> is the midpoint of P<sub>1,3</sub> and P<sub>2,3</sub>.  (Antreas Hatzipolakis and C&eacute;sar Lozada, December 17, 2014; see
<a href="http://www.hyacinthos.epizy.com/message.php?msg=22907" class="bold">22907</a>
</p>

See also Antreas Hatzipolakis and Angel Montesdeoca,
<a href="http://www.hyacinthos.epizy.com/message.php?msg=25814"> <b>Hyacinthos 25814</b></a>.
</p>
