<h3 id="X6580">X(6580)&nbsp;=&nbsp;2nd SHADOW POINT</h3>
Trilinears&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; (2 + cos A)/(2 + cos A - cos B - cos C) : :
<p>
The 1st Shadow Point is X(1480).  See <b>Antreas P. Hatzipolakis and Paul Yiu,</b>
<a href="http://forumgeom.fau.edu/FG2001volume1/FG200112index.html">Pedal Triangles and Their Shadows</a>, <i>Forum Geometricorum</i> 1 (2001) 81-90. (X(6580) is point M&prime; on page 88; the trilinears have been corrected here.)
</p>
<p>
Suppose that t is a number (or symmetric function of A,B,C) and let f(A,B,C,k) = (t + cos A)/(k + cos A - cos B - cos C).  Let P(j) = f(A,B,C,j) : f(B,C,A,j) : f(C,A,B,j) (trilinears).  The appearance of (j,k) in the following list means that P(j) lies on a line X(1)X(k):  (-3,5920), (-1,6), (0,90), (1,84), (2,1406), (3,1407).  (Peter Moses, February 9, 2015)
</p>
<p>
X(6580) lies on these lines: {1,1406}, {56,4550}, {999,3426}, {4846,5434}
</p>
