<h3 id="X6145">X(6145)&nbsp;=&nbsp; ABC-TO-HATZIPOLAKIS-MOSES-TRIANGLE ORTHOLOGY CENTER</h3>
Barycentrics &nbsp;&nbsp; f(a,b,c) : f(b,c,a) : f(c,a,b), where f(a,b,c) = 1/(-a^8+2 a^6 b^2-2 a^2 b^6+b^8+2 a^6 c^2-a^4 b^2 c^2-b^6 c^2-2 a^2 c^6-b^2 c^6+c^8)
<br>
<b>
X(6145) = (J<sup>2</sup> - 3)*X(54) + (2J<sup>2</sup> - 4)*X1594), where J = |OH|/R<br>
</b>
<p>
Let A&prime;B&prime;C&prime; be the orthic triangle of ABC.  Let A<sub>B</sub> be the orthogonal projection of A&prime; onto line AB, and define B<sub>C</sub> and C<sub>A</sub> cyclically.  Let A<sub>C</sub> be the orthogonal projection of A&prime; onto line AC, and define B<sub>A</sub> and C<sub>B</sub> cyclically.  Let A&prime;<sub>B</sub> be the reflection of A in A<sub>B</sub>, and let A&prime;<sub>C</sub> be the reflection of A in A<sub>C</sub> .  Let N<sub>A</sub> be the nine-point center of the triangle AA&prime;<sub>B</sub>A&prime;<sub>C</sub>, and define N<sub>B</sub> and N<sub>C</sub> cyclically.  The triangle N<sub>A</sub>N<sub>B</sub>N<sub>C</sub>, here named the Hatzipolakis-Moses triangle, is orthologic to ABC and perpective to ABC.  The A-vertex of this triangle is given by
</p>
N<sub>A</sub> = -a^4 b^2+2 a^2 b^4-b^6-a^4 c^2-2 a^2 b^2 c^2+b^4 c^2+2 a^2 c^4+b^2 c^4-c^6 :
b^2 (a^4-2 a^2 b^2+b^4-a^2 c^2-b^2 c^2) : c^2 (a^4-a^2 b^2-2 a^2 c^2-b^2 c^2+c^4)
<p>
The ABC-to-N<sub>A</sub>N<sub>B</sub>N<sub>C</sub> center of orthology is X(6145), and the N<sub>A</sub>N<sub>B</sub>N<sub>C</sub>-to-ABC center of orthology is X(6146).  The perspector of ABC and N<sub>A</sub>N<sub>B</sub>N<sub>C</sub> is X(54).  (Based on notes , Antreas Hatzipolakis and Peter Moses, November 19, 2014; see
<a href="http://www.hyacinthos.epizy.com/message.php?msg=22774" class="bold">22774</a>)
