<h3 id="X5596">X(5596)&nbsp;=&nbsp; PERSPECTOR OF ARIES AND ANTICOMPLEMENTARY TRIANGLES</h3>
Barycentrics&nbsp;&nbsp;  b<sup>8</sup> + c<sup>8</sup> - 3a<sup>8</sup> +2a<sup>4</sup>b<sup>4</sup> + 2a<sup>4</sup>c<sup>4</sup> - 2b<sup>4</sup>c<sup>4</sup> : :
<p>
Let A&prime;B&prime;C&prime; be the tangential triangle of an acute triangle ABC. Let
A&Prime; be the touchpoint of the A-excircle of A&prime;B&prime;C&prime; and the line B&prime;C&prime;;
define B&Prime; and C&Prime; cyclically. The triangle A&Prime;B&Prime;C&Prime; is introduced
here as the Aries triangle, with vertices given by Peter Moses (Nov.
21, 2013).
</p>
<p>
A&Prime; = a<sup>4</sup> + b<sup>4</sup> + c<sup>4</sup> -
2b<sup>2</sup>c<sup>2</sup> : 2b<sup>2</sup>(c<sup>2</sup> -
b<sup>2</sup>) : 2c<sup>2</sup>(b<sup>2</sup> - c<sup>2</sup>)<br>
B&Prime; = 2a<sup>2</sup>(c<sup>2</sup> - a<sup>2</sup>) : b<sup>4</sup> +
c<sup>4</sup> + a<sup>4</sup> - 2c<sup>2</sup>a<sup>2</sup> :
2c<sup>2</sup>(a<sup>2</sup> - c<sup>2</sup>)<br>
C&Prime; = 2a<sup>2</sup>(b<sup>2</sup> - a<sup>2</sup>) :
2b<sup>2</sup>(a<sup>2</sup> - b<sup>2</sup>) : c<sup>4</sup> +
a<sup>4</sup> + b<sup>4</sup> - 2a<sup>2</sup>b<sup>2</sup>
</p>
<p>
The Aries triangle is perspective to the tangential triangle, with perspector X(1498).
</p>
<p>
If ABC is acute, the Aries triangle is the extouch triangle of the tangential triangle. (Randy Hutson, July 18, 2014)
</p>
<p>
If ABC is acute, then the vertices of the Aries triangle lie on the cubic K075.  (C&eacute;sar Lozada, October 21, 2015)
</p>
