<h3 id="X5424">X(5424)&nbsp;=&nbsp;HATZIPOLAKIS-EULER-SCHIFFLER POINT</h3>
Trilinears&nbsp;&nbsp; f(a,b,c) : f(b,c,a) : f(c,a,b), where
f(a,b,c) = (2a<sup>3</sup> + 2b<sup>3</sup> + c<sup>3</sup> -
2a<sup>2</sup>b - 2ab<sup>2</sup> - 2ac<sup>2</sup> - 2bc<sup>2</sup> -
a<sup>2</sup>c - b<sup>2</sup>c - abc)(2a<sup>3</sup> + b<sup>3</sup> +
2c<sup>3</sup> - 2a<sup>2</sup>c - 2ac<sup>2</sup> - 2ab<sup>2</sup> -
2b<sup>2</sup>c - a<sup>2</sup>b - bc<sup>2</sup> - abc)<br>
<b>X(5424) = 6(r + R)*X(21) + (2r - R)*X(4867)<br>
X(5424) = R*X(79) + 4(2r + R)*X(2646)&nbsp;&nbsp; (Peter Moses,
February 8, 2013)  </b>

<p>Let I be the incenter and L the Euler line of triangle ABC. Let
L<sub>A</sub> be the Euler line of IBC, and define L<sub>B</sub> and
L<sub>C</sub> cyclically. (The four Euler lines concur in the Schiffler
point, X(21).) Let O<sub>A</sub> be the circumcenter of IBC, and define
O<sub>B</sub> and O<sub>C</sub> cyclically.</p>

<p>Continuing, let A<sub>B</sub>, A<sub>C</sub> be the orthogonal
projections of O<sub>A</sub> on L<sub>B</sub> and L<sub>C</sub>,
respecitively, and define B<sub>C</sub>, B<sub>A</sub> and
C<sub>A</sub>, C<sub>B</sub> cyclically. Let A&prime; be the circumcenter of
O<sub>A</sub>A<sub>B</sub>A<sub>C</sub>, and define B&prime; and C&prime;
cyclically. The lines AA&prime;, BB&prime;, CC&prime; concur in X(5424), and the
circumcenter X(5428) of A&prime;B&prime;C&prime; lies on L.&nbsp;&nbsp; (Antreas
Hatzipolakis, February 8, 2013)</p>

<p>A summary of Hyacinthos discussions of centers X(5424)-X(5429) is
presented at
<a href="http://anthrakitis.blogspot.gr/2013/02/euler-lines-circumcircles.html"
class="bold">Euler Lines, Circumcircles</a>.</p>
