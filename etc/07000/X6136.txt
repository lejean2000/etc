<h3 id="X6136">X(6136)&nbsp;=&nbsp; 2st HUNG-SODDY TOUCHPOINT</h3>
Barycentrics &nbsp;&nbsp; a*(a-b)*(a-c)*(a*b-S)*(a*c-S) : :
<p>
The incircle or one of the excircles of the antipedal triangle of the outer Soddy point, X(176), of a triangle ABC is tangent to the circumcircle of ABC, and X(6136) is the touchpoint.  (Based on a problem by Tran Quang Hung, with contributions by T. Cohl and P. Moses,
<a href="http://www.artofproblemsolving.com/Forum/viewtopic.php?t=610195&p3655162" class="bold">Art of Problem Solving: Tangent to circumcircle, October 16, 2014</a>),
</p>
<p>
X(6136) lies on these lines:  {105,1336}, {662,1306}, {4557,6135}
</p>
<p>
X(6136) = isogonal conjugate of X(6365)
</p>
