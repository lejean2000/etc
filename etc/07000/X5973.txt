<p>Let F<sub>A</sub>F<sub>B</sub>F<sub>C</sub> be the Feuerbach
triangle of a triangle ABC with excircles (I<sub>A</sub>),
(I<sub>B</sub>), (I<sub>C</sub>). Let (K<sub>A</sub>) be the circle,
other than the nine-point circle, which passes through F<sub>B</sub>
and F<sub>C</sub> and is tangent to (I<sub>A</sub>), and let D be the
touchpoint. Define (K<sub>B</sub>, (K<sub>C</sub>) and E, F cyclically.
Let T<sub>A</sub> be the line through D tangent to (I<sub>A</sub>), and
define T<sub>B</sub> and T<sub>C</sub> cyclically. The lines
T<sub>A</sub>, T<sub>B</sub>, T<sub>C</sub> form a triangle [here named
the Hung-Feuerbach triangle] that is perspective to
F<sub>A</sub>F<sub>B</sub>F<sub>C</sub>, and X(5973) is the perspector.
(Tran Quang Hung, ADGEOM, August 19, 2014) See also X(5974) and
X(5975).</p>
