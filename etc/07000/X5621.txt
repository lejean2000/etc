<h3 id="X5621">X(5621)&nbsp;=&nbsp; 1st KIRIKAMI-EULER-LEMOINE POINT</h3>
Barycentrics &nbsp;&nbsp; f(a,b,c) : f(b,c,a) : f(c,a,b),
where f(a,b,c) = a<sup>2</sup>b<sup>2</sup>S<sub>C</sub>(S<sub>A</sub>
- S<sub>B</sub>)(-2S<sub>A</sub>S<sub>B</sub> +

<b>X(5621) = X(6) + 2X(74)<br>
 </b>
<p>Let L be the 1st Lemoine circle of a triangle ABC. Let A<sub>B</sub>
be the point nearer A where line AB meets L, and define B<sub>C</sub>
and C<sub>A</sub> cyclically. Let A<sub>C</sub> be the point nearer A
where line AC meets L, and define B<sub>A</sub> and C<sub>B</sub>
cyclically. The Euler lines of the triangles
AA<sub>B</sub>A<sub>C</sub>, BB<sub>C</sub>B<sub>A</sub>,
CC<sub>A</sub>C<sub>B</sub> concur in X(5621).&nbsp;&nbsp; &nbsp;&nbsp;
(Seiichi Kirikami, November 7, 2013)</p>
