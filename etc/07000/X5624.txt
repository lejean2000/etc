<h3 id="X5624">X(5624)&nbsp;=&nbsp; REFLECTION OF X(14) IN
X(5619)</h3>
Barycentrics &nbsp;&nbsp; f(A,B,C) : f(B,C,A) : f(C,A,B),
where f(A,B,C) = K<sub>A</sub> (K<sub>A</sub>V<sub>A</sub> -
K<sub>B</sub>V<sub>B</sub> - K<sub>C</sub>V<sub>C</sub>), where
K<sub>A</sub> = - 3<sup>1/2</sup>a<sup>2</sup>S + S<sup>2</sup> +
3S<sub>B</sub>S<sub>C</sub> and V<sub>A</sub> = (S<sup>2</sup> -
3S<sub>A</sub>S<sub>B</sub>)(S<sup>2</sup> -
3S<sub>A</sub>S<sub>C</sub>) &nbsp;&nbsp; &nbsp;&nbsp; (Peter Moses,
June 20, 2014)

<p>Referring to the construction of X(5619), the lines
A<sub>B</sub>A<sub>C</sub>, B<sub>C</sub>B<sub>A</sub>,
C<sub>A</sub>C<sub>B</sub> concur in X(5624). &nbsp;&nbsp; (Peter
Moses, December 4, 2013)</p>

<p>X(5624) lies on the Neuberg cubic and these lines: {14,74},
{13,3441}, {15,1138}, {3065,3376}</p>

<p>X(5624) = reflection of X(14) in X(5619)<br>
X(5624) = X(30)-Ceva conjugate of X(14)</p>
