<h3 id="X5606">X(5606)&nbsp;=&nbsp; HATZIPOLAKIS CIRCUMCIRCLE POINT</h3>
Barycentrics&nbsp;&nbsp; f(a,b,c) : f(b,c,a) : f(c,a,b),
where f(a,b,c) = a/[(b - c)(a<sup>3</sup> + b<sup>3</sup> +
c<sup>3</sup> - a<sup>2</sup>b -a<sup>2</sup>c - ab<sup>2</sup> -
ac<sup>2</sup> - abc + 3b<sup>2</sup>c + 3bc<sup>2</sup>)]

<p>Let I be the incenter and A&prime; the nine-point center of triangle IBC.
Define B&prime; and C&prime; cyclically. The circles AB&prime;C&prime;, BC&prime;A&prime;, CA&prime;B&prime; concur in
X(5606). &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; (Antreas Hatzipolakis, June 2, 2013:
see
<a href="http://anthrakitis.blogspot.gr/2013/06/concurrent-circumcircles-n1n2n3-nanbnc.html"
class="bold">Concurrent Circumcircles</a>)</p>

<p>Let L be the Euler line of the incentral triangle of ABC, so that L
is the line X(500)X(1962). Let L<sub>A</sub> be the reflection of L in
line BC, and define L<sub>B</sub> and L<sub>C</sub> cyclically. Let A&Prime;
= L<sub>B</sub>&cap;L<sub>C</sub>, B&Prime; =
L<sub>C</sub>&cap;L<sub>A</sub>, C&Prime; = L<sub>A</sub>&cap;L<sub>B</sub>.
The lines AA&Prime;, BB&Prime;, CC&Prime; concur in X(5606). (Randy Hutson, July 18,
2014)</p>

{229,759}, {2372,5253}
