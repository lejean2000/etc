<h3 id="X5974">X(5974)&nbsp;=&nbsp; CENTER OF THE HUNG-FEUERBACH CIRCLE</h3>
Barycentrics&nbsp;&nbsp; f(a,b,c) : f(b,c,a) : f(c,a,b),
where f(a,b,c) = a (a^6+2 a^5 b+2 a^4 b^2+2 a^3 b^3-2 a b^5-b^6+2 a^5
c+6 a^4 b c+8 a^3 b^2 c-6 a b^4 c-2 b^5 c+2 a^4 c^2+8 a^3 b c^2+a^2 b^2
c^2-8 a b^3 c^2-4 b^4 c^2+2 a^3 c^3-8 a b^2 c^3-6 b^3 c^3-6 a b c^4-4
b^2 c^4-2 a c^5-2 b c^5-c^6)&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; (Peter Moses, August 19,
2014)
<p>Continuing from X(5973), the Hung-Feuerbach circle is here defined
as the circle (K) tangent to each of the following 6 circles:
(K<sub>A</sub>, (K<sub>B</sub>, (K<sub>C</sub>), (I<sub>A</sub>),
(I<sub>B</sub>), (I<sub>C</sub>), so that (K) is also tangent to the
Apollonius circle.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; (Tran Quang Hung and Peter Moses,
ADGEOM, August 19, 2014)</p>
<p>X(5974) lies on these lines:
{72,171}, {191,1045}, {846,3931}, {970,5975}, {1054,5956}, {1490,2629}, {2959,5687}, {3579,5524}}</p>
