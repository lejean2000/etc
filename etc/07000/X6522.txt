<h3 id="X6522">X(6522)&nbsp;=&nbsp; CENTER OF MOSES-LUCAS(-1) CIRCLE</h3>
Trilinears &nbsp;&nbsp; 11 cos A - 6 sin A : :
<br>
Barycentrics &nbsp;&nbsp;  a^2 (11 S<sub>A</sub> - 6 S) : :
<p>
Let (R) be the radical circle of the Lucas(-1) circles, and let (L) = inverse in (R) of the circumcircle.  Then (L) tangent to each of the 3 Lucas(-1) circles.  Let A&prime; be the touchpoint of (L) and the A-Lucas(-1) circle, and define B&prime; and C&prime; cyclically.  Then
</p>
<p>
A&prime; = a<sup>2</sup>(3S - 4S<sub>A</sub>) : 2b<sup>2</sup>(S - 2S<sub>B</sub>) : 2c<sup>2</sup>(S - 2S<sub>C</sub>)  <br>
B&prime; = 2a<sup>2</sup>(S - 2S<sub>A</sub>) : b<sup>2</sup>(3S - 4S<sub>B</sub>) : 2c<sup>2</sup>(S - 2S<sub>C</sub> )  <br>
C&prime; = 2a<sup>2</sup>(S - 2S<sub>A</sub>) : 2b<sup>2</sup>(S - 2S<sub>B</sub>) : c<sup>2</sup>(3S - 4S<sub>C</sub>)
</p>
<p>
Let A&prime;<sub>B</sub> = inverse-in-A-Lucas(-1)-circle of B&prime;, and define B&prime;<sub>C</sub> and C&prime;<sub>A</sub> cyclically.  Let A&prime;<sub>C</sub> = inverse-in-A-Lucas(-1)-circle of C&prime;, and define B&prime;<sub>A</sub> and C&prime;<sub>B</sub> cyclically.  The six points A&prime;<sub>B</sub>, B&prime;<sub>C</sub>, C&prime;<sub>A</sub>, A&prime;<sub>C</sub>, B&prime;<sub>A</sub>, C&prime;<sub>B</sub> lie on a circle with center X(6522).  The circle is here called the Moses-Lucas(-1) Circle.  It is a member of the Schoute coaxal system; see X(6519).  Note that (L) is the Lucas(-1) inner circle and A&prime;B&prime;C&prime; is the Lucas(-1) inner triangle.  (Randy Hutson, February 12, 2015)
</p>
<p>
Let &Gamma; be the circumcircle and O(A) the A-Lucas(-1) circle.  Let K<sub>A</sub> = 1 if O(A) is internally tangent to &Gamma;, and let
K<sub>A</sub> = -1 if O(A) is externally tangent to &Gamma;.  Define K<sub>B</sub> and K<sub>C</sub> cyclically.  Let L<sub>A</sub> and
R<sub>A</sub> be the center and radius of O(A), and define L<sub>B</sub> and L<sub>C</sub>, and also R<sub>B</sub> and R<sub>C</sub>,
cyclically.  Let L* be the center of the Lucas(-1) inner circle and R* its radius.  Then X(6522) = (K<sub>A</sub>/R<sub>A</sub>)
*L<sub>A</sub> + (K<sub>B</sub>/R<sub>B</sub>)*L<sub>B</sub> + (K<sub>C</sub>/R<sub>C</sub>)*L<sub>C</sub> + L*/R* + X(3)/R. (Randy Hutson, February 12, 2015)
</p>
