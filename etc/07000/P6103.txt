<p>
The Dao-Moses-Telv circle is defined as the circle passing through these six points:
</p>

<p>
F<sub>1</sub> = X(13), the 1st Fermat point<br>
F<sub>2</sub> = X(14), the 2nd Fermat point<br>
F<sub>3</sub> = inverse of F<sub>1</sub> in the circumcircle<br>
F<sub>4</sub> = inverse of F<sub>2</sub> in the circumcircle<br>
F<sub>5</sub> = inverse of F<sub>1</sub> in the nine-point circle<br>
F<sub>6</sub> = inverse of F<sub>2</sub> in the nine-point circle
</p>

<p>
Dao Thanh Oai noted that these points are concyclic, Telv Cohl gave a proof, and Peter Moses discovered properties described in this preamble, along with centers X(6103)-X(6111).  The circle is first noted at
<a href="http://www.adgeom.epizy.com/message.php?msg=971"
class="bold">ADGEOM 971</a>.  See also <a  href="http://www.artofproblemsolving.com/Forum/blog.php?u=192421&b=97345"
class="bold">ADGEOM 971 DB</a>.
</p>

<p>
The center of the Dao-Moses-Telv circle is X(1637).  (Francisco Javier,
<a href="http://www.adgeom.epizy.com/message.php?msg=1977" class="bold">ADGEOM 1977</a>, November 3, 2014)
</p>
<p>
The Dao-Moses-Telv circle is orthogonal to the circumcircle, the nine-point circle, and all the other circles in their coaxal family.  The A-power of the circle is
</p>
<p>
(b<sup>2</sup> + c<sup>2</sup> - a<sup>2</sup>)[((b<sup>2</sup> + c<sup>2</sup> - a<sup>2</sup>)<sup>2</sup> - b<sup>2</sup>c<sup>2</sup>]/[6(a<sup>2</sup> - b<sup>2</sup>)(a<sup>2</sup> - c<sup>2</sup>)]
</p>
<p>
The circle passes through X(5000) and X(5001), these being the Walsmith point and its inverse in the circumcircle.  (Peter Moses, November 6, 2014)
</p>
