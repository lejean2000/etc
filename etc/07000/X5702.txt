<h3 id="X5702">X(5702)&nbsp;=&nbsp; CENTER OF MONTESDEOCA
CONIC</h3>
Barycentrics&nbsp;&nbsp; f(a,b,c) : f(b,c,a) : f(c,a,b),
where f(a,b,c) = S<sub>B</sub>S<sub>C</sub>(5a<sup>2</sup>S<sub>A</sub>
- S<sub>B</sub>S<sub>C</sub>)

<p>Let ABC be a triangle, let P<sub>A</sub> be the polar of A with
respect to the circle with diameter BC, and define P<sub>B</sub> and
P<sub>C</sub> cyclically. Let A<sub>B</sub> = P<sub>A</sub>&cap;AB and
A<sub>C</sub> = P<sub>A</sub>&cap;AC, and define B<sub>C</sub>,
C<sub>A</sub>, B<sub>A</sub>, and C<sub>B</sub> cyclically. The six
points A<sub>B</sub>, A<sub>C</sub>, B<sub>C</sub>, B<sub>A</sub>,
C<sub>A</sub>, C<sub>B</sub> lie on, and define, the Montesdeoca conic.
(Angel Montesdeoca, June 23, 2014)</p>

<p>A barycentric equation for the Montesdeoca conic is found from
A<sub>B</sub> = S<sub>C</sub> : 0 : 2S<sub>A</sub> and A<sub>C</sub> =
S<sub>B</sub> : 2S<sub>A</sub> : 0 to be as follows:</p>

<p>2(S<sup>2</sup><sub>A</sub>x<sup>2</sup> +
S<sup>2</sup><sub>B</sub>y<sup>2</sup> +
S<sup>2</sup><sub>C</sub>z<sup>2</sup>) -
5(S<sub>B</sub>S<sub>C</sub>yz + S<sub>C</sub>S<sub>A</sub>zx +
S<sub>A</sub>S<sub>B</sub>xy) = 0 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; (Peter
Moses, June 23, 2014)</p>

<p>The Montesdeoca conic is the anticevian-intersection conic when P =
X(4); this conic is defined by Francisco J. García Capitán
(<a href="http://garciacapitan.blogspot.com/2011/12/anticevian-intersection-conic.html"
class="bold">The Anticevian Intersection Conic</a> and
Hyacinthos #20547 (December 19, 2011). Also, the perspector of the
Montesdeoca conic is X(4).</p>

<p>X(5702) lies on these lines:
{4,6}, {297,5032}, {340,1992}, {376,3284}, {468,5304}, {578,3183}, {631,5158}, {3163,3545}</p>
