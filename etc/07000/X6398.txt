<p>
Let &Gamma; be the circumcircle and O(A) the A-Lucas(-1) circle.  Let K<sub>A</sub> = 1 if O(A) is internally tangent to &Gamma;, and let K<sub>A</sub> = -1 if O(A) is externally tangent to &Gamma;.  Define K<sub>B</sub> and K<sub>C</sub> cyclically.  Let L<sub>A</sub> and R<sub>A</sub> be the center and radius of O(A), and define L<sub>B</sub> and L<sub>C</sub>, and also R<sub>B</sub> and R<sub>C</sub>, cyclically.  Then X(6398) = (K<sub>A</sub>/R<sub>A</sub>)*L<sub>A</sub> + (K<sub>B</sub>/R<sub>B</sub>)*L<sub>B</sub> + (K<sub>C</sub>/R<sub>C</sub>)*L<sub>C</sub>.  Also, the circumsymmedial triangle, Lucas(-1) inner triangle, and Lucas(-2) central triangle are pairwise perspective, and their common perspector is X(6398).  Let A&prime;B&prime;C&prime; be the Lucas(-1) central triangle.  Let A&Prime; be the trilinear pole, wrt A&prime;B&prime;C&prime;, of line BC.  Define B&Prime;, C&Prime; cyclically.  The lines A&prime;A&Prime;, B&prime;B&Prime;, C&prime;C&prime; concur in X(6398).  Let A*B*C* be the Lucas(-1) tangents triangle.  Let A** be the trilinear pole, wrt A*B*C*, of line BC.  Define B**, C** cyclically.  The lines A*A**, B*B**, C*C** concur in X(6398).   (Randy Hutson, January 29, 2015)
