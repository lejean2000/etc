<h3 id="X5504">X(5504)&nbsp;=&nbsp; KIRIKAMI CONCURRENT CIRCLES IMAGE
OF X(3)</h3>
Trilinears &nbsp;&nbsp; (cos A)/(1 + cos 2B + cos 2C) : :
<br>
Barycentrics&nbsp;&nbsp; g(a,b,c) : g(b,c,a) : g(c,a,b), where g(a,b,c)
= a<sup>2</sup>(b<sup>2</sup> + c<sup>2</sup> -
a<sup>2</sup>)/[a<sup>4</sup>(b<sup>2</sup> + c<sup>2</sup>) +
(b<sup>2</sup> - c<sup>2</sup>)<sup>2</sup>(b<sup>2</sup> +
c<sup>2</sup>) - 2a<sup>2</sup>(b<sup>4</sup> -
b<sup>2</sup>c<sup>2</sup> + c<sup>4</sup>)] &nbsp;&nbsp; (Seichii
Kirikami, June 2, 2013)

<p>See X(5503) for the definition of Kirikami concurrent circles image
Q of a point P and X(5509) for an occurrence of X(5504) as a point of
concurrence given by the Hatzipolakis-Moses Theorem.</p>

<p>If P = X(3), then Q = X(5504). &nbsp;&nbsp; (Seichii Kirikami, June
2, 2013)</p>

<p>Let A&prime;B&prime;C&prime; be the tangential triangle. Let L<sub>A</sub> be the line
through A&prime; parallel to the Euler line, and define L<sub>B</sub> and
L<sub>C</sub> cyclically. Let R<sub>A</sub> be the reflection of
L<sub>A</sub> in BC, and define R<sub>B</sub> and R<sub>C</sub>
cyclically. The lines R<sub>A</sub>, R<sub>B</sub>, R<sub>C</sub>
concur in X(5504); see X(399). (Randy Hutson, August 17, 2014)</p>

<p>Continuing, let A&Prime; be the reflection of A&prime; in line BC, and define
B&Prime; and C&Prime; cyclically. The circumcircles of AB&Prime;C&Prime;, BC&Prime;A&Prime;, CA&Prime;B&Prime;
concur in X(5504). Morevoer, X(5504) is the antigonal image of X(68),
the trilinear pole of line X(577)X(647), and the X(92)-isoconjugate of
X(3003). (Randy Hutson, August 17, 2014)</p>

</p>
