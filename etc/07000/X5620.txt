<h3 id="X5620">X(5620)&nbsp;=&nbsp; ISOGONAL CONJUGATE OF X(5127)</h3>
Barycentrics &nbsp;&nbsp; (b + c)[a<sup>6</sup> - a<sup>4</sup>(b<sup>2</sup> + c<sup>2</sup>) - a<sup>2</sup>(b<sup>4</sup> + c<sup>4</sup> - 3b<sup>2</sup>c<sup>2</sup>) - 2abc(b + c)(b - c)<sup>2</sup>  + (b + c)<sup>2</sup>(b - c)<sup>4</sup>]
<br>
<b>
X(5620) = R*X(65) - (2r + R)*X(1365)
</b>
<p>
Let A&prime;B&prime;C&prime; be the excentral triangle of ABC. Let N<sub>A</sub> be the nine-point center of A&prime;BC, and let O<sub>A</sub> be the circumcircle of N<sub>A</sub>BC. Define O<sub>B</sub> and O<sub>C</sub>
cyclically. The circles O<sub>A</sub>, O<sub>B</sub>, O<sub>C</sub> concur in X(5620). &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; (Angel Montesdoca, Anapolis #1120, November 2013: see
<a href="http://anthrakitis.blogspot.gr/2013/06/concurrent-circumcircles-n1n2n3-nanbnc.html"
class="bold">Concurrent Circumcircles</a>)
</p>
<p>
Let A&prime;B&prime;C&prime; be the incentral triangle and let A&Prime; be the point such that triangle A&Prime;BC is similar to A&prime;B&prime;C&prime; and A&Prime; is on the same side of line BC as A. Define B&Prime; and C&Prime; cyclically. The lines AA&Prime;, BB&Prime;, CC&Prime; concur in X(5620); see also X(502). (Randy Hutson, July 7, 2014)
</p>
