<h3 id="X5667">X(5667)&nbsp;=&nbsp; X(30)-CEVA CONJUGATE OF X(4)</h3>
Barycentrics &nbsp;&nbsp; [a^12 - a^10(b^2 + c^2) + a^8(3b^4 - 5b^2c^2 + 3c^4) - 6a^6(b^2 - c^2)^2(b^2 + c^2) + a^4(b^2 - c^2)^2(3b^4 + 20b^2c^2 + 3c^4) - a^2(b - c)^2(b + c)^2(b^2 + c^2)(b^4 + 6b^2c^2 + c^4) + (b^2 - c^2)^4(b^4 - 5b^2c^2 + c^4)]/(b^2 - c^2) : :
<p>
As X(30)-Ceva conjugates of points on the Neuberg cubic
<a href="https://bernard-gibert.pagesperso-orange.fr/Exemples/k001.html" style=
"font-weight: bold">(K001)</a>, the points X(5667) - X(5685) are also
on the Neuberg cubic. Specifically, if P is a point on the Neuberg
cubic, then the points X(74), P, and the X(30)-Ceva conjugate of P are
collinear, since X(74) is the isopivot (or secondary pivot) of the
cubic. See
<a href="https://bernard-gibert.pagesperso-orange.fr/Tables/table19.html" style=
"font-weight: bold">Table 9: Points on the Neuberg Cubic</a>.</p>

<p>X(5667) inverse-in-circumconic-centered-at-X(4) of X(133); also,
X(5667) is the antipode of X(4) in the bianticevian conic of X(1) and
X(4). (Randy Hutson, July 7, 2014)</p>
