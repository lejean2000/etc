<h3 id="X5569">X(5569)&nbsp;=&nbsp; CENTER OF THE DAO 6-POINT CIRCLE</h3>
Barycentrics&nbsp;&nbsp; f(a,b,c) : f(b,c,a) : f(c,a,b),
where f(a,b,c) = 7a<sup>4</sup> + b<sup>4</sup> + c<sup>4</sup> -
7a<sup>2</sup>b<sup>2</sup> - 7a<sup>2</sup>c<sup>2</sup> -
4b<sup>2</sup>c<sup>2</sup>
<p>
Let ABC be a triangle, and let A<sub>B</sub> be the center of the
circle through A and tangent to the B-median at X(2), and define
B<sub>C</sub> and C<sub>A</sub> cyclically. Let A<sub>C</sub> be the
center of the circle through A and tangent to the C-median at X(2), and
define B<sub>A</sub> and C<sub>B</sub> cyclically. The points
A<sub>B</sub>, B<sub>A</sub>, B<sub>C</sub>, C<sub>B</sub>,
C<sub>A</sub>, A<sub>C</sub> lie on a circle, of which X(5569) is the
center. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; (Dao Thanh Oai, Nov. 3, 2013)</p>

<p>The following properties were communicated by Peter Moses, November
4, 2013. Let &Delta; = area of ABC, r = radius of the Dao 6-point
circle, and &omega; = Brocard angle of ABC. Let f<sub>a</sub> =
2b<sup>2</sup> + 2c<sup>2</sup> - a<sup>2</sup>, and define
f<sub>b</sub> and f<sub>c</sub> cyclically. Then
</p>
<p>
r = [f<sub>a</sub>f<sub>b</sub>f<sub>c</sub>(b<sup>2</sup>c<sup>2</sup> +
c<sup>2</sup>a<sup>2</sup> + a<sup>2</sup>b<sup>2</sup>)]<sup>1/2</sup>/(144&Delta;)<sup>2</sup>
</p>
<p>
|A<sub>B</sub>B<sub>A</sub>| = |B<sub>C</sub>C<sub>B</sub>| =
|C<sub>A</sub>A<sub>C</sub>| =
[f<sub>a</sub>f<sub>b</sub>f<sub>c</sub>]<sup>1/2</sup>/(36&Delta;)</p>

<p>Let X = X(5569). Then angle(A<sub>B</sub>XB<sub>A</sub>) =
angle(B<sub>C</sub>XC<sub>B</sub>) = angle(C<sub>A</sub>XA<sub>C</sub>)
= Tan<sup>-1</sup>[(a<sup>2</sup> + b<sup>2</sup> +
c<sup>2</sup>)/(4&Delta;)]</p>

<p>angle(A<sub>B</sub>B<sub>A</sub>X) =
angle(B<sub>C</sub>C<sub>B</sub>X) = angle(C<sub>A</sub>A<sub>C</sub>X)
= &pi;/2 - &omega;</p>

</p>

{5077,5461}
</p>
