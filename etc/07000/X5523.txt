<h3 id="X5523">X(5523)&nbsp;=&nbsp; ORTHOASSOCIATE (BUREK CONCURRENT CIRCLES IMAGE) OF X(6)</h3>
Barycentrics&nbsp;&nbsp; f(a,b,c) : f(b,c,a)
: f(c,a,b), where f(a,b,c) = (a<sup>2</sup> + b<sup>2</sup> -
c<sup>2</sup>)(a<sup>2</sup> - b<sup>2</sup> +
c<sup>2</sup>)(b<sup>6</sup> + c<sup>6</sup> -
a<sup>4</sup>b<sup>2</sup> - a<sup>4</sup>c<sup>2</sup> +
2a<sup>2</sup>b<sup>2</sup>c<sup>2</sup> - b<sup>4</sup>c<sup>2</sup> -
b<sup>2</sup>c<sup>4</sup>) &nbsp;&nbsp; (Peter Moses, June 15, 2013)

<p>Let P be a point in the plane of a triangle ABC with orthocenter H.
Let DEF be the orthic triangle of ABC. The circumcircles of APD, BPE,
CPF concur in two points, P and Q. The point Q = Q(P) is the
orthoassociate, or Burek concurrent circles image, of P. Examples:
Q(X(1)) = X(1785), Q(X(2)) = X(468), Q(X(3)) = X(403), Q(X(4)) = X(4),
Q(X(5)) = X(186). &nbsp;&nbsp; (Dominic Burek, July 15, 2013)</p>

<p>The mapping Q is included as an <b>orthoassociate</b> of P in
Bernard Gibert's paper, "Orthocorrespondence and Orthopivotal Cubics,"
<i>Forum Geometricorum</i> 3 (2003) 1-27. If P is given by barycentrics
p : q : r, then Q is given by</p>

<p>Q = g(a,b,c,p,q,r) : g(b,c,a,q,r,p) : g(c,a,b,r,p,q), where
g(a,b,c,p,q,r) = S<sub>B</sub>S<sub>C</sub>[(pq + pr)S<sub>A</sub> -
q<sup>2</sup>S<sub>B</sub> - r<sup>2</sup>S<sub>C</sub>].</p>
