<h3 id="X6449">X(6449)&nbsp;=&nbsp;PERSPECTOR OF LUCAS CENTRAL TRIANGLE AND 2nd LUCAS SECONDARY TANGENTS TRIANGLE</h3>
Trilinears &nbsp;&nbsp;  5 cos A + 2 sin A : :
<br>
Barycentrics &nbsp;&nbsp;   a^2 (5 S<sub>A</sub> + 2 S) : :

<p>
Let L<sub>A</sub> and R<sub>A</sub> be the center and radius of the A-Lucas circle, and define  L<sub>B</sub> and L<sub>C</sub>, and also R<sub>B</sub> and R<sub>C</sub>, cyclically.
Let L* be the center of the Lucas inner circle and R* its radius.  Then X(6449) = L<sub>A</sub>/R<sub>A</sub> + L<sub>B</sub>/R<sub>B</sub> + L<sub>C</sub>/R<sub>C</sub> - L*/R* - X(3)/R; see also X(6426).  (Randy Hutson, February 3, 2015)
</p>
<p>
Let A<sub>B</sub> = inverse-in-A-Lucas-circle of B, and define B<sub>C</sub> and C<sub>A</sub> cyclically.  Let A<sub>C</sub> = inverse-in-A-Lucas-circle of C, and define B<sub>A</sub> and C<sub>B</sub> cyclically.  The six points A<sub>B</sub>, B<sub>C</sub>, C<sub>A</sub>, A<sub>C</sub>, B<sub>A</sub>, C<sub>B</sub> lie on a circle with center X(6449).   See X(6519).   (Peter Moses, February 11, 2015)
</p>
