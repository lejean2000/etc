<p>
Let U = A&prime;B&prime;C&prime; be the medial triangle and X = X(6).  Let A&Prime; = X-of-AB&prime;C&prime;, B&Prime; = X-of-A&prime;BC&prime;, C&Prime; = X-of-A&prime;B&prime;C, and V = A&Prime;B&Prime;C&Prime;.  Then U and V are cyclologic, and X(6593) is the (V,U)-cyclologic center; the (U,V)-cyclologic center is X(125) .  See
<a href="http://marveloustriangles.blogspot.com/2014/12/ciclology-with-medial-triangle-anopolis.html" class="bold">Cyclology with medial triangle (Seiichi Kirikami, December 11, 2014</a>, posted by C&eacute;sar Lozada.
</p>
<p>
Let A&prime;B&prime;C&prime; be the cevian triangle of X(23).  Let A&Prime; be the inverse-in-circumcircle of A&prime;, and define B&Prime; and C&Prime; cyclically.   The lines AA&Prime;, BB&Prime;, CC&Prime; concur in X(6593).  Let P and U be the circumcircle intercepts of the perpendicular to the Euler line through X(2); then X(6593) = crosssum of P and U.  Also, X(6593) is the QA-P41 center (Involutary Conjugate of QA-P4) of quadrangle ABCX(2).  (Randy Hutson, February 20, 2015)
</p>
