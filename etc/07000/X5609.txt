<p>Let A&prime;B&prime;C&prime; be the Euler triangle. Let L be the line through A&prime;
parallel to the Euler line, and define M and N cyclically. Let L&prime; be
the reflection of L in line BC, and define M&prime; and N&prime; cyclically. The
lines L&prime;, M&prime;, N&prime; concur in X(5609); c.f., X(113), X(399), and X(1511).
Let N<sub>A</sub> be the reflection of X(5) in the perpendicular
bisector of BC, and define N<sub>B</sub> and N<sub>C</sub> cyclically.
Then X(5609) = X(23)-of-N<sub>A</sub>N<sub>B</sub>N<sub>C</sub>. (Randy
Hutson, July 18, 2014)</p>
