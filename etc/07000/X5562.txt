<p>Let A&prime; be the point, other than A, in which the line parallel to BC
meets the circumcircle of ABC, and define B&prime; and C&prime; cyclically. Let
P<sub>A</sub> be the point in which the line through A&prime; perpendicular
to BC meets BC, and define P<sub>B</sub> and P<sub>C</sub> cyclically;
the points P<sub>A</sub>, P<sub>B</sub>, P<sub>C</sub> are collinear,
forming the so-called Simson line of A&prime;. The Simson lines of A&prime;, B&prime;, C&prime;
concur in X(5562). &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; (Dao Thanh Oai, October 2,
2013)</p>
<p>X(5562) = isotomic conjugate of isogonal conjugate of X(418), and
A&prime;B&prime;C&prime; is homothetic to the orthic triangle of ABC from X(2) with ratio
-2. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; (Peter Moses, October 4, 2013)</p>

<p>Let A&prime;B&prime;C&prime; be the cevian triangle of X(3). Let A&Prime;B&Prime;C&Prime; be the
reflection of A&prime;B&prime;C&prime; in X(3). Let A*B*C be the tangential triangle,
with respect to A&prime;B&prime;C&prime;, of the circumconic of A&prime;B&prime;C&prime; centered at X(3)
(that is, the bicevian conic of X(3) and X(394)). The lines A&Prime;B*,
B&Prime;B*, C&Prime;C* concur in X(5562). (Randy Hutson, August 17, 2014)</p>
