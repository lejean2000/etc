<h3 id="X6099">X(6099)&nbsp;=&nbsp; 16th HATZIPOLAKIS-MONTESDEOCA POINT</h3>
Barycentrics &nbsp;&nbsp; f(a,b,c) : f(b,c,a) : f(c,a,b),
where f(a,b,c) = a^2/((b - c) (a^3 b - a^2 b^2 - a b^3 + b^4 + a^3 c +
a b^2 c - a^2 c^2 + a b c^2 - 2 b^2 c^2 - a c^3 + c^4))

<p>Let A&prime;B&prime;C&prime; be the antipedal triangle of the incenter. Let A&Prime; =
X(1986)-of-A&prime;BC, and define B&Prime; and C&Prime; cyclically. Let O<sub>A</sub>
be the circumcenter of A&prime;BC, and define O<sub>B</sub> and O<sub>C</sub>
cyclically. The circumcircles of the four triangles ABC, AB&Prime;C&Prime;,
A&Prime;BC&Prime;, A&Prime;B&Prime;C concur in X(6099). The lines O<sub>A</sub>A&Prime;,
O<sub>B</sub>B&Prime;, O<sub>C</sub>C&Prime; also concur in X(6099).
See
<a href="http://www.hyacinthos.epizy.com/message.php?msg=22617"
class="bold">Hyacinthos</a> 22617, October 8, 2014.</p>
<p>Let T be the triangle whose sidelines are the reflections of the
line X(3)X(11) in the sidelines of ABC. Then T is perspective to ABC,
and X(6099) is the perpsector. (Randy Hutson, October 16, 2014)</p>
