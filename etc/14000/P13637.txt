<p>
Inscribe three squares into a triangle ABC such that each square has two vertices on two distinct sides of ABC and the other vertices of the three squares coincide at the vertices of another triangle A'B'C'.
</p>
<p>
Let the squares be &sigma;<sub>a</sub>=B'C'A<sub>c</sub>A<sub>b</sub>, &sigma;<sub>b</sub>=C'A'B<sub>a</sub>B<sub>c</sub> and &sigma;<sub>c</sub>=A'B'C<sub>b</sub>C<sub>a</sub> with B<sub>a</sub>, C<sub>a</sub> on BC, C<sub>b</sub>, A<sub>b</sub> on CA,  A<sub>c</sub>, B<sub>c</sub> on AB and centers A<sub>o</sub>, B<sub>o</sub>, C<sub>o</sub>, respectively.
This construction has four solutions. For each case, the triangle A'B'C' will be named here the <b>tri-squares triangle</b> of ABC and the triangle A<sub>o</sub>B<sub>o</sub>C<sub>o</sub> will be referred here as <b>tri-squares-central triangle</b> of ABC.
</p>
