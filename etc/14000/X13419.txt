<p>
Let P be a point on the circumcircle and D a point on the line BC. Let U and V  be the  reflections of D in PB and PC, respectively.  The envelope of lines UV when D moves on BC is a parabola.  Let P be its focus and d<sub>a</sub> its directrix. The envelope of  d<sub>a</sub> as P moves on circumcircle is a circle, O<sub>a</sub>. Define (O<sub>b</sub>) and (O<sub>c</sub>) cyclically.  The radical center of the circles (O<sub>a</sub>), (O<sub>b</sub>), (O<sub>c</sub>) is  X(13419); see <a href="https://groups.io/g/euclid/message/611">Euclid #611</a>.   (Angel Montesdeoca, February 6, 2020)
