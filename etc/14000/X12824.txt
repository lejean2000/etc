<p>
Let P = p : q : r be barycentrics for a point P in the plane of a triangle ABC.  Let<br>
A' = reflection of A in P, and define B' and C' cyclically<br>
Ab = orthogonal projection of A' on AC, and define Bc and Ca cyclically<br>
Ac = orthogonal projection of A' on AB, and define Ba and Cb cyclically<br>
(Na) = nine-point circle of AAbAC, and define (Nb) and (Nc) cyclically<br>
The circles concur in the point Q given by

Q =  a^2 (2 a^2 b^2 c^2 p+a^4 c^2 q+b^4 c^2 q-2 a^2 c^4 q-2 b^2 c^4 q+c^6 q+a^4 b^2 r-2 a^2 b^4 r+b^6 r-2 b^4 c^2 r+b^2 c^4 r) (b^2 c^2 p^2+a^2 c^2 p q-c^4 p q+a^2 b^2 p r-b^4 p r+a^4 q r-a^2 b^2 q r-a^2 c^2 q r) : :
