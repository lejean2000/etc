<p>
Let P be the trilinear pole of the tangent to the MacBeath circumconic at a point Q.  (i.e., P = X(3)-cross conjugate of a point Q on the MacBeath circumconic.)
The locus of the polar conjugate of P as Q varies is an inconic centered at X(13567), and passing through X(338), X(1146), and X(3269).  The Brianchon point (perspector) of this inconic is X(2052).  (Randy Hutson, November 2, 2017)
</p>
