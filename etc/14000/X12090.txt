<p>
Let ABC be a triangle, P = X(20) = De Longchamps point of ABC and let A'B'C' be the antipedal triangle of P. The parallel lines to AP through C', B' cut BC at A<sub>b</sub> and A<sub>c</sub>, respectively. Build B<sub>c</sub>, B<sub>a</sub>, C<sub>a</sub>, C<sub>b</sub> cyclically. Then these six points lie on a conic where named the <b>Hatzipolakis-Montesdeoca-De Longchamps conic</b>.
