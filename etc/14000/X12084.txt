<p>
Let La be the polar of X(3) wrt the A-power circle, and define Lb, Lc cyclically.  Let A' = Lb&cap;Lc, B' = Lc&cap;La, C' = La&cap;Lb.  Triangle A'B'C' is homothetic to ABC at X(5094) and to the anticomplementary triangle at X(22).  X(4)-of-A'B'C' = X(1657), and X(5)-of-A'B'C' = X(12084). (Randy Hutson, March 9, 2017)
</p>
