<h3 id="X13443">X(13443)&nbsp;=&nbsp;  CENTER OF 1st DAO-APOLLONIUS CIRCLE </h3>
Barycentrics &nbsp;&nbsp; a ((b-c) (a+b-c) (a-b+c) (-a+b+c)-(a-b-c) (b-c) (a^2-b^2+4 b c-c^2) Sin[A/2]+(a-b+c) (a^3-a b^2-3 a^2 c-b^2 c+3 a c^2+2 b c^2-c^3) Sin[B/2]-(a+b-c) (a^3-3 a^2 b+3 a b^2-b^3+2 b^2 c-a c^2-b c^2) Sin[C/2]) : :
<p>
Let A'B'C' be the intouch triangle of a triangle ABC.  There exists a circle U, here named the <b>1st Dao-Apollonius  circle</b>, that is tangent to each of the four circles (AB'C'), (BC'A'), (CA'B'), (ABC).  The center of U is X(13443), and the tangency point is X(13444).  If, instead,  A'B'C' is the orthic triangle, there exists a circle V, here named the <b>2nd Dao-Apollonius circle</b>,  tangents to each of the circles (AB'C'), (BC'A'), (CA'B'), (ABC); the center of V is X(7952) and the tangent point is X(108).  (Dao Thanh Oai, Peter Moses, June 1, 2017).
</p>
<p>
X(13443) lies on the cubic K259 and these lines:  {1,167}, {57,1130}
</p>
<p>
