<h3 id="X12089">X(12089)&nbsp;=&nbsp; CENTER OF ASHRAFOV-MONTESDEOCA CONIC</h3>
Barycentrics &nbsp;&nbsp;  a(a+b-c)(a-b+c)(b+c)(a^6+2a^5(b+c)-a^4(b^2+c^2)-2a^3(b^3+c^3)+a^2(b^2-c^2)^2-2a b(b-c)^2c(b+c)-(b^2-c^2)^2(b^2+c^2)) :  :
<p>
See Antreas Hatzipolakis and Angel Montesdeoca, <a href="http://www.hyacinthos.epizy.com/message.php?msg=25540"> <b>Hyacinthos 25540</b></a>.
</p>
<p>
Let ABC be a triangle with incenter X(1)=I and let a' be perpendicular line to AI through I. Denote as A'<sub>b</sub> the intersection of a' and the perpendicular line to AB through B and denote as A'<sub>c</sub> the intersection of a' and the perpendicular line through C to AC. Perpendicular lines to a' through A'<sub>b</sub> and A'<sub>c</sub> cut BC at A<sub>b</sub> and A<sub>c</sub>, respectively. Points B<sub>c</sub>, B<sub>a</sub>, C<sub>a</sub>, C<sub>b</sub> are built cyclically. Then these six points lie on a conic here named the <b>Ashrafov-Montesdeoca conic</b>. (See: <a href="http://amontes.webs.ull.es/otrashtm/HGT2017.htm#HG270217">Angel Montesdeoca, HGT-Feb 17, 2017</a>).
</p>
<p>
An alternative construction of A<sub>b</sub> and A<sub>c</sub>: Let ABC be a triangle with incenter I=X(1) and let A'B'C' be the antipedal triangle of I (excentral triangle). The parallel lines to AI through C', B' cut BC at A<sub>b</sub> and A<sub>c</sub>, respectively. (Antreas Hatzipolakis, <a href="http://www.hyacinthos.epizy.com/message.php?msg=25529">Hyacinthos 25529</a>).
</p>
<p>
X(12089) lies on these lines: {65, 603}, {73, 2292}, {1071, 3931}, {1254, 1400}
</p>
