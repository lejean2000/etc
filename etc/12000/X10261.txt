<h3 id="X10261">X(10261)&nbsp;=&nbsp; 1st HUNG-LOZADA-GREBE POINT</h3>
Barycentrics &nbsp;&nbsp; 1/(3*SA^2-2*SA*S+3*S^2-4*SB* SC) : :
<p>
In the plane of a triangle ABC, let A'B'C' be the inner Grebe triangle.  Let A'' = reflection of A' in B'C', and define B'' and C'' cyclically.  Then A''B''C'' is perspective to ABC, and the perspector is X(10261).
</p>
<p>
Choices of a triangle T = A'B'C' other than the inner Grebe triangle provide reflection triangles A''B''C'' that are also perpsective to ABC.  The appearance of (T,n) in the following list means that ABC and A''B''C'' are perspective with perspector X(n):  (ABC,4), (2nd circumperp,1), (Euler, 4), (excentral, 1), (2nd extouch, 943), (inner-Grebe, 10261),  (outer-Grebe, 10262), (incentral, 3065), (1st Morley, 1134), (2nd Morley, 357), (3rd Morley, 1136), (inner-Napoleon, 4), (outer-Napoleon, 4), (orthic, 186), (orthocentroidal, 3), (inner-Vecten, 486), (outer-Vecten, 485).  See <a href="http://www.hyacinthos.epizy.com/message.php?msg=24193"> 24193</a>).
</p>
<p>
X(10261) lies on the Jerabek hyperbola and these lines:  {54,5871}, {1173,6202}
</p>
<p>
X(10261) =isogonal conjugate of X(13616)
</p>
