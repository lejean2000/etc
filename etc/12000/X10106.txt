<h3 id="X10106">X(10106)&nbsp;=&nbsp; 29th HATZIPOLAKIS-MONTESDEOCA POINT</h3>
Barycentrics &nbsp;&nbsp; 2 a^4-a^3 (b+c)+a (b-c)^2 (b+c)-(b^2-c^2)^2-a^2 (b^2-6 b c+c^2) : :
<br>
<b>
X(10106) = (2R - r)*X(1) + r*X(4)
</b>
<p><b></b>
Let I be the incenter of a triangle ABC, and<br>
A'B'C' = intouch triangle (the pedal triangle of I)<br>
H' = X(4)-of-A'B'C'<br>
Ab = orthogonal projection of A on H'B', and define Bc and Ca cyclically<br>
Ac = orthogonal projection of A on H'C', and define Ba and Cb cyclically<br>
La = Euler line of AAbAc, and define Lb and Lc cyclically<br>
Pa = line through A'' parallel to La, and define Pb and Pc cyclically<br>
</p>
<p>
The lines La, Lb, Lc concur in X(10106).  Let
</p>
<p>
Qa = line through A parallel to La, and define Qb and Qc cyclically<br>
Ra = line through A' parallel to La, and define Rb and Rc cyclically<br>
</p>
<p>
The lines La, Lb, Lc concur in X(5836), the midpoint of X(8) and X(65).  The lines Qa, Qb, Qc concur in X(8), and the lines Ra, Rb, Rc concur in X(145).  (Antreas Hatzipolakis and Angel Montesdeoca, August 9, 2016; see <a href="http://www.hyacinthos.epizy.com/message.php?msg=23990">Hyacinthos 23990</a>)
