<h3 id="X10035">X(10035)&nbsp;=&nbsp; 26th HATZIPOLAKIS-MONTESDEOCA POINT</h3>
Barycentrics &nbsp;&nbsp; 2 a^9 (b+c)+b c (b^2-c^2)^4-2 a^8 (b^2+c^2)+a (b-c)^4 (b+c)^3 (b^2+b c+c^2)-a^7 (7 b^3+b^2 c+b c^2+7 c^3)+2 a^2 (b^2-c^2)^2 (b^4-2 b^3 c-2 b c^3+c^4)+a^6 (6 b^4-2 b^3 c+8 b^2 c^2-2 b c^3+6 c^4)-a^3 (b-c)^2 (5 b^5+7 b^4 c+6 b^3 c^2+6 b^2 c^3+7 b c^4+5 c^5)+a^5 (9 b^5-4 b^4 c+b^3 c^2+b^2 c^3-4 b c^4+9 c^5)-a^4 (6 b^6-5 b^5 c+2 b^4 c^2+6 b^3 c^3+2 b^2 c^4-5 b c^5+6 c^6) : :
<p>
Let ABC be a triangle and A'B'C' the cevian triangle of the incenter, I, and let
</p>
<p>
Oa = circumcenter of IB'C'; define Ob and Oc cyclically<br>
Oab = circumcenter of AB'I; define Obc and Oca cyclically<br>
Oac = circumcenter of AC'I; define Oba and Ocb cyclically<br>
Ga = centroid of OaObOc; define Gb and Gc cyclically<br>
Na = nine-point center of IB'C'; define Nb and Nc cyclically<br>
Nab = nine-point center of AB'I; define Nbc and Nca cyclically<br>
Nac = nine-point center of AC'I; define Nba and Ncb cyclically<br>
</p>
<p>
Let Ea be the Euler line of NaNabNac, and define Eb and Ec cyclically; then Ea, Eb, Ec concur in X(10035).  Let Fa be the Euler line of OaOabOac, and define Fb and Fc cyclically; the lines Fa, Fb, Fc are parallel, and they meet in X(517).  (Antreas Hatzipolakis and Angel Montesdeoca, August 1, 2016; see <a href="http://www.hyacinthos.epizy.com/message.php?msg=23914">Hyacinthos 23914</a>)
</p>
