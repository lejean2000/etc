<h3 id="X10122">X(10122)&nbsp;=&nbsp; 32nd HATZIPOLAKIS-MONTESDEOCA POINT</h3>
Barycentrics &nbsp;&nbsp; a (a^4 (b-c)^2-a^5 (b+c)+(b^2-c^2)^2 (b^2-b c+c^2)-a (b-c)^2 (b^3+4 b^2 c+4 b c^2+c^3)+a^3 (2 b^3+3 b^2 c+3 b c^2+2 c^3)+a^2 (-2 b^4+3 b^3 c+6 b^2 c^2+3 b c^3-2 c^4)) : :
<b><br>
X(10122) = (r+2R)*X(1) - r*X(21) = (r+4R)*X(7) - (r+2R)*X(79)

</b>
<p>
Let ABC be a triangle, and let<br>
A'b = orthogonal projection of A on the external bisector of angle ABC<br>
A'c = orthogonal projection of A on the external bisector of angle ACB<br>
Ea = Euler line of AA'bA'c, and define Eb and Ec cyclically<br>
Ia = A-excenter of ABC, and define Ib and Ic cyclically<br>
A'B'C' = intouch triangle (the pedal triangle of the incenter)<br>
A'' = orthogonal projection of IA on B'C', and define B'' and C'' cyclically<br>
Pa = line through A'' parallel to Ea, and define Pb and Pc cyclically<br>
A''' = orthogonal projections of A' on IbIc, and define B''' and C''' cyclically<br>
Qa = line through A''' parallel to Ea, and define Qb and Qc cyclically
</p>
<p>
The lines Pa, Pb, Pc concur in X(10122).  The lines Qa, Qb, Qc concur in X(10123).  The lines Ea, Eb, Ec concur in X(442).   (Antreas Hatzipolakis and Angel Montesdeoca, August 14, 2016.  See <a href="http://www.hyacinthos.epizy.com/message.php?msg=24025">Hyacinthos 24025</a> and href="http://www.hyacinthos.epizy.com/message.php?msg=24015" <b>Hyacinthos 24015</b>.)
</p>
