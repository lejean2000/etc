<p>
As with the Kenmotu squares, we inscribe in a triangle ABC <b>three congruent equilateral triangles</b> PA<sub>b</sub>A<sub>c</sub>, PB<sub>c</sub>B<sub>a</sub> and PC<sub>a</sub>C<sub>b</sub>, with B<sub>a</sub>, C<sub>a</sub> on BC, C<sub>b</sub>, A<sub>b</sub> on CA and A<sub>c</sub>, B<sub>c</sub> on AB. There are two points P making possible this construction: P = P<sub>i</sub>=X(15) and P = P<sub>o</sub>=X(16). The equilateral triangles obtained in each case are here named the <b>A-, B-, C- inner/outer equilateral triangles</b>, respectively.
</p>
<p>
In each case, the points B<sub>a</sub>, C<sub>a</sub>, C<sub>b</sub>, A<sub>b</sub>, A<sub>c</sub>, B<sub>c</sub> are obviously concyclic. Their circles &#915;<sub>i</sub> and &#915;<sub>o</sub>, here named <b>the inner and outer tri-equilateral circles</b>, are denoted and determined as follows:
</p>
<p>
&#915;<sub>i</sub>: center = X(15), radius = 2*R/|sqrt(3)+cot(&#969;)| <br>
&#915;<sub>o</sub>: center = X(16), radius = 2*R/|sqrt(3)-cot(&#969;)|,
</p>
<p>
where R and &#969; are the circumradius and the Brocard angle of ABC, respectively.
<br>
<br>
No ETC center X(i), for 1 &#8804; i &#8804; 10682, lies on either circle.
<br>
<br>
The exsimilcenter of these circles is X(3) and their insimilcenter is X(6). Their radical trace is X(10631).
</p>
<p>
For P = P<sub>i</sub>=X(15), the vertices of the inner equilateral triangles have trilinear coordinates:
<br>
&#8195;&#8195;&#8195;A<sub>b</sub> = 1 : 0 : c*a/(SC+sqrt(3)*S) &#8195;&#8195;&#8195; B<sub>c</sub> = a*b/(SA+sqrt(3)*S) : 1 : 0 &#8195;&#8195;&#8195; C<sub>a</sub> = 0 : b*c/(SB+sqrt(3)*S) : 1
<br>
&#8195;&#8195;&#8195;A<sub>c</sub> = 1 : a*b/(SB+sqrt(3)*S) : 0 &#8195;&#8195;&#8195; B<sub>a</sub> = 0 : 1 : b*c/(SC+sqrt(3)*S) &#8195;&#8195;&#8195; C<sub>b</sub> = c*a/(SA+sqrt(3)*S) : 0 : 1
<br>
<br>and, for P=P<sub>o</sub>=X(16), the vertices of the outer equilateral triangles have trilinear coordinates:
<br>
&#8195;&#8195;&#8195;A<sub>b</sub> = 1 : 0 : c*a/(SC-sqrt(3)*S) &#8195;&#8195;&#8195; B<sub>c</sub> = a*b/(SA-sqrt(3)*S) : 1 : 0 &#8195;&#8195;&#8195; C<sub>a</sub> = 0 : b*c/(SB-sqrt(3)*S) : 1
<br>
&#8195;&#8195;&#8195;A<sub>c</sub> = 1 : a*b/(SB-sqrt(3)*S) : 0 &#8195;&#8195;&#8195; B<sub>a</sub> = 0 : 1 : b*c/(SC-sqrt(3)*S) &#8195;&#8195;&#8195; C<sub>b</sub> = c*a/(SA-sqrt(3)*S) : 0 : 1
</p>
<p>
For the inner-equilateral triangles, let's define the <b>inner tri-equilateral triangle</b> A<sub>i</sub>B<sub>i</sub>C<sub>i</sub> as the triangle bounded by the lines A<sub>b</sub>A<sub>c</sub>, B<sub>c</sub>B<sub>a</sub> and C<sub>a</sub>C<sub>b</sub> and, similarly, define the <b>outer tri-equilateral triangle</b> A<sub>o</sub>B<sub>o</sub>C<sub>o</sub> with the outer-equilateral triangles.  The trilinear coordinates of A<sub>i</sub> and A<sub>o</sub> are:
<br>
A<sub>i</sub> = (SA-sqrt(3)*S)*a/(SA+sqrt(3)*S) : b : c
<br>
A<sub>o</sub> = (SA+sqrt(3)*S)*a/(SA-sqrt(3)*S) : b : c
</p>
