Reflections of circumcircle-points in the orthocenter:  X(10721)-X(10737)

<img src="rightri.svg" style="height:30px" alt="rightri">

<p class="textcenter">
This preamble and centers X(10721)-X(10737) were contributed by Clark Kimberling and Peter Moses, November 9, 2016.
</p>
<p>
Suppose that P is a point on the circumcrcle of a triangle ABC, and let
</p>
<p>
H = X(4), the orthocenter of ABC<br>
P' = reflection of P in H<br>
P<sup>c</sup> = complement of P<br>
P<sup>a</sup> = anticomplement of P<br>
H<sup>a</sup> = anticomplement of H (the orthocenter, X(4)
H<sup>aa</sup> = anticomplement of H<sup>a</sup>.
</p>
<p>
Then
</p>
<p></p>
P' = midpoint of P<sup>a</sup> and H<sup>aa</sup><br>
P' = reflection of H<sup>a</sup> in P<sup>c</sup>.<br>
<p></p>
