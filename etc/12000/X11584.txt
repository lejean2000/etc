<h3 id="X11584">X(11584)&nbsp;=&nbsp;  ANTIGONAL CONJUGATE OF X(1157) </h3>
Trilinears &nbsp;&nbsp; (4*cos(A)*cos(B-C)+2*cos(2*A)- 1)/(2*cos(2*A)*cos(B-C)+cos(3* A)) : :
<br>
Barycentrics &nbsp;&nbsp; (2*SA-3*R^2)/(SA^2-R^2*SA-S^2) : :
<p>
In the plane of a triangle ABC, N = X(5) = nine-point center, and let<br>
A' = reflection of N in BC, and define B' and C' cyclically<br>
A'' = reflection of A in B'C', and define B'' and C'' cyclically<br>
Oa = circumcircle of NAA'', and define Ob and Oc cyclically.<br>
The circles Oa, Ob, Oc are coaxal, and their points of intersection are N and X(11584).  See Antreas Hatzipolakis and César Lozada, <a href="http://www.hyacinthos.epizy.com/message.php?msg=25081">Hyacinthos 25081</a>
</p>
<p>

{5,195}, {30,5684}, {143,11538}, {2070,6343}
</p>
