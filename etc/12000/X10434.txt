Let A<sub>3</sub> be the intersection of the tangents to the Conway circle at B<sub>a</sub> and C<sub>a</sub>, and define B<sub>3</sub>, C<sub>3</sub> cyclically.
Triangle A<sub>3</sub>B<sub>3</sub>C<sub>3</sub> is here named the <b>3rd Conway triangle</b>.  A<sub>3</sub>B<sub>3</sub>C<sub>3</sub> is also the polar triangle of the Conway circle.<br>
