<h3 id="X10111">X(10111)&nbsp;=&nbsp; HATZIPOLAKIS-MOSES NINE-POINT-CIRCLES POINT</h3>
Barycentrics &nbsp;&nbsp; (a^2-b^2-c^2) (2 a^14-6 a^12 b^2+5 a^10 b^4+a^8 b^6-4 a^6 b^8+4 a^4 b^10-3 a^2 b^12+b^14-6 a^12 c^2+12 a^10 b^2 c^2-9 a^8 b^4 c^2+8 a^6 b^6 c^2-12 a^4 b^8 c^2+12 a^2 b^10 c^2-5 b^12 c^2+5 a^10 c^4-9 a^8 b^2 c^4-4 a^6 b^4 c^4+8 a^4 b^6 c^4-21 a^2 b^8 c^4+9 b^10 c^4+a^8 c^6+8 a^6 b^2 c^6+8 a^4 b^4 c^6+24 a^2 b^6 c^6-5 b^8 c^6-4 a^6 c^8-12 a^4 b^2 c^8-21 a^2 b^4 c^8-5 b^6 c^8+4 a^4 c^10+12 a^2 b^2 c^10+9 b^4 c^10-3 a^2 c^12-5 b^2 c^12+c^14)  : :
<p>
Let A'B'C' be the orthic triangle of a triangle ABC, and<br>
O = circumcenter of ABC<br>
Ab = reflection of A' in BO, and define Bc and Ca cyclically<br>
Ac = reflection of A' in CO, and define Ba and Cb cyclically<br>
(Na) = nine-point circle of A'AbAc, and define (Nb) and (Nc) cyclically<br>
Na = nine-point center of A'AbAc, and define Nb and Nc cyclically<br>
The triangle NaNbNc is here named the <b>Hyacinth triangle</b>.
</p>
<p>
The circles (Na), (Nb), (Nc) concur in X(10111).   (Antreas Hatzipolakis and Peter Moses, August 11, 2016.  See <a href="http://www.hyacinthos.epizy.com/message.php?msg=24008">Hyacinthos 24008</a>.  For a related general theorem about isogonal conjugate pairs of points, see <a href="http://www.hyacinthos.epizy.com/message.php?msg=24006">Hyacinthos 24006</a>).
</p>
