<p>As a variant of the construction of the Conway circle at MathWorld <a href="http://mathworld.wolfram.com/ConwayCircle.html"><b>Conway circle</b></a>, define A<sub>b</sub> and A<sub>c</sub> inwards; i.e., A<sub>b</sub> is on the ray AC, and  A<sub>c</sub> on the AB, with |AA<sub>b</sub>| = |AA<sub>c</sub>| = |BC| = a.
</p>
<p>
Construct B<sub>c</sub>, B<sub>a</sub>, C<sub>a</sub>, C<sub>b</sub> cyclically.  The triangle A'B'C' bounded by the lines A<sub>b</sub>A<sub>c</sub>, B<sub>c</sub>B<sub>a</sub>, C<sub>a</sub>C<sub>b</sub> is here named the <b>inner-Conway triangle</b> of ABC, with A-vertex given by trilinears A' = bc : (c - b)c : (b - c)b.  (A'B'C' is also the intouch triangle of the anticomplementary triangle.)
</p>
