<h3 id="X11589">X(11589)&nbsp;=&nbsp; CICUMCIRCLE-INVERSE OF X(64) </h3>
Barycentrics &nbsp;&nbsp;  a^2(b^2 + c^2 - a^2)/[(3a^4 -  b^4 -  c^4 - 2a^2b^2 - 2a^2c^2 + 2b^2c^2)*(a^4  +  b^4 - 2c^4 - 2a^2b^2 +  a^2c^2 +  b^2c^2)*(a^4  - 2b^4 +  c^4 +  a^2b^2 - 2a^2c^2 +  b^2c^2)] : :
<p>
Let P be a point in the plane of a triangle ABC, and let<br>
A'B'C' = cevian triangle of P<br>
A'' = orthogonal projection of P on B'C', and define B'' and C'' cyclically<br>
Oa = circumcircle of APA'', and define Ob and Oc cyclically<br>
The locus of P for which Oa, Ob, Oc are coaxal is the Darboux cubic, K004.  If P = X(3), then Oa, Ob, Oc intersect in X(3) and X(11589).  See Antreas Hatzipolakis and Peter Moses, <a href="http://www.hyacinthos.epizy.com/message.php?msg=25106">Hyacinthos 25106</a>
</p>
<p>
Let P be a point on the Darboux cubic.  Let A'B'C' be the pedal triangle of P.  The circumcircles of AA'P, BB'P, CC'P concur in two points, P and P*.  P* lies on the Darboux quintic (Q071), and is introduced here as the <b>Darboux quintic point of P</b>; See https://bernard-gibert.pagesperso-orange.fr/curves/q071.html.  X(11589) is the Darboux quintic point of X(1498).  (Randy Hutson, March 9, 2017)
</p>
<p>

</p>
