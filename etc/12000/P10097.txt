<p class="preamble-title">
Points associated with Dao circles:  X(10097)-X(10103)
</p>
<img src="rightri.svg" style="height:30px" alt="rightri">
</header>
<div class="preamble-sect">


<p class="textcenter">
This preamble and centers X(10097)-X(10103) were contributed by Peter Moses and Clark Kimberling, August 4, 2016, based findings of Dao Thanh Oai.
</p>
<p>
Suppose that P is a point in the plane of a triangle ABC, but not on a sideline (BC, CA, AB).  Let P' be the isogonal conjugate of P.  Let (O) be the circumcircle of ABC, and let C(P) be the conic through A,B,C,P,P'.  Let D be the point in (O)&#8745;C(P) other than A,B,C; let E be a point on (O), other than A,B,C,D, and let E' the point in DE&#8745;C(P), other than D.  The points P,P',E,E' lie on a circle, here named the <b>Dao circle of P, denoted by D(P)</b>.  The point E' is here named the <b>1st (P,E)-Dao point.</b>  (Based on "A generalization of the Sawayama-Th√©bault theorem", Dao Thanh Oai, July 21, 2016; <a href="http://www.adgeom.epizy.com/message.php?msg=3353">ADGEOM 3353</a>)
</p>
<p>
Write P = p : q : r (barycentrics).  Then C(P) is given by
</p>
<p>
b<sup>2</sup>c<sup>2</sup>p<sup>2</sup>x(y - z) + c<sup>2</sup>a<sup>2</sup>q<sup>2</sup>y(z - x) +
a<sup>2</sup>b<sup>2</sup>r<sup>2</sup>z(x - y)  = 0.
</p>
<p>
The perspector of C(P) is
</p>
<p>
a<sup>2</sup>p(b<sup>2</sup>r<sup>2</sup> - c<sup>2</sup>q<sup>2</sup>) : b<sup>2</sup>q(c<sup>2</sup>p<sup>2</sup> - a<sup>2</sup>r<sup>2</sup>) : c<sup>2</sup>r(a<sup>2</sup>q<sup>2</sup> - b<sup>2</sup>p<sup>2</sup>).
</p>
<p>
The circle D(P) meets (O) in another point, the <b>2nd (P,E)-Dao point</b>, and D(P) meets C(P) in another point, the <b>3rd (P,E)-Dao point</b>.  These points are represented by F and F', respectively, in the following examples.
</p>
<p>
<b>Example 1.</b>  C(X(3)) is the Jerabek conic.  Taking E = X(111) gives E' = X(10097), F = X(5505), F' = X(10098).<br>
<b>Example 2.</b>  Continuing with C(X(3)), take E = X(98).  Then E' = X(879), F = X(67), F' = X(935). <br>
<b>Example 3.</b>  Continuing with C(X(3)), take E = X(105).  Then E' = X(10099), F = X(10100), F' = X(10101); i.e. the 1st, 2nd, 3rd (X(3),X(105))-Dao points are E', F, F'.<br>
<b>Example 4.</b>  1st, 2nd, 3rd (X(2),X(112))-Dao points are X(25), X(10101), X(10102).<br>
</p>
