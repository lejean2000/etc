<h3 id="X10125">X(10125)&nbsp;=&nbsp; 1st HATZIPOLAKIS-MOSES-EULER POINT</h3>
Barycentrics &nbsp;&nbsp;  2 a^10-5 a^8 b^2+2 a^6 b^4+4 a^4 b^6-4 a^2 b^8+b^10-5 a^8 c^2+6 a^6 b^2 c^2-3 a^4 b^4 c^2+5 a^2 b^6 c^2-3 b^8 c^2+2 a^6 c^4-3 a^4 b^2 c^4-2 a^2 b^4 c^4+2 b^6 c^4+4 a^4 c^6+5 a^2 b^2 c^6+2 b^4 c^6-4 a^2 c^8-3 b^2 c^8+c^10 : :
<p>
Suppose that P is a point in the plane of a triangle ABC, and let<br>
Oa = circumcenters of PBC<br>
Oab = orthogonal projection of Oa on AC<br>
Oac = orthogonal projection of Oa on AB<br>
O'a = circumcenter of OaOabOac, and define O'b and O'c cyclically<br>
If P = X(3), then the circumcenter of O'aO'bO'c is X(10125), which is on the Euler line of ABC.  The circumcircles of OaOabOac, ObObcOba, OcOcaOcb, ABC concur in X(110).  (Antreas Hatzipolakis and Peter Moses, August 17, 2016.  See <a href="http://www.hyacinthos.epizy.com/message.php?msg=24042">Hyacinthos 24042</a>.)
</p>
<p>
Let La be the polar of X(4) wrt the circle centered at A and passing through X(5).  Define Lb and Lc cyclically.  (Note: X(4) is the perspector of any circle centered at a vertex of ABC.)  Let A' = Lb&#8745;Lc, and define B' and C'cyclically.  A'B'C' is homothetic to ABC, and X(10125) = X(5) of A'B'C'.  (Randy Hutson, September 14, 2016)
</p>
<p>
Let N<sub>A</sub>N<sub>B</sub>N<sub>C</sub> be the reflection triangle of X(5). Let O<sub>A</sub> be the circumcenter of AN<sub>B</sub>N<sub>C</sub>, and define O<sub>B</sub> and O<sub>C</sub> cyclically.  X(10125) = X(5)-of-O<sub>A</sub>O<sub>B</sub>O<sub>C</sub>.  (Randy Hutson, June 7, 2019)
</p>
<p>
X(10125) lies on these lines:  {2,3}, {125,5944}, {5946,8254}
</p>
