<p class="preamble-title">
H-transforms and K-transforms:  X(10025)-X(10030)
</p>
<img src="rightri.svg" style="height:30px" alt="rightri">
</header>
<div class="preamble-sect">

<p class="textcenter">
This preamble and centers X(10025)-X(10030) were contributed by Clark Kimberling and Peter Moses, July 13, 2016.
</p>

<p>
Suppose that R = r : s : t and U = u : v : w (barycentrics) are points not on a sideline of a triangle ABC.  Let
</p>
<p>
L<sub>A</sub> be the line of the points 0 : t : r and - v : w : u <br>
L<sub>B</sub> be the line of the points s : 0 : r and v : - w : u <br>
L<sub>C</sub> be the line of the points s : t : 0 and v : w :  - u <br>
L'<sub>A</sub> be the line of the points 0 : r : s and - w : u : v <br>
L'<sub>B</sub> be the line of the points t : 0 : s and w : - u : v <br>
L'<sub>C</sub> be the line of the points t : r : 0 and w : u :  - v <br>
</p>
<p>
The lines L<sub>A</sub>, L<sub>B</sub>, L<sub>C</sub> concur in a point, P, and the lines L'<sub>A</sub>, L'<sub>B</sub>, L'<sub>C</sub> concur in a point, P'.  If R and U are triangle centers, then P and P' are a pair of bicentric points and PP' is a central line f*x + g*y + h*z = 0, so that the point H(R,U) = f : g : h is a triangle center, here introduced as the H-transform of R and U, given by first barycentric
</p>
<p>
f = v*w*(u/r + v/s - w/t)(u/r - v/s + w/t) - u^2 (v/s + w/t - u/r)^2 .
</p>
<p>
Next, let
</p>
<p>
L<sub>A</sub> be the line of the points 0 : t : r and - w : u : v <br>
L<sub>B</sub> be the line of the points s : 0 : r and w : - u : v <br>
L<sub>C</sub> be the line of the points s : t : 0 and w : u :  - v <br>
L'<sub>A</sub> be the line of the points 0 : r : s and - v : w : u <br>
L'<sub>B</sub> be the line of the points t : 0 : s and v : - w : u <br>
L'<sub>C</sub> be the line of the points t : r : 0 and v : w :  - u <br>
</p>
<p>
The lines L<sub>A</sub>, L<sub>B</sub>, L<sub>C</sub> concur in a point, P, and the lines L'<sub>A</sub>, L'<sub>B</sub>, L'<sub>C</sub> concur in a point, P'.  If R and U are triangle centers, then P and P' are a pair of bicentric points and PP' is a central line f*x + g*y + h*z = 0, so that the point K(R,U) = f : g : h is a triangle center, here introduced as the K-transform of R and U, given by first barycentric
</p>
<p>
f = v*w*(u/t + w/s - v/r)(u/s + v/t - w/r) - u^2 (v/r + w/s - u/t)(v/t + w/r - u/s)
</p>
<p>
Let G = centroid = X(2).  Then H(G,P) = K(G,P) and H(P,G) = K(P,G) for all P.  For fixed X, the locus of a point P satisfying H(P,X) = G is the circumconic with center X.  In particular, for X = X(125), the locus is the Jerabek hyperbola; for X = X(115), the Kiepert hyperbola; and for X = X(11), the Feuerbach hyperbola.
</p>
<p>
If P = p : q : r, then H(P,P) = qr - p<sup>2</sup> : rp - q<sup>2</sup> : pq - r<sup>2</sup>, which is the Steiner-circumellipse-inverse of P.
</p>
<p>
If P is on the circumcircle, then K(P,X(6)) = X(384).
</p>
