<p>
In the plane of a triangle ABC, let H = X(4) = orthocenter, and let<br>
Ab = point (nearer to B) in which line HB meets the circle (H, |HA'|)<br>
Ac = point (nearer to C) in which line HC meets the circle (H, |HA'|)<br>
A'' = BC&#8745;AbAc, and define B'' and C'' cyclically.<br>
The points A", B", C" are collinear; let L denote their line.<br>
X(11546) = trilinear pole of L<br>
</p>
<p>
If, in the construction, the circle (A', |A'H}) is used instead of (H, |HA'|), the resulting points are collinear; let L' denote their line.  Then X(11547) = trilinear pole of L'.  See Tran Quang Hung and César Lozada, <a href="http://www.hyacinthos.epizy.com/message.php?msg=24922">Hyacinthos 24922</a>
</p>
