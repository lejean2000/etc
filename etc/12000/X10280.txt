<h3 id="X10280">X(10280)&nbsp;=&nbsp;X(5) OF THE DIAGONAL TRIANGLE OF THE FEUERBACH QUADRANGLE</h3>
Barycentrics &nbsp;&nbsp; (a^8-4*(b^2+c^2)*a^6+(4*b^4+5*b^2*c^2+4*c^4)*a^4-3*b^2*c^2*(b^2+c^2)*a^2-(b^2-c^2)^2*(b^4-4*b^2*c^2+c^4))*(b^2-c^2)  :  :  <br>
Trilinears &nbsp;&nbsp; ((cos(A)+2*cos(3*A))*cos(B-C)-(cos(2*A)-2)*cos(2*(B-C))+3/2*cos(2*A)+1/2*cos(4*A))*sin(B-C)  :  :
<br>
<b>
X(10280) = X(3)-9*X(8371)
</b>
<p>
Let ABC be a triangle, P a point and A'B'C' the mid-cevian triangle of P. The locus of P such that the nine-point-circles of PNA', PNB', PNC' are coaxial is the Euler line. The locus of the 2nd intersection (other than the midpoint of PN) is the circle centered at X(10280), passing through X(i) for these i: 111, 476, 6722, 10276, 11746, 15366.  See Antreas Hatzipolakis and Peter Moses, <a href="https://groups.io/g/euclid/message/1761"> <b>euclid 1761</b></a>.
</p>
