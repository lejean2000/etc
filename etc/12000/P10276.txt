Feuerbach quadrangle and related centers: X(10276)-X(10281)
</p>
<img src="rightri.svg" style="height:30px" alt="rightri">

<div class="preamble-sect">

<p class="textcenter">

<p>
Let F<sub>A</sub>, F<sub>B</sub>, F<sub>C</sub> be the A-, B-, C- Feuerbach points of ABC, respectively (i.e., the touchpoints of the nine-points-circle and the excircles). Let F<sub>D</sub>=X(11) be the Feuerbach point of ABC. The cyclic quadrangle QA<sub>F</sub>={F<sub>A</sub>,F<sub>B</sub>,F<sub>C</sub>,F<sub>D</sub>} is here named the <b>Feuerbach quadrangle of ABC</b>. The centroid of QA<sub>F</sub> is X(10276).
</p>
<p>
Properties:
</p>
<p>
(1) A <b>maltitude</b> ("midpoint altitude") is a perpendicular drawn to a side of a quadrilateral from the midpoint of the opposite side. In a <u>cyclic</u> quadrilateral the four maltitudes concur at the <a href="http://mathworld.wolfram.com/Anticenter.html" target="_blank"><b>anticenter</b></a>. The anticenter of QA<sub>F</sub> is X(10277).
</p>
<p>
(2) In a cyclic quadrangle the centroids of the component triangles are the vertices of another cyclic quadrangle. For QA<sub>F</sub> this last quadrangle has centroid coinciding with the centroid of QA<sub>F</sub>.
</p>
<p>
(3) The diagonal triangle A*B*C* of QA<sub>F</sub> has vertices with barycentric coordinates:
<br>
&#8195;&#8195;&#8195;A* = {F<sub>A</sub>,F<sub>B</sub>}&#8745;{F<sub>C</sub>,F<sub>D</sub>} = -(SB-SC) :   SA-SC  :   SA-SB
<br>
&#8195;&#8195;&#8195;B* = {F<sub>B</sub>,F<sub>C</sub>}&#8745;{F<sub>A</sub>,F<sub>D</sub>} =   SB-SC  : -(SC-SA) :   SB-SA
<br>
&#8195;&#8195;&#8195;C* = {F<sub>C</sub>,F<sub>A</sub>}&#8745;{F<sub>B</sub>,F<sub>D</sub>} =   SC-SB  :   SC-SA  : -(SA-SB)
<br>
<br>&#8195;A*, B*, C* lie all on the cubics K237, K238, K239, K672.
<br>
<br>&#8195;A*B*C* has: area=area(ABC)/2, centroid = X(10278) , circumcenter = X(10279), orthocenter = X(5) and nine-point-center=X(10280)
</p>
<p>
In terms of Chris van Tienhoven's <a href="http://www.chrisvantienhoven.nl/index.php/mathematics/encyclopedia" target="_blank">Encyclopedia  of  Quadri-Figures (EQF)</a>, some centers of QA<sub>F</sub> are:
<br>
&#8195;QA-P1 = Quadrangle centroid = X(10276)
<br>
<br>&#8195;QA-P2 = Euler-Poncelot point = X(10277) = common point of the nine-point-circles of the component triangles
<br>
<br>&#8195;QA-P3 = Gergonne-Steiner Point = X(5) = common point of the midray-circles
<br>
&#8195;&#8195;&#8195;<i>The midray circles of the quadrangle {P<sub>1</sub>,P<sub>2</sub>,P<sub>3</sub>,P<sub>4</sub>} are the circumcircles of the triangles M<sub>ij</sub>M<sub>ik</sub>M<sub>il</sub>, for all combinations of (i,j,k,l) in {1,2,3,4}, where M<sub>ij</sub> = midpoint of {P<sub>i</sub>,P<sub>j</sub>}.</i>
<br>
<br>&#8195;QA-P4 = Isogonal Center  = X(5) = common point of the lines {O<sub>i</sub>,Q<sub>i</sub>}, where O<sub>i</sub> is the circumcenter of the triangle P<sub>j</sub>P<sub>k</sub>P<sub>l</sub> and Q<sub>i</sub> is the isogonal conjugate of P<sub>i</sub> w/r to P<sub>j</sub>P<sub>k</sub>P<sub>l</sub>.
<br>
<br>&#8195;QA-P6 = Parabola Axes Crosspoint = X(10276) = intersection point of the axes of the two parabolas that can be constructed through the points
<br>
<br>&#8195;QA-P7 = QA-Nine-point Homothetic Center = X(10281)
<br>
&#8195;&#8195;&#8195;<i> Let QA'<sub>P</sub> be the quadrangle of the nine-point centers of QA<sub>P</sub> and let QA"<sub>P</sub> be the quadrangle of the nine-point centers of QA'<sub>P</sub>. Then QA<sub>P</sub> and QA"<sub>P</sub> are homothetic with homothetic center QA-P7.</i>
<br>
<br>&#8195;QA-P8 = Midray Homothetic Center = X(5)
<br>
&#8195;&#8195;&#8195;<i>Let QA'<sub>P</sub> be the quadrangle of the midray centers of QA<sub>P</sub> and let QA"<sub>P</sub> be the quadrangle of the midray centers of QA'<sub>P</sub>. Then QA<sub>P</sub> and QA"<sub>P</sub> are homothetic with homothetic center QA-P8.</i>
<br>
<br>&#8195;QA-P10, QA-P11, QA-P12 and QA-P13 = centroid, circumcenter, orthocenter and nine-points-center, resp., of the diagonal triangle  = X(10278), X(10279), X(5), X(10280), resp.
</p>
