<h3 id="X11595">X(11595)&nbsp;=&nbsp; PERSPECTOR OF THE COMPLEMENT OF THE JERABEK CIRCUMHYPERBOLA</h3>
Barycentrics &nbsp;&nbsp; a^2 (3 a^6 b^2-3 a^4 b^4-a^2 b^6+b^8-a^6 c^2-3 a^4 b^2 c^2+7 a^2 b^4 c^2-b^6 c^2+2 a^4 c^4-3 a^2 b^2 c^4-3 b^4 c^4-a^2 c^6+3 b^2 c^6) (a^6 b^2-2 a^4 b^4+a^2 b^6-3 a^6 c^2+3 a^4 b^2 c^2+3 a^2 b^4 c^2-3 b^6 c^2+3 a^4 c^4-7 a^2 b^2 c^4+3 b^4 c^4+a^2 c^6+b^2 c^6-c^8)  : :  (Peter Moses, January 5, 2017)
<p>
The complement of the Jerabek hyperbola is given by the barycentric equation
</p>
<p>
f(a,b,c,x,y,z) + f(b,c,a,y,z,x) + f(c,a,b,z,x,y) = 0, where
</p>
<p>
f(a,b,c,x,y,z) = (b^2-c^2) (b^2 c^2 x^2+a^2 (-a^2+b^2+c^2) y z).
</p>
<p>
This conic passes through the vertices of the medial triangle and X(i) for these i:  3, 5, 6, 113, 141, 206, 942, 960, 1147, 1209, 1493, 1511, 2574, 2575, 2883, 4550, 5181, 6593, 8542, 10639, 10640, 10960, 10962.
</p>
<p>
See X(11597) and Antreas Hatzipolakis and César Lozada, <a href="http://www.hyacinthos.epizy.com/message.php?msg=25118">Hyacinthos 25118</a>
</p>
<p>
X(11595) lies on no line X(i)X(j) for 1 &lt;= i &lt; j &lt;= 11599<br>
</p>
