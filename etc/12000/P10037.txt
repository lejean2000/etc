<p>
The Yff circles are the two triplets of congruent circles in which each circle is tangent to two sides of a reference triangle (see <a href="http://mathworld.wolfram.com/YffCircles.html">Mathworld</a>). The circles in each triplet have radius r<sub>1</sub>=r*R/(R+r) and r<sub>2</sub>=r*R/(R-r), respectively. The centers A<sub>1</sub>, A<sub>2</sub> for the A-circles of the first and second triplets have respective trilinear coordinates:
</p>
<p>
A<sub>1</sub>= -(a^4-2*(b^2+b*c+c^2)*a^2+(b^2-c^2)^2)/(2*a^2*b*c) : 1 : 1 <br>
A<sub>2</sub>= +(a^4-2*(b^2-b*c+c^2)*a^2+(b^2-c^2)^2)/(2*a^2*b*c) : 1 : 1
</p>
<p>
and cyclically B<sub>1</sub>, C<sub>1</sub> and B<sub>2</sub>, C<sub>2</sub> for the B- and C- circles.
</p>
<p>
The triangles T<sub>1</sub>=A<sub>1</sub>B<sub>1</sub>C<sub>1</sub> and T<sub>2</sub>=A<sub>2</sub>B<sub>2</sub>C<sub>2</sub> are known as the inner- and outer- Yff triangles, respectively.
</p>
