<h3 id="X11276">X(11276)&nbsp;=&nbsp; MIDPOINT OF X(442) AND X(548)</h3>
Trilinears &nbsp;&nbsp; (5*sin(A/2)-6*sin(3*A/2))*cos( (B-C)/2)+(-cos(A)+1/2)*cos(B-C )-sin(A/2)*cos(3*(B-C)/2)-5*cos(A)-3*cos(2*A)-3 : :
<p class="bold">
X(11276) = (23R+14r)*X(3)+(R+2r)*X(4)
</p>
<p class="bold">
As a point on the Euler line, X(11276) has Shinagawa coefficients  (23R + 14r, -21R - 10r).
</p>
<p><b>
In the plane of a triangle ABC, let<br>
H = X(4) = othocenter<br>
N = X(5) = nine-point center<br>
A'B'C' = pedal triangle of N<br>
N* = nine-point center of A'B'C'<br>
Na = reflection of N* in AH, and define Nb and Nc cyclically<br>
N1 = reflection of Na in BC, and define N2 and N3 cyclically.<br>
The Euler line of N1N2N3 passes through N and N*.<br>
N = X(11276)-of-N1N2N3 if ABC is acute.  See Antreas Hatzipolakis and César Lozada, <a href="http://www.hyacinthos.epizy.com/message.php?msg=24884">Hyacinthos 24884</a></b>
</p>
