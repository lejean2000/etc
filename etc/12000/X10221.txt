<h3 id="X10221">X(10221)&nbsp;=&nbsp; HATZIPOLAKIS-MONTESDEOCA-EULER-PEDAL POINT</h3>
Barycentrics &nbsp;&nbsp;   a<sup>2/3</sup>b<sup>2/3</sup>c<sup>2/3</sup>S<sub>B</sub>S<sub>C</sub> - a<sup>2</sup>S<sub>A</sub>(S<sub>A</sub>S<sub>B</sub>S<sub>C</sub>)<sup>1/3</sup> : :
<b><br>
X(10221) = -2(S<sub>A</sub>S<sub>B</sub>S<sub>C</sub>)<sup>1/3</sup>*X(3) + a<sup>2/3</sup>b<sup>2/3</sup>c<sup>2/3</sup>*X(4)
</b>
<p>
Suppose that W is a triangle center on the Euler line of a triangle ABC.  Let A'B'C' be the pedal triangle of W.  Then W(ABC) = W(A'B'C') if and only if W = X(10221).  (Regarding the notation, recall that a triangle center is a function defined on a set of triangles, so that the notation W(T) is analogous to the notation f(x); i.e., W-of-T.)  (Antreas Hatzipolakis and Angel Montesdeoca, September 13, 2016.)  See <a href="http://www.hyacinthos.epizy.com/message.php?msg=24354"> 24354</a> and
<a href="http://amontes.webs.ull.es/otrashtm/HGT2016.htm#HG100916"> HG100916</a>.</p>
