<h3 id="X10109">X(10109)&nbsp;=&nbsp; MIDPOINT OF X(5)X(547)</h3>
Barycentrics &nbsp;&nbsp; 9*SB*SC+13*S^2 : :
<b><br>
X(10109) = 13 X(3) + 11 X(4) = X(2) + 3 X(5)
</b>
<p><b></b>
Let P  be a point in the plane of a triangle ABC, and<br>
Nab = nine-point center of ABA', and define Nbc and Nca cyclically<br>
Nac = nine-point center of ACA', and define Nba and Ncb cyclically<br>
Ma = midpoint of NabNac, and define Mb and Mc cyclically<br>
</p>
<p>
The locus of P such that the centroid of MaMbMc lies on the Euler line is given by the barycentric equation
</p>
<p>
(SA^2+8*SB*SC-3*S^2)*x*(y^2-z^ 2) + (SB^2+8*SC*SA-3*S^2)*y*(z^2-x^ 2) + (SC^2+8*SA*SB-3*S^2)*z*(x^2-y^ 2) = 0.
</p>
<p>
Only one point X(n), for 1 &lt;= n &lt;= 10108, lies on the cubic, and that is the centroid, X(2).  For P = X(2), the centroid of MaMbMc is X(10109).  (Antreas Hatzipolakis and César Lozada, August 10, 2016; see <a href="http://www.hyacinthos.epizy.com/message.php?msg=23955">Hyacinthos 23955</a>)
</p>
<p>
X(10109) lies on these lines:  <br>
{2,3}, {1327,8252}, {1328,8253}, {1587,6495}, {1588,6494}, {3582,3614}, {3584,7173}, {3655,7989}, {3656,7988}, {3828,9955}, {4669,5844}, {4677,8227}, {4745,9956}, {5355,7603}, {5663,6688}
</p>

</p>
