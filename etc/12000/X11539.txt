<h3 id="X11539">X(11539)&nbsp;=&nbsp;MIDPOINT OF X(2) AND X(5054)</h3>
Barycentrics &nbsp;&nbsp; 8a^4-13a^2 (b^2+c^2)+5 (b^2-c^2)^2 : :
<p>
In the plane of a triangle ABC, let G = X(2) = centroid, and<br>
A'B'C' = cevian triangle of G; i,e., A'B'C' = medial triangle<br>
A''B''C'' = pedal triangle of G<br>
Ma = midpoint of AA', and define Mb and Mc cyclically<br>
P = a point (as a function) on the Euler line of ABC<br>
Pa = P(A''MbMc), and define Pb and Pc cyclically<br>
The triangles ABC and PaPbPc are orthologic.<br>
X(11539) = PaPbPc-to-ABC orthologic center, for P = X(2)<br>
X(11540) = PaPbPc-to-ABC orthologic center, for P = X(3)<br>
X(4) = PaPbPc-to-ABC orthologic center, for P = X(11541)<br>
</p>
<p>
See Antreas Hatzipolakis and Angel Montesdeoca, <a href="https://aphyacinthos.blogspot.com/2019/10/hyacinthos-23914_22.html">Hyacinthos 23914</a>.
</p>
<p>
For another construction see Antreas Hatzipolakis and César Lozada,  <a href="https://groups.io/g/euclid/message/2748">Euclid 2748</a>
</p>
<p>
X(11539) lies on this line: {2,3}
</p>
<p>
X(11539) = midpoint of X(i) and X(j) for these {i,j}:  {2,5054}, {3,3545}, {3524,5055}<br>
X(11539) = center of the Vu pedal-centroidal circle of X(376)<br>
