<p class="textcenter">
This preamble and centers X(10695)-X(10705) were contributed by Clark Kimberling and Peter Moses, November 10, 2016.
</p>
<p>
Suppose that P is a point on the circumcircle of a triangle ABC, and let
</p>
<p>
P' = reflection of P in the incenter, I, of ABC.<br>
P<sup>c</sup> = complement of P<br>
P<sup>a</sup> = anticomplement of P<br>
P'' = reflection of X(8) in P<sup>c</sup>.
</p>
<p>
Then
</p>
<p>
P' = PI&#8745;P<sup>c</sup>X(8)<br>
P' = reflection of X(8) in P<sup>c</sup><br>
P' = midpoint of X(145) and P<sup>a</sup>, where X(145) = anticomplement of anticomplement of I.
</p>
