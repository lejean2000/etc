<h3 id="X11593">X(11593)&nbsp;=&nbsp;  POINT BECRUX 47 </h3>
Barycentrics &nbsp;&nbsp; a^2/(2 a^6 b^2-2 a^4 b^4+2 a^6 c^2+2 a^4 b^2 c^2-a^2 b^4 c^2-2 b^6 c^2-2 a^4 c^4-a^2 b^2 c^4+4 b^4 c^4-2 b^2 c^6) : :
<p>
Let P be a point in the plane of a triangle ABC, and let<br>
A'B'C' = antipedal triangle of P<br>
Oa = circumcenter of PB'C', and define Ob and Oc cyclically<br>
Ab = orthogonal projection of Oa on POb, and define Bc and Ca cyclically<br>
Ac = orthogonal projection of Oa on POc, and define Ba and Cb cyclically<br>
La = Euler line of OaAbAc, and define Lb and Lc cyclically<br>
Ua = Lb&#8745;Lc, and define Ub and Uc cyclically<br>
A* = Ub&#8745;Uc, and define B* and C* cyclically.
</p>
<p>
The triangles ABC and A*B*C* are parallelogic, and the ABC-to-A*B*C* parallelogic center, Q(P) lies on the circumcircle of ABC.  If P = p : q : r (barycentrics), then
</p>
<p>
Q(P) =  1/(b^2 (a^2-b^2) c^2 p^2 q+c^2 (a^2-c^2) (a^2-b^2+c^2) p q^2+b^2 c^2 (a^2-c^2) p^2 r+c^2 (a^4-a^2 b^2-b^4+2 b^2 c^2-c^4) q^2 r+b^2 (a^2-b^2) (a^2+b^2-c^2) p r^2+b^2 (a^4-b^4-a^2 c^2+2 b^2 c^2-c^4) q r^2) : :
</p>
<p>
If P lies on the cubic K003, then Q(P) = X(74).  The appearance of (i,j) in the following list means that Q(X(i)) = X(j): <br>
(2,6325), (13, 11612), (14, 11613), (6, 11593)
</p>
