<h3 id="X11568">X(11568)&nbsp;=&nbsp;  POINT BECRUX 38</h3>

a^8-10 a^6 b^2+10 a^4 b^4-2 a^2 b^6+a^6 c^2+7 a^4 b^2 c^2-2 a^2 b^4 c^2-2 b^6 c^2-14 a^4 c^4+7 a^2 b^2 c^4+10 b^4 c^4+a^2 c^6-10 b^2 c^6+2 c^8) : :
<p>
In the plane of a triangle ABC, let G = X(2) = centroid, and let<br>
A'B'C' = medial triangle = cevian triangle of G<br>
A'' = reflection of G in BC, and define B'' and C'' cyclically<br>
Aa = orthogonal projection of A'' on AG, and define Bb and Cc cyclically<br>
Ab = orthogonal projection of A'' on BG, and define Bc and Ca cyclically<br>
Ac = orthogonal projection of A'' on CG, and define Ba and Cb cyclically<br>
La = Euler line of AaAbAc, and define Lb and Lc cyclically<br>
Ua = Lb&#8745;Lc, and define Ub and Uc cyclically<br>
A* = Ub&#8745;Uc, and define B* and C* cyclically.<br>
Then ABC and A*B*C* are parallelogic, and X(11568) = ABC-to-A*B*C* paralogic center, which lies on the circumcircle of ABC.  Also, A'B'C' and A*B*C* are parallelogic, and X(11569) = A'B'C'-to-A*B*C* paralogic center, which lies on the nine-point circle of ABC.  See Antreas Hatzipolakis and Peter Moses, <a href="http://www.hyacinthos.epizy.com/message.php?msg=25046">Hyacinthos 25046</a>
</p>
