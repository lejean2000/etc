<h3 id="X10610">X(10610)&nbsp;=&nbsp; MIDPOINT OF X(3) AND X(54)</h3>
Trilinears &nbsp;&nbsp;  (3/2 + cos2A) cos(B - C) - cos 3A : :
<p>
Let P be a point if the plane of a triangle ABC, and let A'B'C' be the pedal triangle of P.  Let<br>
Oa = midpoint of AP, and define Ob and Oc cyclically<br>
Ooa = circumcenter of PObOc, and define Oob and Ooc cyclically<br>
Locus1 = locus of P for which the triangles ABC and OoaOobOoc are perspective<br>
Locus2 = Neuberg cubic = locus of P for which OaObOc and OoaOobOoc are perspective<br>
Locus3 = Napoleon-Feurbach cubic = locus of P for which A'B'C' and OoaOobOoc are perspective.<br>
Locus1 is the cubic pK(X(6),X(382)), given by the barycentric equation f(a,b,c,x,y,z) + f(b,c,a,y,z,x) + f(c,a,b,z,x,y) = 0, where f(a,b,c,x,z,y) = (S<sup>2</sup> - 5S<sub>B</sub>S<sub>C</sub>)(c<sup>2</sup>y<sup>2</sup> - b<sup>2</sup>z<sup>2</sup>)x.  Points on Locus1 include X(1), X(3), X(4), and X(382).
</p>
