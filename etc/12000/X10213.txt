In the plane of a triangle ABC, let<br>
O = circumcenter, X(3)<br>
N = nine-point center, X(5)<br>
Na = N-of-OBC, and define Nb and Nc cyclically<br>
Aa = orthogonal projection of Na on OA, and define Ab and Ac cyclically<br>
Ba = orthogonal projection of Nb on OA, and define OB and OC cyclically<br>
Ca = orthogonal projection of Nc on OA, and define OB and OC cyclically<br>
Ea = Euler line of AaAbAc, and define Eb and Ec cyclically<br>
A' = Eb&#8745;Ec, and define B' and C' cyclically<br>
</p>
<p>
Then ABC and A'B'C' are parallelogic, and<br>
X(10213) = (A'B'C',ABC)-parallelogic center<br>
X(1141) = (ABC,A'B'C')-parallelogic center.
