(O) = circumcircle of ABC<br>
(I) = incircle of ABC<br>
A' = (I)&amp;cap'sBC, and define B' and C' cyclically, so that A'B'C' = intouch triangle<br>
(Ia) = circumcircle of AIA', and define (Ib) and (Ic) cyclically<br>
A'' = the point, other than A, where (O) intersects (Ia), and define B'' and C'' cyclically<br>
A* = midpoint of segment B'C', and define B* and C* cyclically<br>
Ua = circumcircle of AA''A*, and define Ub and Uc cyclically<br>
Then Ua, Ub, Uc are coaxal, and they intersect in a bicentric pair of points, of which X(11575) is the midpoint.  See Tran Quang Hung and César Lozada, <a href="http://www.hyacinthos.epizy.com/message.php?msg=25064">Hyacinthos 25064</a>
