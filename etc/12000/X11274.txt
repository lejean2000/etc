<p>
Let P be a point in the plane of a triangle ABC, and let<br>
A' = reflection of P in BC, and define B' and C' cyclically<br>
Ma = midpoint of AP, and define Mb and Mc cyclically<br>
La = Euler line of A'MbMc, and define Lb and Lc cyclically<br>
A* = Lb&#8745;Lc, and define B* and C* cyclically.<br>
If P = X(1), then La, Lb, Lc concur in X(11274).  See Antreas Hatzipolakis and César Lozada, <a href="http://www.hyacinthos.epizy.com/message.php?msg=24880">Hyacinthos 24880</a>
