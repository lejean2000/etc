<h3 id="X10628">X(10628)&nbsp;=&nbsp; INFINITY POINT OF X(54)X(74)</h3>
Barycentrics &nbsp;&nbsp; a^2 (a^12 (b^2+c^2) - 2 a^10 (2 b^4+b^2 c^2+2 c^4) + a^8 (5 b^6+2 b^4 c^2+2 b^2 c^4+5 c^6) + a^6 (-5 b^6 c^2+4 b^4 c^4-5 b^2 c^6) - a^4 (b^2-c^2)^2 (5 b^6+2 b^4 c^2+2 b^2 c^4+5 c^6) + a^2 (b^2-c^2)^2 (4 b^8+3 b^6 c^2+3 b^2 c^6+4 c^8) - (b^2-c^2)^4 (b^2+c^2)^3)  : :
<p>
Let H = X(4) be the orthocenter of a triangle ABC, and suppose that t &gt; 0.  Let<br>
A' =  the point on line AH such that  |A'A|/|A'H| = t, and define B' and C' cyclically<br>
Ab = orthogonal projection of A' on AB, and define Bc and Ca cyclically<br>
Ac = orthogonal project of A' on AC, and define Ba and Cb cyclically<br>
Ea = Euler line of A'AbAc, and define Eb and Ec cyclically<br>
E'a = Euler line of AAbAc, and define E'b and E'c cyclically.
</p>
<p>
The lines Ea, Eb, Ec concur in a point whose locus as t varies is the line W =X(4)X(54).  The lines E'a, E'b, E'c concur in a point whose locuse as t varies is the line W' = X(4)X(74).  The lines W and W' are parallel, and X(10628) is their point of intersection on the line at infinity. See Antreas Hatzipolakis and Angel Montesdeoca, <a href="http://www.hyacinthos.epizy.com/message.php?msg=24703">Hyacinthos 24703</a>.
</p>
