<p class="preamble-title">
Points associated with mid-triangles and cross-triangles:  X(10290)-X(10608)
</p>
<img src="rightri.svg" style="height:30px" alt="rightri">
</header>
<div class="preamble-sect">

<p class="textcenter">
This preamble and centers X(10290)-X(10608) were contributed by Randy Hutson, October 22, 2016.
</p>

<p>
Let T<sub>1</sub> = A<sub>1</sub>B<sub>1</sub>C<sub>1</sub> and T<sub>2</sub> = A<sub>2</sub>B<sub>2</sub>C<sub>2</sub> be central triangles (or a pair of bicentric triangles) in the plane of a triangle ABC.
</p>
<p>
Let A' = midpoint of A<sub>1</sub> and A<sub>2</sub>, and define B' and C' cyclically.  The triangle A'B'C' is here named the <b>mid-triangle</b> of T<sub>1</sub> and T<sub>2</sub>, denoted by MT(T<sub>1</sub>,T<sub>2</sub>).<br>
Let A'' = B<sub>1</sub>C<sub>2</sub>&#8745;C<sub>1</sub>B<sub>2</sub>, and B'' and C'' cyclically.  The triangle A''B''C'' is here named the <b>cross-triangle</b> of T<sub>1</sub> and T<sub>2</sub>, denoted by XT(T<sub>1</sub>,T<sub>2</sub>).<br>
</p>
<p>
If T<sub>1</sub> and T<sub>2</sub> are homothetic, then both MT(T<sub>1</sub>,T<sub>2</sub>) and XT(T<sub>1</sub>,T<sub>2</sub>) are homothetic to T<sub>1</sub> and T<sub>2</sub>.
</p>
<p>
If T<sub>1</sub> and T<sub>2</sub> are directly similar, then MT(T<sub>1</sub>,T<sub>2</sub>) is also directly similar to T<sub>1</sub> and T<sub>2</sub>, with the same center of similitude.
</p>
<p>
If any pair in {T<sub>1</sub>, T<sub>2</sub>, XT(T<sub>1</sub>,T<sub>2</sub>)} are perspective, then every pair in the set are perspective.
</p>
<p>
If the vertices of T<sub>1</sub> and T<sub>2</sub> lie on a conic, then XT(T<sub>1</sub>,T<sub>2</sub>) is degenerate (consisting of 3 collinear points).  If T<sub>1</sub> and T<sub>2</sub> are also perspective,
XT(T<sub>1</sub>,T<sub>2</sub>) lies on the polar of the perspector wrt the conic.  If T<sub>1</sub> and T<sub>2</sub> are the cevian triangles of P and Q, resp., then XT(T<sub>1</sub>,T<sub>2</sub>) is degenerate and collinear with P and Q.
</p>
<p>
If the vertices of T<sub>2</sub> lie on the respective sidelines of T<sub>1</sub> (e.g., A<sub>2</sub> lies on B<sub>1</sub>C<sub>1</sub>)), then XT(T<sub>1</sub>,T<sub>2</sub>) = T<sub>1</sub>.
</p>
<p>
For many choices of triangles T<sub>1</sub>, T<sub>2</sub>, T<sub>3</sub>,<br>
(perspector of T<sub>1</sub> and XT(T<sub>2</sub>,T<sub>3</sub>)) = (perspector of T<sub>2</sub> and XT(T<sub>1</sub>,T<sub>3</sub>)) = (perspector of T<sub>3</sub> and XT(T<sub>1</sub>,T<sub>2</sub>)).
</p>
<p>
If T<sub>1</sub> is the cevian triangle of P and T<sub>2</sub> is the anticevian triangle of Q, then XT(T<sub>1</sub>,T<sub>2</sub>) is perspective to ABC, and the perspector is collinear with these 3 points: P, P-Ceva conjugate of Q, Q-cross conjugate of P.  Also, XT(T<sub>1</sub>,T<sub>2</sub>) is perspective to T<sub>1</sub> at Q.
</p>
<p>
If T<sub>1</sub> is the circumcevian triangle of P, then XT(ABC,T<sub>1</sub>) is perspective to the circumcevian triangle of P*, where P* is the circumcircle-inverse of P.  The perspector lies on the circumcircle.
</p>
<p>
The cross-triangle of the cevian and anticevian triangles of P is perspective to ABC at P.
</p>
<p>
The cross-triangle of the cevian and circumcevian triangles of P is perspective to ABC at gcgP, where g = isogonal conjugate and c = complement.
</p>
<p>
The (degenerate) cross-triangle of the circumcevian triangles of P and Q is perspective to ABC at &#923;(gP, gQ). Also, the centroid of the (degenerate) cross-triangle of the anticevian triangles of P and Q is the tripolar centroid of the cevapoint of P and Q.
</p>
<p>
If T1 is perspective to ABC at X(2), then the perspector of ABC and XT(ABC,T1) is the barycentric product A1*B1*C1.
</p>
