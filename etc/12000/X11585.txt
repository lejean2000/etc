<h3 id="X11585">X(11585)&nbsp;=&nbsp; COMPLEMENT OF X(24) </h3>
Barycentrics &nbsp;&nbsp; (b^2-c^2)^4 (b^2+c^2) - 2 (b^2-c^2)^2 (b^4+c^4) a^2 - 4 b^2 c^2 (b^2+c^2) a^4 + 2 (b^2+c^2)^2a^6 + (-b^2-c^2) a^8  : :
<br>
Barycentrics &nbsp;&nbsp; tan B cos 2B + tan C cos 2C : :
<p>
In the plane of a triangle ABC, let O = X(3) = circumcenter, and let<br>
A'B'C' = pedal triangle of O<br>
A''B''C'' = cevian triangle of O.<br>
Oa = circumcircle of AA'A'', and define Ob and Oc cyclically.<br>
The radical center of the circles Oa, Ob, Oc is X(1147), and X(11585) is the A'B'C'-isogonal conjugate of X(1147), which lies on the Euler line.  See Tran Quang Hung and Angel Montesdeoca, <a href="http://www.hyacinthos.epizy.com/message.php?msg=25087">Hyacinthos 25087</a>
</p>
<p>
Let A'B'C' be the triangle whose barycentric vertex matrix is the sum of the matrices for the 3rd and 4th Euler triangles, so that A' = b^2 + c^2 : c^2 - a^2 : b^2 - a^2.  Then A'B'C' is the complement of the tangential triangle (or tangential-of-medial triangle), and A'B'C' is also the reflection of the Kosnita triangle in X(140).  Moreover, A'B'C' is homothetic to the 2nd Euler triangle at X(11585).  (Randy Hutson, March 9, 2017)
</p>
