<h3 id="X10154">X(10154)&nbsp;=&nbsp;CENTER OF THE CENTROIDAL CONIC OF THE ARA TRIANGLE</h3>
Barycentrics &nbsp;&nbsp; 4*a^6-(b^2+c^2)*a^4-4*(b^4-b^2*c^2+c^4)*a^2+(b^2-c^2)*(b^4-c^4)  :  :  <p><b>
X(10154) = (8*R^2-SW)*X(2)-SW*X(3)
</b></p>
<p>
As a point on the Euler line, X(10154) has Shinagawa coefficients (-E-5*F, 3*F+3*E)
</p>
<p>
Let A'B'C' be the medial triangle of a triangle ABC.  Let<br>
Ba, Ca be the circumcircle intercepts of line B'C'; define Cb and Ab cyclically, and define Ac and Bc cyclically.<br>
Let Ba' be the {Ba,Ca}-harmonic conjugate of B', and define Cb' and Ac' cyclically.<br>
Let Ca' be the {Ba,Ca}-harmonic conjugate of C', and define Ab' and Bc' cyclically.<br>
   Then X(10154) is the centroid of {Ba',Ca',Cb',Ab',Ac',Bc'}.  (Randy Hutson, September 14, 2016)
</p>
