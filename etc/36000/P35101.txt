<p class="preamble-title">
Barycentric positive square roots of points on the Steiner inellipse: X(35101)-X(35104)
</p>
<img src="rightri.svg" style="height:30px" alt="rightri">
</header>
<div class="preamble-sect">
<p>
Contributed by Clark Kimberling and Peter Moses, December 4, 2019.
</p>
<p>
This section extends from the introduction to permutation ellipses in the preamble just before X(35025).  The points X(35101)-X(35104) are positive square roots of points the form E(X(115),X(k),X(115)), where E(X(115)) is the Steiner inellipse.
</p>
<p>
Suppose that a point f : g : h lies on the line at infinity (i.e., f + g + h = 0).  Then the point f<sup>2</sup> : g<sup>2</sup> : h<sup>2</sup> lies on the Steiner inellipse, E(X(115)).  Conversely, if  F<sup>2</sup> : G<sup>2</sup> : H<sup>2</sup> lie on the ellipse, then the following six points lie on the line at infinity:
</p>

<table>
<tr><td>f : g : h&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;</td> <td>g : h : f&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;</td> <td>h : f : g&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;</td> <td>f : h : g&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;</td> <td>g : f : h&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;</td> <td>h : g : f</td> </tr>
</table>

<p>
where f, g, h are the positive square roots of F,G,H.
</p>
<p>
The appearance of (i,j) in the following list means that the positive square root of the point X(i) on E(X(115)) is X(j):
</p>
<p>
(35066,17768), (35067,3564), (35068,740), (35069,758), (35070,35101), (35071,520), (35072,521), (35073,538), (35074,35102), (35075,8680), (35076,4977}, <br>
{35077,5969), (35078,804), (35079,2787), (35080,2786), (35081,2792), (35082,2784), (35083,2783), (35084,2795), (35085,2796), (35086,2785}, <br>
{35087,543), (35088,2799), (35089,35103), (35090,8674), (35091,6366), (35092,900), (35093,5845), (35094,918), (35095,35104)
</p>
<p>
The isogonal conjugates of the points X(35101)-X(35104), all on the circumcircle, are indexed as X(35105)-X(35108).
