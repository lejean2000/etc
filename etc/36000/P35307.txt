<i>Let A'B'C', A"B"C" be two triangles inscribed in ABC. The <b>areal center</b> of the two triangles is a point S such that the triangles SA'A", SB'B", SC'C" have the same area. </i> (Reference:  <a href="http://www.xtec.cat/~qcastell/ttw/ttweng/definicions/d_centre_areal_p.html">The Triangles Web</a>)
</p>
<p><i>
    Construction of the areal center of two inscribed triangles</i></p>
<ol>
<li>Let A'B'C', A"B"C" be the two inscribed triangles, L<sub>a</sub> the line through A parallel to the line joining the midpoints of B'C" and B"C'.</li>
<li>Construct analogously L<sub>b</sub>, L<sub>c</sub>.</li>
<li>The lines L<sub>a</sub>, L<sub>b</sub>, L<sub>c</sub> intersect at the areal center S.
<br>
 (Reference:  <a href="http://www.xtec.cat/~qcastell/ttw/ttweng/construccions/c62.html">The Triangles Web</a>)
</li>
</ol>
