<p>
Let K<sub>1</sub>, K<sub>2</sub> be two conics (circles included) with centers O<sub>1</sub> and O<sub>2</sub>, respectively. The locus of the poles with respect to K<sub>1</sub> of the tangents to K<sub>2</sub> is another conic &Phi;<sub>21</sub> named the <b>reciprocal polar conic</b> of K<sub>2</sub> with respect to K<sub>1</sub>. By swapping K<sub>1</sub> and K<sub>2</sub>, the reciprocal polar conic &Phi;<sub>12</sub> of K<sub>2</sub> with respect to K<sub>1</sub> is found.
</p>
The center of &Phi;<sub>21</sub> is the pole, with respect to K<sub>1</sub>, of the polar of O<sub>1</sub> wrt K<sub>2</sub> and, similarly, the center of &Phi;<sub>12</sub> is the pole, with respect to K<sub>2</sub>, of the polar of O<sub>2</sub> wrt K<sub>1</sub>. (Reference: <b>Eagles, T.H.</b>: <i>Constructive Geometry of Plane Curves,</i> McMillan and Co, London, 1885. pp 201-202.)
