Frégier points: X(34341)-X(34344)
</p>
<img src="rightri.svg" style="height:30px" alt="rightri">

<div class="preamble-sect">
<p>
This preamble and centers X(34341)-X(34342) were contributed by Clark Kimberling and Peter Moses, September 30, 2019.
<p>
Suppose that P = p : q : r is a point  in the plane of a triangle ABC. Suppose further that p,q,r are distinct homogeneous functions of a,b,c. The <b>permutation ellipse of P</b>, denoted by E(P), is the ellipse that passes through the six points p : q : r, &nbsp;&nbsp; q : r : p, &nbsp;&nbsp; r : p : q, &nbsp;&nbsp; p : r : q, &nbsp;&nbsp; q : p : r,&nbsp;&nbsp;  r : q : p, given by
</p>
<p>
(q r + r p + p q)(x^2 + y^2 + z^2) - (p^2 + q^2 + r^2)(y z + z x + x y) = 0.
</p>
<p>
The centroid, X(2), is the center and the perspector of E(P).
</p>
<p>
E(X(99)) = Steiner circumellipse<br>
E(X(115)) = Steiner inellipse<br>
E(X(148)) = Steiner circumellipse of anticomplementary triangle.
</p>
<p>
E(X(1)) passes through X(i) for i = 1, 3679, 24338, 24345, 24411.<br>
E(X(6)) passes through X(i) for i = 6, 599, 24281, 24289, 30229.<br>
E(X(10)) passes through X(i) for i = 10, 551, 24348, 25382.<br>
E(X(98)) passes through X(i) for i = 98, 6039, 6040, 6054.<br>
E(X(69)) passes through X(i) for i = 69, 1992, 30225, 30226, 30227, 30228, 34341, 34342, 34343, 34344.<br>
E(X(125)) passes through X(i) for i = 125, 5642, 16278.<br>
E(X(620)) passes through X(i) for i = 620, 4422, 5461, 17044, 23583, 27076<br>
</p>
<p>
Further discussion of permutation ellipses is given in the preamble just before X(35025).
</p>
<p>
The Frégier point of P is the point P' shown at <a href="http://mathworld.wolfram.com/FregiersTheorem.html"> <b>Frégier's Theorem</b></a>.  Suppose that P = p : q : r lies on the Steiner circumellipse.  Then the Frégier point of P is given by
</p>
<p>
F(P) = (-5 a^2 + b^2 + c^2) q^2 r^2 + p^2 (r^2 (a^2 + b^2 - 5 c^2) + q^2 (a^2 - 5 b^2 + c^2)) + p q r ((-7 a^2 + 5 b^2 + 5 c^2) p + (5 a^2 + 5 b^2 - 7 c^2) q + (5 a^2 - 7 b^2 + 5 c^2) r) : : .
</p>
<p>
The locus of F(P) for P on the Steiner circumellipse is the permutation ellipse E(X(69)).  Selected points on the Steiner circumellipse and the associated Frégier points on E(X(69)) are shown here:
</p>
<p>
X(99) &srarr; X(69), X(671) &srarr; X(1992), X(190) &srarr; X(30225), X(290) &srarr; X(30226), <br>
X(648) &srarr; X(30227), X(664) &srarr; X(30228), X(670) &srarr; X(32341), X(903) &srarr; X(34342), <br>
X(3227) &srarr; X(34343), X(3228) &srarr; X(34344); some of these are noted at X(30225).
</p>
<p>
An equation for the ellipse E(X(69)), that is, Frégier image of the Steiner circumellipse, is
</p>
<p>
(a-b-c) (a+b-c) (a-b+c) (a+b+c) x^2+(3 a^4-2 a^2 b^2+3 b^4-2 a^2 c^2-2 b^2 c^2+3 c^4) y z + (cyclic) = 0.
</p>
<p>
An equation for the ellipse E(X(6)), which is the Frégier image of the Steiner inellipse, is
</p>
<p>
(a^2 b^2+a^2 c^2+b^2 c^2) x^2 - (a^4+b^4+c^4) y z + (cyclic) = 0.
</p>
<p>
In general, the Frégier image of an ellipse is an ellipse; specifically, it is the dilation of the first ellipse by the following factor: <br>
(semiMajor^2-semiMinor^2)/(semiMajor^2+semiMinor^2).  (Peter Moses, September 30, 2019)
