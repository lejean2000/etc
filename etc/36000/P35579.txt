<p><i>Let P and Q be antipodal points on the circumcircle of ABC. Then the Simson lines of P and Q intersect at a point on the nine-point circle of ABC.</i><p>
<p>If X is such point of intersection, and P<sup>-1</sup>, Q<sup>-1</sup> are the isogonal conjugates of P and Q, then:</p>
<ol>
<li>X is crosssum of P and Q; therefore, X is the crosspoint of  P<sup>-1</sup> and Q<sup>-1</sup>.</li>
<li>X is crossdifference of every pair of points on the polar trilinear of X<sup>-1</sup>.</li>
<li>X is center of the rectangular circum-hyperbola  {{A, B, C, X(4), P<sup>-1</sup>, Q<sup>-1</sup>}}.</li>
</ol>
<p>Moreover, if Y is the intersection, other than A,B,C, of the circum-parabolas {{A, B, C, P<sup>-1</sup>}} and {{A, B, C, Q<sup>-1</sup>}}, then:</p>
<ol start="4">
<li>X is the complement of Y.</li>
<li>X is the X(4)-Ceva conjugate-of Y<sup>-1</sup>.</li>
</ol>
