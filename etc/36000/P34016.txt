Perspectors involving Talitha triangles:  X(34016)-X(34088)
</p>
<img src="rightri.svg" style="height:30px" alt="rightri">
</header>
<div class="preamble-sect">
<p>
This preamble and centers X(34016)-X(34088) were contributed by Clark Kimberling and Peter Moses, August 19, 2019.
</p>
<p>
Suppose that P = p : q : r is a point.  The P-Talitha triangle of X, denoted by TT(P,X), is the triangle A'B'C' is defined by its vertices:
<p>
A' = -p/(y - z) : q/(z + x) : -r/(x + y)<br>
B' = -p/(y + z) : -q/(z - x) : r/(x + y)<br>
C' = p/(y + z) : -q/(z + x) : -r/(x - y).
</p>
<p>
The triangle A'B'C' is inscribed in the circumconic -p y z + q z x + r x y = 0.
</p>
<p>
The name: <a href="http://www.constellationsofwords.com/stars/Talitha.html">Talitha</a> is a star on the front paw of Ursa Major.
</p>
<p>
In the following table, contributed by CÃ©sar Lozada, August 21, 2019, the notation X(i) is abbreviated as i.  For example,
