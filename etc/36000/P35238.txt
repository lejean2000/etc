<p class="preamble-title">
Centroids of three points on the circumcircle: X(35238)-X(35306)
</p>
<img src="rightri.svg" style="height:30px" alt="rightri">
</header>
<div class="preamble-sect">
<p>
Contributed by Peter Moses, December 11, 2019.
<p>
According to a theorem by Bernard Gibert, every cubic of the type nK0(X6,R), where R = u : v : w, meets the circumcircle in three points, other than A,B,C, and the centroid of those three points is given by
</p>
<p>
(-3a<sup>2</sup> + b<sup>2</sup> + c<sup>2</sup>)u + 2a<sup>2</sup>v + 2a<sup>2</sup>w : :
</p>
<p>
For centers X(35238)-X(35306), the type is written as nK0(X(6),X(k)), and the circumcircle as &Gamma; .
</p>
<p>
See <a href="https://bernard-gibert.pagesperso-orange.fr/Tables/table4.html"> <b>Table 4. nK0(X6,R)</b></a>.
</p>
