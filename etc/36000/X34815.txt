<h3 id="X34815">X(34815)&nbsp;=&nbsp; CENTER OF PERSPECONIC OF ABC AND CIRCUMCEVIAN TRIANGLE OF X(64)</h3>
Barycentrics &nbsp;&nbsp; a^2*(a^2 - b^2 - c^2)*(3*a^8 - 2*a^4*b^4 - 8*a^2*b^6 + 7*b^8 + 4*a^4*b^2*c^2 + 8*a^2*b^4*c^2 - 12*b^6*c^2 - 2*a^4*c^4 + 8*a^2*b^2*c^4 + 10*b^4*c^4 - 8*a^2*c^6 - 12*b^2*c^6 + 7*c^8) : :
<p>
X(34815) lies on the Cubic K004 (the Darboux cubic) and these lines: {3, 6}, {4, 20208}, {20, 32001}, {25, 1073}, {64, 2130}, {393, 15312}, {1593, 11397}, {1657, 18437}, {2883, 15594}, {3089, 33546}, {3516, 23635}, {6389, 29181}, {6617, 33586}, {7390, 18643}, {13611, 26958}, {14379, 33578}, {26906, 33522}, {30517, 33553}, {31860, 33924}, {33582, 34146}<br>
</p>
<p>
Let A'B'C' be the circumcevian triangle of X(64). Let A"=AO&cap;B'C', and define B'' and C'' cyclically.  The points A'A", B'B", C'C" concur in X(34815). More generally, suppose that a point P lies on the Darboux cubic, and let A'B'C' be the circumcevian triangle of P. Define B'' and C'' cyclically.  The points A'A", B'B", C'C" concur in a point, Q(P).  The appearance of (i,j) in the following list means that X(i) is on the Darboux cubic and Q(X(i)) = X(j): (1,991), (,3), (4,5890), (64,34815). (Angel Montesdeoca, August 3, 2021).  This list can be extended to include (20,44073), (40,44074), (84,44075).  (Peter Moses, August 5, 2021)
