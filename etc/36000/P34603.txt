<p>Let T'=A'B'C' and T"=A"B"C" be two homothetic triangles. Denote (B'<sub>a</sub>, C'<sub>a</sub>) the reflections of B' and C' in A", respectively, and define (C'<sub>b</sub>, A'<sub>b</sub>) and (A'<sub>c</sub>, B'<sub>c</sub>) cyclically. Then these six points lie on an ellipse here named the  <b>HR-ellipse of T' to T"</b> (letters H and R stands for <i>homothetic</i> and <i>reflections</i>).</p>
<p>By swapping T' and T", the HR-ellipse T" to T' is found.</p>
<p>Suppose T' and T" are homothetic to the reference triangle ABC, with H' = homothetic center (ABC, T') and H"=homothetic center (ABC, T"). Denote &lambda;'=AA'/AH' and &lambda;"=AA"/AH". Then, if H', H" have normalized barycentrics coordinates H' = x' : y' : z' and H" = x" : y" : z", the centers O', O" of the HR-ellipses T' to T" and T" to T' are:</p>
<p>&emsp;&emsp;O' = (3*x' - 1)*&lambda;' - 2*(3*x" - 1)*&lambda;" - 1 : :</p>
<p>&emsp;&emsp;O" = (3*x" - 1)*&lambda;" - 2*(3*x' - 1)*&lambda;' - 1 : :</p>
<p>In general, { H', H", O' , O"} are aligned, O' = complement-of-O" with respect to T' and O" = complement-of-O' with respect to T".</p>
