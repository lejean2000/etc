<p>
Let ABC be an acute triangle with circumcircle &omega;. Let t be a tangent line to &omega; and denote t<sub>a</sub>, t<sub>b</sub>, t<sub>c</sub> the lines obtained by reflecting t in the lines BC, CA and AB, respectively. Then the circumcircle &omega;&prime; of the triangle determined by the lines t<sub>a</sub>, t<sub>b</sub>, t<sub>c</sub> is tangent to the circle &omega;. References: <i>2011 IMO Shortlist G8 (JPN) problem 6</i>, <a href="https://imogeometry.blogspot.com/p/1.html">IMOgeometry</a> and <a href="https://artofproblemsolving.com/community/c6h418983p2365045">AOPS</a>.
</p>
<p>If P = u:v:w (trilinears) is the touchpoint of t and &omega; and H(P) is the touchpoint of &omega; and &omega;&prime;, then </p>
<p style="padding-left:25px;">
H(P) = u*SA*((a^2*u^2 + 3*b^2*v^2 + 3*c^2*w^2)*SA^2*SB*SC*a^3*u  <br>
&emsp;&emsp;&emsp;&emsp;&emsp;&emsp;+ ((2*SA - SB)*S^2 - 3*(SB + SC)*SA^2)*SA*a^4*b*u^2*v <br>
&emsp;&emsp;&emsp;&emsp;&emsp;&emsp;+ ((2*SA - SC)*S^2 - 3*(SB + SC)*SA^2)*SA*a^4*c*u^2*w <br>
&emsp;&emsp;&emsp;&emsp;&emsp;&emsp;+ (S^2 - 3*SA*SB)*SB*SC^2*b^2*c*v^2*w <br>
&emsp;&emsp;&emsp;&emsp;&emsp;&emsp;+ (S^2 - 3*SA*SC)*SC*SB^2*c^2*b*w^2*v <br>
&emsp;&emsp;&emsp;&emsp;&emsp;&emsp;- (S^2 + SA*SC)*SB^2*SC*b^3*v^3 <br>
&emsp;&emsp;&emsp;&emsp;&emsp;&emsp;- (S^2 + SA*SB)*SC^2*SB*c^3*w^3 <br>
&emsp;&emsp;&emsp;&emsp;&emsp;&emsp;+ (5*S^2 + 6*SA^2 - 24*R^2*SA - 4*SB*SC)*S^2*a^3*b*c*u*v*w) : :
</p>
<p>
H(P) = IsotomicConjugate( PolarConjugate( BarycentricProduct( P, X<sub>4</sub>-antipode-of-P) ) ) <br>
where X<sub>4</sub>-antipode-of-P means the intersection, other than P, of &omega; and the line X(4)P, as defined in the preamble just before X(26700).
</p>
<p>
The point H(P) is here named here the <b>Haedus transform of P</b>.
</p>
