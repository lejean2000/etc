<h3 id="X34112">X(34112)&nbsp;=&nbsp; CENTER OF 1-ERHMANN CIRCLE</h3>
Barycentrics &nbsp;&nbsp; a^2*(b^4 - 2*b^3*c - 2*b*c^3 + c^4 + 2*a*b*c*(b + c) - a^2*(b^2 + c^2) + (a - b)*(-a + c)*S) : :
<b><br>
X(34112) = (a^2 + b^2 + c^2) X[6] - (a^2 + b^2 + c^2 + S) X[101]
</b>
<p>
On page 18 of  <b>Jean-Pierre Ehrmann,</b>  <a href="http://forumgeom.fau.edu/FG2002volume2/FG200203.pdf"><b>Congruent Inscribed Rectangles</b></a>, a circle is described.  For any &mu; &gt; 0, the circle, which passes through points X(100), X(106) and a certain point P(&mu;), is here named the <b>&mu;-Ehrmann circle</b>.  Its center is given by
</p>
<p>
a^2*(b - c)*(b*c*(a + b + c)*(a^3 - a^2*b - a*b^2 + b^3 - a^2*c + 5*a*b*c - 2*b^2*c - a*c^2 - 2*b*c^2 + c^3) - ((a - b)*(a - c)*(a^2 - b^2 - c^2)*S)/&mu;) : :
</p>
<p>
This center lies on the line {3,2827} and is given by the combos (r/&mu; - s) X[3] + s X[4491] = ((a + b + c)^2 - 2 S/&mu;) X[3] - (a + b + c)^2 X[4491].  X(34112) is obtained by putting &mu; = 1.  (Peter Moses, July 15, 2019)
</p>
<p>
