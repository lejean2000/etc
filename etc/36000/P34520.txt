<p class="preamble-title">
Vu points: X(34520)-X(34544)
</p>
<img src="rightri.svg" style="height:30px" alt="rightri">

<div class="preamble-sect">
<p>
This preamble and centers X(34520)-X(34544) are based on notes contributed by Vu Thanh Tung and Vu Quoc My, October 24, 2019.<br>
</p>
<p>
Let P = p:q:r (barycentrics) be a point in the plane of a triangle ABC. Let A' be the point, other than P, in which the line AP meets the circle (PBC), and define B' and C' cyclically; the triangle A'B'C' is called the <b>circlecevian triangle of P</b> with respect to triangle ABC  by Floor van Lamoen ( Hyacinthos # 10039).
</p>
<p>
Let T<sub>A</sub> and T<sub>A'</sub> be the tangents to (PBC) at P and A', respectively. <br>
Let A<sub>1</sub> = T<sub>A</sub>&cap;BC and A<sub>2</sub> = T<sub>A'</sub>&cap;BC.<br>
Define B<sub>1</sub> and C<sub>1</sub> cyclically, and define B<sub>2</sub> and C<sub>2</sub> cyclically.<br>
Then A<sub>1</sub>,B<sub>1</sub>,C<sub>1</sub> are collinear and A<sub>2</sub>,B<sub>2</sub>,C<sub>2</sub> are collinear.
</p>
<p>
The trilinear pole of the line B<sub>1</sub>C<sub>1</sub> is given by Q<sub>1</sub>(P) = 1/(-a^2 q r + c^2 q (q + r) + b^2 r (q + r))  :  : , here named the <b>1st Vu point</b> of P.
</p>
<p>
The trilinear pole of the line B<sub>2</sub>C<sub>2</sub> is given by Q<sub>2</sub>(P) = p^2 (-a^2 q r + c^2 q (q + r) + b^2 r (q + r))  :  : , here named the <b>2nd Vu point</b> of P.
</p>
<p>
Examples:
</p>
<p>
Q<sub>1</sub>(X(1)) = X(57) and Q<sub>2</sub>(X(1)) = X(9)<br>
Q<sub>1</sub>(X(2)) = X(598) and Q<sub>2</sub>(X(2)) = X(599)<br>
Q<sub>1</sub>(X(3)) = X(2) and Q<sub>2</sub>(X(3)) = X(32)<br>
Q<sub>1</sub>X(4)) = X(2052) and Q<sub>2</sub>(X(4)) = X(6)<br>
</p>
<p>
If P lies on the circumcircle, then Q<sub>1</sub>(P) is the trilinear pole of the tangent to the circumcircle at P, and Q<sub>2</sub>(P) = X(6).  (Randy Hutson, October 24, 2019)
</p>
<p>
Q<sub>2</sub>(P) is the X(2)-Ceva conjugate of the Dao image of P.  (Randy Hutson, November 17, 2019)
