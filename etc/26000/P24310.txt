Perspectors involving N-obverse triangles: X(24310)-X(24312)

<div class="preamble-sect">
<p>
Let P = p : q : r (barycentrics) be a point in the plane of a triangle ABC.  The <b>N-obverse triangle of P</b> is here introduced as the triangle A'B'C', where A' = - p : r : q, B' = r : - q : p, C' = q : p : - r. If P is a triangle center, then the obverse triangle of P is a central triangle.  (Clark Kimberling, October 3, 2018)
