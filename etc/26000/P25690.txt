<p>
Extending the preamble just before X(24537), let m(X) denote the (A,B,C,X(2); A',B',C',X(2)) collineation image of X = x : y : z, where A'B'C' = Gemini triangle 28, as in centers X(25690)-X(25710).  Then
</p>
<p>
m(X) = (a - b - c) (2 a + 2 b - c) (2 a - b + 2 c) x - (a + c)(a - 2 b - 2 c)(2 a + 2 b - c) y : (a + b)(a - 2 b - 2 c)(2 a + 2 c - b) z:
</p>
<p>
The point m(X) is on the Euler line if and only if the point X is on the Euler line. (Clark Kimberling, October 25, 2018)
