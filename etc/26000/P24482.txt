Collineation mappings involving trilinear obverse triangles: X(24482)-X(24518)
</p>
<img src="rightri.svg" style="height:30px" alt="rightri">
</header>
<div class="preamble-sect">
<p>
Let P = p : q : r (barycentrics) be a point in the plane of a triangle ABC.  Trilinears for P are p/a : q/b : r/c.  The <b>trilinear obverse triangle of P</b> is here introduced as the triangle A'B'C', where A' = p/a : r/c : q/b, B' = r/c : q/b : p/a, C' = q/b : p/a : r/c (trilinears).  The same triangle is given by barycentrics A' = b c p : b^2 r : c^2 q, B' = a^2 r : c a q : p c^2 p, C' = a^2 q : b^2 p : a b r. For example, the trilinear obverse triangle of X(2) has first barycentric b c : b^2 : c^2. (Clark Kimberling, October 5, 2018)
