<p class="preamble-title">
4th intersections of inconics and the circumcircles of their polar triangles: X(24973)-X(24980)
</p>
<img src="rightri.svg" style="height:30px" alt="rightri">
</header>
<div class="preamble-sect">
<p>
Let E be an inconic (other than the incircle) with Brianchon point (perspector) P.  Let A'B'C' be the polar triangle of E, which is the cevian triangle of P.  Let O* be the circumcircle of A'B'C' (i.e., the cevian circle of P).  E and O* intersect in 4 points: A', B', C' and a 4th intersection which is a triangle center.  The appearance of (E,i,O*,j) in the following list means that inconic E with perspector X(i) intersects circle O* (other than at A', B', C') at X(j):
</p>
<p>
(Brocard inellipse, 6, symmedial circle, <b>24973</b>)<br>
(incentral inellipse, 1, incentral circle, 23063)<br>
(Kiepert parabola, 99, 2nd Steiner circle, <b>24974</b>)<br>
(Lemoine inellipse, 598, 3rd Lemoine circle, 20383)<br>
(MacBeath inconic, 264, MacBeath circle, <b>24977</b>)<br>
(Mandart inellipse, 8, Mandart circle, 11)<br>
(orthic inconic, 4, nine-point circle, 125)<br>
(Steiner inellipse, 2, nine-point circle, 115)<br>
(Yff parabola, 190, cevian circle of X(190), <b>24979</b>)<br>
</p>
