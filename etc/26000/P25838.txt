<div class="preamble-sect">
<p>
Extending the preamble just before X(24537), let m(X) denote the (A,B,C,X(2); A',B',C',X(2)) collineation image of X = x : y : z, where A'B'C' = Gemini triangle 34, as in centers X(25838)-X(25874).  Then
</p>
<p>
m(X) = a^2 (b^2 + a c) (c^2 + a b) x - a c (a^2 + b c) (c^2 + a b) y - a b (a^2 + b c) (b^2 + a c) z : :
</p>
<p>
A point X lies on the Euler line if and only if m(X) also lies on the Euler line. (Clark Kimberling, October 27, 2018)
