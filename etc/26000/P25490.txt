<p>
Extending the preamble just before X(24537), let m(X) denote the (A,B,C,X(2); A',B',C',X(2)) collineation image of X = x : y : z, where A'B'C' = Gemini triangle 23, as in centers X(25490)-X(25542).  Then
</p>
<p>
m(X) = b c (a + b + c) x + a c (a + c) y + a b ( a + b) z : :
</p>
<p>
Fixed points of m include X(i) for these i: 2, 36, 238, 667.  A point X lies on the Euler line if and only m(X) lies on the Euler line. (Clark Kimberling, October 19, 2018)
