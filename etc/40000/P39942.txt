Points on cubics: X(39942)-X(39984)

<img src="rightri.svg" style="height:30px" alt="rightri">

<div class="preamble-sect">
<p>
This preamble is contributed by Clark Kimberling, October 7, 2020.
<p>
Suppose that P = p(a,b,c) : p(b,c,a) : p(c,a,b) is an polynomial triangle center such that the point (b-c)p(a,b,c) : (c-a)p(b,c,a) : (a-b)p(c,a,b) is on the line at infinity; i.e.,
</p>
<p>
(b-c)p(a,b,c) + (c-a)p(b,c,a) + (a-b)p(c,a,b) = 0.
</p>
<p>
Let &zeta;(P) denote the cubic given by
</p>
<p>
(b+c)p(a,b,c)(cy-bz)(bx+ay)(cx+az) + (c+a)p(b,c,a)(az-cx)(cy+bz)(ay+bx) + (a+b)p(c,a,b)(bx-ay)(az+cx)(bz+cy) = 0.
</p>
<p>
Let A'B'C' denote the excentral triangle of ABC. It is easy to check that the following 9 points lie on &zeta;(P): A, B, C, A', B', C', X(1), X(2), X(6) .
</p>
<p>
It is also easy to check that if U is a point on &zeta;(P), then the isotomic conjugate of U also lies on &zeta;(P). This section consists of isogonal conjugates of points on cubics &zeta;(P) for selected points P. In column 1 of the following table, the appearance of u(a,b,c) in a row means that the points indicated in column 2 lie on the cubic
</p>
<p>
u(a,b,c)(cy-bz)(bx+ay)(cx+az) + u(b,c,a)(az-cx)(cy+bz)(a+bx) + u(c,a,b)(bx-ay)(az+cx)(bz+cy) = 0;
</p>
<p>
so that the point P is given by u(a,b,c) = (b+c)p(a,b,c).  This is the cubic pK(X(6),P*), where P* = a*((b + c)*p - (a + c)*q - (a + b)*r) : :
</p>
<p>
The appearance of {i,j} in column 2 means that {X(i),X(j)} are a pair of isogonal conjugate points that lie on &zeta;(P).
</p>
