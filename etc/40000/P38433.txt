<p class="preamble-title">
Points associated with Vu (k)-conics: X(38433)-X(38449)
</p>
<img src="rightri.svg" style="height:30px" alt="rightri">
</header>
<div class="preamble-sect">
<p>
This preamble is based on notes from Vu Thanh Tung, May 13-14, 2020 and Peter Moses, May 13, 2020.<br>
<p>
Suppose that 0 &lt; k &lt; &pi;/2.  Let A' and A" be the points on line BC such that |AA'| = |AA"| and angle A'AA" has measure 2k.  Likewise, let B' and B" on CA and C' and C" on AB be the points such that the triangles B'BB" and C'CC" are similar to A'AA". Then the points A', A", B', B", C', C" lie on a conic, here named the <b>Vu (k)-conic</b>.
</p>
<p>
Peter Moses found that these conics all have center X(6) and that the perspector, V(k), of the Vu (k)-conic is given by
</p>
<p>
V(k) = 1/((a^2 - b^2 - c^2)^2*(a^2 + b^2 - c^2)*(a^2 - b^2 + c^2)*Cos[k]^2 - 4*(a^4 - b^4 - c^4)*S^2*Sin[k]^2) : :  (barycentrics)
</p>
<p>
Moses also found that he ratio of minor axis to major axis is Sqrt[(J-1) (J+3) / ((J-3) (J+1))], where J = |OH|/R, as at X(1113).
</p>
<p>
Accordingly, the isotomic conjugate, T(k), and the isogonal conjugate, U(k), are given by
</p>
<p>
T(k) = (a^2 - b^2 - c^2)^2*(a^2 + b^2 - c^2)*(a^2 - b^2 + c^2)*Cos[k]^2 - 4*(a^4 - b^4 - c^4)*S^2*Sin[k]^2 : :
</p>
<p>
U(k) = a^2 ((a^2 - b^2 - c^2)^2*(a^2 + b^2 - c^2)*(a^2 - b^2 + c^2)*Cos[k]^2 - 4*(a^4 - b^4 - c^4)*S^2*Sin[k]^2) : :
</p>
<p>
V(k) lies on the Jerabek rectangular hyperbola {{A,B,C,X(3),X(4)}}. <br>
T(k) lies on the line X(69)X(264). <br>
U(k) lies on the Euler line. <br>
</p>
<p>
X(6145) = perspector of the Vu (&pi;/4) conic, and X(18434) = perspector of the Vu (&pi;/6) conic.
</p>
<p>
For every real number x, the perspector of the Vu (acrctan(x))-conic is
</p>
<p>
1/((a^2 - b^2 - c^2)^2*(a^2 + b^2 - c^2)*(a^2 - b^2 + c^2) - 4*(a^4 - b^4 - c^4)*S^2*x^2) : : ,
with isogonal conjugate
</p>
<p>
a^2*((a^2 - b^2 - c^2)^2*(a^2 + b^2 - c^2)*(a^2 - b^2 + c^2) - 4*(a^4 - b^4 - c^4)*S^2*x^2) : :
</p>
<p>
on the Euler line. (Peter Moses, May 15, 2020)
</p>
