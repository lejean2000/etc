YIU INFINITY POINTS: X(39469)-X(39474)
</p>
<img src="rightri.svg" style="height:30px" alt="rightri">

<div class="preamble-sect">
<p>
This preamble is contributed by Clark Kimberling and Peter Moses, August 30, 2020.
</p>
<p>
Suppose that F = f : g : h (barycentrics) is a point on the infinity line, L. The orthopoint (a.k.a. orthogonal conjugate) of F, denoted here by F' = f' : g' : h' also lies on L. In <i>Introduction to the Geometry of the Triangle,</i>,
Paul Yiu notes that
</p>
<p>
S<sub>A</sub>*f*f' + S<sub>B</sub>*g*g' +  S<sub>C</sub>*h*h' = 0.
</p>
<p>
Equialvently, the point
F'' = S<sub>A</sub>*f*f' : S<sub>B</sub>*g*g' :  S<sub>C</sub>*h*h'
</p>
<p>
also lies on L. The point F'' is here named the <b>Yiu infinity point of F</b>. Note that F'' = F*F'/H, where H = X(4), the orthocenter.
</p>
<p>
The appearance of (i,j,k) in the following list means that X(i) is on L, X(j) = orthopoint of X(i), and X(k) = Yiu infinity point of X(i):
</p>
<p>
(30, 523, 9033)<br>
(511, 512, 39469)<br>
(512, 511, 39469)<br>
(513, 517, 8677)<br>
(514, 516, 39470)<br>
(515, 522, 39471)<br>
(516, 514, 39470)<br>
(517, 513, 8677)<br>
(519, 3667, 39472)<br>
(522, 515, 39471)<br>
(523, 30, 9033)<br>
(525, 1503, 39473)<br>
(542, 690, 39574)<br>
(690, 542, 39474)<br>
(1503, 525, 39475)<br>
</p>
<p>
There is a connection between Yiu infinity point and the Psi mapping (TCCT, p. 80): If Q is the the Yiu infinite point of a point P and the orthopoint of P), and if U is a point on the line of X(3) and the isogonal conjugate of P, then Psi(U) is the isogonal conjugate of Q. (Peter Moses, January 27, 2021)
</p>
