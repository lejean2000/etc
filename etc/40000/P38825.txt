Vu cevian-circles perspectors and related cyclocevian conjugates: X(38825)-X(38843)

<p>
This preamble is based on notes from Vu Thanh Tung, June 1-3, 2020.<br>
<p>
Let P = p : q : r (barycentrics) be a point in the plane of a triangle ABC, but not on any of the medians (i.e., the lines AG, BG, CG), and let
</p>
<p>
A<sub>1</sub>B<sub>1</sub>C<sub>1</sub> = cevian triangle of P<br>
A<sub>2</sub> = the point, other than A, in which the circles (ABC) and (AB<sub>1</sub>C<sub>1</sub>) intersect, and define B<sub>2</sub> and C<sub>2</sub> cyclically<br>
A' = BB<sub>2</sub>&cap;CC<sub>2</sub>, and define B' and C' cyclically
</p>
<p>
The triangles ABC and A'B'C' are perspective, and the perspector is the point
</p>
<p>
V(P) = a^2*(p + q)*(p + r) : b^2*(q + r)*(q + p) : c^2*(r + p)*(r + q).
</p>
<p>
Also, V(P) = complement of the anticomplementary conjugate of P (CÃ©sar Lozada, June 4, 2020)
</p>
<p>
Also, V(P) is the isogonal conjugate of the complement of P.  (Randy Hutson, June 9, 2020)
</p>
<p>
A'B'C' is the anticevian triangle of V(P), given by
</p>
<p>
A' = -a^2*(p + q)*(p + r) : b^2*(q + r)*(q + p) : c^2*(r + p)*(r + q).
</p>
<p>
See <a href="VuCevianCirclesPerspector.pdf"><b>Vu Cevian-Circles Perspector</b></a>.
</p>
<p>
The triangle A'B'C' is here named the <b>Vu cevian-circles triangle of P</b>.
</p>
<p>
The appearance of (i,j) in the following list means that V(X(i)) = X(j): <br>
(1,58), (3,54), (4,4), (5,1173), (6,251), (7,57), (8,1), (9,1174), (10,1126), (11,18771), (21,38825), (22, 38828), (23, 38831), (32,38826), (75,81), (76,83), (560,38827), (1501,38829), (1502,38830), (1928,38843)
</p>
<p>
Let V'(P) = cyclocevian conjugate of V(P) wrt A'B'C', which is also the perspector of A'B'C' and A<sub>2</sub>B<sub>2</sub>C<sub>2</sub>, given by
</p>
<p>
V'(P) = a^2*(p + q)*(p + r)*(p*q + p*r - q*r) : :
</p>
<p>
V'(P) is the barycentric product V(P)*[P-Ceva conjugate of X(2)] = V(P)*[anticompliment of isotomic conjugate of P].  (Randy Hutson, June 9, 2020)
</p>
<p>
The appearance of (i,j) in the following list means that V'(X(i)) = X(j): <br>
(1,38832), (3, 26887), (4, 6353), (5,38833), (6,38834), (7,1420), (8,165), (9,38835), (10,38836), (31,38837), (32,38838), (75,21), (76,1799), (560,38839), (561,38840), (1501, 38841), (1502, 38842)
</p>
<p>
The triangle A<sub>2</sub>B<sub>2</sub>C<sub>2</sub> is here named the <b>Vu perspectivities triangle of P</b>; see the preamble just before X(38848).  Barycentrics for A<sub>2</sub> are given by
</p>
<p>
A<sub>2</sub> = a^2*(p + q)*(p + r) :  b^2*(q - r)*(p + q):  c^2*(r - q)*(p + r) : :
</p>
