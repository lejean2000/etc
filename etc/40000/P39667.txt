<p>
Let A<sub>1</sub>B<sub>1</sub>C<sub>1</sub> be the cevian triangle of a point P = p : q : r in the plane of a triangle ABC. Let G<sub>a</sub> be the centroid of PB<sub>1</sub>C<sub>1</sub>.  Let A' be the isogonal conjugate of G<sub>a</sub> with respect to AB<sub>1</sub>C<sub>1</sub>, and define B' and C' cyclically. The triangle A'B'C' is perspective to ABC, and the perspector is given by
</p>
<p>
V(P) = a^2 q r (p + q)(p + r)(p + 2q + 2r) : :
</p>
<p>
The appearance of (i,j) in the following list means that V(X(i)) = X(j): (1,4658), (2,6), (3,49094), (4,631), (5,39667), (6,39668), (7,10389), (8,3361), (9,39669), (10,39670), (31,39671), (32,39672), (75,39673), (76,39674), (83,39675), (141,39676), (560,39677), (561,39678)
</p>
