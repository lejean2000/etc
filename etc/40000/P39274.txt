</p>
<p>
Suppose that T = U<sub>1</sub>V<sub>1</sub>W<sub>1</sub>, T = U<sub>2</sub>V<sub>2</sub>W<sub>2</sub>, T = U<sub>3</sub>V<sub>3</sub>W<sub>3</sub> are triangles such that U<sub>1</sub>,U<sub>2</sub>,U<sub>3</sub> are collinear, V<sub>1</sub>,V<sub>2</sub>,V<sub>3</sub> are collinear, and W<sub>1</sub>, W<sub>2</sub>, W<sub>3</sub> are collinear, so that the three pairs {T<sub>2</sub>,T<sub>3</sub>}, {T<sub>3</sub>,T<sub>1</sub>}, {T<sub>1</sub>,T<sub>2</sub>} have the same vertex triangle.
</p>
<p>
Theorem: The Vietnamese points (defined in the preamble just before X(39177)) of the three pairs {T<sub>2</sub>,T<sub>3</sub>}, {T<sub>3</sub>,T<sub>1</sub>}, {T<sub>1</sub>,T<sub>2</sub>} are collinear.
</p>
<p>
Corollary: Let T<sub>P</sub>, T<sub>U</sub>, T<sub>X</sub> be the cevian triangles of points P = p : q : r, U = u : v : w, X = x : y : z (barycentrics). Then the three Vietnamese points of the three pairs {T<sub>P</sub>,T<sub>U</sub>}, {T<sub>U</sub>,T<sub>X</sub>}, {T<sub>X</sub>,T<sub>P</sub>}} lie on a line L'(P,U,X), here named the <b>Vietnamese line of {P,U,X}</b>, of which the trilinear pole is given by
</p>
<p>
L(P,U,X) = a^2 (p + q) (p + r) (u + v) (u + w) (x + y) (x + z) (p r v^2 x y + q r v^2 x y - q^2 u w x y - q r u w x y - q^2 v w x y + p r v w x y - p r u v y^2 - q r u v y^2 + p q u w y^2 - q r u w y^2 + p q v w y^2 + p r v w y^2 + q^2 u v x z + q r u v x z - p q v^2 x z + q r v^2 x z - p q v w x z -    q^2 v w x z + q^2 u v y z - p r u v y z - p q v^2 y z - p r v^2 y z + p q u w y z + q^2 u w y z) (-q r u w x y - r^2 u w x y + p r v w x y + r^2 v w x y + p r w^2 x y - q r w^2 x y + q r u v x z + r^2 u v x z - p q v w x z + r^2 v w x z - p q w^2 x z - q r w^2 x z - p r u v y z - r^2 u v y z + p q u w y z - r^2 u w y z + p q w^2 y z + p r w^2 y z - p r u v z^2 + q r u v z^2 + p q u w z^2 + q r u w z^2 - p q v w z^2 - p r v w z^2) : :
</p>
