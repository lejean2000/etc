<p>
In the plane of a triangle ABC, let P be a point such that P and its isogonal conjugate, P', are distinct finite points.  Let A<sub>1</sub>B<sub>1</sub>C<sub>1</sub> be the pedal triangle of P, and let A'<sub>1</sub>B'<sub>1</sub>C'<sub>1</sub> be the pedal triangle of P'. Let A<sub>2</sub> be the point of intersection, other than A, of the circles (ABC) and (AA<sub>1</sub>A'<sub>1</sub>), and define B<sub>2</sub> and C<sub>2</sub> cyclically. Let A<sub>3</sub> = BB<sub>2</sub>&cap;CC<sub>2</sub>, and define B<sub>3</sub> and C<sub>3</sub> cyclically. The triangle A<sub>3</sub>B<sub>3</sub>C<sub>3</sub> is perpsective to ABC, and the perspector, V(P), is here named the <b>Vu isogonal perspector</b> of P. Note that V(P') = V(P), and A<sub>3</sub>B<sub>3</sub>C<sub>3</sub> is the anticevian triangle of V(P).
</p>
<p>
If P = p : q : r (barycentrics), then
</p>
<p>
V(P) = 1/(p*(b^2*r + SA*q)*(c^2q + SA*r)) : :
</p>
<p>
The appearance of (i,j) in the following list means that V(X(i)) = X(j): (2,39353), (3,4), (4,4), (5,39454), (6,39353), (7,39455), (8,39456), (9,39457), (10,39458), (31,39459), (32,39460), (75,39459), (76, 39460)
</p>
<p>
Generalization (Vu Thanh Tung, August 28, 2020):
</p>
<p>
<b>Theorem:</b>  In the plane of a triangle ABC, let A<sub>1</sub> and A'<sub>1</sub> be points on BC such that B, C, A<sub>1</sub>, and A'<sub>1</sub> are distinct. Define B<sub>1</sub> and C<sub>1</sub> cyclically, and define B'<sub>1</sub> and C'<sub>1</sub> cyclically.
Let A<sub>2</sub> be the point of intersection, other than A, of the circles (ABC) and (AA<sub>1</sub>A'<sub>1</sub>), and define B<sub>2</sub> and C<sub>2</sub> cyclically. Let A<sub>3</sub> = BB<sub>2</sub>&cap;CC<sub>2</sub>, and define B<sub>3</sub> and C<sub>3</sub> cyclically. Then A<sub>3</sub>B<sub>3</sub>C<sub>3</sub> is perspective to ABC if and only if the points A<sub>1</sub>, A'<sub>1</sub>, B<sub>1</sub>, B'<sub>1</sub>, C<sub>1</sub>, C'<sub>1</sub> lie on a conic.
</p>
<p>
<b>Corollary:</b> Let A<sub>1</sub>B<sub>1</sub>C<sub>1</sub> be the cevian triangle of P, and let A'<sub>1</sub>B'<sub>1</sub>C'<sub>1</sub> be the cevian triangle of P'. Then the six vertices lie on the bicevian conic of P and P', and the perspector of the triangles ABC and A<sub>3</sub>B<sub>3</sub>C<sub>3</sub> is the barycentric product P*P'.
</p>

</p>
