This preamble and centers X(38723)-X(38807) were contributed by CÃ©sar Eliud Lozada, May 28, 2020.
</p>
<p><i>The centroids of the four triangles determined by four concyclic points are concyclic.</i>. (Reference: Halsted, George Bruce, Elementary Synthetic Geometry, 1892, problem 24, pp. 84.)</p>
<p>Indeed, the above result is true for other points distinct from the centroids, although the exact locus is still unknown.<p>
<p>Part of this locus is as follows: let Q be a point on the circumcircle of ABC and P a point on the Euler line of ABC such that |OP|/|OH| = &lambda;, &lambda; being a real number not depending on ABC. Denote P<sub>a</sub> = P-of-QBC, P<sub>b</sub> = P-of-QCA and P<sub>c</sub> = P-of-QAB. Then the points P, P<sub>a</sub>, P<sub>b</sub>, P<sub>c</sub> are concyclic on a circle with radius r' = |&lambda;| R and whose center O'(Q, P) is here named the <b>Q-circum-Euler-point of P</b>. In this case:</p>
<p>&emsp;&emsp;&emsp;O'(Q, P) = &lambda; (Q + X(4)) + (1 - 2 &lambda;) X(3) </p>
<p>and it lies on the line {X(3), midpoint(X(4), Q) }.  Moreover, when P is fixed and Q varies, the locus of O'(Q, P) is another circle with the same radius r' and center P.</p>
<p>There are other P such that P, P<sub>a</sub>, P<sub>b</sub>, P<sub>c</sub> are concyclic. A numerical calculus shows that a partial list of such points contains P=X(n) for n &in; {1, 13, 14, 15, 16, 23, 26, 36, 40, 80, 125, 155, 165, 186, 265, 368, 369, 370, 399, 1144, 1147, 1385, 1482, 1511, 1658, 2070, 2071, 2072, 2077, 3167, 3232, 3576, 3579, 5159, 5373, 5394, 5473, 5474, 5537, 5609, 5611, 5615, 5626, 5899, 5961, 5962, 5963, 5964, 6104, 6105, 6699, 6771, 6774, 7387, 7464, 7575, 7689, 7982, 7987, 7991, 8008, 8009, 8148, 8697, 9909} (n&lt;10000). When P=X(1), P, P<sub>a</sub>, P<sub>b</sub>, P<sub>c</sub> are vertices of a rectangle.</p>
<p>Curiously, when P=X(15) and Q varies, the locus of O'(Q,P) are the sides of a central triangle having A-vertex with barycentric coordinates A' = (sqrt(3)*S+SA)*(SB+SC): S^2-SA*SC : S^2-SA*SB. This triangle is perspective to the following triangles with perspector X(3): (ABC, ABC-X3 reflections, 2nd anti-extouch, 2nd Hyacinth, Lucas antipodal(&plusmn;1), Lucas central(&plusmn;1),  X3-ABC reflections). It is also perspective to the following triangles with the given perspectors: (circumsymmedial, 11485), (outer-Le Viet An, 3129), (symmedial, 61), (tangential, 22236). A similar locus does not occur for P=X(16).</p>
