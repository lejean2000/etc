<div class="preamble-sect">
<p>
This preamble is based on notes from Vu Thanh Tung, May 31, 2020.<br>
<p>
Let P = p : q : r (barycentrics) be a point in the plane of a triangle ABC, and let
</p>
<p>
A<sub>1</sub>B<sub>1</sub>C<sub>1</sub> = cevian triangle of P<br>
t<sub>a</sub> = line tangent to the circle (AB'C') at A, and define t<sub>b</sub> and t<sub>c</sub> cyclically<br>
A' = t<sub>b</sub>&cap;t<sub>c</sub>, and define B' and C' cyclically.
</p>
<p>
The triangle A'B'C' is the anticevian triangle of the point
</p>
<p>
V(P) =  a^2/(p q + p r) : b^2/(q r + q p) : c^2/(r p + r q),
</p>
<p>
which is the perspector of ABC and A'B'C'.
</p>
<p>
See <a href="VuCevianTangentialPerspector.pdf"><b>Vu Cevian Tangential Perspector</b></a>.
</p>
<p>
The appearance of (i,j) in the following list means that V(X(i)) = X(j): <br>
(1,81), (2,6), (3,275), (4,2), (5,288), (6,83), (7,1), (8,57), (9,1170), (10,1171), (11,38809), (31, 38810), (32, 38811), (75,58), (76,251), (560,38812), (561, 38813)
</p>
<p>
The triangle A'B'C' is also perspective to A<sub>1</sub>B<sub>1</sub>C<sub>1</sub> , the perspector, denoted by V'(P), being the P-Ceva-conjugate of V(P):
</p>
<p>
V'(P) = a^2/(p(q+r)) * (b^2/(q^2(r+p)) + c^2/(r^2(p+q)) - a^2/(p^2(q+r))) : :
</p>
<p>
The appearance of (i,j) in the following list means that V'(X(i)) = X(j): <br>
(1,38814), (2,3), (3,38815), (4,193), (5,38816), (6,38817), (7,57), (8,34488), (9,38818), (10,38819), (31,38820), (32,38821)
</p>
<p>
V(P) is the cevapoint of X(6) and P', where P' is the isogonal conjugate of P.  (Randy Hutson, May 31, 2020)
</p>
