<div class="preamble-sect">
<p>
This preamble and centers X(38247)-X(38304) were contributed by César Eliud Lozada, May 1, 2020.
</p>
<p>The following theorem is enunciated in the preamble just before <a href="ETCPart19.html#X36598">X(36598)</a>:
</p>
<p style="padding-left:25px;">
<i>Let ABC be a triangle, P a point, A'B'C' the cevian triangle of P and &#8493; a conic through A', B', C'. If A", B", C" are the points, others than A', B', C', at which &#8493; cuts BC, CA, AB, respectively, then AA", BB", CC" are concurrent.</i>
</p>
<p>
It is also written in that preamble that if P = x : y : z and &#8493; has perspector X' = x' : y' : z' with respect to ABC (barycentrics), then the triangles ABC and A"B"C" have perspector:
</p>
<p style="padding-left:25px;">
Q(P, X') = x (x' y z + x (z' y - 3 y' z)) (x' y z + x (y' z - 3 z' y)) : :
</p>
<p>
Vu Thanh Tung observed that if the previous construction is applied to a quadrangle P<sub>1</sub>P<sub>2</sub>P<sub>3</sub>P<sub>4</sub> and every Q<sub>i</sub> is calculated as above in the triangle P<sub>j</sub>P<sub>k</sub>P<sub>&#8467;</sub>, then the four lines P<sub>n</sub>Q<sub>n</sub> are also concurrent (<a href="https://groups.io/g/Quadri-and-Poly-Geometry/message/249">Quadri-and-Poly-Geometry #243</a>). César Lozada found that this point of concurrence T(P, X') has barycentrics coordinates:</p>
<p style="padding-left:25px;">T(P, X') = x' (x' y z + x (y' z - 3 z' y)) (x' y z + x (z' y - 3 y' z)) : : </p>
<p>The point T(P, X') is nere named the <b>Vu-Lozada QA-point of (P, X')</b>.</p>
<p>Notes:</p>
<ul>
 <li>If P = X' or P lies on the ABC-circumconic with perspector X' then T(P, X') = P.</li>
 <li>If X' = X(2) then T(P, X') = IsotomicConjugate(Anticomplement(Anticomplement(IsotomicConjugate(P)))).</li>
 <li>If X' = X(4) then T(P, X') = PolarConjugate(Anticomplement(Anticomplement(PolarConjugate(P)))).</li>
 <li>If X' = X(6) then T(P, X') = IsogonalConjugate(Anticomplement(Anticomplement(IsogonalConjugate(P)))).</li>
 </ul>
