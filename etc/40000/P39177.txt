Vietnamese points: X(39177)-X(39201)

<img src="rightri.svg" style="height:30px" alt="rightri">

<div class="preamble-sect">
<p>
This preamble and centers X(39177)-X(39201) were contributed by Vu Thanh Tung, July 3, 2020.
</p>
<p>
Suppose that T<sub>1</sub> = U<sub>1</sub>V<sub>1</sub>W<sub>1</sub> and T<sub>2</sub> = U<sub>2</sub>V<sub>2</sub>W<sub>2</sub> are triangles and that T<sub>1</sub> is not perspective to T<sub>2</sub>. Let UVW be the vertex triangle of T<sub>1</sub> and T<sub>2</sub>.
Let L<sub>U</sub> = radical axis to the circles (UV<sub>1</sub>W<sub>1</sub>) and (UV<sub>2</sub>W<sub>2</sub>), and define L<sub>V</sub> and L<sub>W</sub> cyclically.
</p>
<p>
The lines L<sub>U</sub>, L<sub>V</sub>, L<sub>W</sub> concur in a point Vn(T<sub>1</sub>,T<sub>2</sub>), here named the <b>Vietnamese point of T<sub>1</sub> and T<sub>2</sub></b>, denoted by Vn(T<sub>1</sub>, T<sub>2</sub>).  This result is based on Problem 1 in the IMO (International Mathematics Olympiad) 1995 Vietnam TST (Team Selection Test).
</p>
<p>
See <a href="VietnamesePoint.pdf"><b>Vietnamese Point</b></a>.
</p>
<p>
Next, suppose that P = p : q : r and U = u : v : w are points, and let T<sub>1</sub> = cevian triangle of P, and T<sub>2</sub> = cevian triangle of U. Then
</p>
<p>
Vn(T<sub>1</sub>, T<sub>2</sub>) = a^2 (p + q) (p + r) (u + v) (u + w) (r v - q w) : :
</p>
<p>
Let T<sub>3</sub> = anticevian triangle of P, and T<sub>4</sub> = anticevian triangle of U. Then
</p>
<p>
Vn(T<sub>3</sub>, T<sub>4</sub>) = (p - q - r) (-q u + p v) (u - v - w) (-r u + p w) (b^2 (-(q - r)^3 u^2 w + p^3 u w (-v + w) + p^2 (r u^2 (v - 2 w) - r (v - w)^3 + q u^2 w) + p u (q^2 (v - w) w + r^2 (u^2 - v^2 + 3 v w - 2 w^2) + q r (-u^2 + v^2 - 4 v w + 3 w^2))) + c^2 ((q - r)^3 u^2 v + p^3 u v (v - w) + p u (r^2 v (-v + w) + q^2 (u^2 - 2 v^2 + 3 v w - w^2) + q r (-u^2 + 3 v^2 - 4 v w + w^2)) +  p^2 (r u^2 v + q ((v - w)^3 + u^2 (-2 v + w)))) + a^2 (p^3 u v w + (q - r) (r^2 v (-u^2 + v (v - w)) + 2 q r v w (-v + w) + q^2 w (u^2 + (v - w) w)) - p u (q^2 v w + r^2 v w + q r (-u^2 + v^2 - 4 v w + w^2)) - p^2 (q w (u^2 + (v - w) w) + r v (u^2 + v (-v + w))))) : :
</p>
<p>
Let T<sub>5</sub> = circumcevian triangle of P, and T<sub>6</sub> = circumcevian triangle of U. Then
</p>
<p>
Vn(T<sub>5</sub>, T<sub>6</sub>) = a^2 (b^2 (r u - p w) + c^2 (p v - q u) - a^2 (q w - r v)) : : , this being the pole wrt the circumcircle of the line PU
</p>
<p>
Let O = X(3) = circumcenter of ABC.  If O, P, U are collinear, then Vn(T<sub>5</sub>, T<sub>6</sub>) is the infinite point on the line OPU.
</p>
