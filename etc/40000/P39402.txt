Vu Isodynamic Perspectors: X(39402)-X(39411)

<div class="preamble-sect">
<p>
This preamble is contributed by Vu Thanh Tung, August 21, 2020.
</p>
<p>
Let P be a point in the plane of a triangle ABC. Let A<sub>1</sub> be the 1st isodnamic point, X(15), of triangle PBC, and define B<sub>1</sub> and C<sub>1</sub> cyclically. Let A<sub>2</sub> be the point of intersection, other than A, of the circles (ABC) and (AB<sub>1</sub>C<sub>1</sub>), and define B<sub>2</sub> and C<sub>2</sub> cyclically. Let A<sub>3</sub> = BB<sub>2</sub>&cap;CC<sub>2</sub>, and define B<sub>3</sub> and C<sub>3</sub> cyclically. The triangle A<sub>3</sub>B<sub>3</sub>C<sub>3</sub> is perpsective to ABC, and the perspector, V(P), is here named the <b>1st Vu isodynamic perspector</b>. Note that A<sub>3</sub>B<sub>3</sub>C<sub>3</sub> is the anticevian triangle of V(P).
</p>
<p>
If the above construction is carried out with the 2nd isodynamic point, X(16), instead of X(15), the resulting perspector, T(P), is the <b>2nd Vu isodynamic perspector</b>.
</p>
<p>
Barycentrics were found by Peter Moses (August 22, 2020):
</p>
<p>
V(P) = a^2/(Sqrt[3]*a^2*((a^2 - b^2 - c^2)*q*r - b^2*r^2 - c^2*q^2) - 2*(a^2*q*r + b^2*r*p + c^2*p*q)*S) : :
<br>
T(P) = a^2/(Sqrt[3]*a^2*((a^2 - b^2 - c^2)*q*r - b^2*r^2 - c^2*q^2) + 2*(a^2*q*r + b^2*r*p + c^2*p*q)*S) : :
</p>
<p>
The appearance of (i,j) in the following list means that V(X(i)) = X(j): (1,39402), (2,39404), (3,2981), (4,39406), (5,39408), (6,39410)
</p>
<p>
The appearance of (i,j) in the following list means that T(X(i)) = X(j): (1,39403), (2,39405), (3,6151), (4,39407), (5,39409), (6,39411)
</p>
<p>
See <a href="IsodynamicPerspector.pdf"><b>Isodynamic Perspector</b></a>.  (Vu Thanh Tung)
</p>
