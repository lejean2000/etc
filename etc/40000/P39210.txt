<p class="preamble-title">
Centers of Vietnamese circles: X(39210)-X(39228)
</p>
<img src="rightri.svg" style="height:30px" alt="rightri">
</header>
<div class="preamble-sect">
<p>
This preamble and centers X(39210)-X(39228) were contributed by Vu Thanh Tung, July 4, 2020.
</p>
<p>
Suppose that T<sub>1</sub> = U<sub>1</sub>V<sub>1</sub>W<sub>1</sub> and T<sub>2</sub> = U<sub>2</sub>V<sub>2</sub>W<sub>2</sub> are triangles and that T<sub>1</sub> is not perspective to T<sub>2</sub>. Let UVW be the vertex triangle of T<sub>1</sub> and T<sub>2</sub>. Let L<sub>U</sub> = radical axis to the circles (UV<sub>1</sub>W<sub>1</sub>) and (UV<sub>2</sub>W<sub>2</sub>), and define L<sub>V</sub> and L<sub>W</sub> cyclically.
</p>
<p>
The lines L<sub>U</sub>, L<sub>V</sub>, L<sub>W</sub> concur in a point Vn(T<sub>1</sub>,T<sub>2</sub>), the Vietnamese point of T<sub>1</sub> and T<sub>2</sub>, denoted by Vn(T<sub>1</sub>, T<sub>2</sub>), introduced in the preamble just before X(39177).
Continuing, let U<sub>0</sub> be the point, other than U, in which the circles (UV<sub>1</sub>W<sub>1</sub>) and (UV<sub>2</sub>W<sub>2</sub>) intersect and define V<sub>0</sub> and W<sub>0</sub> cyclically. Let M<sub>i</sub> be the Miquel point of  T<sub>i</sub> for i = 1, 2; e.g.,
</p>
<p>
M<sub>1</sub> = (UV<sub>1</sub>W<sub>1</sub>)&cap;(UV<sub>1</sub>W<sub>1</sub>)&cap;(U<sub>1</sub>V<sub>1</sub>W).
</p>
<p>
The six points U<sub>0</sub>,V<sub>0</sub>,W<sub>0</sub>,M<sub>1</sub>,M<sub>2</sub>,Vn lie on a circle, here named the <b>Vietnamese circle of T<sub>1</sub> and T<sub>2</sub></b>, denoted by v(T<sub>1</sub>,T<sub>2</sub>). The center of this circle is denoted by Vo(T<sub>1</sub>,T<sub>2</sub>).
</p>
<p>
See <a href="VietnameseCircle.pdf"><b>Vietnamese Circle</b></a>.
</p>
<p>
If P = p : q : r and U = u : v : w are distinct with distinct circumcevian triangles T<sub>1</sub>, T<sub>2</sub> then v(T<sub>1</sub>,T<sub>2</sub>) passes through the circumcenter, O, and is the circumcircle-inverse of the line PU.  If P, U, O are collinear then v(T<sub>1</sub>,T<sub>2</sub>) is the circle of infinite radius - that is, the line at infinity, and Vo(T<sub>1</sub>,T<sub>2</sub>) = O.
</p>
<p>
If T<sub>1</sub> = cevian triangle of P, and T<sub>2</sub> = cevian triangle of U, then the appearance of (i,j,k) in the following list means that the center of the Vietnamese circle of the cevian triangle of X(i) and the cevian triangle of X(j) is X(k): (2,3,37084), (3,4,15451), (2,4,523)
</p>
<p>
The appearance of (i,j,k) in the following list means that center of the Vietnamese circle of the anticevian triangle of X(i) and the anticevian triangle of X(j) is X(k): (1,2,2957), (2,6,30715)
</p>
<p>
The appearance of (i,j,k) in the following list means that the center of the Vietnamese circle of the circumcevian triangle of X(i) and the circumcevian triangle of X(j) is X(k): (1,3,513), (2,3,523), (2,4,523), (2,6,5926), (3,4,523), (3,6,523)
</p>
