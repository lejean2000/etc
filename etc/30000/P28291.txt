<p class="preamble-title">
Points on circumcircle and line at infinity: X(28291)-X(28236)
</p>
<img src="rightri.svg" style="height:30px" alt="rightri">
</header>
<div class="preamble-sect">
<p>
Suppose that X = x : y : z is a point on the line at infinity.  All the lines that meet in X are parallel, so that X can be regarded as a direction in the plane of the reference triangle ABC.  Let X' be the isogonal conjugate of X, so that X' lies on the circumcircle.  Let X'' be the circumcircle-antipode of X', and let X''' be its isogonal conjugate, on the line at infinity.  As a direction, X''' is perpendicular to X.  In this section, X is given by the form h(-2a^2 + b^2 + c^2) + k(-2bc + ab + ac) : : , where h and k are constants, and K is given the name <b>Point Porrima(h,k)</b>.  See also the preamble just before X(28145).  (Clark Kimberling, November 19, 2018)
</p>
<p>
In the table below, Columns 1 and 2 show h and k.
</p>
<p>
Column 3.  h(-2a^2 + b^2 + c^2) + k(-2bc + ab + ac ) : : , on infinity line, referenced below as x : y : z
</p>
<p>
Column 4.  (isogonal conjugate of x : y : z) = a^2/x + b^2/y + c^2/z : : on circumcircle, referenced below as u : v : w
</p>
<p>
Column 5.  (antipode of u : v : w) = (a^2+b^2-c^2)(a^2-b^2+c^2)u + 2a^2 (a^2-b^2-c^2)v + 2a^2 (a^2-b^2-c^2)w : : on circumcircle, referenced below as u1 : v1 : w1
</p>
<p>
Column 6.  (isogonal conjugate of u1 : v1 : w1) = a^2/u1 + b^2/v1 + c^2/w1
</p>
<p>
For each row, let X be the point in Column 3 and X' the point in Column 6.  Let U be any point in the finite plane of ABC.  Then the lines UX and UX' are perpendicular.
</p>
