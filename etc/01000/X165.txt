<h3 id="X165">X(165)&nbsp;=&nbsp;CENTROID OF THE EXCENTRAL TRIANGLE</h3>
Trilinears &nbsp;&nbsp; tan(B/2) + tan(C/2) - tan(A/2) : tan(C/2) + tan(A/2) - tan(B/2) : tan(A/2) + tan(B/2) - tan(C/2)
<br>
Trilinears &nbsp;&nbsp; 3a<sup>2</sup> - 2a(b + c) - (b - c)<sup>2</sup> : :
<br>
Trilinears &nbsp;&nbsp; (r/R) - 4 cos A : :
<br>
Barycentrics &nbsp;&nbsp; a[tan(B/2) + tan(C/2) - tan(A/2)] : :
<b><br>
X(165) = X(1) - 4 X(3)
</b>
<p>
If DEF is the pedal triangle of X(165), then |AE| + |AF| = |BF| + |BD| = |CD| + |CE|. (Seiichi Kirikami, October 8, 2010.)
</p>
<p>
Let A'B'C' be the anticevian triangle, wrt intouch triangle, of X(1).  Let A&Prime; be the reflection of A' in A, and define B' and C' cyclically.  The centroid of A&Prime;B&Prime;C&Prime; is X(165). (Randy Hutson, December 2, 2017)
</p>
<p>
Let A'B'C' be the excentral triangle.  Let A&Prime; be the symmedian point of triangle A'BC, and define B&Prime; and C&Prime; cyclically.  The lines A'A&Prime;, B'B&Prime;, C'C&Prime; concur in X(165).
(Randy Hutson, July 31 2018)
</p>
