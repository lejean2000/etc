<h3 id="X53">X(53)&nbsp;=&nbsp;SYMMEDIAN POINT OF ORTHIC TRIANGLE</h3>
Trilinears &nbsp;&nbsp; tan A cos(B - C) : tan B cos(C - A) : tan C cos(A - B)
<br>
Barycentrics&nbsp;&nbsp;a tan A cos(B - C) : b tan B cos(C - A) : c tan C cos(A - B)
<br>

Let A'B'C' be the Euler triangle.  Let L<sub>A</sub> be the trilinear polar of A', and define L<sub>B</sub> and L<sub>C</sub> cyclically.  Let A&Prime; = L<sub>B</sub>&cap;L<sub>C</sub>, and define B&Prime; and C&Prime; cyclically.  The lines AA&Prime;, BB&Prime;, CC&Prime; concur in X(53).  (Randy Hutson, June 7, 2019)
</p>
