<h3 id="X399">X(399)&nbsp;=&nbsp;PARRY REFLECTION POINT</h3>
 <a id="ParryReflectionPoint"></a>
Trilinears &nbsp;&nbsp; 5 cos A - 4 cos B cos C - 8 sin B sin C cos<sup>2</sup>A : :
<br>
Barycentrics&nbsp;&nbsp; Barycentrics &nbsp;&nbsp; a^2[a^8 - 4a^6(b^2 + c^2) + a^4(6b^4 + b^2c^2 + 6c^4) - a^2(4b^6 - b^4c^2 - b^2c^4 + 4 c^6) + (b^2 - c^2)^2(b^4 + 4b^2c^2 + c^4)] : :

<p>
Let L, M, N be lines through A, B, C, respectively, parallel to the Euler line. Let L' be the reflection of L in sideline BC, let M' be the reflection of M in sideline CA, and let N' be the reflection of N in sideline AB. The lines L', M', N' concur in X(399), as proved in
</p>
<p>
<b>Cyril Parry,</b> Problem 10637, <i>American Mathematical Monthly</i> 105 (1998) 68.
</p>
<p>
In <b>Cosmin Pohoata,</b> "On the Parry reflection point," <i>Forum Geometricorum</i> 8 (2008), 43-48
<a href="http://forumgeom.fau.edu/FG2008volume8/FG200806.pdf" style= "font-weight: bold">here</a>, the following is proved:
</p>
<p>
Let A' be the reflection of vertex A in line BC, and define B', C' cyclically. Let A<sub>t</sub>B<sub>t</sub>C<sub>t</sub> be the tangential triangle of ABC. The circumcircles of the triangles A<sub>t</sub>B'C', A'B<sub>t</sub>C', A'B'C<sub>t</sub> concur in X(399). Moreover, the circumcircles of triangles A'B<sub>t</sub>C<sub>t</sub>, A<sub>t</sub>B'C<sub>t</sub>,  A<sub>t</sub>B<sub>t</sub>C' concur in a point Q = X8157), here named the <b>Parry-Pohoata point</b>. Barycentric coordinates for Q, of degree 22 in a,b,c, were found by J. F. Garcia Captit&aacute;n (Hyacinthos #15827, Nov. 19, 2007) and are included in Pohoata's article. Pohoata notes that the point Q lies on the circumcircle of the points X(3), X(4), X(399).
</p>
<p>
Let I, I<sub>A</sub>, I<sub>B</sub>, I<sub>C</sub>, denote the incenter and excenters of ABC. Lawrence Evans (Hyacinthos #6878) found that the circumcircles of the triangles IA'I<sub>A</sub>, IB'I<sub>B</sub>, IC'<sub>C</sub> concur in X(399).
</p>
<p>
The Pohoata article includes a proof that the circumcircles of the triangles A'I<sub>B</sub>I<sub>C</sub>, I<sub>A</sub>B'I<sub>C</sub>, I<sub>A</sub>I<sub>B</sub>C' also pass through X(399). Similar results involving the Fermat points, X(13) and X(14), are proved.
</p>
<p>
Pohoata reports that the following points are concyclic: X(13), X(16), X(110), X(399), X(1338), as are the points X(14), X(15), X(110), X(399), X(1337). Randy Hutson adds (Aug. 13, 2012) that the first of
these circles also passes through X(2381), and the second, through X(2380).
</p>
<p>
X(399) is the point P on the line X(3)X(74) for which the P-Brocard triangle is perspective to ABC. (Randy Hutson, August 26, 2014)
</p>
<p>
Let AaBaCa, AbBbCb, AcBcCc be the A-, B- and C-anti-altimedial triangles.  Let La be the line through Aa parallel to the Euler line of AaBaCa.  Define Lb and Lc cyclically.  The lines La, Lb, Lc concur in X(399). (Randy Hutson, November 2, 2017)
</p>
<p>
Let O<sub>A</sub> be the circle centered at the A-vertex of the Walsmith triangle and passing through A; define O<sub>B</sub> and O<sub>C</sub> cyclically.  X(399) is the radical center of O<sub>A</sub>, O<sub>B</sub>, O<sub>C</sub>.  (Randy Hutson, August 30, 2020)
</p>
