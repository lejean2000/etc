<h3 id="X21">X(21)&nbsp;=&nbsp;SCHIFFLER POINT<a id="SchifflerPoint"></a></h3>
Trilinears&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;1/(cos B + cos C) : 1/(cos C + cos A) : 1/(cos A + cos B)
<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;= f(a,b,c) : f(b,c,a) : f(c,a,b), where f(a,b,c) = (b + c - a)/(b + c)
<br>
Barycentrics&nbsp;&nbsp;a/(cos B + cos C) : b/(cos C + cos A) : c/(cos A + cos B)
<b><br>
X(21) = 3R*X(2) + 2r*X(3)
</b>
<p>
As a point on the Euler line, X(21) has Shinagawa coefficients ($aS<sub>A</sub>$, abc - $aS<sub>A</sub>$).
</p>
<p>
The name of this point honors <a href="http://faculty.evansville.edu/ck6/bstud/schiffler.html">Kurt Schiffler</a>.
</p>
<p>
Let A'B'C' be the incentral triangle of ABC, and let L<sub>A</sub> be the reflection of line B'C' in line BC; define L<sub>B</sub> and L<sub>C</sub> cyclically. The triangle formed by the lines L<sub>A</sub>, L<sub>B</sub>, L<sub>C</sub> is perspective to ABC, and the perspector is X(21). (Randy Hutson, 9/23/2011)
</p>
<p>
Write I for the incenter; the Euler lines of the four triangles IBC, ICA, IAB, and ABC concur in X(21). This configuration extends to         Kirikami-Schiffler points and generalizations found by Peter Moses, as introduced just before X(3648).
</p>
<p>
Let A'B'C' be the 2nd circumperp triangle. Let A&Prime; be the cevapoint of B' and C', and define B&Prime; and C&Prime; cyclically.  The lines AA&Prime;, BB&Prime;, and CC&Prime; concur in X(21).  (Randy Hutson, April 9, 2016)
</p>
<p>
Let A'B'C' be the Feuerbach triangle.  Let La be the tangent to the nine-point circle at A', and define Lb and Lc cyclically.  Let A&Prime; be the isogonal conjugate of the trilinear pole of La, and define B&Prime; and C&Prime; cyclically.  Let A*B*C* be the circumcevian triangle, wrt A&Prime;B&Prime;C&Prime;, of X(1).  The lines AA*, BB*, CC* concur in X(21).  (Randy Hutson, April 9, 2016)
</p>
<p>
Let A'B'C' be the 2nd circumperp triangle.  Let A&Prime; be the trilinear product B'*C', and define B&Prime; and C&Prime; cyclically.  Then A&Prime;, B&Prime;, C&Prime; are collinear on line X(36)X(238) (the trilinear polar of X(81)).  The lines A'A&Prime;, B'B&Prime;, C'C&Prime; concur in X(21).  (Randy Hutson, April 9, 2016)
</p>
<p>
Let Oa be the reflection of the A-excircle in the perpendicular bisector of BC, and define Ob, Oc cyclically.  Then X(21) is the radical center of Oa, Ob, Oc.  (Randy Hutson, April 9, 2016)
</p>
<p>
Let Ab, Ac, Bc, Ba, Ca, Cb be as in the construction of the Conway circle (see http://mathworld.wolfram.com/ConwayCircle.html).  Let Oa be the circumcircle of AAbAc, and define Ob, Oc cyclically.  X(21) is the radical center of Oa, Ob, Oc; see also X(8) and X(274).  (Randy Hutson, April 9, 2016)
</p>
<p>
Let A'B'C' be the excentral triangle.  X(21) is the radical center of the circles O(3,4) of triangles A'BC, B'CA, C'AB. (Randy Hutson, July 31 2018)
</p>
