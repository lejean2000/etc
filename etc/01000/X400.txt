<h3 id="X400">X(400)&nbsp;=&nbsp;YFF-MALFATTI POINT</h3>
 <a id="YffMalfattiPoint"></a>
Trilinears&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;csc<sup>4</sup>(A/4) :  csc<sup>4</sup>(B/4) : csc<sup>4</sup>(C/4)<br>
Barycentrics&nbsp;&nbsp;sin A csc<sup>4</sup>(A/4) : sin B csc<sup>4</sup>(B/4) : sin C csc<sup>4</sup>(C/4)
<p>
In 1997, Yff considered the configuration for the 1st Ajima-Malfatti
point, X(179). He proved that the same tangencies are possible in
another way if the circles are not required to lie inside ABC. With
tangency points labeled as before, the lines AA', BB', CC' concur in
X(400).  If you have The Geometer's Sketchpad, you can view
<a href="X(400).gsp" class="bold">X(400)</a>.
</p>
