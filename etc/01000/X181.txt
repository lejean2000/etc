<h3 id="X181">X(181)&nbsp;=&nbsp;APOLLONIUS POINT</h3>
 <a id="ApolloniusPoint"></a>Trilinears&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;a(b +
        c)<sup>2</sup>/(b + c - a) : b(c + a)<sup>2</sup>/(c + a - b) : c(a +
        b)<sup>2</sup>/(a + b - c)<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;=
        a<sup>2</sup>cos<sup>2</sup>(B/2 - C/2) :
        b<sup>2</sup>cos<sup>2</sup>(C/2 - A/2) :
        c<sup>2</sup>cos<sup>2</sup>(A/2 - B/2)
        <p>
Trilinears&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; h(a,b,c) :
        h(b,c,a) : h(c,a,b), where h(a,b,c) = [r cos(A/2) + s
        sin(A/2)]<sup>2</sup>, s = semiperimeter, r = inradius</p>
	<p>Barycentrics&nbsp;&nbsp;a<sup>3</sup>cos<sup>2</sup>(B/2 - C/2) :
        b<sup>3</sup>cos<sup>2</sup>(C/2 - A/2) :
        c<sup>3</sup>cos<sup>2</sup>(A/2 - B/2)</p>
	<p>Let O(A),O(B),O(C) be the excircles. Apollonius's Problem includes
        the construction of the circle O tangent to the three excircles and
        encompassing them. (The circle is called the Apollonius circle.) Let A'
        = O&cap;O(A), B'=O&cap;O(B), C'=O&cap;O(C). The lines AA',BB',CC'
        concur in X(181). Yff derived trilinears in 1992.</p>
	<p>X(181) is the external center of similitude (or exsimilicenter) of
        the incircle and Apollonius circle. The internal center is X(1682).
        (Peter J. C. Moses, 8/22/2003)</p>
	<p>X(181) is the isogonal conjugate of the isotomic conjugate of X(12);
        also, X(181) is the {X(i) ,X(j) }-harmonic conjugate of X(k) for these
        (i,j,k)): (31,51,3271), (42,1400,1402), (57,1401,1357), (57,1469,1401).
        (Peter J. C. Moses, 6/20/2014)</p>
	<p>A proof of the the concurrence of lines AA',BB',CC' follows.<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;A =
        exsimilicenter(incircle, A-excircle)<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;A' =
        exsimilicenter(A-excircle, Apollonius circle)<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Let J =
        exsimilicenter(incircle, Apollonius circle).<br>
By Monge's theorem, the points A, A', J are collinear. In particular, J
        lies on line AA', and cyclically, J lies on lines BB' and CC'.
        Therefore, J = X(181). (Darij Grinberg, Hyacinthos, 7461, 8/10/03)
</p>
<p>
Let A' be the inverse-in-excircles-radical-circle of the midpoint of BC, and define B', C' cyclically.  Triangle A'B'C' is inscribed in the Apollonius circle and homothetic to the intouch triangle at X(181).  (Randy Hutson, March 21, 2019)
</p>
