<h3 id="X593">X(593)&nbsp;=&nbsp;1st HATZIPOLAKIS-YIU POINT</h3>
Trilinears&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; a/(b +
        c)<sup>2</sup> : b/(c + a)<sup>2</sup> : c/(a + b)<sup>2</sup><br>
Barycentrics&nbsp;&nbsp;[a/(b + c)]<sup>2</sup> : [b/(c +
        a)]<sup>2</sup> : [c/(a + b)]<sup>2</sup>
	<p>Let O(A) be the circle tangent to line BC and to the circumcircle of
        triangle ABC at vertex A. Let A<sub>B</sub> and A<sub>C</sub> be where
        O(A) meets lines AB and AC, respectively. Let L(A) be the line joining
        A<sub>B</sub> and A<sub>C</sub>, and define L(B) and L(C) cyclically.
        Let A' be where L(B) and L(C) meet, and define B' and C' cyclically.
        Then triangle A'B'C' is homothetic to triangle ABC, and the center of
        homothety is X(593). See Antreas Hatzipolakis, Paul Yiu, Hyacinthos #2070.
</p>
<p>
X(593) lies on these lines: 2,261&nbsp;&nbsp; 31,110&nbsp;&nbsp;
        36,58&nbsp;&nbsp; 81,757&nbsp;&nbsp; 115,1029&nbsp;&nbsp; 229,1104</p>

X(593) = barycentric square of X(81)<br>
