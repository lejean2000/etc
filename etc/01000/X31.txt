<p>
Let A'B'C' be the circumsymmedial triangle.  Let A&Prime; be the trilinear product B'*C', and define B&Prime; and C&Prime; cyclically.  Then A&Prime;, B&Prime;, C&Prime; are collinear on line X(667)X(788) (the trilinear polar of X(31)).  The lines AA&Prime;, BB&Prime;, CC&Prime; concur in X(31).  (Randy Hutson, February 10, 2016)
</p>

<p>
Define the <b>1st Kenmotu diagonals triangle</b> as the triangle formed by the diagonals of the squares in the Kenmotu configuration with center X(371) that do not include X(371).  Define the <b>2nd Kenmotu diagonals triangle</b> as the triangle formed by the diagonals of the squares in the Kenmotu configuration with center X(372) that do not include X(372).  (Randy Hutson, February 10, 2016)
</p>
<p>
Let A<sub>1</sub>B<sub>1</sub>C<sub>1</sub> and A<sub>2</sub>B<sub>2</sub>C<sub>2</sub> be the 1st and 2nd Kenmotu diagonals triangles.  Let A' be the trilinear product A<sub>1</sub>*A<sub>2</sub>, and define B' and C' cyclically.  The lines AA', BB', CC' concur in X(31).   (Randy Hutson, February 10, 2016)
</p>
<p>
X(31) is the Brianchon point (perspector) of the inellipse that is the trilinear square of the Lemoine axis.  The center of the inellipse is X(16584).  (Randy Hutson, October 15, 2018)
