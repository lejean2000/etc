<h3 id="X351">X(351)&nbsp;=&nbsp;CENTER OF THE PARRY CIRCLE</h3>
Trilinears&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;f(a,b,c) : f(b,c,a) : f(c,a,b), where f(a,b,c) = a(b<sup>2</sup> - c<sup>2</sup>)(b<sup>2</sup> + c<sup>2</sup> - 2a<sup>2</sup>)

        <p>
X(351) is the center of the Parry circle introduced in TCCT (Art. 8.13) as the circle that passes through X(i)  for I = 2, 15, 16, 23, 110, 111, 352, 353.
</p>
<p>
Let O<sub>A</sub> be the circle centered at the A-vertex of the 1st anti-Parry triangle and passing through A; define O<sub>B</sub> and O<sub>C</sub> cyclically.  X(351) is the radical center of O<sub>A</sub>, O<sub>B</sub>, O<sub>C</sub>.  (Randy Hutson, August 30, 2020)
</p>
<p>
Let O<sub>A</sub> be the circle centered at the A-vertex of the 2nd anti-Parry triangle and passing through A; define O<sub>B</sub> and O<sub>C</sub> cyclically.  X(351) is the radical center of O<sub>A</sub>, O<sub>B</sub>, O<sub>C</sub>.  (Randy Hutson, August 30, 2020)
</p>
