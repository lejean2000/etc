<h3 id="X389">X(389)&nbsp;=&nbsp;CENTER OF THE TAYLOR CIRCLE</h3>
Trilinears &nbsp;&nbsp; cos A - cos 2A cos(B - C) : cos B - cos 2B cos(C - A) : cos C - cos 2C cos(A - B) : :
<br>
Trilinears &nbsp;&nbsp; cos A - sin A (tan A + tan B + tan C) : :
<br>
Trilinears &nbsp;&nbsp; cos A (cot A cot B cot C) - sin A : :
<br>
Barycentrics &nbsp;&nbsp; a[cos A - cos 2A cos(B - C)] : b[cos B - cos 2B cos(C - A)] : c[cos C - cos 2C cos(A - B)] : :
<br>
Barycentrics &nbsp;&nbsp; a^2[a^6(b^2 + c^2) - 3a^4(b^4 + c^4) + 3a^2(b^2 - c^2)^2(b^2 + c^2) - (b^2 - c^2)^2(b^4 + c^4)] : :
<p>
If ABC is acute then X(389) is the Spieker center of the orthic triangle. Peter Yff reports (Sept. 19, 2001) that since X(389) is on the Brocard axis, there must exist T for which X(389) is sin(A+T) : sin(B+T) : sin(C+T), and that tan(T) = - cot A cot B cot C.
</p>
<p>
Let H<sub>A</sub> be the A-altitude of triangle ABC, and let A' be the midpoint of segment AH<sub>A</sub>. Let L<sub>A</sub> be the line through A' parallel to AO, where O denotes the circumcenter. Define L<sub>B</sub> and L<sub>C</sub> cyclically. The lines L<sub>A</sub>, L<sub>B</sub>, L<sub>C</sub> concur in X(389). (Construction by Alexei Myakishev, March 24, 2010.)
</p>
<p>
Let O<sub>A</sub> be the circle with center A tangent to line BC, and define O<sub>B</sub> and O<sub>C</sub> cyclically. X(389) is the radical center of the three circles. (Randy Hutson, 9/23/2011)
</p>
<p>
Let A'B'C' be the orthic triangle, let A'' be the orthocenter of AB'C', and define B'' and C'' cyclically. Triangle A''B''C'' is the medial triangle of the circumorthic triangle. The triangle A''B''C'' is homothetic to A'B'C', and the center of homothety is X(389). (Randy Hutson, 9/23/2011)
</p>
<p>
Let A'B'C' be the orthic triangle.  Let Oa be the circle with center A tangent to line B'C', and define Ob and Oc cyclically.  The radical circle of Oa, Ob, Oc is the Taylor circle, which is also the Spieker radical circle of the orthic triangle if ABC is acute. (Randy Hutson, December 2, 2017)
</p>
<p>
Let A'B'C' be the orthic triangle.  X(389) is the radical center of the polar circles of AB'C', BC'A', CA'B'. (Randy Hutson, December 2, 2017)
</p>
<p>
A construction of X(389) is given at <a href="http://www.hyacinthos.epizy.com/message.php?msg=24162"> 24162</a>. (Antreas Hatzipolakis, August 29, 2016)
</p>
<p>
Let A'B'C' be the midheight triangle.  Let A<sub>B</sub>, A<sub>C</sub> be the orthogonal projections of A' on CA, AB, resp.  Define B<sub>C</sub>, B<sub>A</sub>, C<sub>A</sub>, C<sub>B</sub> cyclically.  Let A&Prime; = C<sub>A</sub>A<sub>C</sub>&cap;A<sub>B</sub>B<sub>A</sub>, and define B&Prime; and C&Prime; cyclically.  Triangle A&Prime;B&Prime;C&Prime; is homothetic to ABC at X(6).  The lines A'A&Prime;, B'B&Prime;, C'C&Prime; concur in X(389), which is also X(3)-of-A&Prime;B&Prime;C&Prime;.  (Randy Hutson, March 29, 2020)
</p>
