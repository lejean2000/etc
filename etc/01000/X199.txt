<p>
As a point on the Euler line, X(199) has Shinagawa coefficients (E + 2F + $bc$, -2E - 2F + $bc$).
</p>
<p>
Let I<sub>A</sub>, I<sub>B</sub>, I<sub>C</sub> be the excenters.  Let (O<sub>A</sub>) be the circle tangent to the circumcircle at A and passing through I<sub>A</sub>.  Let A' be the antipode of A in (O<sub>A</sub>).  Let L<sub>A</sub> be the tangent to (O<sub>A</sub>) at A'.  Define L<sub>B</sub> and L<sub>C</sub> cyclically.  Let T<sub>A</sub> = L<sub>B</sub>&cap;L<sub>C</sub>, and define T<sub>B</sub> and T<sub>C</sub> cyclically.  Triangle T<sub>A</sub>T<sub>B</sub>T<sub>C</sub> is homothetic to the tangential triangle at X(199).  (Randy Hutson, June 7, 2019)
</p>
