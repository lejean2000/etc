Trilinears &nbsp;&nbsp; bc/(b + c - 2a) : ca/(c + a - 2b) : ab/(a + b - 2c) : :
<br>
Barycentrics &nbsp;&nbsp; 1/(b + c - 2a) : 1/(c + a - 2b) : 1/(a + b - 2c) (Darij Grinberg, 12/28/02)
<p>
James Blaikie (1847-1929) proposed the following problem. Let O be any point in the plane of triangle ABC, and let any straight line g through O meet BC in P, CA in Q, AB in R; then, if points P', Q', R' be taken on the line so that
PO = OP', QO = OQ', RO = OR'.  Prove that AP', BQ', CR' concur.
</p>
<p>
Darij Grinberg introduces the term <i>Blaikie point of O and g</i> for the point Z of concurrence. If
</p>
<p>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;O
        = x : y : z and g = [k : l : m] (barycentric coordinates),
</p>
<p>
then Z has first barycentric 1/[k(y-z) - (ly-mz)]. Given a point S = u : v : w, Grinberg then defines the <i>S-Blaikie transform of O</i> as the Blaikie point of O and OS. The first barycentric of Z can be written as
</p>
<p>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;1/[yw(y+x)         + zv(z+x) - yz(2u+v+w)].
</p>
