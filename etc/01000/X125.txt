<h3 id="X125">X(125)&nbsp;=&nbsp;CENTER OF JERABEK HYPERBOLA<a id="CenterOfJerabekHyperbola"></a></h3>
Trilinears &nbsp;&nbsp; cos A sin<sup>2</sup>(B - C) : cos B sin<sup>2</sup>(C - A) : cos C sin<sup>2</sup>(A - B)
<br>

<p>
<b>Roland H. Eddy and R. Fritsch,</b> "The conics of Ludwig Kiepert:  a comprehensive lesson in the geometry of the triangle," <i>Mathematics Magazine</i> 67 (1994) 188-205.</p>
<p>
X(125) is the pole of the Fermat axis with respect to the Dao-Moses-Telv circle.  (Randy Hutson, December 14, 2014)
</p>
<p>
Let La, Lb, Lc be the lines through A, B, C, resp. parallel to the Euler line.  Let Ma, Mb, Mc be the reflections of BC, CA, AB in La, Lb, Lc, resp.  Let A' = Mb&cap;Mc, and define B', C' cyclically.  Triangle A'B'C' is inversely similar to, and 3 times the size of, ABC.  Let A&Prime;B&Prime;C&Prime; be the reflection of A'B'C' in the Euler line.  The triangle A&Prime;B&Prime;C&Prime; is homothetic to ABC, with center of homothety X(125); see Hyacinthos #16741/16782, Sep 2008.  (Randy Hutson, March 25, 2016)
</p>
<p>
Let A'B'C' be the orthic triangle.  The Euler lines of AB'C', BC'A', CA'B' concur in X(125).  (Randy Hutson, March 25, 2016)
</p>
