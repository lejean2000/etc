<h3 id="X508">X(508)&nbsp;=&nbsp;6th ISOSCELIZER POINT</h3>
Trilinears&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;a<sup>-1/2</sup>sec(A/2) : b<sup>-1/2</sup>sec(B/2) : c<sup>-1/2</sup>sec(C/2)
<br>
Barycentrics&nbsp;&nbsp;a<sup>1/2</sup>sec(A/2) : b<sup>1/2</sup>sec(B/2) : c<sup>1/2</sup>sec(C/2)
<p>
The isoscelizer equations</p>
	<p></p>
a[area of T(A,X)] = b[area of T(B,X)] = c[area of T(C,X)]
<p>have solution X = X(508). (Peter Yff, 4/9/99)
</p>
<p>
If you have The Geometer's Sketchpad, you can view <a href="X(508).gsp" class="bold">X(508)</a>.
</p>
<p>
X(508) = trilinear product X(366)*X(174)<br>
X(508) = trilinear square root of X(85)<br>
X(508) = barycentric square root of X(7)<br>
</p>
