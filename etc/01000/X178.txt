<h3 id="X178">X(178)&nbsp;=&nbsp;2nd MID-ARC POINT<a id="SecondMidArcPoint"></a></h3>
Trilinears&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;(cos B/2 + cos C/2) csc A : (cos C/2 + cos A/2) csc B : (cos A/2 + cos B/2) csc C
<br>
Barycentrics&nbsp;&nbsp;cos B/2 + cos C/2 : cos C/2 + cos A/2 : cos A/2 + cos B/2
<p>
Let A',B',C' be the first points of intersection of the angle bisectors of ABC with its incircle. Let A&Prime;,B&Prime;,C&Prime; be the midpoints of         segments BC,CA,AB, respectively. The lines A'A&Prime;,B'B&Prime;,C'C&Prime; concur in X(178).
</p>
<p>
<b>Clark Kimberling,</b> Problem 804, <i>Nieuw Archikef voor Wiskunde</i> 6 (1988) 170.
</p>
<p>
X(178) lies on these lines: 2,188&nbsp;&nbsp; 8,236
</p>
<p>
X(178) = complement of X(188)<br>
X(178) = crosspoint of X(2) and X(508)</p>
