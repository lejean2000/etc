        parallel to the Euler line, and define M and N cyclically. Let L' be

<p>
Let A'B'C' be the orthic triangle. Let N<sub>A</sub> be the orthic
        axis of AB'C', and define N<sub>B</sub> and N<sub>C</sub> cyclically.
        Let A'' = N<sub>B</sub>&cap;N<sub>C</sub>, B'' =
        N<sub>C</sub>&cap;A<sub>C</sub>, C'' = N<sub>A</sub>&cap;B<sub>C</sub>.

        center X(6), and the lines A'A'', B'B'', C'C'' concur in X(113). Also,
        X(113) = X(3)-of-A''B''C''. (Randy Hutson, August 26, 2014)
