Let P be a point on the circumcircle, and let L<sub>P</sub> be its Steiner line.  The locus of the orthopole of L<sub>P</sub>, as P varies, is an ellipse with center X(4) and perspector X(66).  (Randy Hutson, March 29, 2020)
</p>
<p>
In the plane of a triangle ABC, let<br>
A'B'C' = anticomplementary triangle;<br>
Oa = circle with diameter BC, and define Ob and Oc cyclically;<br>
Ab = A'BC'&cap;Oa, and define Bc and Ca cyclically;<br>
Ac = B'CA'&cap;Oa, and define Ba and Cb cyclically;<br>
A&Prime;= BcBa&cap;CaCb, and define B&Prime; and C&Prime; cyclically.<br>
The triangle A&Prime;B&Prime;C&Prime; is perspective to ABC, and the perspector is X(66).<br>
(Dasari Naga Vijay Krishna, April 15, 2021)
</p>
