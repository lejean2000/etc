<h3 id="X933">X(933)&nbsp;=&nbsp;X(4)-CROSS CONJUGATE OF X(250)</h3>
Trilinears &nbsp;&nbsp; 1/(bw - cv) : : , where u : v : w = X(54)
<br>
Trilinears &nbsp;&nbsp; sec A csc(2B - 2C) : :
<br>
Trilinears &nbsp;&nbsp; a/[(b^2 - c^2)(b^2 + c^2 - a^2)(b^4 + c^4 - a^2b^2 - a^2c^2 - 2b^2c^2)] : :
<p>
Let A' = BC&cap;X(186)X(523), and define B' and C' cyclically.  The circumcircles of AB'C', BC'A', CA'B' concur in X(933).  Note: Line X(186)X(523) is the polar of X(4) wrt the circumcircle.  (Randy Hutson, February 10, 2016)
</p>
<p>
Let P be a point on the circumcircle, and let
</p>
<p>
(Oab) = circle through A tangent to line BC at B<br>
(Oac) = circle through A tangent to line BC at C<br>
Ba = point other than A where the line PB meets (Oab)<br>
Ca = point other than A where the line PC meets (Oac)<br>
Hab = orthocenter of APBa<br>
Hac = orthocenter of APCa<br>
</p>
<p>
Define Hbc and Hca cyclically, and define Hba and Hcb cyclically. The points Hab, Hac, Hbc, Hba, Hca, Hcb lies on a hyperbola with center = midpoint(P and H), where H = X(4), the orthocenter. This hyperbola is rectangular if and only if P = X(933). This note is related to a problem posed in <a href="https://artofproblemsolving.com/community/c6t48f6h2603674_collinear_orthocenters_wanted"
><b>2017 Olympiad of Rusanovsky Lyceum in Kyiv, Ukraine , IX-X Team p9</b></a>.  For details, see
<a href="https://amontes.webs.ull.es/otrashtm/HGT2021.htm#HG300621"><b>El centro X(933) y una hip&eacute;rbola rectangular</b></a>.  (Angel Montesdeoca, July 3, 2021)
</p>
