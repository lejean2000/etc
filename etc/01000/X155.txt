<p>

<p>
X(155) = X(4)-of-tangential-triangle. This point is also the center of the circle which cuts (extended) lines BC, CA, AB in pairs of points A' and A&Prime;, B' and B&Prime;, C' and C&Prime;, respectively, such that angles A'AA&Prime;, B'BB&Prime;, C'CC&Prime; are all right angles. This is the <b>Dou circle,</b> described in <b>Jordi Dou,</b> Problem 1140, <i>Crux Mathematicorum,</i> 28 (2002) 461-462.
</p>
