<h3 id="X359">X(359)&nbsp;=&nbsp;HOFSTADTER ONE POINT</h3>
<a id="HofstadterOnePoint"></a>
Trilinears &nbsp;&nbsp; a/A : b/B : c/C
<br>
Barycentrics &nbsp;&nbsp; a<sup>2</sup>/A : b<sup>2</sup>/B :  c<sup>2</sup>/C
<p>
This point is the limit as r approaches 1 of the Hofstadter r point.  See X(360) for details.
</p>
<p>
If you have The Geometer's Sketchpad, you can view <a href="X(359)&X(360).gsp" class="bold">X(359) and X(360)</a> and <a href="HofstadterTriangles.gsp" class="bold">Hofstadter Triangles</a>. These sketches include the Hofstadter ellipse (actually a family of ellipses, indexed by a parameter r) introduced (February 4,        2005) by Peter J. C. Moses. The ellipse E(r) is given for 0 &lt; r &lt; 1 by the following equation in trilinears:
</p>
<p>
x<sup>2</sup> + y<sup>2</sup> + z<sup>2</sup> + yz(D + 1/D) + zx(E + 1/E) + xy(F + 1/F) = 0, where D = cos A - sin A cot rA, E = cos B - sin B cot rB, F = cos C - sin C cot rC.
</p>
<p>
The Hofstadter ellipse E(1/2), given by x<sup>2</sup> + y<sup>2</sup> + z<sup>2</sup> - 2yz - 2xz - 2xy = 0, has center X(37) and passes through X(i)  for these i: 244, 678, 2310, 2632, 2638, 2643, 3248, 4094, 4117, 10501, 23063, 24012.
</p>
<p>
Taking the limit of E(r) as r tends to 0 gives information about the circumellipse, E(0) (which is also E(1)):
</p>
<p>
Equation:&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; ayz/A + bzx/B + cxy/C = 0<br>
Center:&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; X(5945) = (a/A)(b<sup>2</sup>/B + c<sup>2</sup>/C - a<sup>2</sup>/A) : (b/B)(c<sup>2</sup>/C + a<sup>2</sup>/A - b<sup>2</sup>/B) : (c/C)(a<sup>2</sup>/A + b<sup>2</sup>/B - c<sup>2</sup>/C)
<br>
Intersection with circumcircle (other than A, B, C):&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; X(3067) = a/[A(B - C)] : b/[B(C - A)] : c/[C(A - B)]
</p>
<p>
The Hofstadter ellipse E(1/2) is also the incentral inellipse, centered at X(37), and touching ABC at the traces of X(1).  The ellipse also passes through X(4094), and is the incentral isotomic conjugate of line X(512)X(4895).  (Randy Hutson, October 15, 2018)
</p>
