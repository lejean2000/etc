<h3 id="X278">X(278)&nbsp;=&nbsp;ISOGONAL CONJUGATE OF X(219)</h3>

<p>
Let A'B'C' be the extouch triangle.  Let A&Prime; be the pole, wrt the polar circle, of line B'C', and define B&Prime; and C&Prime; cyclically.  The lines AA&Prime;, BB&Prime;, CC&Prime; concur in X(278).  (Randy Hutson, September 14, 2016)
</p>
<p>
Let A'B'C' be the 2nd extouch triangle.  Let A&Prime; be the pole, wrt the polar circle, of line B'C', and define B&Prime; and C&Prime; cyclically.  The triangle A'B'C' is homothetic to ABC at X(278).  (Randy Hutson, September 14, 2016)
</p>
<p>
Let Ha be the hyperbola passing through A, and with foci at B and C.  This is called the A-Soddy hyperbola in (Paul Yiu, Introduction to the Geometry of the Triangle, 2002; 12.4 The Soddy hyperbolas, p. 143).  Let La be the polar of X(3) with respect to Ha.  Define Lb and Lc cyclically.  Let A' = Lb&cap;Lc, B' = Lc&cap;La, C' = La&cap;Lb.  Triangle A'B'C' is homothetic to ABC, and the center of homothety is X(278).  (Randy Hutson, September 14, 2016)
</p>
