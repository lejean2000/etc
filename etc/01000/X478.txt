<h3 id="X478">X(478)&nbsp;=&nbsp;CENTER OF YIU CONIC</h3>
<a id="CenterOfYiuConic"></a>
Trilinears&nbsp;&nbsp; a[a<sup>4</sup> - 2abc(b + c - a) - (b<sup>2</sup> - c<sup>2</sup>)<sup>2</sup>]/(b + c - a) : :
<br>
Barycentrics &nbsp;&nbsp; a^2*(a + b - c)*(a - b + c)*(a^4 - b^4 + 2*a^2*b*c - 2*a*b^2*c - 2*a*b*c^2 + 2*b^2*c^2 - c^4)
<p>
Center of the Yiu conic, which passes through the points outside the circumcircle at which the excircles of ABC are tangent to the sidelines of ABC. See Paul Yiu's
</p>
<p>	<a href="http://www.math.fau.edu/yiu/clawson.pdf">The Clawson point and excircles</a>.
</p>
<p>
</p>
<p>
Let Ea be the ellipse through A having foci B and C, and define Eb and Ec cyclically. The minor vertices, A<sub>1</sub>,
A<sub>2</sub>; B<sub>1</sub>, B<sub>2</sub>; C<sub>1</sub>, C<sub>2</sub> are the touchpoints of the excircle with sidelines external to the circumcircle; i.e., they lie on the Yiu conic, centered at X(478). See
<a href="https://user-images.githubusercontent.com/16231062/145788967-e1c21905-87e2-4eef-8990-a7589e9f0d04.png"><b>Figure</b></a>.
If ABC is a right triangle, then the Yiu conic is a pair of lines meeting in X(478).

(Dan Reznik, December 13, 2021)
</p>
