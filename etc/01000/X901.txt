<h3 id="X901">X(901)&nbsp;=&nbsp;ISOGONAL CONJUGATE OF X(900)</h3>
Trilinears &nbsp;&nbsp; a/[(b - c)(b + c - 2a)] : :
<p>
Let L<sub>A</sub> be the line of reflection of line X(1)X(5) in line BC, and define L<sub>B</sub> and L<sub>C</sub> cyclically. Let A' = L<sub>B</sub>&cap;L<sub>C</sub>, and define B' and C' cyclically. The         lines AA', BB', CC' concur in X(901). (Randy Hutson, 9/23/2011)
</p>
<p>
</p>
<p>
Let La, Lb, Lc be the lines through A, B, C, resp. parallel to the antiorthic axis.  Let Ma, Mb, Mc be the reflections of BC, CA, AB in La, Lb, Lc, resp.  Let A' = Mb&cap;Mc, and define B' and C' cyclically.  Triangle A'B'C' is inversely similar to, and 3 times the size of, ABC.  The centroid of A'B'C' is X(901); see Hyacinthos #16741/16782, Sep 2008.  (Randy Hutson, March 25, 2016)
</p>
<p>
Let A', B', C' be the intersections of the Nagel line and lines BC, CA, AB, resp.  The circumcircles of AB'C', BC'A', CA'B' concur in X(901).   (Randy Hutson, March 25, 2016)
</p>
