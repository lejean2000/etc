<h3 id="X600">X(600)&nbsp;=&nbsp;3rd HATZIPOLAKIS-YIU POINT</h3>
Barycentrics&nbsp;&nbsp; f(a,b,c) : f(b,c,a) : f(c,a,b),
        where f(a,b,c) = a<sup>2</sup>(bc + 2S)[abc(b + c - a) +
        2(b<sup>2</sup> + c<sup>2</sup> - a<sup>2</sup>)S]

        <p>Let O<sub>A</sub> be the circle with center A and radius R, the
        circumradius of triangle ABC. Let B<sub>A</sub> be the point where
        O<sub>A</sub> meets line AB farthest from B. Define C<sub>B</sub> and
        A<sub>C</sub> cyclically. Let C<sub>A</sub> be the point where
        O<sub>A</sub> meets line AC farthest from C. Define A<sub>B</sub> and
        B<sub>C</sub> cyclically. X(600) is the radical center of the circles
        AB<sub>A</sub>C<sub>A</sub>, BC<sub>B</sub>A<sub>B</sub>,
        CA<sub>C</sub>B<sub>C</sub>. If "farthest from" is changed to "nearest
        to" in the construction, the resulting point is X(5507). (Antreas
        Hatzipolakis, Paul Yiu, 1/5/01, Hyacinthos #2344, #2346-8; for a
        correction in coordinates, see Paul Yiu, Adgeom #202.)</p>

<a href="X(600).gsp" class="bold">X(600)</a>.
</p>
<p>
X(600) lies on this line: 4640,5507.
