<h3 id="X177">X(177)&nbsp;=&nbsp;1st MID-ARC POINT<a id="FirstMidArcPoint"></a></h3>
Trilinears &nbsp;&nbsp; (cos B/2 + cos C/2) sec A/2 : (cos C/2 + cos A/2) sec B/2 : (cos A/2 + cos B/2) sec C/2
<br>
Trilinears &nbsp;&nbsp; (b' + c')/a' : :, where a', b', c' are sidelengths of excentral triangle
<br>
Barycentrics &nbsp;&nbsp; (sin A)(cos B/2 + cos C/2) sec A/2 : :
<p>
Let A',B',C' be the first points of intersection of the angle
        bisectors of ABC with its incircle. The tangents at A', B', C' form a
        triangle A&Prime;B&Prime;C&Prime;, and the lines AA&Prime;,BB&Prime;,CC&Prime; concur in X(177). Also,
        X(177) = X(1) of the intouch triangle.</p>
<p><b>Clark Kimberling and G. R. Veldkamp,</b> Problem 1160 and
        Solution, <i>Crux Mathematicorum</i> 13 (1987) 298-299 [proposed
        1986].</p>
<p>X(177) is the perspector of ABC and the Yff central triangle, and
        X(177) is X(65)-of-the-Yff-central-triangle . (Darij Grinberg,
        Hyacinthos #7689, 8/25/2003)</p>
