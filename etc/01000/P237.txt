<p>
Centers X(237)-X(248) are line conjugates. (The P-line conjugate of Q is the point where line PQ meets the trilinear polar of the isogonal conjugate of Q.)
</p>
<p>
See Clark Kimberling and Peter J. C. Moses, <a href="https://trebuchet.public.springernature.app/get_content/9bf860cd-18a5-4f8a-a5b0-90acefaf051b"> <b>Line conjugates in the plane of a triangle,</b></a> <i>Aequationes Mathematicae</i>, August 2022. 
</p>
