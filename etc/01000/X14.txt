<p>
Construct the equilateral triangle BA'C having base BC and vertex A' on the positive side of BC; similarly construct equilateral triangles CB'A and AC'B based on the other two sides. The lines AA',BB',CC' concur in X(14). The antipedal triangle of X(14) is equilateral.</p>

<p>
Let NaNbNc, Na'Nb'Nc' be the outer and inner Napoleon triangles, resp.  Let A' be the isogonal conjugate of Na, wrt Na'Nb'Nc', and define B' and C' cyclically. The lines Na'A', Nb'B', Nc'C' concur in X(14). (Randy Hutson, January 29, 2015)
</p>
<p>
Let A'B'C' be the outer Napoleon triangle and A&Prime;B&Prime;C&Prime; the inner Napoleon triangle.  Let A* be the isogonal conjugate, wrt A&Prime;B&Prime;C&Prime;, of A', and define B* and C* cyclically. The lines A&Prime;A*, B&Prime;B*, C&Prime;C* concur in X(14). (Randy Hutson, December 2, 2017)
</p>
