<b>
X(187) = X(15) + X(16)	</b><p>
Let L denote the line having trilinears of X(187) as coefficients. Then L is the line passing through X(2) perpendicular to the Euler line.
</p>
<p>
Let A'B'C' be the 1st Brocard triangle.  Let A&Prime;B&Prime;C&Prime; be the 2nd Brocard triangle.  Let A* = &Lambda;((A',A&Prime;), and define B* and C* cyclically. The lines AA*, BB*, CC* concur in X(187).  (Randy Hutson, December 26, 2015)
</p>
<p>
Let (O<sub>A</sub>) be the circumcircle of BCX(2).  Let P<sub>A</sub> be the perspector of (O<sub>A</sub>).  Let L<sub>A</sub> be the polar of P<sub>A</sub> wrt (O<sub>A</sub>).  Define L<sub>B</sub> and L<sub>C</sub> cyclically.  Let A' = L<sub>B</sub>&cap;L<sub>C</sub>, and define B' and C' cyclically.  The lines AA', BB', CC' concur in X(187).  (Randy Hutson, June 7, 2019)
</p>
