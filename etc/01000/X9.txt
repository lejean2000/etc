<p>
Let E be the circumellipse of T = ABC with center X(9).  Then ABC is a billiard orbit of E (3-periodic). If we fix E in the plane, all its triangular orbits (a set of "rotating" triangles T) have the same X(9). Note that X(9) is the point of concurrence of lines drawn from each excenter to the midpoint of the corresponding side of T. (Dan Reznik, June 30, 2019)  See [1] <a href="https://youtu.be/tMrBqfRBYik"><b>Triangular Orbits in Elliptic Billiards: the Mittenpunkt X(9) is stationary at the origin</b></a> and [2] <a href="https://dan-reznik.github.io/Elliptical-Billiards-Triangular-Orbits/"><b>Triangular Orbits in Elliptic Billiards: the Mittenpunkt X(9) is stationary at the origin</b></a>.

<br>
Let  g = length of semi-major axis of E; then g = Sqrt[(2 R (r + R + Sqrt[R(R - 2 r)]) s^2)/(r + 4 R)^2].
<br>
Let h = length of semi-minor axis of E: then h = Sqrt[(2 R (r + R - Sqrt[R(R - 2 r)]) s^2)/(r + 4 R)^2].
<br>
h/g = Sqrt[(OI - R) (OI + 3 R) / ((OI - 3 R) (OI + R))] = Sqrt[(r + R - Sqrt[R (R - 2 r)]) / (r + R + Sqrt[R (R - 2 r)])].
<br>
eccentricity of E: 2 Sqrt[OI R / ((3 R - OI) (OI + R))].
<br>
If F is a focus of E, then |FX(9)|^2 = 4 s^2 R Sqrt[R (R - 2 r)] / (r + 4 R)^2/.
<br>
The axes of E are the asymptotes of the Feuerbach hyperbola.
<br>
The area of E is &pi; R S /(r (r + 4 R))^(3/2) = 4 &pi; a b c / ( 2 b c + 2 c a + 2 a b - a^2 - b^2 - c^2 )^(3/2) * area(ABC).
<br>
The triangle tangent to the vertices of E is the excentral triangle.
