<h3 id="X385">X(385)&nbsp;=&nbsp;X(2)-HIRST INVERSE OF X(6)</h3>
Trilinears &nbsp;&nbsp; bc(a<sup>4</sup> - b<sup>2</sup>c<sup>2</sup>) : :
<br>
Barycentrics &nbsp;&nbsp; cot B + cot C - cot A - tan &omega; : :
<p>
Contributed by John Horton Conway, 1998.
</p>
<p>
X(385) is the perspector of triangle ABC and the tangential triangle
        of the conic that passes through A, B, C, and the pair P(1) and U(1) of
        bicentric points (see the notes just before X(1908). (Randy Hutson,
        9/23/2011)
</p>
<p>
Let A'B'C' be the 1st Brocard triangle.  Let A&Prime; be the trilinear pole of line B'C', and define B&Prime; and C&Prime; cyclically.  The lines AA&Prime;, BB&Prime;, CC&Prime; concur in X(385).  (Randy Hutson, November 30, 2015)
</p>
<p>
Let La be the line through A parallel to the Lemoine axis, and define Lb and Lc cyclically.  Let Ma be the reflection of BC in La, and define Mb and Mc cyclically.
Let A' = Mb&cap;Mc, and define B', C' cyclically.  Triangle A'B'C' is inversely similar to, and 3 times the size of, ABC.  Let A&Prime;B&Prime;C&Prime; be the reflection of A'B'C' in the Lemoine axis.  The triangle A&Prime;B&Prime;C&Prime; is homothetic to ABC, with center of homothety X(325) and centroid X(385); see Hyacinthos #16741/16782, Sep 2008.  (Randy Hutson, November 30, 2015)
</p>
