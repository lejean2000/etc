<p>
Let A' be the apex of the isosceles triangle BA'C constructed inward on BC such that &ang;A'BC = &ang;A'CB = &omega;.  Define B' nad C' cyclically.  Let Ha be the orthocenter of BA'C, and define Hb and Hc cyclically.  The lines AHa, BHb, CHc concur in X(98).  (Randy Hutson, July 20, 2016)

<p>

<p>

Triangle A&Prime;B&Prime;C&Prime; is inversely similar to ABC, with similicenter X(2).  The lines AA&Prime;, BB&Prime;, CC&Prime; concur in X(98), which is X(4)-of-A&Prime;B&Prime;C&Prime;. (Randy Hutson, July 31 2018)

<p>

The locus of Q as P varies is an ellipse similar and orthogonal to the Steiner circumellipse, and also centered at X(2).  When P = X(671), Q = X(98).  See also X(6054) and X(24808).  (Randy Hutson, October 15, 2018)
