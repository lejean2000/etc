<b>
X(37) = (r<sup>2</sup> + 2rR - s<sup>2</sup>)*X(1) - 6rR*X(2) - 2r<sup>2</sup>*X(3)&nbsp;&nbsp; (Peter Moses, April 2, 2013)

<p>
Let A'B'C' be the cevian triangle of X(1). Let A&Prime; be the centroid of
        triangle AB'C', and define B&Prime; and C&Prime; cyclically. Then the lines AA&Prime;,
        BB&Prime;, CC&Prime; concur in X(37). (Eric Danneels, Hyacinthos 7892, 9/13/03)
</p>
<p>
A simple construction of X(37) as a crosspoint can be generalized as
        follows: let DEF be the medial triangle of ABC and let A'B'C' be the
        cevian triangle of a point U other than the centroid, X(2). The
        crosspoint of X(2) and U is then the point of concurrence of lines
        LD,ME,NF, where L,M,N are the respective midpoints of AA', BB', CC'. If
        U=u : v : w (trilinears), then crosspoint(X(2),U) = b/w+c/v : c/u+a/w :
        a/v+b/u, assuming that uvw is nonzero. In particular, if U=X(1), then
        the crosspoint is X(37). (Seiichi Kirikami, July 10, 2011)
</p>
<p>
X(37) = perspector of ABC and the medial triangle of the incentral
        triangle of ABC. (Randy Hutson, August 23, 2011)
</p>
<p>
X(37) = center of the Hofstadter ellipse E(1/2); see X(359). This is the incentral inellipse, which is the trilinear square of the antiorthic axis.  (Randy Hutson, August 9, 2014)
</p>
<p>
Let A' be the trilinear pole of the tangent to the Apollonius circle where it meets the A-excircle, and define B' and C' cyclically.  The triangle A'B'C' is homothetic to ABC at X(37).   (Randy Hutson, April 9, 2016)
</p>
<p>
Let Oa be the A-extraversion of the Conway circle  (the circle centered at the A-excenter and passing through A, with radius sqrt(r<sub>a</sub>^2 + s^2), where r<sub>a</sub> is the A-exradius).  Let La be the radical axis of the circumcircle and Oa.  Let A' = Lb&cap;Lc, B' = Lc&cap;La, C' = La&cap;Lb.  The lines AA', BB', CC' concur in X(37).   (Randy Hutson, April 9, 2016)
</p>
