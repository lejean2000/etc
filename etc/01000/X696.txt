Trilinears&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; f(a,b,c)
        : f(b,c,a) : f(c,a,b), where f(a,b,c) = a <sup>-5</sup>(b <sup>-3</sup>
        + c <sup>-3</sup>) - a <sup>-4</sup>(b <sup>-4</sup> + c
        <sup>-4</sup>)<br>
Barycentrics&nbsp;&nbsp;g(a,b,c) : g(b,c,a) : g(c,a,b), where g(a,b,c)
        = af(a,b,c)
        <p>As the isogonal conjugate of a point on the circumcircle, X(696)
        lies on the line at infinity. The first trilinear coordinate has the
        form</p>
<p>a<sup>m-1</sup>(b<sup>n</sup> + c<sup>n</sup>) -
        a<sup>n-1</sup>(b<sup>m</sup> + c<sup>m</sup>).</p>
<p>If m and n are distinct integers, this form fits the definition of
        <b>even</b> polynomial center as in Clark Kimberling, "Functional
        equations associated with triangle geometry," <i>Aequationes
        Mathematicae</i> 45 (1993) 127-152. This form, perhaps appearing
        initially here (July 7, 2001) defines a triangle center for arbitrary
        distinct real numbers m and n. Selected even infinity and circumcircle
        points begin at X(696); odd ones begin at X(768).</p>
	<p>Certain points of this type occur prior to this section. They are as
