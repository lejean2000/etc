<p>
X(962) is shown in <b>Michael S. Longuet-Higgins,</b> "On the principal centers of a triangle," <i>Elemente der Mathematik</i> 56 (2001) 122-129,  to complete a simple pattern of collinearities.
</p>
<p>
Let I = X(1) and H = X(4) in a triangle ABC. Let L<sub>b</sub> be the perpendicular to B at I, and let I<sub>c</sub> be the perpendicular to CI at I.  Let A<sub>b</sub> = L<sub>b</sub>&cap;AH and A<sub>c</sub> = L<sub>c</sub>&cap;AH. Let U be the circle {I, A<sub>b</sub>, A<sub>c</sub>}}. Let M<sub>b</sub> be the point, other than I, where BI meets U, and let M<sub>c</sub> be the point, other than I, where CI meets U.  Let &ell;<sub>a</sub> be the line M<sub>b</sub>M<sub>c</sub>, and define &ell;<sub>b</sub> and &ell;<sub>c</sub> cyclically.  The lines &ell;<sub>a</sub>,  &ell;<sub>b</sub>, &ell;<sub>c</sub> concur in X(962).  (Angel Montesdeoca, March 12, 2020)
</p>
