<p>
X(184) is the homothetic center of triangles ABC and A'B'C', the
        latter defined as follows: let B<sub>1</sub> and C<sub>1</sub> be the
        points where the perpendicular bisector BC meets sidelines CA and AB,
        and cyclically define C<sub>2</sub>, A<sub>2</sub>; A<sub>3</sub>,
        B<sub>3</sub>. Then A'B'C' is formed by the perpendicular bisectors of
        segments B<sub>1</sub>C<sub>1</sub>, C<sub>2</sub>A<sub>2</sub>,
        A<sub>3</sub>B<sub>3</sub>. (Fred Lang, Hyacinthos #1190)
</p>
<p>
X(184) is the subject of Hyacinthos messages 5423-5441 (May, 2002).
        In #5423, Alexei Myakishev notes that X(184) serves as a common vertex
        of three triangles inside ABC, mutually congruent and similar to ABC.
        (The triangles can be labeled XB<sub>C</sub>C<sub>B</sub>,
        XC<sub>A</sub>A<sub>C</sub>, XA<sub>B</sub>B<sub>A</sub>, with
        B<sub>C</sub> and C<sub>B</sub> on side BC, C<sub>A</sub> and
        A<sub>C</sub> on side CA, and A<sub>B</sub> and B<sub>A</sub> on side
        AB.) See 	<p class="bold">Alexei Myakishev,
        <a href="http://forumgeom.fau.edu/FG2003volume3/FG200302.pdf" class="bold">On the Procircumcenter and Related Points</a>,
        <i>Forum Geometricorum</i> 3 (2003) 29-34.</p>

        <p>In #5435, Paul Yiu cites Fred Lang's construction of X(184) and
        notes that the three triangles are then easily constructed from X(184).
        The triangles determine three other triangles with common vertex
        X(184); in #5437, Nikos Dergiades notes that the vertex angles of these
        are 4A - &pi;, 4B - &pi;, 4C - &pi;, and that
</p>

X(184) = homothetic center of the orthic triangle and the medial triangle of the tangential triangle.

<p>
Let A'B'C' be the intersections, other than X(3), of the X(3)-cevians and the Brocard circle.  Let A&Prime;B&Prime;C&Prime; be the intersections, other than X(6), of the X(6)-cevians and the Brocard circle.  Then A'B'C' and A&Prime;B&Prime;C&Prime; are perspective at X(184).  Also, X(184) = U&cap;V, where U = isotomic conjugate of polar conjugate of Brocard axis (i.e., line X(3)X(49)), and V = polar conjugate of isotomic conjugate of Brocard axis (i.e., line X(6)X(25)).  Let DEF be the orthic triangle.  Let D' be the isotomic conjugate of X(4) wrt AEF, and define E' and F' cyclically; then the lines AD', BE', CF' concur in X(184).  (Randy Hutson, June 1, 2015)
</p>
<p>
Let A'B'C' be the triangle described in ADGEOM #2697 (8/26/2015, Tran Quang Hung).  A'B'C' is homothetic to ABC at X(184).
</p>
<p>
In the plane of a triangle ABC, let<br>
A'B'C' = tangential triangle;<br>
Oa = circle with diameter BC, and define Ob and Oc cyclically;<br>
Ab = A'BC'&cap;Oa, and define Bc and Ca cyclically;<br>
Ac = B'CA'&cap;Oa, and define Ba and Cb cyclically;<br>
A&Prime;= BcBa;&cap;CaCb, define B&Prime; and C&Prime; cyclically.<br>
The triangle A&Prime;B&Prime;C&Prime; is perspective to ABC, and the perspector is X(184).<br>
(Dasari Naga Vijay Krishna, April 15, 2021)
</p>
