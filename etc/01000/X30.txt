<p>
As a point on the Euler line, X(30) has Shinagawa coefficients (1, -3).
</p>
<p>
Let A'B'C' be the reflection triangle.  Let A&Prime; be the cevapoint of B' and C', and define B&Prime; and C&Prime; cyclically.         The lines AA&Prime;, BB&Prime;, CC&Prime; are parallel to the Euler line, and therefore concur in X(30).    (Randy Hutson, December 10, 2016)
</p>
<p>
X(30) is the point of intersection of the Euler line and the line at infinity. Thus, each of the lines listed below is parallel  to the Euler line.
</p>
