<h3 id="X944">X(944)&nbsp;=&nbsp;HOFSTADTER TRAPEZOID POINT</h3>
Trilinears &nbsp;&nbsp; bc[3a<sup>4</sup> - 2a<sup>3</sup>(b + c) + (b - c)<sup>2</sup>(2ab + 2ac - 2bc - b<sup>2</sup> - c<sup>2</sup> - 2a<sup>2</sup>)]
<br>
Trilinears &nbsp;&nbsp; 1 - cos A - cos B - cos C + cos B cos C
<br>
Trilinears &nbsp;&nbsp; r - R cos B cos C : :
<b><br>
X(944) = 2 X(1) - X(4)
</b>
<p>
X(944) is the point in which the extended legs X(4)X(1) and X(3)X(8) of the trapezoid X(4)X(1)X(3)X(8) meet. The point is introduced in
</p>
<p>
<b>Hofstadter, Douglas. R.,</b> "Discovery and dissection of a geometric gem," in <i>Geometry Turned On!</i> editors J. R. King and D. Schattschneider, Mathematical Association of America, Washington, D. C., 1997, 3-14.
</p>
<p>
The centroid of ABC is also the centroid of triangle X(4)X(8)X(944). (Darij Grinberg, August 22, 2002)
</p>
<p>
Let Ha be the hyperbola passing through A, and with foci at B and C.  This is called the A-Soddy hyperbola in (Paul Yiu, Introduction to the Geometry of the Triangle, 2002; 12.4 The Soddy hyperbolas, p. 143).  Let La be the polar of X(3) with respect to Ha.  Define Lb and Lc cyclically.  Let A' = Lb &cap; Lc, B' = Lc &cap; La, C' = La &cap; Lb.  Triangle A'B'C' is homothetic to ABC, and its orthocenter is X(944). (Randy Hutson, January 29, 2018)
</p>
<p>
Let Ma be the polar of X(4) wrt the circle centered at A and passing through X(1), and define Mb, Mc cyclically.  (Note: X(4) is the perspector of any circle centered at a vertex of ABC.)  Let A&Prime; = Mb &cap; Mc, and define B&Prime; and C&Prime; cyclically.  Triangle A&Prime;B&Prime;C&Prime; is homothetic to ABC, and its orthocenter is X(944). (Randy Hutson, January 29, 2018)
</p>
