<h3 id="X942">X(942)&nbsp;=&nbsp; INCIRCLE-INVERSE OF X(36)</h3>
Trilinears &nbsp;&nbsp; 2abc + (b + c)(a - b + c)(a + b -c) : :
<br>
Trilinears &nbsp;&nbsp; 1 + cos B + cos C : :
<br>
Barycentrics&nbsp;&nbsp;g(a,b,c) : g(b,c,a) : g(c,a,b), where g(a,b,c) = af(a,b,c)

<p>
Let A'B'C' be the incentral triangle of triangle ABC. Let L<sub>A</sub> be the line of reflection of line BC in line B'C', and define L<sub>B</sub> and L<sub>C</sub> cyclically. Let A'' =         L<sub>B</sub>&cap;L<sub>C</sub>, and define B'' and C'' cyclically. The lines AA'', BB'', CC'' concur in X(942). (Randy Hutson, 9/23/2011)
</p>
<p>
Let I be the incenter of ABC.  Let N<sub>A</sub> be the nine-point center of the triangle IBC, and define N<sub>B</sub> and N<sub>C</sub> cyclically.  Let A<sub>A</sub> be the reflection of N<sub>A</sub> in the line AI, Let A<sub>B</sub> be the reflection of N<sub>A</sub> in the line BI, let Let A<sub>C</sub> be the reflection of N<sub>A</sub> in the line CI, and define B<sub>A</sub>, B<sub>B</sub>, and B<sub>C</sub> cyclically, and define C<sub>A</sub>, C<sub>B</sub>, and C<sub>C</sub> cyclically.  Let O<sub>A</sub> be the nine-point center of the triangle A<sub>A</sub>A<sub>B</sub>A<sub>C</sub>, let O<sub>B</sub> be the nine-point center of the triangle B<sub>A</sub>B<sub>B</sub>B<sub>C</sub>, and let O<sub>C</sub> be the nine-point center of the triangle C<sub>A</sub>C<sub>B</sub>C<sub>C</sub>.  The circles O<sub>A</sub>, O<sub>B</sub>, O<sub>C</sub> concur in X(942).  (Antreas Hatzipolakis and CÃ©sar Lozada, January 28, 2015,

<p>
X(942) is the center of the conic which is the locus of poles, wrt the incircle, of tangents to the circumcircle.  This conic has foci at X(1) and X(65).  (Randy Hutson, July 20, 2016)
</p>
<p>
Let A'B'C' be the intouch triangle.  Let A&Prime; be the orthocenter of AB'C', and define B&Prime; and C&Prime; cyclically. The triangle A&Prime;B&Prime;C&Prime; is homothetic to A'B'C', and the center of homothety is X(942).  (Randy Hutson, July 20, 2016)
</p>
<p>
Let A'B'C' be the orthic triangle.  Let A&Prime; be the incenter of AB'C', and define B&Prime; and C&Prime; cyclically.  The triangle A&Prime;B&Prime;C&Prime; is homothetic to the intouch triangle, and the center of homothety is X(942).  (Randy Hutson, July 20, 2016)
</p>
<p>
Let A' be the homothetic center of the orthic triangles of the intouch and A-extouch triangles, and define B' and C' cyclically.  The triangle A'B'C' is perspective to the half-altitude triangle at X(942).  In fact, A'B'C' is the cevian triangle of X(942), wrt the half-altitude triangle.  (Randy Hutson, July 20, 2016)
</p>
