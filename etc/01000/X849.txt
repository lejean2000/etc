<h3 id="X849">X(849)&nbsp;=&nbsp;4th HATZIPOLAKIS-YIU POINT</h3>
Trilinears&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; [a/(b +
        c)]<sup>2</sup> : [b/(c + a)]<sup>2</sup> : [c/(a + b)]<sup>2</sup><br>
Barycentrics&nbsp;&nbsp;a[a/(b + c)]<sup>2</sup> : b[b/(c +
        a)]<sup>2</sup> : c[c/(a + b)]<sup>2</sup>
	<p>Let D denote the circumcircle of triangle ABC. Let D<sub>A</sub> be
        the circle tangent to sideline BC and tangent to D at A. Let
        B<sub>A</sub> = AC&cap;D<sub>A</sub> and C<sub>A</sub> =
        AB&cap;D<sub>A</sub>, and define C<sub>B</sub>, C<sub>B</sub> and
        A<sub>C</sub>, A<sub>B</sub> cyclically. Define A' =
        C<sub>B</sub>A<sub>B</sub>&cap;A<sub>C</sub>B<sub>C</sub>, and define
        B' and C' cyclically. Then triangle A'B'C' is homothetic to ABC, and
        X(849) is the center of homothety. See A. Hatzipolakis and P. Yiu,
        Hyacinthos #2056-2070, December, 2000.</p>
	<p>X(849) lies on these lines: 32,163&nbsp;&nbsp; 36,58&nbsp;&nbsp;
        110,595&nbsp;&nbsp; 249,1110&nbsp;&nbsp; 741,827&nbsp;&nbsp;
        757,763</p>
