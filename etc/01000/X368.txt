<h3 id="X368">X(368)&nbsp;=&nbsp;EQUI-BROCARD CENTER</h3>
<a id="EquiBrocardCenter"></a>
Trilinears&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;(reasonable
        trilinears are sought)<br>
Barycentrics&nbsp;&nbsp;(reasonable barycentrics are sought)

<p>The center X for which the triangle XBC, XCA, XAB have equal Brocard
angles. Peter Yff proved that X(368) lies on the self-isogonal
conjugate cubic with trilinear equation f(a,b,c)u + f(b,c,a)v +
f(c,a,b)w = 0, where f(a,b,c) = bc(b<sup>2</sup> - c<sup>2</sup>) and,
for variable x : y : z, the cubics u, v, w are given by u(x,y,z) =
x(y<sup>2</sup> + z<sup>2</sup>), v = u(y,z,x), w = u(z,x,y).</p>
<p>Cyril Parry proved that X(368) lies on the anticomplement of the
Kiepert hyperbola, this anticomplement being given by the trilinear
equation a<sup>2</sup>(b<sup>2</sup> - c<sup>2</sup>)x<sup>2</sup> +
b<sup>2</sup>(c<sup>2</sup> - a<sup>2</sup>)y<sup>2</sup> +
c<sup>2</sup>(a<sup>2</sup> - b<sup>2</sup>)z<sup>2</sup> = 0.</p>
