<p>
Let A'B'C' be the excentral triangle of ABC, so that A' = -1 : 1 : 1 (trilinears). Let A'' be the point where the bisector of angle BA'C meets the line BC. Define B'' and C'' cyclically. The lines AA'', BB'', CC'' concur in X(188). (Seiichi Kirikami, February 14, 2010)
</p>
<p>
Let Ea be the ellipse with B and C as foci and passing through the A-excenter, and define Eb and Ec cyclically.  Let La be the line tangent to Ea at the A-excenter, and define Lb and Lc cyclically.  Let A' = La&cap;BC, B' = Lb&cap;CA, C' = Lc&cap;AB. Then         A', B', C' are collinear, and the trilinear pole of line A'B'C' = X(188). Note: The triangle formed by La, Lb, Lc is also the excentral triangle of the excentral triangle. Alternately, let A&Prime; be the trilinear pole of line La, and define B&Prime;, C&Prime; cyclically.  The lines AA&Prime;, BB&Prime; and CC&Prime; concur at X(188); see also X(174).  (Randy Hutson, December 2, 2017)
</p>
