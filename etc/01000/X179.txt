<h3 id="X179">X(179)&nbsp;=&nbsp;1st AJIMA-MALFATTI POINT<a id="FirstAjimaMalfattiPoint"></a></h3>
Trilinears &nbsp;&nbsp; sec<sup>4</sup>(A/4) : sec<sup>4</sup>(B/4) : sec<sup>4</sup>(C/4)
<br>
Barycentrics &nbsp;&nbsp; sin A sec<sup>4</sup>(A/4) : sin B sec<sup>4</sup>(B/4) : sin C sec<sup>4</sup>(C/4)
<p>
The famous Malfatti Problem is to construct three circles O(A), O(B), O(C) inside ABC such that each is externally tangent to the other two, O(A) is tangent to lines AB and AC, O(B) is tangent to BC and BA, and O(C) is tangent to CA and CB. Let A' = O(B)&cap;O(C), B' = O(C)&cap;O(A), C' = O(A)&cap;O(B). The lines AA',BB',CC' concur in X(179). Trilinears are found in Yff's unpublished notes. See also the Yff-Malfatti Point, X(400), having trilinears csc<sup>4</sup>(A/4) : csc<sup>4</sup>(B/4) : csc<sup>4</sup>(C/4), and the references for historical notes.
</p>
<p>
<b>H. Fukagawa and D. Pedoe,</b> <i>Japanese Temple Geometry Problems (San Gaku),</i> The Charles Babbage Research Centre, Winnipeg, Canada, 1989.
</p>
<p>
<b>Michael Goldberg,</b> "On the original Malfatti problem," <i>Mathematics Magazine,</i> 40 (1967) 241-247.
</p>
<p>
<b>Clark Kimberling and I. G. MacDonald,</b> Problem E 3251 and Solution, <i>American Mathematical Monthly</i> 97 (1990) 612-613.
</p>
<p>
Let A', B', C' be the centers of the Malfatti circles.  Let A&Prime; be the trilinear product B'*C', and define B&Prime; and C&Prime; cyclically.  The lines AA&Prime;, BB&Prime;, CC&Prime; concur in X(1142).  The lines A'A&Prime;, B'B&Prime;, C'C&Prime; concur in X(179).  (Randy Hutson, July 11, 2019)
</p>
