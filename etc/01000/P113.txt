<div class="preamble-sect">
Centers X(113)-X(139) lie on the nine-point circle.

<p>Suppose that X is a point on the nine-point circle, and let X' be
        the reflection of X in the orthocenter, H. Then X is the anticenter of
        the cyclic quadrilateral ABCX'. Let H<sub>A</sub> be the orthocenter of
        triangle BCX, Let H<sub>B</sub> be the orthocenter of CAX, and let
        H<sub>C</sub> be the orthocenter of triangle ABX. Then the
        quadrilateral HH<sub>A</sub>H<sub>B</sub>H<sub>C</sub> is homothetic to
        and congruent to the cyclic quadrilateral ABCX', and X is the center of
        homothety. (Randy Hutson, 9/23/2011)</p>
