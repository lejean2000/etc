<p>
Let A'B'C' be the vertex-triangle of the 2nd and 3rd Brocard triangles.  The lines AA', BB', CC' concur in X(805).  (Randy Hutson, March 25, 2016)
</p>
<p>
Let La, Lb, Lc be the lines through A, B, C, resp. parallel to the Brocard axis.  Let Ma, Mb, Mc be the reflections of BC, CA, AB in La, Lb, Lc, resp.  Let A' = Mb&cap;Mc, and define B' and C' cyclically.  Triangle A'B'C' is inversely similar to, and 3 times the size of, ABC.   The centroid of A'B'C' is X(805); see Hyacinthos #16741/16782, Sep 2008.   (Randy Hutson, March 25, 2016)
</p>
