<p>
As a point on the Euler line, X(5) has Shinagawa coefficients (1, 1).
</p>
<p>
X(5) is the center of the nine-point circle. Euler showed in that
        this circle passes through the midpoints of the sides of ABC and the
        feet of the altitudes of ABC, hence six of the nine points. The other
        three are the midpoints of segments A-to-X(4), B-to-X(4), C-to-X(4).
        The radius of the nine-point circle is one-half the circumradius.</p>
	<p><b>Dan Pedoe,</b> <i>Circles: A Mathematical View,</i> Mathematical
        Association of America, 1995.
</p>
