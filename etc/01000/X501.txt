	<p>Miquel's theorem states that if A', B', C' are points (other than A,
        B, C) on sidelines BC, CA, AB, respectively, then the circles AB'C',
        BC'A', CA'B' meet at a point. Suppose P is a point and A' = P&cap;BC,
        B' = P&cap;CA, C' = P&cap;AB; the point in which the three circles is
        the <i>Miquel associate</i> of P. (Paul Yiu, 7/6/99)</p>
