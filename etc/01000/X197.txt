<h3 id="X197">X(197)&nbsp;=&nbsp;X(8)-CEVA CONJUGATE OF X(6)</h3>
Trilinears &nbsp;&nbsp; a[-a<sup>2</sup>tan A/2 + b<sup>2</sup>tan B/2 + c<sup>2</sup>tan C/2] : :
<p>
 Let A'B'C' be the extouch triangle.  Let A&Prime; be the crosspoint of the circumcircle intercepts of line B'C', and define B&Prime;, C&Prime; cyclically.  The lines A'A&Prime;, B'B&Prime;, C'C&Prime; concur in X(197). (Randy Hutson, July 31 2018)
</p>
<p>
For a construction of X(197), see Dasari Naga Vijay Krishna, "On A Simple Construction of Triangle Centers X(8), X(197), X(K) &amp; X(594)",  <a href="https://admin.umt.edu.pk/Media/Site/SSC/SubSites/SIR/FileManager/Sir2Three/Article-3%20(1).pdf">
    <b>Scientific Inquiry and Review, Vol. 2, Issue 3, July 2018.</b></a>
</p>
<p>
