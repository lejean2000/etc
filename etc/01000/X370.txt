<h3 id="X370">X(370)&nbsp;=&nbsp;EQUILATERAL CEVIAN TRIANGLE POINT</h3>
	<a id="EquilateralCevianTrianglePoint"></a>
    Trilinears&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;(see below)<br>
Barycentrics&nbsp;&nbsp;(see below)
        <p>A point P is an equilateral cevian triangle point if the cevian
        triangle of P is equilateral. Jiang Huanxin introduced this notion in
        1997.</p>
	<p>Jean-Pierre Ehrmann notes (11/6/02) that the normalized barycentric
        coordinates (x,y,z) of X(370) are the unique solution of this
        system:</p>
	<p>y(1 - y)S<sub>B</sub> + z(1 - z)S<sub>C</sub> = x(1 + x)F<br>
z(1 - z)S<sub>C</sub> + x(1 - x)S<sub>A</sub> = y(1 + y)F<br>x(1 - x)S<sub>A</sub> + y(1 - y)S<sub>B</sub> = z(1 + z)F<br>x + y + z = 1,</p>
	<p>where S<sub>A</sub> = (b<sup>2</sup> + c<sup>2</sup> -
        a<sup>2</sup>)/2; S<sub>B</sub>, S<sub>C</sub> are defined cyclically,
        F = [2 area(ABC)]/sqrt(3), and x&gt;0, y&gt;0, z&gt;0.</p>
	<p><b>Jiang Huanxin and David Goering,</b> Problem 10358* and Solution,
        "Equilateral cevian triangles," <i>American Mathematical Monthly</i>
        104 (1997) 567-570 [proposed 1994].</p>
	<p>X(370) lies on the Neuberg cubic.</p>
