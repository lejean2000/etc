<p>
X(493) is a homothetic center of triangles associated with squares that circumscribe ABC. For details, see
<a href="http://home.wxs.nl/~lamoen/wiskunde/vierkant4.html" style="font-style: italic">Vierkanten in een driehoek: 4. Ingeschreven vierkanten</a> (Floor van Lamoen, 4/27/98)
</p>
<p>
X(493) is the homothetic center of triangle ABC and the Lucas homothetic triangle; see X(371). Writing t for the ratio L:W at X(371), let L<sub>A</sub> be the line through the intersections, other than A, of the A-Lucas(t) circle and sides CA and AB. Define L<sub>B</sub> and L<sub>C</sub> cyclically. Let A' = L<sub>B</sub>&cap;L<sub>C</sub>, and define B' and C' cyclically. Then the triangle A'B'C', here introduced as the Lucas(t) homothetic triangle, is homothetic to triangle ABC. If t = 1, the center of homothety is X(493); for t = -1, it is X(494); for t = 2, it is X(588); and for t = -2, it is X(589). (Randy Hutson, February 9, 2013)
</p>
<p>
Let A&Prime; be the intersection of line BC and the common tangent to the B- and C-Lucas circles (at their touchpoint).  Define B&Prime; and C&Prime; cyclically.  Then A&Prime;B&Prime;C&Prime; is the cevian triangle of X(493).  Also, X(493) is the point of intersection of the tangents at X(371) and X(485) to the orthocubic K006.  (Randy Hutson, July 23, 2015)
</p>
