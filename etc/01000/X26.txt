<h3 id="X26">X(26)&nbsp;=&nbsp;CIRCUMCENTER OF THE TANGENTIAL TRIANGLE</h3>
Trilinears&nbsp; &nbsp;&nbsp; a[b<sup>2</sup>cos 2B + c<sup>2</sup>cos 2C - a<sup>2</sup>cos 2A] : :
<br>
Trilinears &nbsp;&nbsp; (J<sup>2</sup> - 3) cos A + 4 cos B cos C : : , where J is as at X(1113)
<br>
Barycentrics &nbsp;&nbsp;  a<sup>2</sup>(b<sup>2</sup>cos 2B + c<sup>2</sup>cos 2C - a<sup>2</sup>cos 2A) : :
<br>
Barycentrics &nbsp;&nbsp; a^2 (a^8 - 2 a^6 (b^2 + c^2) + 2 a^2 (b^6 + c^6) - (b^2 - c^2)^2 (b^4 + c^4)) : :
<b><br>
X(26) = 6 X(2) + (J^2 - 7) X(3)<br>
X(26) = (J^2 - 3) X(3) + 2 X(4)
</b>
<p>
As a point on the Euler line, X(26) has Shinagawa coefficients (E + 4F, -3E - 4F).
</p>

<p>
Theorems involving X(26), published in 1889 by A. Gob, are discussed in<br>
<b>Roger A. Johnson,</b> <i>Advanced Euclidean Geometry,</i> Dover, 1960, 259-260.
</p>
<p>
Let O<sub>A</sub> be the circle centered at the A-vertex of the circumorthic tangential triangle and passing through A; define O<sub>B</sub> and O<sub>C</sub> cyclically.  X(26) is the radical center of O<sub>A</sub>, O<sub>B</sub>, O<sub>C</sub>.  (Randy Hutson, August 30, 2020)
</p>
