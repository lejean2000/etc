<p>
Five constructions by Randy Hutson, January 29, 2015:
</p>
<p>
(1)  Let A'B'C' be the tangential triangle.  Let A&Prime; be the pole, wrt the polar circle, of line B'C', and define B&Prime;, C&Prime; cyclically.  The lines AA&Prime;, BB&Prime;, CC&Prime; concur in X(264).
</p>
<p>
(2)  Let A'B'C' be the symmedial triangle.  Let A&Prime; be the pole, wrt the polar circle, of line B'C', and define B&Prime;, C&Prime; cyclically.  The lines AA&Prime;, BB&Prime;, CC&Prime; concur in X(264).
</p>
<p>
(3)  Let A'B'C' be the circumsymmedial triangle.  Let A&Prime; be the pole, wrt the polar circle, of line B'C', and define B&Prime;, C&Prime; cyclically.  The lines AA&Prime;, BB&Prime;, CC&Prime; concur in X(264).
</p>
<p>
(4)  Let A'B'C' be the Lucas(t) central triangle (for any t).  Let A&Prime; be the pole, wrt the polar circle, of line B'C', and define B&Prime;, C&Prime; cyclically.  The lines AA&Prime;, BB&Prime;, CC&Prime; concur in X(264).
</p>
<p>
(5)  X(264) is the trilinear pole of the line X(297)X(525).  This line is the isotomic conjugate of the MacBeath circumconic, which is the isogonal conjugate of the orthic axis.  The line is also the polar of X(6) wrt the polar circle, and the radical axis of the polar and orthosymmedial circles, and the polar conjugate of the circumcircle)
</p>
<p>
Let A' be the trilinear product of the vertices of the A-anti-altimedial triangle, and define B' and C' cyclically.  The lines AA', BB', CC' concur in X(264). (Randy Hutson, November 2, 2017)
</p>
