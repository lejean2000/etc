<h3 id="X769">X(769)&nbsp;=&nbsp;o-(- 4, - 3) CIRCUMCIRCLE POINT</h3>
Trilinears&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; f(a,b,c)
        : f(b,c,a) : f(c,a,b), where f(a,b,c) = 1/[a <sup>-5</sup>(b
        <sup>-3</sup> - c <sup>-3</sup>) + a <sup>-4</sup>(b <sup>-4</sup> - c
        <sup>-4</sup>)]<br>
Barycentrics&nbsp;&nbsp;g(a,b,c) : g(b,c,a) : g(c,a,b), where g(a,b,c)
        = af(a,b,c)
        <p>X(769) lies on the circumcircle. This is one of several points of
        the form given by first trilinear</p>

        a<sup>n-1</sup>(b<sup>m</sup> - c<sup>m</sup>)],</p>
<p>hence the name "(m, n)-circumcircle point".</p>
