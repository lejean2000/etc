<h3 id="X442">X(442)&nbsp;&nbsp;COMPLEMENT OF SCHIFFLER POINT</h3>
Trilinears&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;bc(v + w) : ca(w + u) : ab(u + v), where<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;u : v : w = X(284); e.g., u(A,B,C) = (sin A)/(cos B + cos C)<br>Barycentrics&nbsp;&nbsp;v + w : w + u : u + v
<p>
As a point on the Euler line, X(442) has Shinagawa coefficients
($aS<sub>A</sub>$ + abc,$aS<sub>A</sub>$ - abc)
</p>
<p>
Let I<sub>A</sub>, I<sub>B</sub>, I<sub>C</sub> be the excenters,
        let A<sub>B</sub>, A<sub>C</sub> be the projections of A onto
        I<sub>A</sub>I<sub>B</sub> and I<sub>A</sub>I<sub>C</sub>,
        respectively, and define B<sub>C</sub>, B<sub>A</sub> and
        C<sub>A</sub>, C<sub>B</sub> cyclically. The Euler lines of the four
        triangles ABC, AA<sub>B</sub>A<sub>C</sub>,
        BB<sub>C</sub>B<sub>A</sub>, CC<sub>A</sub>C<sub>B</sub> concur in
        X(442). (Jean-Pierre Ehrmann, 11/24/01)</p>
	<p>In the plane of triangle ABC, let DEF denote the intouch triangle of
        ABC, and let<br>
H<sub>A</sub> = orthocenter of IBC<br>M<sub>A</sub> = midpoint of segment BC<br>N<sub>A</sub> = midpoint of the arc BC which does not include A<br>S<sub>A</sub> = reflection of I in line DE,<br>and define cyclically the points H<sub>B</sub>, H<sub>C</sub>,
        M<sub>B</sub>, M<sub>C</sub>, N<sub>B</sub>, N<sub>C</sub>, and
        S<sub>B</sub>, S<sub>C</sub>. The lines S<sub>A</sub>M<sub>A</sub>,
        S<sub>B</sub>M<sub>B</sub>, S<sub>CA</sub>M<sub>C</sub> concur in
        X(442), and X(442) is the pole, with respect to the incircle, of the
        perspectrix of the triangles H<sub>A</sub>H<sub>B</sub>H<sub>C</sub>
        and N<sub>A</sub>&cap;<sub>B</sub>&cap;<sub>C</sub>. (Dominik Burek, January 18
        2012)
</p>
<p>
Let A'B'C' be the excentral triangle.  X(442) is the radical center of the nine-point circles of A'BC, B'CA, C'AB. (Randy Hutson, September 14, 2016)	</p>
<p>
Let (Ja) be the A-excircle of the A-altimedial triangle, and define (Jb) and (Jc) cyclically.  X(442) is the radical center of (Ja), (Jb), (Jc). (Randy Hutson, November 2, 2017)
</p>
