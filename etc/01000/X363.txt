<h3 id="X363">X(363)&nbsp;=&nbsp;EQUAL PERIMETERS ISOSCELIZER POINT</h3>
<a id="EqualPerimetersIsoscelizerPoint"></a>
Trilinears &nbsp;&nbsp; b/(1 + sin B/2) + c/(1 + sin C/2) - a/(1 + sin A/2) : :
<p>
If X = X(363), the isoscelizer triangles of X have equal perimeters.
</p>
<p>
X(363) is the homothetic center of the excentral triangle and the
        inner Hutson triangle. A construction of the latter follows. The
        internal bisector of angle A meets the A-excircle in two points. Let
        P<sub>A</sub> be the point closer to line BC and let Q<sub>A</sub> be
        the other point. Define P<sub>B</sub> and P<sub>C</sub> cyclically, and
        define Q<sub>B</sub> and Q<sub>C</sub> cyclically. Let L<sub>A</sub> be
        the line tangent to the A-excircle at P<sub>A</sub>, and define
        L<sub>B</sub> and L<sub>C</sub> cyclically. Let M<sub>A</sub> be the
        line tangent to the A-excircle at Q<sub>A</sub>, and define
        M<sub>B</sub> and M<sub>C</sub> cyclically. The inner Hutson triangle
        is the triangle A'B'C' given by A' = L<sub>B</sub>&cap;L<sub>C</sub>,
        B' = L<sub>C</sub>&cap;L<sub>A</sub>, C' =
        L<sub>A</sub>&cap;L<sub>B</sub>; the outer Hutson triangle is given by
        A'' = M<sub>B</sub>&cap;M<sub>C</sub>, B'' =
        M<sub>C</sub>&cap;M<sub>A</sub>, C' = M<sub>A</sub>&cap;M<sub>B</sub>.
        (Based on a description of A'B'C' by Randy Hutson, September 23,
        2011)
</p>
<p>
Peter Moses (November 10, 2011) found trilinears for A'B'C' and A''B''C''. As these are central triangles, trilinears for A' and A'' suffice:
</p>
<p>
A' = aU<sub>A</sub>(a<sup>2</sup> + b<sup>2</sup> + c<sup>2</sup> - 2bc - 2ca - 2ab) + bU<sub>B</sub>(b + c - a)(c + a - b) + cU<sub>C</sub>(b + c - a)(a + b - c)
<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; : aU<sub>A</sub>(a<sup>2</sup> + b<sup>2</sup> - 3c<sup>2</sup> + 2bc + 2ca - 2ab) + U<sub>B</sub>(c - a)(-a + b + c)(a - b + c) - cU<sub>C</sub>(-a + b + c)(a + b - c)
<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; : aU<sub>A</sub>(a<sup>2</sup> - 3b<sup>2</sup> + c<sup>2</sup> + 2bc - 2ca + 2ab) - bU<sub>B</sub>(-a + b + c)(a - b + c) + U<sub>C</sub>(b - a)(-a + b + c)(a + b - c)
</p>
<p>
A'' = aU<sub>A</sub>(a<sup>2</sup> + b<sup>2</sup> + c<sup>2</sup> - 2bc - 2ca - 2ab) - bU<sub>B</sub>(b + c - a)(c + a - b) - cU<sub>C</sub>(b + c - a)(a + b - c)
<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; : aU<sub>A</sub>(a<sup>2</sup> + b<sup>2</sup> - 3c<sup>2</sup> + 2bc + 2ca - 2ab) - U<sub>B</sub>(c - a)(-a + b + c)(a - b + c) + cU<sub>C</sub>(-a + b + c)(a + b - c)
<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; : aU<sub>A</sub>(a<sup>2</sup> - 3b<sup>2</sup> + c<sup>2</sup> + 2bc - 2ca + 2ab) + bU<sub>B</sub>(-a + b + c)(a - b + c) - U<sub>C</sub>(b - a)(-a + b + c)(a + b - c),
</p>
<p>
where U<sub>A</sub> = sqrt[bc/((a - b + c)(a + b - c)] = (1/2)csc(A/2), and U<sub>B</sub> and U<sub>C</sub> are defined cyclically.
</p>
<p>
A'B'C' is perspective to the following triangles: intouch, hexyl,Yff, and the 1st and 2nd circumperp triangles.          A''B''C'' is perspective to these: ABC, intouch, hexyl, Yff, and the 1st and 2nd circumperp triangles. A'B'C' is homothetic to A''B''C'', which is perspective to the excentral triangle at X(168). (Peter Moses, 11/10/11)
</p>
