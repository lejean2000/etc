<b>
X(482) = s*X(1) + (r + 4R)*X(7)<br>
X(482) =  [A]/Ra + [B]/Rb + [C]/Rc + X(1)/r, where Ra, Rb, Rc = radii of Soddy circles<br>

</b>
<p>
Let S' be the outer Soddy circle and S<sub>A</sub>, S<sub>B</sub>,
        S<sub>C</sub> the Soddy circles tangent to S. Let J<sub>A</sub> =
        S'&cap;S<sub>A</sub>, E<sub>A</sub> = S<sub>B</sub>&cap;S<sub>C</sub>,
        and determine J<sub>B</sub>, J<sub>C</sub>, E<sub>B</sub>,
        E<sub>C</sub> cyclically. Then X(482) is the point of concurrence of
        lines J<sub>A</sub>-to-E<sub>A</sub>, J<sub>B</sub>-to-E<sub>B</sub>,
        J<sub>C</sub>-to-E<sub>C</sub>.</p>
	<p><b>David Eppstein,</b> "Tangent spheres and triangle centers,"
        <i>American Mathematical Monthly,</i> 108 (2001) 63-66.
</p>
<p>
Let S be the inner Soddy center, X(176).  X(482) is the point of concurrence of the Soddy lines of BSC, CSA, and ASB.  (Randy Hutson, September 14, 2016)
</p>
<p>
Let Ia, Ib, Ic be the centers of the Elkies companion incircles.  Let A' be the trilinear product Ib*Ic, and define B' and C'cyclically.  The lines AA', BB', CC' concur in X(482).  The lines IaA', IbB', IcC' concur in X(176).  (Randy Hutson, September 14, 2016)
</p>
<p>
Let La be the line tangent to the inner Soddy circle at the touchpoint with the A-Soddy circle.  Define Lb and Lc cyclically.  Let A' = Lb&cap;Lc and define B' and C' cyclically.  Triangle A'B'C', here introduced as the <b>inner Soddy tangential triangle</b>, is perspective to ABC, and the perspector is X(482).  (Randy Hutson, September 14, 2016)
</p>
