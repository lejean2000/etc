Centers on the circumcircle: X(74), X(98)-X(112)

<div class="preamble-sect">
<br>
Centers X(74), X(98)-X(112), and many others in ETC lie on the circumcircle. Mappings <b>&Lambda;</b> and <b>&Psi;</b> derived from such a point P for application to points X, are defined here: <p class="bold">&Lambda;(P,X) = isogonal conjugate of the point where line PX meets the line at infinity.
</p>
<p>
Let Y = &Lambda;(P,X), let Q = isogonal conjugate of P, and let Y and Z be the points where line YQ meets the circumcircle;<br>
then <b>&Psi;(P,X) = Z.</b>
<br>
<br></p>
