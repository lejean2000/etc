<h3 id="X588">X(588)&nbsp;=&nbsp;1st KENMOTU-VAN LAMOEN POINT</h3>
Trilinears&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; f(a,b,c) : f(b,c,a) : f(c,a,b), where f(a,b,c) = a/(a<sup>2</sup> + 4*area(ABC))<br>
Barycentrics&nbsp;&nbsp;g(a,b,c) : g(b,c,a) : g(c,a,b), where g(a,b,c) = af(a,b,c)
<p>
X(588) is the perspector of triangles ABC and A'B'C', where A' is the circumcenter of X(371) and the points where the squares in the Kenmotu configuration with center X(371) meet sideline BC, and B' and
C' are defined cyclically. See <p class="bold">Floor van Lamoen,
<a href="http://forumgeom.fau.edu/FG2002volume2/FG200202index.html">Some concurrences from Tucker hexagons,</a>
<i>Forum Geometricorum</i> 2 (2002) 5-13.
</p>
<p>
X(588) is the homothetic center of triangle ABC and the Lucas(2:1) homothetic triangle; see X(371) and X(589). (Randy Hutson, 9/23/2011)
</p>
<p>
Let A'B'C' be the Lucas central triangle.  Let A&Prime; be the trilinear pole of line B'C'; define B&Prime; and C&Prime; cyclically.  Let A* be the trilinear pole of line B&Prime;C&Prime;; define B* and C* cyclically.  The lines AA*, BB*, CC* concur in X(588).  (Randy Hutson, January 29, 2015)
</p>
<p>
X(588) lies on this line: 39,589
</p>
<p>
X(588) = isogonal conjugate of X(590)<br>
X(588) = cevapoint of X(6) and X(371)<br>
X(588) = X(1994)-cross conjugate of X(589)
</p>
