<p>
X(58) is the point of concurrence of the Brocard axes of triangles BIC, CIA, AIB, ABC, (where I denotes the incenter, X(1)), as proved in <b>Antreas P. Hatzipolakis, Floor van Lamoen, Barry Wolk, and Paul Yiu,</b> <a href=        "http://forumgeom.fau.edu/FG2001volume1/FG200109index.html">Concurrency of Four Euler Lines,</a> <i>Forum Geometricorum</i> 1 (2001) 59-68.
</p>
<p>
Let (Sa) be the reflection of the Spieker circle in BC, and define (Sb), (Sc) cyclically.  X(58) is the radical center of (Sa), (Sb), (Sc).  (Randy Hutson, July 20, 2016)
</p>
<p>
Let A'B'C' be the anticomplement of the Feuerbach triangle.  Let A&Prime;B&Prime;C&Prime; be the circumcevian triangle, wrt A'B'C', of X(1).  The lines AA&Prime;, BB&Prime;, CC&Prime; concur in X(58).  (Randy Hutson, July 20, 2016)
</p>
<p>
Let A'B'C' be the Feuerbach triangle.  Let La be the tangent to the nine-point circle at A', and define Lb, Lc cyclically.  Let A&Prime; be the isogonal conjugate of the trilinear pole of La, and define B&Prime;, C&Prime; cyclically.  Let A* = BB&Prime;&cap;CC&Prime;, B* = CC&Prime;&cap;AA&Prime;, C* = AA&Prime;&cap;BB&Prime;.  The lines AA*, BB*, CC* concur in X(58).  (Randy Hutson, July 20, 2016)
</p>
<p>
Let A'B'C' be the 2nd circumperp triangle.  Let A&Prime; be the trilinear product B'*C', and define B&Prime;, C&Prime; cyclically.  A&Prime;, B&Prime;, C&Prime; are collinear on line X(36)X(238) (the trilinear polar of X(81)).  The lines AA&Prime;, BB&Prime;, CC&Prime; concur in X(58).  (Randy Hutson, July 20, 2016)
</p>
