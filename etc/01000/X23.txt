<h3 id="X23">X(23)&nbsp;=&nbsp;FAR-OUT POINT<a id="FarOutPoint"></a></h3>

<br>
Barycentrics&nbsp;&nbsp;2 sin 2A - 3 tan &omega; : 2 sin 2B - 3 tan &omega; : 2 sin 2C - 3 tan &omega;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; (M. Iliev, 5/13/07)
<br>
Tripolars &nbsp;&nbsp; Sqrt[2(b^2 + c^2) - a^2] : :<br>
<b><br>
X(23) = 9 R^2 X(2) - 2 SW X(3)
</b>
<p>
As a point on the Euler line, X(23) has Shinagawa coefficients (E + 4F, -4E - 4F).
</p>
<p>
Let A'B'C' be the antipedal triangle of X(3) (the tangential triangle).  The circumcircles of AA'X(3), BB'X(3), CC'X(3) concur in two points:  X(3) and X(23).  (Randy Hutson, Octobe3r 13, 2015)
</p>
<p>
Let A'B'C' be the anti-orthocentroidal triangle.  Let A&Prime; be the reflection of A' in line BC, and define B&Prime; and C&Prime; cyclically.  Then X(23) is the centroid of A&Prime;B&Prime;C&Prime;; see X(9140), X(11002).  (Randy Hutson, December 10, 2016)
