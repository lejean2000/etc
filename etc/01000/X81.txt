<h3 id="X81">X(81)&nbsp;&nbsp;CEVAPOINT OF INCENTER AND SYMMEDIAN POINT</h3>
Trilinears&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;1/(b + c)
        : 1/(c + a) : 1/(a + b)
<br>
Barycentrics&nbsp;&nbsp;a/(b + c) : b/(c + a) : c/(a + b)
<br>
Barycentrics &nbsp;&nbsp; eccentricity of A-Soddy ellipse : :
<p class="bold">
X(81) = (r<sup>2</sup> + 2rR + s<sup>2</sup>)*X(1) - 3rR*X(2) - 2r<sup>2</sup>*X(3) &nbsp;&nbsp;
        (Peter Moses, April 2, 2013)  </p>
	<p>Let A'B'C' be the cevian triangle of X(1). Let A&Prime; be the symmedian
        point of triangle AB'C', and define B&Prime; and C&Prime; cyclically. Then the
        lines AA&Prime;, BB&Prime;, CC&Prime; concur in X(81). (Eric Danneels, Hyacinthos 7892,
        9/13/03)</p>
	<p>Let A'B'C' be the incentral triangle. Let L<sub>A</sub> be the
        reflection of B'C' in the internal angle bisector of vertex angle A,
        and define L<sub>B</sub> and L<sub>C</sub> cyclically. Let A'' =
        L<sub>B</sub>&cap;L<sub>C</sub>, B'' = L<sub>C</sub>&cap;L<sub>A</sub>,
        C'' = L<sub>A</sub>&cap;L<sub>B</sub>. The lines AA'', BB'', CC''
        concur in X(81). (Randy Hutson, 9/23/2011)
</p>
<p>
Let H* be the Stammler hyperbola.  Let A'B'C' be the tangential triangle and A&Prime;B&Prime;C&Prime; be the excentral triangle.  Let A* be the intersection of the tangents to H* at A' and A&Prime;, and define B* and C* cyclically.  The lines AA*, BB*, CC* concur in X(81).  (Randy Hutson, February 10, 2016)
</p>
<p>
Let A'B'C' be the 2nd circumperp triangle.  Let A&Prime; be the trilinear pole of line B'C', and define B&Prime; and C&Prime; cyclically.  The lines AA&Prime;, BB&Prime;, CC&Prime; concur in X(81).  (Randy Hutson, February 10, 2016)
</p>
<p>
Let A'B'C' be the anticomplement of the Feuerbach triangle.  Let A&Prime; be BB'&cap;CC', and define B&Prime; and C&Prime; cyclically.  The lines AA&Prime;, BB&Prime;, CC&Prime; concur in X(81).  (Randy Hutson, February 10, 2016)
</p>
<p>
Let Ba, Ca be the intersections of lines CA, AB, resp., and the antiparallel to BC through X(1).  Define Cb, Ab, Ac, Bc cyclically.  Triangles ABaCa, AbBCb, AcBcC are similar to each other and inversely similar to ABC.  Let Sa be the similitude center of triangles AbBCb and AcBcC.  Define Sb and Sc cyclically.  The lines ASa, BSb, CSc concur in X(81).  (Randy Hutson, February 10, 2016)
</p>
<p>
Let A<sub>10</sub>B<sub>10</sub>C<sub>10</sub> be Gemini triangle 10.  Let A' be the perspector of conic {A,B,C,B<sub>10</sub>,C<sub>10</sub>}}, and define B' and C' cyclically.  The lines AA', BB', CC' concur in X(81).  (Randy Hutson, January 15, 2019)
</p>

Let A<sub>11</sub>B<sub>11</sub>C<sub>11</sub> be Gemini triangle 11.  Let A' be the perspector of conic {A,B,C,B<sub>11</sub>,C<sub>11</sub>}}, and define B' and C' cyclically.  The lines AA', BB', CC' concur in X(81).  (Randy Hutson, January 15, 2019)
