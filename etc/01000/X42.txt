<p>
Let A'B'C' be the extangents triangle.  Let La be the trilinear polar of A', and define Lb and Lc cyclically.  Let A&Prime; = Lb&cap;Lc, B&Prime; = Lc&cap;La, C&Prime; = La&cap;Lb.  The lines AA&Prime;, BB&Prime;, CC&Prime; concur in X(42). (Randy Hutson, December 26, 2015)
</p>
<p>
Let A'B'C' be the extangents triangle.  Let A&Prime; be the trilinear product B'*C', and define B&Prime; and C&Prime; cyclically.  The lines AA&Prime;, BB&Prime;, CC&Prime; concur in X(42).(Randy Hutson, December 26, 2015)
</p>
<p>
Let Ab, Ac, Bc, Ba, Ca, Cb be as defined at X(3588).  Let A* be the intersection of the tangents to the Myakishev conic at Ba and Ca, and define B* and C* cyclically.  The lines AA*, BB*, CC* concur in X(42).(Randy Hutson, December 26, 2015)
</p>
