<p>
Let A', B', C' denote the points in which the incircle meets the sides BC, CA, AB, respectively. The lines AA', BB', CC' concur in X(7).
</p>
<p>
An <b>exarc circle</b> is a circle tangent to two sides of a triangle ABC and externally tangent to the circumcircle of ABC. The point whose distance to the sides BC, CA, AB are proportional to the respective radii of the exarc circles is X(279).  The point having distances to the sides proportional to the radii of the inarc circles is X(7).  See <b>Martin Lukarevski</b>, "Exarc radii and the Finsler-Hadwiger inequality", <i> The Mathematical Gazette</i> 106, issue 565, March 2022, pp. 138-143.
</p>
