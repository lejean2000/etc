<h3 id="X64">X(64)&nbsp;=&nbsp;ISOGONAL CONJUGATE OF X(20)</h3>
Trilinears &nbsp;&nbsp; 1/(cos A - cos B cos C) : 1/(cos B - cos C cos A) : 1/(cos C - cos A cos B)
<br>
Barycentrics &nbsp;&nbsp; a/(cos A - cos B cos C) : b/(cos B - cos C cos A) : c/(cos C - cos A cos B)
<br>
Barycentrics &nbsp;&nbsp; a^2/[3a^4 - 2a^2(b^2 + c^2) - (b^2 - c^2)^2] : :
<p>
A construction of X(64) appears in Lemoine's 1886 paper cited at X(19).
</p>
<p>
Let A'B'C' be the half-altitude triangle.  Let La be the trilinear polar of A', and define Lb and Lc cyclically.  Let A&Prime; = Lb&cap;Lc, and define B&Prime; and C&Prime; cyclically.  The lines AA&Prime;, BB&Prime;, CC&Prime; concur in X(64). (Randy Hutson, November 18, 2015)
</p>
<p>
Let Oa be the circle with segment BC as diameter.  Let A' be the perspector of Oa.  Let La be the polar of A' wrt Oa.  Define Lb and Lc cyclically.  Let A&Prime; = Lb&cap;Lc, B&Prime; = Lc&cap;La, C&Prime; = La&cap;Lb.  The lines AA&Prime;, BB&Prime;, CC&Prime; concur in X(64).  (Randy Hutson, November 18, 2015)
</p>
<p>
Let A'B'C' be the cevian triangle of X(69).  Let A&Prime; be the orthocenter of AB'C', and define B&Prime; and C&Prime; cyclically.  The lines AA&Prime;, BB&Prime;, CC&Prime; concur in X(64).  (Randy Hutson, November 18, 2015)
</p>
<p>
Let A'B'C' be the reflection of the orthic triangle in X(4).  Let A''B''C'' be the trangential triangle, with respect ot the orthic triangle, of the circumconic of the orthic triangle with center X(4); i.e., the bicevian conic of X(4) and X(459).  Then X(64) is the perspector of A'B'C' and A''B''C''.  (Randy Hutson, November 18, 2015)
</p>
<p>
The tangents at A, B, C to the Darboux cubic K004 concur in X(64). (Randy Hutson, November 18, 2015)
</p>
