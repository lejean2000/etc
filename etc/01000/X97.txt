In the plane of a triangle ABC, let<br>
Ia =  line through X(3) parallel to BC, and define Ib and Ic cyclically.<br>
Ac = Ia&cap;AB, and define Ba and Cb cyclically.<br>
Ab = Ia&cap;AC, and define Bc and Ca cyclically.<br>
Oa = circumcircle of A, Bc, Cb, and define Ob and Oc cyclically.<br>
Then X(97) = radical center of Oa, Ob, Oc. See also X(77). (Ivan Pavlov, April 1, 2022)
