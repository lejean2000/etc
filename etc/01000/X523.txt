<p>
As the isogonal conjugate of a point on the circumcircle, X(523) lies on the line at infinity.
</p>
<p>
Let A'B'C' be the 1st Brocard triangle.  Let A&Prime;B&Prime;C&Prime; be the 2nd Brocard triangle.  Let A* be the trilinear pole of line A'A&Prime;, and define B* and C* cyclically.  The lines AA*, BB*, CC* are parallel and meet the line at infinity at X(523).  (Randy Hutson, December 26, 2015)
</p>
<p>
Let A'B'C' be the Feuerbach triangle.  Let La be the tangent to conic {A,B,C,B',C'}} at A, and define Lb and Lc cyclically.  Let A&Prime; = Lb&cap;Lc, B&Prime; = Lc&cap;La, C&Prime; = La&cap;Lb.  The lines A'A&Prime;, B'B&Prime;, C'C&Prime; concur in X(523); see also X(1109).
</p>
<p>
Let L be the line X(115)X(125); then X(523) = trilinear pole of L.  The line L is also PU(40), the tangent to Steiner inellipse at X(115), the Lemoine axis of the 4th Brocard triangle, the Fermat axis of the 2nd Parry triangle, and the isogonal conjugate of hyperbola {A,B,C,PU(2)})  (Randy Hutson, December 26, 2015)
</p>
