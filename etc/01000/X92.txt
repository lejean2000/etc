<b>
X(92) = 2r(r+2R)*X(4) - ((r + 2R)^2 - s^2)*X(8)

X(92) = (sec A)*[A] + (sec B)*[B] + (sec C)*[C], where A, B, C denote angles and [A], [B], [C] denote vertices
</b>
<p>Let L<sub>A</sub> be the line through X(4) parallel to the internal bisector of angle A, and let<br>
A' = BC&cap;L<sub>A</sub>. Define B' and C' cyclically.
</p>
<p><b>Alexei Myakishev,</b> "The M-Configuration of a Triangle," <a href="http://forumgeom.fau.edu/FG2003volume3/FG200315.pdf" class="bold"><i>Forum Geometricorum</i> 3 (2003) 135-144</a>,
</p>
<p>
proves that the lines AA', BB', CC' concur in X(92). He notes that another construction follows from Proposition 2 of the article: let A<sub>1</sub> be the midpoint of the arc BC of the circumcircle that passes through A, and let A<sub>2</sub> be the point, other than A, in which the A-altitude meets the circumcircle. Let A&Prime; =         A<sub>1</sub>A<sub>2</sub>&cap;BC. Define B&Prime; and C&Prime; cyclically. Then the lines AA&Prime;, BB&Prime;, CC&Prime; concur in X(92).
</p>
<p>
Suppose that T = A'B'C' is a central triangle.  Let A'' be the pole with respect to the polar circle of the line B'C', and define B'' and C'' cyclically.  The appearance of T in the following list means that the lines AA'', BB'', CC'' concur in X(92):  Feurerbach, incentral, excentral, extangents, Apollonius, mixtilinear excentral.  (Randy Hutson, December 26, 2015)
</p>
