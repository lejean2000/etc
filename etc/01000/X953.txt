Let L be the line tangent to the incircle and the nine-point circle; i.e., the line X(11)X(244), called the Feuerbach tangent line. Let L<sub>A</sub> be the reflection of L in BC, and define L<sub>B</sub> and L<sub>C</sub> cyclically. Let A'' = L<sub>B</sub>&cap;L<sub>C</sub>, and define B'' and C'' cyclically. The lines AA'', BB'', CC'' concur in X(953). (Randy Hutson, 9/23/2011)
