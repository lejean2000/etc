<p>
Let La be the line through A parallel to the Lemoine axis, and define Lb and Lc cyclically.  Let Ma be the reflection of BC in La, and define Mb and Mc cyclically.  Let A' = Mb&cap;Mc, and define B' and C' cyclically.  The triangle A'B'C' is inversely similar to, and 3 times the size of, ABC.  Let A&Prime;B&Prime;C&Prime; be the reflection of A'B'C' in the Lemoine axis.  The triangle A&Prime;B&Prime;C&Prime; is homothetic to ABC, with center of homothety X(325); see Hyacinthos #16741/16782, Sep 2008.  (Randy Hutson, November 30, 2015)
</p>
