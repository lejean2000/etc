<b>
X(56) = R*X(1) - r*X(3)
</b>
<p>
X(56) is the perspector of the tangential triangle and the reflection of the intangents triangle in X(1).
</p>
<p>
Let A'B'C' be the Fuhrmann triangle.  Let La be the line through A' parallel to BC, and define Lb and Lc cyclically.  Let A&Prime; = Lb&cap;Lc, B&Prime; = Lc&cap;La, C&Prime; = La&cap;Lb.  The lines AA&Prime;, BB&Prime;, CC&Prime; concur in X(56).  Also, let AaBaCa be the poristic triangle (i.e., a triangle with common circumcircle and incircle as ABC) such that BaCa is parallel to BC.  Define AbBbCb and AcBcCc cyclically.  The lines AAa, BBb, CCc concur in X(56).  (Randy Hutson, November 18, 2015)
</p>
<p>
Let A'B'C' be the intouch triangle.  Let A&Prime; be the barycentric product of the circumcircle intercepts of line B'C', and define B&Prime;, C&Prime; cyclically.  The lines AA&Prime;, BB&Prime;, CC&Prime; concur in X(56). (Randy Hutson, June 27, 2018)
</p>

<p>
In the plane of a triangle ABC, let<br>
A'B'C' = circumcevian triangle of X(1);<br>
Ta = line tangent to circumcircle at A', and define Tb and Tc cyclically;<br>
Va = Tb&cap;Tc, and define Vb and Vc cyclically.<br>
The triangle VaVbVc is perspective to ABC, and the perspector is X(56).<br>
(Dasari Naga Vijay Krishna, June 19, 2021)
</p>
<p>
In the plane of a triangle ABC, let (Oa) be the circle tangent internally to the incircle and tangent internally to the circumcircle at A.  Define (Ob) and (Oc) cyclically. The radical center of (Oa), (Ob), (Oc) is X(56).  (Ivan Pavlov, February 24, 2022)
</p>
