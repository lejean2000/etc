<h3 id="X190">X(190)&nbsp;=&nbsp;YFF PARABOLIC POINT</h3>
<a id="YffParabolicPoint"></a>
Trilinears &nbsp;&nbsp; bc/(b - c) : ca/(c - a) : ab/(a - b)
<br>
Barycentrics&nbsp;&nbsp;1/(b - c) : 1/(c - a) : 1/(a - b)
<p>
In unpublished notes, Yff has studied the parabola tangent to sidelines BC, CA, AB and having focus X(101). If A',B',C' are the respective points of tangency, then the lines AA', BB', CC' concur in X(190).
</p>
<p>
The line X(100)X(190) is tangent to the Steiner circumellipse at X(190) and to the circumcircle at X(100). (Peter Moses, July 7, 2009)
</p>
<p>
Let Ha be the hyperbola passing through A, and with foci at B and C.  This is called the A-Soddy hyperbola in (Paul Yiu, <I>Introduction to the Geometry of the Triangle,</I> 2002; 12.4 The Soddy hyperbolas, p. 143). Let La be the polar of X(8) with respect to Ha.  Define Lb and Lc cyclically.  Let A' = Lb&cap;Lc, B' = Lc&cap;La, C' = La&cap;Lb.  The triangle A'B'C' is perspective to ABC, and the perspector is X(190).  (Randy Hutson, December 26, 2015)
</p>
<p>
Let Ia be the reflection of X(1) in the perpendicular bisector of BC, and define Ib and Ic cyclically.  X(190) = X(238) of IaIbIc.  (Randy Hutson, December 26, 2015)
</p>
<p>
Let A<sub>5</sub>B<sub>5</sub>C<sub>5</sub> and A<sub>6</sub>B<sub>6</sub>C<sub>6</sub> be Gemini triangles 5 and 6, resp.  Let L<sub>A</sub> be the tangent at A to conic {A,B<sub>5</sub>,C<sub>5</sub>,B<sub>6</sub>,C<sub>6</sub>}}, and define L<sub>B</sub> and L<sub>C</sub> cyclically.  Let A' = L<sub>B</sub>&cap;L<sub>C</sub>, and define B' and C' cyclically.  The lines AA', BB', CC' concur in X(190).  (Randy Hutson, January 15, 2019)
</p>
