<h3 id="X591">X(591)&nbsp;=&nbsp;1st VAN LAMOEN PERPENDICULAR BISECTORS POINT</h3>
Trilinears&nbsp; &nbsp;&nbsp; bc[b<sup>2</sup> + c<sup>2</sup> - 2a<sup>2</sup> + 4*area(ABC)]
<p>
Erect squares outwardly on the sides of triangle ABC. Two edges emanate from A; let P and Q be their endpoints. Let a' be the perpendicular bisector of PQ, and define b' and c' cyclically. Then a', b', c' concur in X(591). See also X(1991).  (Floor van Lamoen, 1/4/2001, Hyacinthos #2123)
</p>
<p>If you have The Geometer's Sketchpad, you can view <a href="X(591).gsp" class="bold">1st Van Lamoen Perpendicular Bisectors Point</a>.

        <p>X(591) lies on these lines: 2,6&nbsp;&nbsp; 372,754&nbsp;&nbsp;
        488,3071&nbsp;&nbsp; 637,1152</p>
	<p>
X(591) = reflection of X(1991) in X(2)<br>
X(591) = perspector of inner Vecten triangle and outer Vecten of outer Vecten triangle<br>
X(591) = centroid of {Ab, Ac, Bc, Ba, Ca, Cb} used in construction of 4th Lozada circle
</p>
