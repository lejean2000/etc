<div class="preamble-sect">

<p>
Suppose that P = p : q : r (barycentrics) is a point in the plane of a triangle ABC, not on one of the sidelines BC, CA, AB.  Let pK(K, P) denote the pivotal isocubic with pole K and pivot P (see <a href="https://bernard-gibert.pagesperso-orange.fr/notations.html"> <b>Bernard Gibert's Notations</b></a>.
</p>
<p>
The cubic pK(K,P) intersects the circumcircle in A, B, C and three other points, Q1, Q2, Q3.  The centroid of {Q1, Q2, Q3} is the point, here named the P-Gibert-Moses centroid, given by the combo G(P) = 2*X(3) + P and
</p>
<p>
G(P) = (b<sup>2</sup> + c<sup>2</sup> - a<sup>2)</sup>(3a<sup>4</sup> + b<sup>4</sup> + c<sup>4</sup> - 4 a<sup>2</sup> b<sup>2</sup> - 4 a<sup>2</sup> c<sup>2</sup> - 2 b<sup>2</sup> c<sup>2</sup>)p - 2 a<sup>2</sup> (q + r) : :
</p>
<p>
These centroids and formulas were contributed by Peter Moses, August 20, 2018.
