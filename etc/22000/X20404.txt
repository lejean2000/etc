<p>
Let La, Lb, Lc be the lines through A, B, C, resp. parallel to the Fermat axis.  Let Ma, Mb, Mc be the reflections of BC, CA, AB in La, Lb, Lc, resp. Let A' = Mb&cap;Mc, and define B' and C' cyclically. Triangle A'B'C' is inversely similar to, and 3 times the size of, ABC. Also, X(20404) = X(2)-of-A'B'C'. (See Hyacinthos #16741/16782, Sep 2008.)  (Randy Hutson, August 29, 2018)
</p>
