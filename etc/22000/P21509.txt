<p>
Suppose that X is a point in the plane of a triangle ABC and that X has barycentrics
</p>
<p>
a<sup>2</sup>(h a<sup>2</sup> + k b<sup>2</sup> + k c<sup>2</sup>) : b<sup>2</sup>(h b<sup>2</sup> + k c<sup>2</sup> + k a<sup>2</sup>) : c<sup>2</sup>(h c<sup>2</sup> + k a<sup>2</sup> + k b<sup>2</sup>),
</p>
<p>
where h and k are symmetric functions of (a,b,c) having the same degree of homogeneity.  Then X is a triangle center that lies on the Brocard axis, X(3)X(6), and the point
</p>
<p>
(X(3),X(6),X(1),X(2);X(3),X(2),X(1),X(6)) COLLINEATION IMAGE OF X
</p>
<p>
lies on the Euler line of ABC.  This point is here named the <b>(h,k) Brocard-Euler point of type 1.</b>  (For type 2, see the preamble just before X(21544).)  The appearance of (h,k,q) in the following list means that X(q) is the (h,k) Brocard-Euler point of X(q):
</p>
