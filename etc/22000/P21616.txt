<p>
Let ABC be a triangle, A'B'C' its incentral triangle and (I) its incircle. Denote (O<sub>a</sub>) the circle with diameter AA' and r<sub>a</sub> the radical axis of (O<sub>a</sub>) and (I) and cyclically (O<sub>b</sub>), (O<sub>c</sub>) and r<sub>b</sub>, r<sub>c</sub>. Let A*B*C* be the triangle bounded by r<sub>a</sub>, r<sub>b</sub>, r<sub>c</sub>. Then: 1) ABC and A*B*C* share the same nine-point-center; 2) the medial triangle of ABC is the orthic triangle of A*B*C*; and 3) the medial triangle of A*B*C* is the 3rd Euler triangle of ABC. (See Antreas Hatzipolakis, <a href="http://www.hyacinthos.epizy.com/message.php?msg=28081">Hyacinthos #28081</a>).
</p>
<p>The triangle A*B*C* is here named the <b>Wasat triangle of ABC</b>. For Wasat triangle:
<p>
<p>
The Wasat triangle is also the extraversion triangle of X(10), the complement of the excentral triangle, the reflection of the 2nd circumperp triangle in X(1125), and the mid-triangle of the Ursa-minor and Ursa-major triangles.  Also, the anti-Wasat triangle is also the anticomplementary triangle of the orthic triangle, and the reflection of the circumorthic triangle in X(389). (Randy Hutson, August 29, 2018)
<p>
<p>
&emsp;&emsp;&emsp;A* = b+c : c-a : b-a  (barycentrics)&emsp;&emsp;&emsp;|B*C*| = 2*R*cos(A/2)&emsp;&emsp;&emsp;&emsp;Area(A*B*C*) = (R*/r)*Area(ABC)/2
</p>

<p>If ABC is acute then the anti-Wasat triangle A*<sup>-</sup>B*<sup>-</sup>C*<sup>-</sup>, i.e. the triangle whose Wasat triangle is ABC, has the following properties:</p>
<p>&emsp;&emsp;&emsp;A*<sup>-</sup> = (S^2+SB SC) a^2 : SB (SA-SC) b^2 : SC (SA-SB) c^2 (barycentrics)&emsp;&emsp;&emsp;|B*<sup>-</sup>C*<sup>-</sup>|=2 a |cos(A)|&emsp;&emsp;&emsp;Area(A*<sup>-</sup>B*<sup>-</sup>C*<sup>-</sup>) = |4-(SW/R^2)| Area(ABC).
</p>
<p>The anti-Wasat triangle of ABC is the (3rd anti-Euler triangle)-of-(the medial triangle)-of ABC.</p>
