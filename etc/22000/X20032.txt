<h3 id="X20032">X(20032) = (name pending)</h3>
Barycentrics &nbsp;&nbsp; a^2 (2 b c - Sqrt[(a^2 + b^2 - c^2) (a^2 - b^2 + c^2)]) / Sqrt[-a^2 + b^2 + c^2] : :
<p>
Let ABC be an acute triangle, and let<br>
A'B'C' = medial triangle<br>
A''B''C'' = orthic triangle<br>
U = circumcircle<br>
O<sub>A</sub> = circle through A' and A'' tangent to U on the negative side of line BC; define O<sub>B</sub> and O<sub>C</sub> cyclically<br>
Then X(20032 is the radical center of the circles O<sub>A</sub>, O<sub>B</sub>, O<sub>C</sub>.
</p>
<p>
See Kadir Altintas and Peter Moses, <a href="http://www.hyacinthos.epizy.com/message.php?msg=27800"> <b>Hyacinthos 27800</b></a>.  See also X(20033) and X(20034).
</p>
<p>
Note that X(20032)-X(20034) are real if and only if ABC is an acute triangle.
</p>
<p>
X(20032) lies on the cubics K171 and K969, and also on these lines: {2, 20033}, {3, 31386}, {25, 20034}, {5374, 19588}
</p>
