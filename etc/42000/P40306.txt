<p class="preamble-title">
Points on Vu orthogonal conics: X(40306)-X(40315)
</p>
<img src="rightri.svg" style="height:30px" alt="rightri">
</header>
<div class="preamble-sect">
<p>
This preamble is based on notes contributed by Vu Thanh Tung, November 5, 2020.
<p>
In the plane of a triangle ABC, let P and U be points.  Let L be the line through P perpendicular to line AU, and let A<sub>1</sub> = L&cap;BC. Define B<sub>1</sub> and C<sub>1</sub> cyclically.  Let L' be the line through U perpendicular to line AP. and A<sub>2</sub> = L'&cap;BC. Define B<sub>2</sub> and C<sub>2</sub> cyclically. The six points A<sub>1</sub>,  B<sub>1</sub>, C<sub>1</sub>, A<sub>2</sub>, B<sub>2</sub>, C<sub>2</sub> lie on a conic, here named the <b>Vu orthogonal conic</b> of P and U, denoted by VOC(P,U).
</p>
<p>
Let V(P,U) denote the center, and T(P,U) the perspector, of VOC(P,U). Note that VOC(U,P) = VOC(P,U), V(U,P) = V(P,U), and T(U,P) = T(P,U).
</p>
<p>
See <a href="VuOrthogonalConic.pdf">Vu Orthogonal Conic</a>.
</p>
