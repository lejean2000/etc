<p>
This preamble is contributed by Clark Kimberling and Peter Moses, January 4, 2021.
<p>
Let T denote the pedal triangle of X(2). There is exactly one dilation of T from X(2) that is perspective to ABC. It is the (-1)-dilation, here named the <b>special dilation triangle</b>, with vertices given by
</p>
<p>
A' = 4 a^2 : a^2 - b^2 + c^2 : a^2 + b^2 - c^2 <br>
B' = b^2 + c^2 - a^2 : 4 b^2 : b^2 - c^2 + a^2 <br>
C' = c^2 - a^2 + b^2 : c^2 + a^2 - b^2 : 4 c^2.
</p>
<p>
See Clark Kimberling and Peter J. C. Moses, "Dilation-Induced Perspectivities among Triangles", <i>Journal for Geometry and Graphics</i> 14 (2010) 1-14.
</p>
<p>
X(2)-of-A'B'C' = X(5650), and X(3)-of-A'B'C' = X(141)
</p>
<p>
