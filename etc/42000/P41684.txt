<p>If P is point on the plane of ABC, the reflections of P in the sidelines of ABC are the vertices of a new triangle named the <b>reflection triangle of P</b>.</p>
<p><i>Let P', P" be any two distinct points on the plane of a triangle ABC and such that they are collinear with its circumcenter O=X(3). Then the reflection triangles A'B'C' and A"B"C" of P' and P" are orthologic.</i></p>
<p>Let Q' and Q" be the orthologic centers (A'B'C' to A"B"C") and (A"B"C" to A'B'C'), respectively. If P' is fixed and P" moves along the line OP', then Q' describes a rectangular hyperbola &#8461;(P') circumscribed to A'B'C', whilst Q" moves on a line &#120131;(P'). &#8461;(P') is named here the <b>bireflection circumcentral conic of P'</b> (shortened to the <b>BRC-conic of P'</b>) and &#120131;(P') is named here the <b>bireflection circumcentral line of P'</b>, abbreviated as the <b>BRC-line of P'</b>.</p>
<p>If P' = x : y : z (barycentrics), then:

<ul>
<li>the center of &#8461;(P') is:<br>
&#8461;*(P') = (a^2*b^2*c^2*x - (S^2 - SB^2)*b^2*z - (S^2 - SC^2)*c^2*y)*(b^2*c^2*x^2 - 2*SA*a^2*y*z - (SA - SB)*b^2*x*z - (SA - SC)*c^2*x*y)*a^2 : :
<br>
<br>&#8461;*(P') coincides with the HATZIPOLAKIS-MOSES NINE-POINT IMAGE OF P', or HM(P'), defined in <a href="ETCPart7.html#X12824">X(12824)</a>.
</li>

<li>the tripole of &#120131;(P') is:<br>
&#120131;*(P')&nbsp; = &nbsp;b^2*c^2*(b^2*SB*x - a^2*SA*y)*(c^2*SC*x - a^2*SA*z)*
<br>
&emsp;&emsp;&emsp;&emsp;(2*a^2*c^2*SB*y^2 + c^2*(S^2 - (SB - 2*SC)*SB)*x*y + b^2*(S^2 - 3*SB^2)*x*z + a^2*(S^2 + (2*SA - SB)*SB)*y*z)*
<br>
&emsp;&emsp;&emsp;&emsp;(2*a^2*b^2*SC*z^2 + b^2*(S^2 - (SC - 2*SB)*SC)*x*z + c^2*(S^2 - 3*SC^2)*x*y + a^2*(S^2 + (2*SA - SC)*SC)*y*z) : :
</li>
</ul>

<p>Note: The orthologic center (reflection-of-P' to reflection-of-P") is the reflection in P' of the orthologic center (pedal-of-P' to pedal-of-P"). (See <a href="ETCPart21.html#X41537">X(41537)</a>)</p>
