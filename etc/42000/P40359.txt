<p>
Let P(t) = a<sup>t</sup> : b<sup>t</sup> : c<sup>t</sup>, on the power curve, as in the preambles just before X(40297) and X(40345).  The P(t)-Ceva conjugate of P(u), denoted by P(t)&#169;P(u) is given by
</p>
<p>
a<sup>u</sup>(-a<sup>u-t</sup> + b<sup>u-t</sup> + c<sup>u-t</sup>) : b<sup>u</sup>(a<sup>u-t</sup> - b<sup>u-t</sup> + c<sup>u-t</sup>) :
c<sup>u</sup>(a<sup>u-t</sup> + b<sup>u-t</sup> - c<sup>u-t</sup>),
</p>
<p>
P(t)&#169;P(u) is the perspector of the cevian triangle of P(t) and the anticevian triangle of P(u).
</p>
