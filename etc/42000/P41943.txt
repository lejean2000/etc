<div class="preamble-sect">
<p>
Points X(41943)-X(41980) are contributed by Peter Moses, March 14-16, 2021.
</p>
<p>
For a discussion of the Evans conic, see <a href="https://mathworld.wolfram.com/EvansConic.html"><b>Evans Conic</b></a>.
</p>
<p>
If k1 and k2 are functions symmetric in a,b,c and homogeneous of degree 0, then the points
</p>
<p>
E1(k1,k2) = 3*a^2*(a^2 - b^2 - c^2)*k2 - 4*k1*S^2 - 2*a^2*Sqrt[k1^2 + 2*k1*k2 + 3*k2^2]*S : :  and
E2(k1,k2) = 3*a^2*(a^2 - b^2 - c^2)*k2 - 4*k1*S^2 + 2*a^2*Sqrt[k1^2 + 2*k1*k2 + 3*k2^2]*S : :
</p>
<p>
lie on the Evans conic. The lines tangent to the Evans conic at the two points meet on the Euler line in the point
</p>
<p>
T(k1,k2) = (k1 + 3*k2)*X[2] - (k1 + k2)*X[3].
</p>
<p>
The appearance of (k1,k2) &srarr; (i,j); h in the following list means that E1(k1,k2) = X(i), E2(k1,k2) = X(j), and T(k1,k2) = X(h).
</p>
