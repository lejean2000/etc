<p>
This preamble is contributed by Clark Kimberling and Peter Moses, December 31, 2020.
<p>
Let P = p : q : r (barycentrics) be a point in the plane of a triangle ABC. The point P' = p : r : q is here named the <b> parapoint of P</b>. To contruct P', let L be the line through P parallel to BC. Let Q = L&cap;AG , where G denotes the centroid of ABC. Then P' is the reflection of P in Q.
</p>
<p>
The <b>paratriangle of P</b> is the triangle A'B'C' with vertices given by
</p>
<p>
A' = p : r : q<br>
B' = r : q : p <br>
C' = q : p : r
</p>
<p>
If P is a triangle center other than X(1), then A'B'C' is a noncentral triangle, but its perspector with other triangles can be a triangle center.
</p>
<p>
(More generally, the paratriangle of a triangle UVW is here defined as the triangle U'V'W'.)
</p>
<p>
For fixed P with paratriangle A'B'C', the locus of X such that the cevian triangle of X is perspective to A'B'C' is the <b>cevian-associated cubic</b> given by
</p>
<p>
q (p q - r^2) y z^2 - r (p r - q^2) y^2 z + (cyclic) = 0,
</p>
<p>
which is the cubic pK(P1,P2), where P1 = (p r - q^2)(p q - r^2) and P2 = P*P1.
</p>
