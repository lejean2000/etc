<p>
This preamble is based on notes received from Rados&#322;aw &#379;ak (October 29, 2020) and Peter Moses (October 29-30, 2020).
</p>
<p>
In the plane of a triangle ABC, let P be a point, not on a sideline of ABC, and let DEF be the cevian triangle of P. The isogonal conjugate of line EF is a conic. Let A' be the center of of the conic, and define B' and C' cyclically. Then the lines AA', BB', CC' concur in a point here named the <b>hodpiece</b> of P, denoted by H(P). The name <i>hodpiece</i> is taken from James Joyce's book, <i>Finnegans Wake.</i> The unique point P such that H(P) = P, indexed below as X(40139), is named the <b>Bloom point</b> after Leopold Bloom, the main character in Joyce's <i>Ulysses</i>. The point H(X(5)) = X(40140) is the <b>Dedalus point</b>, and the H(X(7)) = X(40141), the <b>Zana point</b>.
</p>
<p>
An <a href="http://www.deltami.edu.pl/delta/redakcja/konkurs_prac_uczniowskich/2020/09/26/zak-radoslaw.pdf">article (in Polish) </a>
about hodpieces by &#379;ak won a gold medal in a competition for high school students organized by the Polish Mathematical Society.
For an English translation, see <a href="Isogonal%20conjugate%20and%20a%20few%20properties%20of%20the%20point%20X(25).pdf">Isogonal conjugate and a few properties of the point X(25)</a>.
</p>
<p>
If P = p : q : r (barycentrics), then H(P) = a<sup>2</sup>/(p*(-a<sup>2</sup>/p + b<sup>2</sup>/q + c<sup>2</sup>/r) : : .
</p>
<p>
Let P* = P-Ceva conjugate of X(6).  Then H(P) = isogonal conjugate of P*-cross conjugate of P.
</p>
