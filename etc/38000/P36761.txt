<p>Considere all equilateral triangles A<sub>e</sub>B<sub>e</sub>C<sub>e</sub> circumscribing ABC and such that A lies between B<sub>e</sub> and C<sub>e</sub> <sup>(1)</sup>, B lies between C<sub>e</sub> and A<sub>e</sub> <sup>(2)</sup> and C lies between A<sub>e</sub> and B<sub>e</sub> <sup>(3)</sup> (see note at the end of this preamble). The A-vertex of the largest A<sub>e</sub>B<sub>e</sub>C<sub>e</sub> is the antipode of X(13) in the circle {{X(13), B, C}}, and the other two vertices are found cyclically. If this triangle is denoted as A'B'C' then A' has barycentric coordinates:<p>
<p style="padding-left:25px;">
A' = -6*sqrt(3)*S*a^2 - (3*(a^2 + b^2 + c^2))*a^2 + 2*(b^2 - c^2)^2 :
<br>
&emsp;&emsp;(7*b^2 + 2*c^2)*a^2 - 3*b^4 + 5*b^2*c^2 - 2*c^4 + 2*sqrt(3)*S*(2*a^2 + b^2) :
<br>
&emsp;&emsp;(7*c^2 + 2*b^2)*a^2 - 3*c^4 + 5*c^2*b^2 - 2*b^4 + 2*sqrt(3)*S*(2*a^2 + c^2)</p>
<p>The center of A'B'C' is X(5463) and its squared-sidelength is 4*S*(cot(&omega;)+sqrt(3))/3, where S and &omega; are double-area and Brocard angle of ABC, respectively.</p>
