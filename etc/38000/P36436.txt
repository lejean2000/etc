<p class="preamble-title">
Homothetors involving triangles T(k): X(36436)-X(36472)
</p>
<img src="rightri.svg" style="height:30px" alt="rightri">
</header>
<div class="preamble-sect">
<p>
This preamble and centers X(36436)-X(36472) were contributed by Clark Kimberling and Peter Moses, January 17, 2020, and Randy Hutson, January 29, 2020.
<p>
Suppose that ABC is a triangle.  The trisectors of segment BC are 0:1:2 and 0:2:1; these are two of the points on the permutation ellipse E(0:1:2), here named the <b>trisection ellipse,</b> given by the equation
</p>
<p>
5(x^2 + y^2 + z^2) - 2(y z + z x + x y) = 0.
</p>
<p>
For every real number k, let T(k) denote the central triangle with A-vertex 1 : k : k.  The line AG, where G = 1:1:1 = X(2) meets the trisection ellipse in two points, 1 : k : k, where k = sqrt(27) - 5 and k = - sqrt(27) - 5. For these two values of k, the triangle T(k) is homothetic to many triangles, of which 17 for each k give homothetors (centers of homothety) included in this section:
</p>
<p>
Euler; (a^2+b^2-c^2) (a^2-b^2+c^2)-2 (a^2 b^2-b^4+a^2 c^2+2 b^2 c^2-c^4) k : : <br>
reflection of ABC in X(3): 2 a^2 (a^2-b^2-c^2)-(a^2+b^2-c^2) (a^2-b^2+c^2) k : : <br>
reflection of X(3) in ABC; a^2 (a^2-b^2-c^2)+(a^4-3 a^2 b^2+2 b^4-3 a^2 c^2-4 b^2 c^2+2 c^4) k : : <br>
reflection of ABC in X(5) (aka Carnot, Johnson); a^2 b^2-b^4+a^2 c^2+2 b^2 c^2-c^4-a^2 (a^2-b^2-c^2) k : : <br>
outer Garcia; b+c+a k : : <br>
Mandart-incircle triangle; (a-b-c) (a^2-(b-c)^2 k) : : <br>
inner Yff; 2 a^2 b c+(a^4-2 a^2 b^2+b^4-2 a^2 b c-2 a^2 c^2-2 b^2 c^2+c^4) k : : <br>
outer Yff; 2 a^2 b c-(a^4-2 a^2 b^2+b^4-2 a^2 b c-2 a^2 c^2-2 b^2 c^2+c^4) k : : <br>
anti-Aquila; a-(2 a+b+c) k : : <br>
infinite altitude; (a^2+b^2-c^2) (a^2-b^2+c^2)-2 a^2 (a^2-b^2-c^2) k : : <br>
3rd tri-squares central; a^2+S-k (a^2+2 S) : : <br>
4th tri-squares central; a^2-S-k (a^2+2 S) : : <br>
Ehrmann mid-triangle; a^4+a^2 b^2-2 b^4+a^2 c^2+4 b^2 c^2-2 c^4-(2 a^4-a^2 b^2-b^4-a^2 c^2+2 b^2 c^2-c^4) k : : <br>
anti-inner-Grebe; a^2-k (a^2-S) : : <br>
anti-outer-Grebe; a^2-k (a^2+S) : : <br>
1st Kenmotu free-vertices triangle; a^2 (a^2-b^2-c^2+2 S)+(a^4-3 a^2 b^2+2 b^4-3 a^2 c^2-4 b^2 c^2+2 c^4-2 a^2 S) k : : <br>
2nd Kenmotu free-vertices triangle; a^2 (a^2-b^2-c^2-2 S)+(a^4-3 a^2 b^2+2 b^4-3 a^2 c^2-4 b^2 c^2+2 c^4+2 a^2 S) k : :
</p>
<p>
For barycentrics and references for the various triangles, see <a href="IndexOfTrianglesReferencedInETC.html">Index of Triangles Referenced in ETC, by </a>.
</p>
<p>
For every k, the homothetor of T(k) with each of the following triangles lies on the Euler line: Euler, reflection of X(3) in ABC, reflection of ABC in X(5), infinite altitude.<br>
For every k, the homothetor of T(k) with each of the following triangles lies on the line X(2)X(6): 3rd tri-squares central, 4th tri-squares central, anti-inner Grebe, anti-outer Grebe.<br>
For every k, the homothetor of T(k) with each of the following triangles lies on the line X(1)X(2): outer Garcia, inner Yff, outer Yff, anti-Aquila.<br>
</p>
<p>
For k a nonconstant function symmetric in a,b,c, see the preamble just before X(36473).
</p>
<p>
The trisection ellipse is also the conic Cpar(X(2)); see the preamble before X(10001).  (Randy Hutson, March 29, 2020)
</p>
