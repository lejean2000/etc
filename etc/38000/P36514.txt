V transforms on the circumcircle: X(36514)-X(36517)
</p>
<img src="rightri.svg" style="height:30px" alt="rightri">

<div class="preamble-sect">
<p>
This preamble was contributed by Vu Thanh Tung, and centers X(36436)-X(36472) by Peter Moses, January 20, 2020.
<p>
Let X = x : y : z be a point in the plane of a triangle ABC, let A'B'C'= circumcevian triangle of X, and let O<sub>A</sub> = circumcenter of triangle XBC; define O<sub>B</sub> and O<sub>C</sub> cyclically.
</p>
<p>
The triangles O<sub>A</sub>O<sub>B</sub>O<sub>C</sub> and A'B'C' are perspective, and their perspector, on the circumcircle, is given by
</p>
<p>
V(X) = a^2 / (a^4 y (y - z) z + (b^2 - c^2) x^2 (c^2 y + b^2 z) +  a^2 (-b^2 z (x^2 + 2 x z + y (y + z)) +  c^2 y (x^2 + 2 x y + z (y + z)))) :  :
</p>
<P>
Let X* denote the isogonal conjugate of X; then V(X*) = V(X), as in the following examples:

<p>
V(X(2)) = V(X(6)) = X(1296)<br>
V(X(3)) = V(X(4)) = X(110)<br>
V(X(5)) = V(X(54)) = X(1291)<br>
V(X(7)) = V(X(55)) = X(20219)<br>
V(X(9)) = V(X(57)) = X(28291)<br>
V(X(17)) = V(X(61)) = X(36514)<br>
V(X(18)) = V(X(62)) = X(36515)<br>
V(X(19)) = V(X(63)) = X(36516)<br>
V(X(39)) = V(X(83)) = X(36517)<br>
</p>
<p>
V(X) is the perspector of ABC and the triangle formed by reflecting line XX' in the sides of ABC, where X' denotes the isogonal conjugate of X.  (Randy Hutson, March 29, 2020)
</p>
