<p>
In the plane of a triangle ABC, let A' = reflection of A in BC, and define B' and C' cyclically. Let Ab be the point in which the incircle of triangle A'BC meets A'B, and let Ac be the point in which the same circle meets A'C.  Define Bc and Ca cyclically, and define Ca and Ab cyclically. Let
</p>
<p>
Pa = BcBa&cap;CaCb, &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Pb = CaCb&cap;AbAc, &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Pc = AbAc&cap;BcBa.
</p>
<p>
The triangle PaPbPc is perspective to ABC, and the perspector is X(37741).  (Vijay Krishna, March 31, 2020)
</p><p>
Let A'B'C' be the cevian triangle of X(7) (i.e. the intouch triangle).  Let L<sub>A</sub> be the reflection of line B'C' in line BC, and define L<sub>B</sub> and L<sub>C</sub> cyclically.  Let A" = L<sub>B</sub>&cap;L<sub>C</sub>, B"=L<sub>C</sub>&cap;L<sub>A</sub>, C" = L<sub>A</sub>&cap;L<sub>B</sub>.  The lines AA", BB", CC" concur in X(37741). (Randy Hutson, March 29, 2020)
</p><p>

La = line EF, and define Lb and Lc cyclically <br>
