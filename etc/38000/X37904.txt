<h3 id="X37904">X(37904) &nbsp;=&nbsp; CIRCUMCIRCLE-INVERSE OF X(11284)</h3>
Barycentrics &nbsp;&nbsp; 10*a^6 - a^4*b^2 - 10*a^2*b^4 + b^6 - a^4*c^2 + 12*a^2*b^2*c^2 - b^4*c^2 - 10*a^2*c^4 - b^2*c^4 + c^6 : :
<p>
Let P and P' be circumcircle antipodes.  Let Q be the midpoint of X(2) and P.  Let Q' be the midpoint of X(2) and P'.  The rectangular hyperbola passing through P, P', Q, Q' has center X(37904) for all P.  (Randy Hutson, April 24, 2020)
</p>
<p>
See the preamble just before X(38001), where this hyperbola is named the <b>Hutson right hyperbola of P</b>.
