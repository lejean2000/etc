Perspectors associated with mid-trace triangles: X(36810)-X(36413)
</p>
<img src="rightri.svg" style="height:30px" alt="rightri">

<div class="preamble-sect">
<p>
This preamble and centers X(36810)-X(36413) were contributed by Clark Kimberling and Peter Moses, February 21, 2020<br>
<p>
Let P = p : q : r and U = u : v : w be points not on the sidelines BC, CA, AB of a triangle ABC<br>
Let A' = AP&cap;BC, A' = AU&cap;BC', and A* = midpoint of A' and A''<br>
Define B* and C* cyclically<br>
The triangle A*B*C* is here named the <b>mid-trace triangle of P and U</b>, denoted by M(P,U)<br>
</p>
<p>
A* =  0 : 2 q v + r v + q w : 2 r w + r v + q w<br>
B* = 2 p u + p w + r u : 0 : 2 r w + p w + r u<br>
C* = 2 p u + q u + p v : 2 q v + q u + p v
</p>
<p>
For given P, the locus of a point X = x : y : z such that M(P,X) is a cevian triangle is given by the cubic
</p>
<p>
p (q r + r^2 + q p) y^2 z - p (q r + q^2 + r p) y z^2 + (cyclic) + (p - q) (p - r) (q - r) x y z = 0,
</p>
<p>
here named the <b>mid-cevian cubic of P</b>, denoted by MC(P)<br>
  If P is on the line at infinity, then MC(P) is the union of the line at infiniity (x + y + z = 0) and the circumconic
</p>
<p>
p<sup>2</sup>(q - r) y z + q<sup>2</sup>(r - p) z x + r<sup>2</sup>(p - q) x y = 0<br>
</p>
<p>
The following points lie on MC(P):  A, B, C, P, 1/p : : , p - 2q - 2r : : 1/r, and p(- p + q + r) : : <br>
</p>
<p>
For further developments, see Bernard Gibert's page, <A HREF="https://bernard-gibert.pagesperso-orange.fr/Classes/cl069.html
"> CL069 Mid-Cevian Cubics</A>.
</p>
