<h3 id="X36808">X(36808)&nbsp;=&nbsp; X(6)X(31)&cap;X(9)X(32771)</h3>
Barycentrics &nbsp;&nbsp; a^2*(a^2*b^2 - a*b^3 + a^2*b*c - b^3*c + a^2*c^2 - a*c^3 - b*c^3) : :
<p>
Let E9 be the circumellipse centered at X(9).  Dan Reznik discovered a triangle indicated by arrows in the following video: <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=s-h72iorZKw&feature=youtu.be"> Thomson cubic of family of 3-periodics is E9</A>.  Peter Moses found (February 17, 2020) that its vertices, A', B', C' can by found by solving the following system for x,y,z:
</p>
<p>
a^2 z y^2 - a^2 y z^2  + b^2 x z^2 - b^2 z x^2 + c^2 y x^2 - c^2 x y^2 = 0 <br>
a y z + b z x + c x y = 0 <br>
x + y + z = 1
</p>
<p>
The vertices A', B', C' lie on these cubics: K002, K101, K317.  The tangents to E9 at A', B', C' form a triangle A'', B'', C'' whose vertices lie on the cubic K002 and on the following conic:
</p>
<p>
bc(b + c)x^2 + ca(c + a)y^2 + ab(a + b)z^2 + 2(bc + ca + ab)(ayz + bzx + cxy) = 0,
</p>
<p>
which passes through X(i) for i = 5540, 9359, 16554, 24578 and the vertices of the excentral triangle.  The perspector of this conic is X(6), and the center is X(36808).
</p>
