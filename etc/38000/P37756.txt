<p>
This preamble is based on notes contributed by Vu Thanh Tung, April 5, 2020.
</p>
<p>
Suppose that P = p : q : r and U = u : v : w are distinct points in the plane of a triangle ABC.  Let
</p>
<p>
A<sub>0</sub> = point of intersection, other than P, of the circles (PBC) and (PUA)<br>
A<sub>1</sub> = AP&cap;BC<br>
A<sub>2</sub> = point of intersection, other than A<sub>1</sub>, of the circle (PA<sub>0</sub>A<sub>1</sub>) and line BC, and define B<sub>2</sub> and C<sub>2</sub> cyclically<br>
</p>
<p>
The points A<sub>2</sub>,B<sub>2</sub>,C<sub>2</sub> are collinear; denote their line by v(P,U). Let
</p>
<p>
V(P,U) = trilinear pole of v(P,U).  The point V(P,U) is here named the <b>Vu pole of P and U</b>.
</p>
<p>
Let P* and U* denote the isogonal conjugates of P and U, respectively.  Then V(P,U) = V(U*,P*).
</p>
<p>
Examples:
</p>
<p>
V(X(1),X(3)) = X(3218)<br>
V(X(1),X(4)) = V(X(3),X(1)) = X(17923)<br>
V(X(1),X(5)) = X(24145) <br>
V(X(1),X(6)) = V(X(2),X(1)) = X(7292)<br>
V(X(1),X(9)) = X(26015)<br>
V(X(2),X(4)) = V(X(3),X(6)) = X(468) <br>

</p>
<p>
Peter Moses (April 5, 2020) found that
</p>
<p>
V(P, U) = q r (a^2 (q r u (u+v+w) - p v w (p+q+r)) - b^2 p u (w (p+q) - r (u+v)) - c^2 p u (v (p+r) - q (u+w))) : :
</p>
<p>
and that if U is on the Euler line, then V(X(2),U) is also on the Euler line.  Specifically, if U is given by the combo X(2) + k X(3), then
</p>
<p>
V(X(2), U) = (-a^2 - b^2 + c^2)(a^2 - b^2 + c^2)(-a^2 + b^2 + c^2) + a^2 (3 a^4 - 3 b^4 + 4 b^2 c^2 - 3 c^4) +  3 k a^2 (a^4 - b^4 + b^2 c^2 - c^4)  : :
