<p>
Parallels-conics are defined in the preamble just before <a href="ETCPart6.html#X10001">X(10001)</a>:
</p>

<blockquote>
    <i>Let A' be the line through a point P parallel to line BC.  Let A<sub>B</sub> = A'&cap;AB and A<sub>C</sub> = A'&cap;AC.  Define B<sub>C</sub> and C<sub>A</sub> cyclically, and define B<sub>A</sub> and C<sub>B</sub> cyclically.  The six points A<sub>B</sub>, B<sub>C</sub>, C<sub>A</sub>, A<sub>B</sub>, B<sub>C</sub>, C<sub>A</sub> lie on a conic, here named the parallels-conic of P, denoted by Cpar(P).</i>
</blockquote>
<p>
Also, the lines AB<sub>A</sub>, AC<sub>A</sub>, BC<sub>B</sub>, BA<sub>B</sub>, CA<sub>C</sub>, CB<sub>C</sub> are tangent to another conic, here named the <b>parallels-tangential-conic of P</b> and denoted by <i>TCpar(P)</i>. If P = x : y : z (barycentrics), then TCpar(P) has center O(P) and perspector W(P) given by</p>
<p>&emsp;&emsp;O(P) = 2*x^3 + x*y*(5*x + 3*y + 2*z) + x*z*(5*x + 2*y + 3*z) + 2*y*z*(x + y + z) : : </p>
<p>&emsp;&emsp;W(P) = (x + y)*(x + z)*(y*(x + y + z) + 2*x*z)*(z*(x + y + z) + 2*x*y) : : </p>
<p>
The type of TCpar(P) depends on the position of P into the regions determined by the cubic K327 and the uncatalogued cubic &sum;(x^3+5*(y+z)*y*z)+9*x*y*z=0. For P on any of these cubics, TCpar(P) is a parabola. There are no known centers on these cubics.
</p>
<p>A numeric analysis shows that there are four points such that TCpar(p) is a circle, one of which is interior to ABC. When ABC is the ETC-reference triangle with sides (a,b,c) = (6, 9, 13), this interior point has trilinear coordinates approximately equal to {3.6762895306825345540, 2.0858529720642513577, 0.49986341016334298439}.</p>
<p>If P lies in the infinity, or P=X(2) or P=X(14091) and a &le; b &le; c then O(P) = W(P) = X(2). </p>
