Centers and perspectors of Moses Conics: X(36949)-X(36957)

<div class="preamble-sect">
<p>
This preamble, based on notes by Peter Moses, was contributed by Clark Kimberling, March 10-11, 2020.
<p>
Suppose that U = u : v : w is a point. The <b>Moses conic of U</b>, denoted by M(U), is here defined by the barycentric equation
</p>
<p>
vwx<sup>2</sup> + wuy<sup>2</sup> + uvz<sup>2</sup> - u(v+w)yz - v(w+u)zx - w(u+v)xy = 0
</p>
<p>
Suppose that P = p : q : r lies on M(U).  Let CM(P) denote the circumconic of the medial triangle that has center P.  <b>Theorem: if P lies on M(U), then U lies on CM(P).</b>
</p>
<p>
The center of M(U) is 2u + v + w : 2v + w + u : 2 w + u + v .<br>
The perspector of M(U) is u/(u<sup>2</sup> + vw + 3wu + 3uv) : v/(v<sup>2</sup> + wu + 3uv + 3vw) : w/(w<sup>2</sup> + uv + 3vw + 3uv).
</p>
<p>
The appearance of (n,CM) in the following list means that the point U = X(n) lies on the conic CM for every P on M(X(n)):
</p>
<p>
(1, circumellipse of medial and incentral triangles); see X(34585))<br>
(2, Steiner inellipse)<br>

</p>
