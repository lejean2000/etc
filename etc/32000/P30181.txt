<p>
<i>Let </i>&Gamma;<i> be a conic and P a fixed point on it. The sides of any right angle with vertex P cut </i>&Gamma;<i> again in P' and P". Then all the lines P'P" intersect in a common point</i> Q<sub>&Gamma;</sub>(P).
<br>
(See: <a href="http://mathworld.wolfram.com/FregiersTheorem.html">Fr&egrave;gier's Theorem in MathWorld</a>)
</p>
<p>
This section deals with some named conics in the plane of a triangle ABC. The point Q<sub>&Gamma;</sub>(P) is denoted here as the <b>P-Fr&egrave;gier point of &Gamma;</b>.
</p>
<p>
It can be proved that if &Gamma; is a rectangular hyperbola then Q<sub>&Gamma;</sub>(P) lies in the line at infinity. Isogonal conjugates of these points in the infinity are also included in this section.
</p>
