<p class="preamble-title">
Centers associated with line-reflected triangles: X(30715)-X(30721)
</p>
<img src="rightri.svg" style="height:30px" alt="rightri">

<div class="preamble-sect">
<p>
In the plane of a triangle ABC, suppose that A'B'C' is a triangle and L is a line.  Let A'' be the reflection A' in L, and define B'' and C'' cyclically.  The triangle A''B''C'' is here named the <b>L-reflection of A'B'C'</b>.  If A'B'C' is a central triangle and L a central line, then A''B''C'' is a central triangle.
(Clark Kimberling), January 11, 2019)
</p>
Let T denote the Euler-line-reflection of ABC. Peter Moses  (January 12, 2019) found that T is perspective to the following triangles, with perspectors as indicated:

<p>
ABC: X(523)<br>
Schroeter (anticevian triangle of X(523); see X(8286), X(10276)): X(523)<br>
tangential: X(30715)<br>
Macbeath: X(30716)<br>
orthic-of-medial (anti-6th-mixtilinear; see X(11363)): X(30717)<br>
5th Euler (see X(3758): X(30718)<br>
circum-medial: X(23)<br>
Gemini 44: X(23)<br>
Gossard: X(30)<br>
reflection of ABC in X(3):  X(30)<br>
infinite altitude: X(74)<br>
circum-orthic:  X(186)<br>
Carnot (Johnson, the reflection of ABC in X(5)): X(30)<br>
Kosnita: X(186)<br>
</p>
<p>
The triangle T is also perspective to these triangles: Euler, Trinh, 2nd Euler, 5th Euler, Artzt, anti-Artzt, tangential of tangential, anti-1st-Euler, anti-Hutson intouch, anti-incircle-circles (see X(11363), orthic-of-medial, Ehrmann side-triangle.
</p>
<p>
The locus of a point P such that the cevian triangle of P is perspective to T is the cubic pK(14618,264).  The locus of P such that the anticevian triangle of P is perspective to T is the cubic pK(112,648).  (Peter Moses, January 13, 2019)
</p>
<p>
For the Nagel-line-reflection of ABC, see X(30719)-X(30721).
</p>
