<p><i>Let A'B'C' be the intouch triangle of ABC and (S<sub>a</sub>), (S<sub>b</sub>), (S<sub>c</sub>) their Soddy circles. Let A<sub>o</sub>B<sub>o</sub>C<sub>o</sub> be the outer-Soddy triangle of ABC (touchpoints of Soddy circles and the circle internally tangent to them) and  and A<sub>i</sub>B<sub>i</sub>C<sub>i</sub> the inner-Soddy triangle of ABC (touchpoints of Soddy circles and the circle externally tangent to them). Then quadrilaterals B'C'C<sub>o</sub>B<sub>o</sub> and B'C'B<sub>i</sub>C<sub>i</sub> are cyclic.</i>

(See <a href="http://www.hyacinthos.epizy.com/message.php?msg=28871">Antreas Hatzipolakis, Hyacinthos #28871</a>)</p>


<p>The circles (O<sub>a</sub>) and (I<sub>a</sub>) circumscribing B'C'C<sub>o</sub>B<sub>o</sub> and B'C'C<sub>i</sub>B<sub>i</sub>, respectively, will be named here the <b>2nd A-outer-Soddy circle</b> and <b>2nd A-inner-Soddy circle</b>, respectively and triangles O<sub>a</sub>O<sub>b</sub>O<sub>c</sub> and I<sub>a</sub>I<sub>b</sub>I<sub>c</sub> are called here the <b>2nd outer-Soddy triangle</b> and <b>2nd inner-Soddy triangle</b>, respectively. Barycentric coordinates of A-vertices are:</p>
<p>&emsp;&emsp;O<sub>a</sub> = (-a+b+c)*a-2*S : b*(-a+b+c) : c*(-a+b+c)</p>
<p>&emsp;&emsp;I<sub>a</sub> = (-a+b+c)*a+2*S : b*(-a+b+c) : c*(-a+b+c)</p>

<p>
Peter Moses found that vertices B<sub>o</sub>, C<sub>o</sub>, B<sub>i</sub>, C<sub>i</sub> are concyclic, and similarly the other quartets of vertices
(see <a href="http://www.hyacinthos.epizy.com/message.php?msg=28873">Hyacinthos #28873</a>).
Denote Q<sub>a</sub> the center of B<sub>o</sub>C<sub>o</sub>B<sub>i</sub>C<sub>i</sub> and cyclically Q<sub>b</sub> and Q<sub>c</sub>. The triangle Q<sub>a</sub>Q<sub>b</sub>Q<sub>c</sub> will be named here the <b>Soddy triangle</b> of ABC and has first vertex Q<sub>a</sub> = -1/(-a+b+c) :  1/(a-b+c) : 1/(a+b-c).
</p>
