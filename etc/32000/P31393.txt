Centroids of curvatures: X(31393)-X(31502)
</p>
<img src="rightri.svg" style="height:30px" alt="rightri">

<div class="preamble-sect">
<p>
This preamble and centers X(31393)-X(31502) were contributed by C&eacute;sar Eliud Lozada, February 08, 2019.
</p>
<p>
<i>Let</i>
(O<sub>1</sub>), (O<sub>2</sub>), (O<sub>3</sub>)
<i> be three circles with distinct and non-collinear centers. Denote</i>
I<sub>1</sub>, I<sub>2</sub>, I<sub>3</sub>
<i>the internal centers of similitude of</i>
{(O<sub>2</sub>), (O<sub>3</sub>)}, {(O<sub>3</sub>), (O<sub>1</sub>)} and {(O<sub>1</sub>), (O<sub>2</sub>)}
<i>, respectively. Then the lines </i>
O<sub>1</sub>I<sub>1</sub>, O<sub>2</sub>I<sub>2</sub>, O<sub>3</sub>I<sub>3</sub>
<i> concur</i>.
</p>
<p>Assume that exact trilinear coordinates of the centers are O<sub>i</sub> = ( U<sub>i</sub>, V<sub>i</sub>, W<sub>i</sub> ) and their radius are R<sub>i</sub> (i = 1..3). If M is the trilinear matrix of the centers then the given point of intersection Q is:</p>
<p>&emsp;&emsp; Q = M<sup>T</sup>.| &rho;<sub>1</sub>&emsp;&rho;<sub>2</sub>&emsp;&rho;<sub>3</sub> |<sup>T</sup>,&emsp;where &rho;<sub>i</sub> = 1/R<sub>i</sub>, i.e., &rho;<sub>i</sub> is the curvature of the circle (O<sub>i</sub>).</p>
<p>In fewer words:</p>
<p>&emsp;&emsp; Q = &Sum; &rho;<sub>i</sub>U<sub>i</sub> :  &Sum; &rho;<sub>i</sub>V<sub>i</sub> : &Sum; &rho;<sub>i</sub>W<sub>i</sub>, &emsp;i=1..3</p>
<p>Then, for obvious reasons, the point Q is here named here the <b>centroid of curvatures of the circles (O<sub>1</sub>), (O<sub>2</sub>), (O<sub>3</sub>)</b>.</p>
<p>Suppose a fourth circle (O<sub>4</sub>) is added to the above configuration and let Q<sub>&#8467;</sub> be the centroid of curvatures of (O<sub>i</sub>), (O<sub>j</sub>), (O<sub>k</sub>). Then the lines O<sub>i</sub>Q<sub>i</sub> concur at:</p>
<p>&emsp;&emsp; Q = &Sum; &rho;<sub>i</sub>U<sub>i</sub> :  &Sum; &rho;<sub>i</sub>V<sub>i</sub> : &Sum; &rho;<sub>i</sub>W<sub>i</sub>, &emsp;i=1..4</p>
<p>The last extension provides a geometrical recursive construction of the centroid of curvatures of any number n &ge; 3 of circles with distinct and not collinear centers.</p>

</p>
