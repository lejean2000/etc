<p>
<i>Let</i>
A<sub>o</sub>B<sub>o</sub>C<sub>o</sub>
<i> be the orthic triangle of ABC. Denote by A', B', C' the 1st isodynamic points X(15) of </i>
AB<sub>o</sub>C<sub>o</sub>, BC<sub>o</sub>A<sub>o</sub>
<i> and</i>
CA<sub>o</sub>B<sub>o</sub>,
<i> respectively, and A", B", C" the 2nd isodynamic points X(16) of</i>
AB<sub>o</sub>C<sub>o</sub>, BC<sub>o</sub>A<sub>o</sub>
<i>and</i>
CA<sub>o</sub>B<sub>o</sub>
<i>, respectively. Then the triangles A'B'C' and A"B"C" are equilateral</i>.
(Dao Thanh Oai, pers. comm., March 12, 2019).
</p>
<p>
The triangles A'B'C' and A"B"C" are here named here <b>3rd isodynamic-Dao</b> and <b>4th isodynamic-Dao</b> triangles, respectively. (See also triangles A*B*C* and A**B**C** constructed in Hyacinthos 28021 [Aug 11, 2018, Tran Quang Hung and Randy Hutson]).  These triangles have the following properties and relations:
