Conics associated to pairs of orthologic or parallelogic triangles: X(31353)-X(31375)

<img src="rightri.svg" style="height:30px" alt="rightri">

<p>
I) Conics associated to a pair of orthologic triangles:
</p>
<p>
<ol>
<li>Let T'=A'B'C' and T"=A"B"C" be two orthologic triangles. Denote a'<sub>b</sub> and a'<sub>c</sub> the perpendicular lines from A' to A"C" and A"B", respectively, and build b'<sub>c</sub>, b'<sub>a</sub>, c'<sub>a</sub>,c'<sub>b</sub> cyclically. Then these six lines are tangent to a conic &Phi;'<sub>t</sub>, here named the <b>orthologic tangential-conic T' to T"</b>.
<p>
Swap T' and T", repeat the above construction and name the respective lines a"<sub>b</sub>, a"<sub>c</sub>, b"<sub>c</sub>, b"<sub>a</sub>, c"<sub>a</sub>, c"<sub>b</sub>. Then these six lines are tangent to another conic &Phi;"<sub>t</sub>, here named the <b>orthologic tangential-conic T" to T'</b>.
</p>
</li>
<li>Let A'<sub>b</sub>=a'<sub>b</sub> &cap; B'C' and A'<sub>c</sub>=a'<sub>c</sub> &cap; B'C' and define B'<sub>c</sub>, B'<sub>a</sub>, C'<sub>a</sub>, C'<sub>b</sub> cyclically. Then these six points lie on a conic &Phi;'<sub>p</sub>, here named the <b>orthologic conic T' to T"</b>.
<p>
Swap T' and T", repeat the above construction and name the respective points A"<sub>b</sub>, A"<sub>c</sub>, B"<sub>c</sub>, B"<sub>a</sub>, C"<sub>a</sub>, C"<sub>b</sub>. Then these six points lie on another conic &Phi;"<sub>p</sub>, here named the <b>orthologic conic T" to T'</b>.
</p>
</li>
</ol>
<p>
II) Conics associated to a pair of parallelogic triangles:
</p>
<p>
<ol>
<li>Let T'=A'B'C' and T"=A"B"C" be two parallelogic triangles. Denote a'<sub>b</sub> and a'<sub>c</sub> the parallel lines from A' to A"C" and A"B", respectively, and build b'<sub>c</sub>, b'<sub>a</sub>, c'<sub>a</sub>,c'<sub>b</sub> cyclically. Then these six lines are tangent to a conic &Psi;'<sub>t</sub>, here named the <b>parallelogic tangential-conic T' to T"</b>.
<p>
Swap T' and T", repeat the above construction and name the respective lines a"<sub>b</sub>, a"<sub>c</sub>, b"<sub>c</sub>, b"<sub>a</sub>, c"<sub>a</sub>, c"<sub>b</sub>. Then these six lines are tangent to another conic &Psi;"<sub>t</sub>, here named the <b>parallelogic tangential-conic T" to T'</b>.
</p>
</li>
<li>Let A'<sub>b</sub>=a'<sub>b</sub> &cap; B'C' and A'<sub>c</sub>=a'<sub>c</sub> &cap; B'C' and define B'<sub>c</sub>, B'<sub>a</sub>, C'<sub>a</sub>, C'<sub>b</sub> cyclically. Then these six points lie on a conic &Psi;'<sub>p</sub>, here named the <b>parallelogic conic T' to T"</b>.
<p>
Swap T' and T", repeat the above construction and name the respective points A"<sub>b</sub>, A"<sub>c</sub>, B"<sub>c</sub>, B"<sub>a</sub>, C"<sub>a</sub>, C"<sub>b</sub>. Then these six points lie on another conic &Psi;"<sub>p</sub>, here named the <b>parallelogic conic T" to T'</b>.
</p>
</li>
</ol>
<p>
If T' and T" are orthologic (parallelogic) triangles and P is the orthologic (parallelogic) center T' to T", then the orthologic (parallelogic) conic T' to T" does not depend on T". Moreover, the orthologic conic T' to T" and the parallelogic conic T' to T" coincide. Therefore, when T' are P are given, a more convenient name for this conic &Phi;'<sub>p</sub>= &Psi;'<sub>p</sub> is <b>P-orthoparallelogic conic of T'</b>. A similar coincidence occurs for the tangential conics &Phi;'<sub>t</sub>= &Psi;'<sub>t</sub> and therefore a better name for this conic is <b>P-orthoparallelogic tangential-conic of T'</b>.
</p>
<p>If P = x:y:z (barycentrics) then the center of the P-orthoparallelogic conic of ABC is:</p>
<p>&emsp;&emsp;O&prime;<sub>p</sub> = x^2*(y+z)*((y+z)*(x^4-(y-z)^2*y*z)-x*(y^2+z^2)*(-x^2+y^2+z^2)-(y^3+z^3)*x^2) : :</p>
<p>and its perspector is:</p>
<p>&emsp;&emsp;Q&prime;<sub>p</sub> = x*(y+z)*F(x,y,z)*F(x,z,y) : :, where F(x,y,z) = (x^2*(2*x*y+x*z+2*z^2)-(y-z)*(2*y*((x+z)*y+z^2)+x*z*(y+z)))</p>
<p>The center of the P-orthoparallelogic tangential-conic of ABC is O'<sub>t</sub>=complement-of-P and its perspector is:</p>
<p>&emsp;&emsp;Q&prime;<sub>t</sub> = (x*(2*z+y)+y*(y+z))*(x*(2*y+z)+z*(y+z)) : :<p>
