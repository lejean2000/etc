<p class="preamble-title">
Points associated with cubics pK(U',U'): X(31610)-X(31643)
</p>
<img src="rightri.svg" style="height:30px" alt="rightri">
</header>
<div class="preamble-sect">
<p>
This preamble and centers X(31610)-X(31643) were contributed by Clark Kimberling and Peter Moses, February 20, 2019.
</p>
<p>
Suppose that U = u : v : w (barycentrics) is a point in the plane of a triangle ABC.  The <b>self-inverse Gemini triangle based on U</b> is defined as the triangle with vertices
</p>
<p>
A' = - u : v + w : v + w,&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; B' = - v : w + u : w + u,&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; C' = - w : u + v : u + v .
</p>
<p>
The triangle A'B'C' is perspective to the cevian triangle of a point X = x : y : z if and only if X lies on the cubic
</p>
<p>
v w (y - z) (y z - x y - x z) + w u (z - x )(z x - y z - y x) + u v (x - y)(x y - z x - z y) = 0,
</p>
<p>
this being, Bernard Gibert's notation (<a href="https://bernard-gibert.pagesperso-orange.fr/Classes/cl007.html "> <b>Parallel Tripolars Cubics</b></a>) the cubic pK(U',U'), where U' = isotomic conjugate of the crosspoint of X(2) and U; that is, U' = 1/(v+w) : 1/(w+u) : 1/(u+v).  Points on this cubic, which we denote by &Gamma;(U), include the following:
</p>
<p>
1 : 1 : 1 = X(2) = centroid<br>
v w : w u : u v = isotomic conjugate of U<br>
1/(v + w ) : 1/(w + u) : 1/(u + v) = isotomic conjugate of the complement of U<br>
u/(v + w ) : v/(w + u) : w/(u + v)<br>
v w/(v + w ) : w u/(w + u) : u v/(u + v)<br>
(-u + v + w)/(v + w ) : (u - v + w)/w + u) : (u + v - w)/(u + v)<br>
u - d : v - d : w - d, where d = sqrt(v w + w u + u v)<br>
u + d : v + d : w + d, where d = sqrt(v w + w u + u v)<br>
</p>
<p>
Let t1 = u^2(v+w) + v^2(w+u) + w^2(u+v) + uvw,  t2 = (-u+v+w)(u-v+w)(u+v-w), and t = sqrt(t1/t2).  Then two more points on the cubic are
</p>
<p>
u/(-u + v + w) - t : v(u - v + w) - t : w(u + v - w) - t <br>
u/(-u + v + w) + t : v(u - v + w) + t : w(u + v + w) - t
</p>
<p>
If U is a triangle center, then A'B'C' is a central triangle and those ten points on &Gamma;(U) are triangle centers.  Note that the points involving square roots are nonreal for some choices of U and a,b,c.
</p>
<p>
Let DEF be the cevian triangle of v w : w u : u v, so that D = 0 : 1/v : 1/w, E = 1/u : 0 : 1/w, F = 1/u : 1/v : 0.  The perspector of A'B'C' and DEF is the point
</p>
<p>
u(-u^2 + v^2 + w^2) : v(u^2 - v^2 + w^2) : w(u^2 + v^2 - w^2) = U*-Ceva conjugate of U, where U* = isotomic conjugate of U.
</p>
<p>
