Let DEF be the cevian triangle of X(5).   Let &ell;<sub>b</sub> be the perpendicular to BE at E, and let &ell;<sub>c</sub> be the perpendicular to CF at F.  Let A' = &ell;<sub>b</sub>&cap;&ell;<sub>c</sub>, and define B' and C' cyclically.  The triangles ABC and A'B'C' are orthologic (by construction), and the orthologic center of  A'B'C' respect to ABC is X(31868). (Angel Montesdeoca, March 13, 2021)
