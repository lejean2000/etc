<p class="preamble-title">
Collineation mappings involving Gemini triangle 101: X(30738)-X(30803)
</p>
<img src="rightri.svg" style="height:30px" alt="rightri">

<div class="preamble-sect">
<p>
Extending the preambles just before X(24537), X(26153), and X(27378), Gemini triangles A'B'C', indexed as 101 to 111, are introduced here, given by barycentrics for A', followed by the range of associated triangle centers.  Each range of centers is preceded by a preamble.
</p>
<p>
Gemini triangle 101:  A' = a^2 - b^2 - c^2 : 2 a^2 : 2 a ^2; range X(30738)-X(30803)<br>
Gemini triangle 102:  A' = a - b - c: 2 a : 2 a; range X(30808)-X(30869)<br>
Gemini triangle 103:  A' = - a^3 : b^3 + c^3 : b^3 + c^3; range X(30878)-X(30939)<br>
Gemini triangle 104:  A' = - b c : a(b + c) : a(b + c); range X(30942)-X(31007)<br>
Gemini triangle 105:  A' = - b - c : 2a + b + c : 2a + b + c; range X(31014)-X(31063)<br>
Gemini triangle 106:  A' = - b^2 - c^2 : 2a^2 + b^2 + c^2 : 2a^2 + b^2 + c^2; range X(31071)-X(31132)<br>
Gemini triangle 107:  A' = -1 : 2 : 2; range X(31133)-X(31181)<br>
Gemini triangle 108:  A' = 3a - b - c : 2(a - b - c) : 2(a - b - c); range X(31183)-X(31234)<br>
Gemini triangle 109:  A' = 1 : 2 : 2; range X(31235)-X(31283)<br>
Gemini triangle 110:  A' = 2 : 1 : 1; range X(31284)-X(31289)<br>
Gemini triangle 111:  A' = -3 : 1 : 1; range X(31290)-X(31205)<br>
</p>
<p>
Let m(X) denote the (A,B,C,X(2); A',B',C',X(2)) collineation image of X = x : y : z, where A'B'C' = Gemini triangle 101, as in centers X(30738)-X(30803).  Then
</p>
<p>
m(X) = a^3 x - (a + c) (a^2 - a c + c^2) y - (a + b) (a^2 - a b + b^2) z : :
</p>
<p>

and m(X) is a self-inverse mapping such that m(X) is collinear with X(2) and X; e.g., m(Euler line) = Euler line and m(Nagel line) = Nagel line.
Also, m(nine-point circle) = nine-point circle.  The fixed points of m are X(2) and every point on the line X(325)X(523), which is the isotomic conjugate of the circumcircle.  Among the fixed points are X(i) for these i:  2,  325, 523, 684, 850 ,3260, 3265, 3266, 3267, 20735, 30736, 30737.  (Clark Kimberling, January 19, 2019)
